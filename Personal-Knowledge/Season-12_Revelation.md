# SEASON 12 — REVELATION & PERSECUTION
### *The first revelation, the open call, the tortured believers, and a journey beyond the stars*
*(✅ Qur'an/Sahih · ⚠️ Weak/disputed · ❌ Isra'iliyyat · ❓ Unknown · Seerah: Ar-Raheeq al-Makhtum)*

---

# EPISODE 44 — "READ!"

## The Story
In the cave of **Hira**, in the month of Ramadan, the angel **Jibreel** suddenly appeared and commanded: *"Iqra!" — "Read!"* Muhammad ﷺ replied, *"I am not one who reads."* Jibreel **embraced him tightly** until he could bear no more, released him, and repeated the command — three times — and then revealed the **first words of the Qur'an**: *"Read in the name of your Lord who created…"* Trembling with awe, the Prophet ﷺ rushed home to Khadijah: *"Cover me! Cover me!"* She wrapped him and reassured him with timeless words: *"Never! By Allah, Allah will never disgrace you. You keep good relations, bear the burdens of the weak, give to the destitute, honor the guest, and help those struck by calamity."* She took him to her cousin **Waraqah ibn Nawfal**, a learned man, who recognized it instantly: *"This is the same Namus (Jibreel) that came to Musa. I wish I were young… for your people will drive you out."* The Prophet ﷺ was stunned: *"Will they drive me out?"* Waraqah: *"Yes. No man has ever brought the like of what you bring without being opposed."* Then revelation paused (the *fatrah*), and resumed with: *"O you who covers himself! Arise and warn!"*

## The Qur'an Says
- **96:1–5** — *"Read in the name of your Lord who created — created man from a clinging clot. Read, and your Lord is the most Generous — who taught by the pen — taught man that which he knew not."*
- **74:1–7** — *"O you who covers himself! Arise and warn! And your Lord glorify, and your garments purify…"*
- **93** (ad-Duha) — revealed during the pause, reassuring him Allah had not abandoned him.

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim** ('Aisha): the full account of the first revelation — the three embraces, *"Iqra,"* the trembling return, Khadijah's words, and Waraqah's confirmation.

## Historical / Theological Context
- The **first word "Read"** — in a largely illiterate society, to an unlettered Prophet ﷺ — elevated knowledge as central to Islam from the very first moment.
- The Prophet's ﷺ **fear and trembling** show his humanity and sincerity; he did not "expect" or "design" this.
- **Waraqah's testimony** independently confirmed the experience by reference to previous scripture-knowledge.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Reports that the Prophet ﷺ contemplated harming himself during the pause (*fatrah*) appear in some narrations of Bukhari's *balagh* (without full chain); scholars discuss them — handle carefully; the core account is sound.

## The Lessons
1. **Knowledge is sacred** — the first command was "Read," in Allah's name.
2. **Good character is a shield** — Khadijah's proof that Allah wouldn't disgrace him was his *track record of kindness.* Build that now.
3. **Truth invites opposition** — Waraqah warned that every truth-bearer is opposed; expect it, and persevere.

# EPISODE 45 — THE SECRET BECOMES PUBLIC

## The Story
For about **three years**, the call was quiet. The earliest believers (*as-Sabiqun al-Awwalun*) came in: **Khadijah** (first of all), **Abu Bakr** (the first free man), **Ali ibn Abi Talib** (the first child, ~10), and **Zayd ibn Harithah** (the freed servant) — then a trickle of sincere souls, often the poor and the enslaved. Then Allah commanded the Prophet ﷺ to go public: *"Warn your closest kindred,"* and *"Proclaim what you are commanded, and turn away from the polytheists."* He climbed **Mount Safa** and called the clans of Quraysh by name. When they gathered, he asked: *"If I told you an army was behind this mountain about to attack you, would you believe me?"* They answered, *"Yes — we have never known you to lie."* He said, *"Then I am a warner to you before a severe punishment."* His own uncle **Abu Lahab** snapped, *"May you perish! Is this why you gathered us?"* — and Allah revealed **Surah al-Masad** in response. The open call had begun, and with it, the hostility of Quraysh: mockery, slander, and pressure.

## The Qur'an Says
- **26:214** — *"And warn your closest kindred."*
- **15:94** — *"So proclaim what you are commanded, and turn away from the polytheists."*
- **111 (al-Masad)** — *"May the hands of Abu Lahab perish, and perish he… His wealth will not avail him… He will burn in a Fire of blazing flame…"*
- **74:1–7** — the command to "arise and warn."

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** the **Safa gathering** — *"…would you believe me?" "Yes, we have never experienced a lie from you."* — and Abu Lahab's outburst, occasioning Surah al-Masad.

## Historical / Theological Context
- The earliest Muslims were largely the **weak and enslaved** — those with the least worldly power but the most sincere hearts.
- The Quraysh's hostility was not because they doubted his honesty (they affirmed it at Safa!) — it was because *tawhid* threatened their idols, their status, and their economy (pilgrimage to the idols).

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Exact order of the very first converts (who was "first") — minor scholarly discussion (first woman: Khadijah; first free man: Abu Bakr; first child: Ali; first freed slave: Zayd; first enslaved man to go public: Bilal).

## The Lessons
1. **Truth threatens the comfortable** — they trusted him completely, yet turned on him when his message endangered their interests.
2. **Faith often takes root in the humble first** — the early believers were the "nobodies" of society, the richest in sincerity.
3. **Stand firm regardless of relation** — even his uncle's enmity didn't move the Prophet ﷺ from the truth.

**→ Next: The price of faith — torture, martyrdom, exile, and the Year of Sorrow.**

# EPISODE 46 — THE TORTURED BELIEVERS

## The Story
The powerless paid the highest price. **Bilal ibn Rabah**, an enslaved African, was dragged onto the burning sand by his master Umayyah, a heavy rock crushing his chest — yet he only repeated, *"Ahad! Ahad!" — "One! One!"* (Allah is One). **Abu Bakr** bought and freed him. The family of **Yasir** were tortured savagely; **Sumayyah**, Yasir's wife, was killed by Abu Jahl — becoming the **first martyr in Islam.** Her son **Ammar** was forced under torture to utter words of disbelief while his heart stayed firm — and Allah revealed permission for those *"compelled while their heart is at rest with faith"* (16:106). As persecution intensified, the Prophet ﷺ sent a group to migrate to **Abyssinia (Habashah)**, ruled by the just Christian king **An-Najashi (Negus)**. When Quraysh demanded their return, **Ja'far ibn Abi Talib** recited Surah Maryam in the king's court; the king wept and refused to hand them over. Then came the **boycott** — Banu Hashim were besieged in a valley for ~3 years, near starvation. And finally, the **Year of Sorrow ('Am al-Huzn)**: in a single year, the Prophet ﷺ lost both **Khadijah** and **Abu Talib.** When he sought support in **Ta'if**, its people drove him out with stones — yet when the Angel of the Mountains offered to crush them, he ﷺ refused, praying instead that their descendants might believe.

## The Qur'an Says
- **16:106** — *"…except one who is forced while his heart is secure in faith…"* (revealed about Ammar).
- **85:4–8** (the People of the Ditch) — recited as solace, on those burned for their faith — *"…they resented them only because they believed in Allah."*
- **39:10** — *"…the patient will be given their reward without measure."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari:** Khabbab ibn al-Aratt complained of the torture; the Prophet ﷺ reminded them of past believers sawn in half yet unwavering, and promised that Allah would complete this matter.
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** the Prophet's ﷺ response at **Ta'if** — when offered the destruction of its people, he said, *"Rather, I hope Allah will bring from their descendants those who worship Allah alone."*
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** the immense grief of losing Khadijah; her unmatched virtues.

## Historical / Theological Context
- **Sumayyah (R.A.)** — the first to give her life for Islam, an elderly enslaved woman: a permanent symbol that faith's nobility isn't tied to status.
- The **Negus** episode shows early Islam's relationship with sincere People of the Book, and the power of the Qur'an's account of Maryam and Isa to move a Christian king.
- The **Year of Sorrow** stripped the Prophet ﷺ of his two great supports — setting the stage for the divine gift of the Night Journey.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Some dramatic details of individual torture scenes vary across seerah sources; the core events (Bilal, Sumayyah, Ammar, Abyssinia, the boycott, Ta'if) are well-established.

## The Lessons
1. **Faith can cost everything — and the patient are rewarded without measure** (39:10).
2. **Mercy even toward persecutors** — Ta'if's stones were met with a prayer for their guidance, not their destruction. This is prophetic character.
3. **Sincerity outranks status** — an enslaved man (Bilal) and an elderly woman (Sumayyah) are among Islam's greatest heroes.

# EPISODE 47 — THE NIGHT JOURNEY (AL-ISRA WAL-MI'RAJ)

## The Story
At his lowest point — grief-stricken, rejected, persecuted — Allah honored His Prophet ﷺ with a miracle like no other. In a single night, Jibreel took him on the **Buraq** from the Sacred Mosque in **Makkah** to the **Al-Aqsa Mosque in Jerusalem** (*al-Isra*). There, he led **all the previous prophets in prayer** — the final Prophet leading Adam, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, and the rest. Then began the **Ascension (al-Mi'raj)**: he was raised through the **seven heavens**, meeting prophets at each level — Adam, Yahya and Isa, Yusuf, Idris, Harun, Musa, and Ibrahim — until he reached the **Sidrat al-Muntaha** (the Lote Tree of the utmost boundary), a station no creation had passed. There, Allah gifted this ummah its greatest treasure: the **five daily prayers** — originally **fifty**, reduced by Allah's mercy (with Musa repeatedly advising him to ask for reduction) to **five in practice, with the reward of fifty.** He returned the same night. When he told the Quraysh, they mocked him — but **Abu Bakr** said without hesitation, *"If he said it, then it is true,"* earning the title **As-Siddiq (the Truthful-Affirmer).**

## The Qur'an Says
- **17:1** — *"Glory to the One who took His servant by night from the Sacred Mosque to the Farthest Mosque (al-Aqsa), whose surroundings We blessed, to show him of Our signs…"*
- **53:1–18** — *"…He certainly saw of the greatest signs of his Lord… by the Lote Tree of the utmost boundary (Sidrat al-Muntaha)…"*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** the detailed accounts of the Isra and Mi'raj — the Buraq, leading the prophets in Jerusalem, the ascent through the heavens, meeting each prophet, Sidrat al-Muntaha, and the **prescription of the prayers (50 → 5)** with Musa's counsel.
- **Bukhari:** Abu Bakr's instant affirmation — earning *As-Siddiq.*

## Historical / Theological Context
- This was a **real, physical journey** (body and soul, while awake) — the majority position of Ahl as-Sunnah — not merely a dream.
- The timing is profound: after the **Year of Sorrow**, Allah lifted His Prophet ﷺ from the depths of grief to the heights of the heavens — a divine embrace after devastating loss.
- The **gift was salah** — Islam's daily, direct connection to Allah, brought down from the highest station. This is why prayer is so central.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The exact sequence/details of which prophet is in which heaven vary slightly between narrations (the core is sound and in Bukhari/Muslim).
- ❌ Highly embellished "tour of Paradise and Hell" details beyond the authentic narrations — be cautious of folk additions.

## The Lessons
1. **After hardship comes elevation** — *"Indeed, with hardship comes ease"* (94:6). The Mi'raj followed the Year of Sorrow.
2. **Prayer is a gift, not a burden** — it was brought from the highest heaven; treat it as your daily ascent to Allah.
3. **True faith trusts the truthful** — Abu Bakr's certainty (*"if he said it, it's true"*) is the model of conviction.
