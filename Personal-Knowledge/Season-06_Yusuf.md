# SEASON 6 — YUSUF (A.S.): BETRAYAL & DESTINY
### *"The best of stories" (12:3) — patience, chastity, forgiveness, and a divine plan hidden in every wound*
*(✅ Qur'an/Sahih · ⚠️ Weak/disputed · ❌ Isra'iliyyat · ❓ Unknown — Source: Surah Yusuf (12), told in full by Allah Himself)*

---

# EPISODE 22 — THE DREAM & THE JEALOUS BROTHERS

## The Story
Yusuf, the beloved son of the Prophet **Ya'qub (A.S.)**, had a dream: **eleven stars, the sun, and the moon** prostrating to him. His father, recognizing its meaning, warned him: *"Do not relate your vision to your brothers, lest they plot against you — indeed Shaytan is a clear enemy."* Yusuf's brothers already burned with envy, feeling their father favored him. Their jealousy hardened into a plot. They convinced Ya'qub to let Yusuf come "play" with them — then threw him into the bottom of a **well** and returned at night, weeping, with his shirt stained with false blood, claiming a wolf had devoured him. Ya'qub, unconvinced, responded with *"beautiful patience" (sabrun jameel)*.

## The Qur'an Says
- **12:3** — *"We relate to you the best of stories…"*
- **12:4–5** — the dream; Ya'qub's warning about Shaytan.
- **12:8–10** — *"Yusuf and his brother are more beloved to our father than we… kill Yusuf or cast him to a land…"*; one says, *"throw him into the bottom of the well."*
- **12:11–18** — the deception; the well; the false blood on the shirt; Ya'qub: *"…So patience is most fitting (fa-sabrun jameel)."*

## Historical / Theological Context
- The dream foreshadowed Yusuf's future rank (his parents + eleven brothers honoring him), fulfilled decades later (12:100).
- **Sabrun jameel** = patience without complaint to people, taking the matter only to Allah.
- The disease again: **envy (hasad)** — the same poison from Iblis and the sons of Adam.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The brothers' detailed ages/names and exact dialogue beyond the Qur'an — tafsir embellishment. (The brothers, the "asbat," are referenced collectively in the Qur'an.)

## The Lessons
1. **Guard blessings from envious eyes** — Ya'qub's advice to not broadcast the dream is timeless wisdom.
2. **Envy blinds even family** — it turned brothers into would-be murderers.
3. **Sabrun jameel** — when wronged, complain to Allah, not to creation.

**→ Next: From the well to slavery in Egypt — and Allah's hidden hand.**

# EPISODE 23 — SOLD INTO SLAVERY

## The Story
Alone in the dark well, Allah revealed to the frightened boy that one day he would remind his brothers of this deed *while they would not recognize him.* A passing caravan lowered a bucket — *"Good news! A boy!"* — and they took him as merchandise, selling him cheaply in **Egypt**. There, a powerful man (the **'Aziz**, Egypt's minister) bought him and told his wife to honor him: *"Perhaps he will benefit us, or we will adopt him as a son."* Thus — through betrayal, a well, and slavery — Allah was quietly **establishing Yusuf in the land**, preparing him for greatness.

## The Qur'an Says
- **12:15** — Allah's reassurance to Yusuf in the well.
- **12:19–20** — the caravan; *"O good news! Here is a boy!"*; sold for *"a reduced price, a few dirhams."*
- **12:21** — the 'Aziz to his wife: *"Make his residence comfortable…"*; *"And thus We established Yusuf in the land…to teach him the interpretation of events. And Allah is predominant over His affair, but most people do not know."*
- **12:22** — *"And when he reached maturity, We gave him judgment and knowledge…"*

## Historical / Theological Context
- The repeated theme: **what looks like ruin is Allah's setup.** The well led to Egypt; slavery led to the palace.
- *"Allah is predominant over His affair"* (12:21) — His plan never fails, even through human cruelty.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Names of the caravan leader / exact price details — tafsir, varied.

## The Lessons
1. **Trust the unseen plan** — your worst moment may be the doorway to your purpose.
2. **Allah is with the wronged** — He reassured Yusuf personally in the well.
3. **Character travels** — even as a slave, Yusuf's excellence shone, earning honor in a stranger's house.

**→ Next: The test that could have destroyed him — and he chose prison over sin.**

# EPISODE 24 — TEMPTATION & PRISON

## The Story
Yusuf grew into a man of extraordinary beauty and character. The **wife of the 'Aziz** (unnamed in the Qur'an), in whose house he lived, became infatuated. One day she locked the doors and called him to herself. Yusuf refused — *"I seek refuge in Allah! Indeed, he (your husband) is my master who has made good my residence. Wrongdoers never succeed."* He fled to the door; she tore his shirt **from behind** as he ran — and her husband appeared. A witness reasoned: if the shirt is torn from the front, she's truthful; from behind, he is. It was torn from behind — Yusuf was innocent. Later, the city women gossiped; she invited them, gave them knives, and had Yusuf appear — and they were so stunned by his beauty they **cut their own hands**. She then threatened him with prison if he refused her. Yusuf prayed: *"My Lord, prison is dearer to me than what they call me to."* And he was imprisoned — innocent.

## The Qur'an Says
- **12:23** — *"…she locked the doors and said, 'Come, you.' He said, 'I seek the refuge of Allah (ma'adhAllah)…'"*
- **12:24** — *"…and he would have inclined to her had he not seen the proof (burhan) of his Lord. Thus [it was] that We should avert from him evil and immorality. He was of Our chosen servants."*
- **12:25–29** — the torn shirt; the witness; her husband: *"…this is of your women's plot…"*
- **12:30–32** — the city women; the cut hands; her threat of prison.
- **12:33–35** — *"My Lord, prison is more to my liking than that to which they invite me…"*; he is imprisoned.

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** Among the **seven** whom Allah shades on the Day of Judgment is *"a man called by a woman of beauty and status, who says, 'I fear Allah.'"* — Yusuf's choice embodied.

## Historical / Theological Context
- **The woman is unnamed in the Qur'an.** "Zulaykha" is a **traditional/Isra'iliyyat** name — avoid presenting it as Qur'anic.
- **12:24 ("the proof of his Lord"):** scholars affirm Yusuf was **protected by Allah and did NOT sin** — the verse highlights his human struggle and divine protection; the *mukhlasin* (chosen/sincere) are shielded (cf. Iblis's admission, 15:40).
- *Iffah* (chastity) under pressure, with no one watching, is among the highest forms of *taqwa*.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ❌ Romanticized "Yusuf and Zulaykha" love-poems and later legends — folklore, not Islam.
- ⚠️ Over-detailed interpretations of 12:24 that suggest Yusuf nearly sinned — scholars caution against this; he was divinely protected.

## The Lessons
1. **Real strength = what you refuse for Allah** — especially when no one is watching and the door is locked.
2. **"Ma'adhAllah"** — flee to Allah's refuge the instant temptation strikes.
3. **Choose hardship over sin** — Yusuf preferred prison to disobedience; dignity over indulgence.

**→ Next: From a forgotten prisoner to the ruler of Egypt — and the reunion that defined forgiveness.**

# EPISODE 25 — FROM PRISONER TO POWER

## The Story
In prison, Yusuf called his fellow inmates to *tawhid* and interpreted two prisoners' dreams accurately. He asked the one who'd be freed to mention him to the king — but the man **forgot**, and Yusuf remained imprisoned for years. Then the **king had a dream** no one could interpret — seven fat cows eaten by seven lean ones, seven green ears and seven dry. The freed servant remembered Yusuf. From his cell, Yusuf interpreted it: **seven years of abundance, then seven years of famine** — so store grain. The king summoned him — but Yusuf first insisted his **name be cleared**; the women confessed the truth, and the 'Aziz's wife admitted, *"I tried to seduce him; he is truthful."* Yusuf was made **minister over Egypt's storehouses.** When famine struck, his brothers came begging for grain — not recognizing him. After testing them and reuniting with his full brother **Bunyamin**, Yusuf revealed himself and forgave them completely: *"No blame upon you today."* He sent his shirt to restore his blind father's sight; the family was reunited — and the childhood dream came true.

## The Qur'an Says
- **12:36–42** — the two prisoners; Yusuf's da'wah (*"Are diverse lords better, or Allah, the One?"*); the interpretations; he's forgotten.
- **12:43–49** — the king's dream; Yusuf's interpretation; the plan to store grain.
- **12:50–53** — Yusuf insists on clearing his name; the women confess; *"…I do not free myself [from blame]. Indeed, the soul is ever inclined to evil, except those upon which my Lord has mercy."*
- **12:54–57** — appointed: *"Appoint me over the storehouses of the land; I am a knowing guardian."*
- **12:58–93** — the brothers arrive; Bunyamin; the cup; the gradual reunion.
- **12:92** — *"No blame upon you today. May Allah forgive you; He is the most merciful of the merciful."*
- **12:96, 100** — the shirt restores Ya'qub's sight; the dream fulfilled; *"My Lord had certainly been good to me…"*
- **12:101** — Yusuf's final du'a: *"…cause me to die a Muslim and join me with the righteous."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari** (Abu Hurayrah): The Prophet ﷺ praised Yusuf's lineage of prophets: *"The noble, son of the noble, son of the noble, son of the noble: Yusuf, son of Ya'qub, son of Ishaq, son of Ibrahim."*
- **Bukhari:** On Yusuf's patience — when the messenger came to free him, he first sought to clear his name; the Prophet ﷺ expressed admiration for his composure (*"Had I stayed in prison as long as Yusuf, I would have responded to the caller"*).

## Historical / Theological Context
- **12:53** ("the soul inclines to evil…") — said either by Yusuf in humility or by the woman in confession; either way, a profound lesson on the *nafs*.
- Yusuf's insistence on **clearing his name first** = dignity + justice; he refused to leave under a cloud of suspicion.
- The **forgiveness** of those who ruined his life is the climax — power used for mercy, not revenge.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The king's name (later tradition gives names); the exact number of years forgotten ("bid'" = a few, 3–9 years, 12:42) — the Qur'an leaves it as "several years."

## The Lessons
1. **Every wound was a step to the throne** — the well, slavery, the false accusation, the prison: all were Allah weaving a destiny. *Trust the process.*
2. **Forgiveness is the highest power** — Yusuf could have crushed his brothers; instead, *"no blame upon you today."*
3. **Humility in success** — at the peak of power, Yusuf's final prayer was not for more dunya, but to die as a Muslim among the righteous (12:101).
4. **Clear your name with dignity, then forgive** — justice and mercy together.

## ✅ SEASON 6 COMPLETE
Allah called it *"the best of stories"* — and told it in full. A boy betrayed, enslaved, falsely accused, and imprisoned, who never let go of Allah and rose to rule Egypt, then forgave those who broke him. The thread: **Allah's timing is never late, and never wasted — and forgiveness is greater than revenge.**
