# SEASON 8 — KINGS OF POWER: DAWUD & SULAYMAN (A.S.)
### *Two prophet-kings given power unlike any other — and the lesson that all power ends at a single, silent death*
*(✅ Qur'an/Sahih · ⚠️ Weak/disputed · ❌ Isra'iliyyat · ❓ Unknown)*

---

# EPISODE 32 — THE BOY WHO KILLED A GIANT

## The Story
Long after Musa, the Children of Israel asked their prophet (traditionally Samuel) to appoint a king so they could fight in Allah's cause. Allah appointed **Talut (Saul)** — and the people objected because he wasn't wealthy. The prophet replied: *Allah chose him, and increased him in knowledge and stature.* The sign of his kingship was the return of the **Ark (Tabut)**, carried by angels. Marching to battle, Talut tested his army at a **river**: *whoever drinks from it is not with me, except a single handful.* Most drank — and lost heart. Only a faithful few crossed. Facing the giant **Jalut (Goliath)** and his army, the small band prayed for patience. And a young man stepped forward — **Dawud** — and with Allah's permission, **struck down the giant.** Allah gave Dawud kingship and prophethood, taught him, revealed to him the **Zabur (Psalms)**, made the **mountains and birds glorify Allah with him**, and **softened iron** in his hands so he could fashion armor.

## The Qur'an Says
- **2:246–251** — the request for a king; Talut chosen (*"increased him in knowledge and physique"*); the Ark; the river test; *"How many a small company has overcome a large company by Allah's permission"*; *"And Dawud killed Jalut, and Allah gave him kingship and wisdom and taught him…"*
- **21:78–80** — Dawud's judgment; *"We subjected the mountains to glorify with Dawud, and the birds… and We taught him the making of coats of armor."*
- **34:10–11** — *"…O mountains, repeat [Our] praises with him, and the birds. And We softened iron for him…"*
- **38:17–26** — Dawud's worship, strength, and just judgment; the two litigants.
- **17:55** — *"…and We gave Dawud the Zabur (Psalms)."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** *"The most beloved fasting to Allah was the fasting of Dawud — he would fast one day and not the next."* And *"the most beloved prayer to Allah was the prayer of Dawud — he would sleep half the night, pray a third, and sleep a sixth."*
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** Dawud's recitation was so beautiful it's referenced as *mizmar* (a sweet, melodious voice) — *"Abu Musa was given a 'mizmar' from the mizmars of the family of Dawud."*

## Historical / Theological Context
- **Talut/Jalut = Saul/Goliath.** The **Ark (Tabut)** held relics from the family of Musa and Harun, and *sakinah* (tranquility) — its return validated Talut.
- The **river test** filtered the sincere from the weak — *"how many a small company has overcome a large by Allah's permission"* (2:249) is a timeless principle.
- Dawud was both a mighty king and a humble, fasting, weeping worshipper — power *and* devotion together.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ❌ **Isra'iliyyat slandering Dawud** regarding the "two litigants" (38:21–24) — claiming he sinned to take another man's wife — these are **rejected**; prophets are protected from such major sins. The Qur'anic point is about *justice* and Dawud's quick repentance over a lapse in judgment.
- ⚠️ Details of the sling/stone and the duel with Jalut — tafsir; the Qur'an states simply that Dawud killed Jalut.

## The Lessons
1. **Allah judges by the heart and capacity, not wealth or status** — Talut was chosen for knowledge and strength, not riches.
2. **Victory is from Allah, not numbers** — a small, sincere band defeated a giant's army.
3. **Power must sit on a foundation of worship** — Dawud, a king, fasted half his life and wept before Allah.

# EPISODE 33 — THE KING WHO COMMANDED THE WIND

## The Story
**Sulayman** inherited prophethood and kingship from his father Dawud — and Allah gave him a dominion *unlike anyone before or after.* He was taught **the speech of birds and animals.** His armies included **humans, jinn, and birds**, marshaled in perfect order. The **wind** was placed under his command, carrying him a month's journey in a morning. The **jinn** worked for him — building, diving, crafting. Once, as his army marched, an **ant** warned its colony: *"O ants, enter your dwellings, lest Sulayman and his soldiers crush you unknowingly!"* Sulayman *smiled* at her words and — note this — instead of pride, he turned to gratitude: *"My Lord, enable me to be grateful for Your favor… and to do righteousness that pleases You."* He had asked Allah for *"a kingdom that will not belong to anyone after me"* — and it was granted.

## The Qur'an Says
- **27:15–16** — Dawud and Sulayman given knowledge; *"O people, we have been taught the language of birds, and we have been given of all things…"*
- **27:17–19** — the armies of jinn, men, and birds; the **ant**; Sulayman's smile and his prayer of gratitude.
- **21:81–82** — the wind subjected to him; the diving, working jinn.
- **34:12–13** — the wind (a month's journey morning and evening); molten copper; jinn building *"places of worship, statues, basins, and cauldrons."*
- **38:30–35** — Sulayman's excellence; *"My Lord… grant me a kingdom such as will not belong to anyone after me."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** The Prophet ﷺ mentioned a jinn (an 'ifrit) tried to disrupt his prayer, and he wanted to tie it to a pillar, *"but I remembered the supplication of my brother Sulayman: 'grant me a kingdom that will not belong to anyone after me' (38:35),"* so he let it go.
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** Sulayman once said he would visit all his wives in one night so each would bear a knight for Allah's cause, but didn't say *"in sha Allah"* — and none bore a child except one (a half-formed child). The Prophet ﷺ said: *"Had he said 'in sha Allah,' they would all have fought in Allah's cause."* (A lesson on saying *in sha Allah*.)

## Historical / Theological Context
- Sulayman's dominion (wind, jinn, animal speech) was a **unique gift**, never repeated — and he received it with *gratitude*, not arrogance (the opposite of 'Aad/Pharaoh).
- His **smile at the ant** and immediate turn to *shukr* is the model of how to hold power.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The "horses" incident (38:31–33) — interpretations differ (did he miss a prayer, or slaughter/dedicate the horses for Allah?); the precise meaning is debated. Avoid the Isra'iliyyat that he sinned.
- ⚠️ The "throne test" (38:34 — the body placed on his throne) — multiple interpretations; details uncertain.

## The Lessons
1. **The more you're given, the more gratitude (and responsibility) you owe** — Sulayman's first response to power was *shukr*.
2. **Say "in sha Allah"** — even a prophet-king's plan was incomplete without attaching it to Allah's will.
3. **Power is a test, not a trophy** — Sulayman used the mightiest dominion in history to serve Allah, not his ego.

# EPISODE 34 — THE QUEEN OF SHEBA

## The Story
Reviewing his birds, Sulayman noticed the **hoopoe (hudhud)** was missing. It returned with astonishing news: a powerful **queen** ruled the people of **Saba (Sheba)**, who had everything and a magnificent throne — *but she and her people worshipped the sun instead of Allah.* Sulayman sent a letter inviting her to submit to Allah. She tested him with a lavish **gift**, which he refused: *"What Allah has given me is better than what He has given you."* Then he asked who could bring her throne before she arrived; one who had **knowledge of the Book** brought it *"in the blink of an eye."* When the queen arrived, she was shown a palace with a floor of polished **glass** — she mistook it for water and lifted her garment, then realized her error. Recognizing the truth, she declared: *"My Lord, I have wronged myself, and I submit with Sulayman to Allah, Lord of the worlds."* — Later, Sulayman **died** while leaning on his staff; the jinn kept working, unaware, until a **termite** ate through the staff and he fell — proving the jinn do **not** know the unseen.

## The Qur'an Says
- **27:20–26** — the missing hoopoe; its report of Saba and the sun-worshipping queen.
- **27:27–35** — the letter; the queen consults her chiefs; she sends a gift to test him.
- **27:36–37** — Sulayman refuses the gift: *"…you rejoice in your gift? What Allah gave me is better…"*
- **27:38–40** — the throne brought *"before your glance returns to you"* by one *"who had knowledge of the Scripture."*
- **27:41–44** — the glass palace; she mistakes it for water; *"My Lord, I have wronged myself, and I submit with Sulayman to Allah, Lord of the worlds."*
- **34:14** — *"…nothing indicated to the jinn his death except a creature of the earth eating his staff. When he fell, the jinn realized that if they had known the unseen, they would not have remained in humiliating punishment."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- The Qur'anic narrative is the source; the death-by-termite (34:14) is a powerful Qur'anic teaching that **only Allah knows the unseen.**

## Historical / Theological Context
- **The queen is unnamed in the Qur'an.** "Bilqis" is a **traditional** name.
- The "one with knowledge of the Book" who moved the throne is **unnamed** (tradition says Asif ibn Barkhiya — not confirmed in Qur'an/Sahih).
- Sulayman used overwhelming power **to guide, not to crush** — da'wah, not conquest of ego.
- **Sulayman's death (34:14)** is the season's climax-lesson: the mightiest king who ever lived died quietly on his staff — *all power ends at death.*

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ❌ Isra'iliyyat about the queen's parentage/physical features (e.g., that she was part-jinn or had unusual legs) — folklore; the Qur'an only mentions the glass floor and her submission.
- ⚠️ The identity of the throne-mover (Asif ibn Barkhiya) — traditional, not confirmed.

## The Lessons
1. **Use your strengths to guide others to Allah, not to dominate them** — Sulayman's goal was the queen's *faith*, not her humiliation.
2. **True greatness is bowing to the truth** — a powerful queen humbled herself the moment she recognized Allah.
3. **All power ends at a single death** — even Sulayman, master of wind and jinn, died unnoticed on his staff. *Only Allah is eternal; only Allah knows the unseen.*

## ✅ SEASON 8 COMPLETE
Two prophet-kings: one who toppled a giant and wept in worship, and one who commanded the wind yet died on his staff. The thread: **power is a test, gratitude is the response, and every kingdom — however vast — ends at a silent death. Only Allah remains.**
