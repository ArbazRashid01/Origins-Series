# SEASON 13 — HIJRAH & THE RISE OF MADINAH
### *The migration that reset the calendar, the first Muslim nation, and the battles that decided Islam's survival*
*(✅ Qur'an/Sahih · ⚠️ Weak/disputed · ❌ Isra'iliyyat · ❓ Unknown · Seerah: Ar-Raheeq al-Makhtum)*

> Battles are recorded soberly — never glorifying violence. The focus is the lessons: trust, obedience, sacrifice, and Allah's help.

---

# EPISODE 48 — THE ESCAPE

## The Story
Hope arrived from **Yathrib** (later Madinah): pilgrims from its tribes met the Prophet ﷺ, embraced Islam, and at the **Pledges of Aqabah** swore to protect him as their own. As Muslims began migrating there, the Quraysh grew alarmed and hatched a final plot: **a young man from every clan** would strike the Prophet ﷺ together, so the blood-guilt would be spread among all clans and Banu Hashim couldn't retaliate. On the chosen night, **Ali ibn Abi Talib** bravely slept in the Prophet's ﷺ bed as a decoy, while the Prophet ﷺ slipped out — and the assassins never saw him leave. With **Abu Bakr**, he hid in the **Cave of Thawr.** The pursuers came so close that Abu Bakr whispered in fear, *"If one of them looks down at his feet, he will see us."* The Prophet ﷺ replied with perfect calm: ***"Do not grieve; indeed, Allah is with us."*** The searchers turned back. After a perilous desert journey (including the pursuer **Suraqah**, whose horse sank into the sand), they reached Madinah to a joyous welcome.

## The Qur'an Says
- **8:30** — *"And [remember] when those who disbelieved plotted against you to imprison you, kill you, or expel you. They plan, and Allah plans — and Allah is the best of planners."*
- **9:40** — *"…the second of two, when they were in the cave, when he said to his companion, 'Do not grieve; indeed Allah is with us.' So Allah sent down His tranquility (sakinah) upon him…"*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari:** the Hijrah account — the Cave of Thawr, *"Do not grieve, Allah is with us,"* Abu Bakr's devotion, and the journey.
- **Bukhari:** the story of **Suraqah ibn Malik**, who pursued them for the bounty but whose horse repeatedly sank into the ground.

## Historical / Theological Context
- The **Hijrah** is so pivotal that 'Umar (R.A.) later began the **Islamic (Hijri) calendar** from it — it marked the birth of the Muslim *community/state.*
- *"Allah is with us"* (9:40) is the spiritual peak: total trust at the moment of maximum danger.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The famous **spider's web and the two pigeons** at the mouth of the Cave of Thawr (camouflaging it) — this is a **weak narration**; the authentic account ("Allah is with us," the sakinah) needs no embellishment. Often retold, but not sound.

## The Lessons
1. **Plan fully — then trust Allah completely.** The Prophet ﷺ planned the escape meticulously *and* relied on Allah; both together.
2. **"Allah is with us"** — the believer's anchor in the most frightening moments.
3. **Sacrifice for the truth** — Ali risked his life in the bed; Abu Bakr risked everything as companion.

# EPISODE 49 — THE FIRST MUSLIM CITY

## The Story
The Prophet ﷺ entered **Yathrib** to overwhelming joy; it became **Madinat an-Nabi** — the City of the Prophet. His first acts built a *nation:* He established the **mosque** (first at Quba, then the Prophet's Mosque) as the heart of the community — a place of worship, learning, governance, and unity. He forged the **Brotherhood (Mu'akhah)** between the **Muhajirun** (the emigrants who left everything in Makkah) and the **Ansar** (the helpers of Madinah) — pairing them so that the Ansar shared their homes, wealth, and land with selfless generosity. He drew up the **Constitution of Madinah (the Sahifah)** — a remarkable document organizing rights and duties among the Muslims and the city's other groups, including the Jewish tribes, into one mutual-defense community. From a town of feuding tribes, a society founded on **faith, brotherhood, and justice** was born.

## The Qur'an Says
- **59:9** — *"…[the Ansar] love those who emigrated to them and find no desire in their hearts for what they were given, but give preference over themselves, even though they are in need. And whoever is protected from the stinginess of his soul — those are the successful."*
- **3:103** — *"…remember the favor of Allah upon you — when you were enemies and He brought your hearts together, and you became, by His favor, brothers…"*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari:** the Brotherhood — e.g., **Sa'd ibn ar-Rabi'** (Ansari) offered **Abdur-Rahman ibn Awf** (Muhajir) half his wealth and more; Abdur-Rahman replied, *"May Allah bless your wealth and family — just show me the marketplace,"* and built his own success.
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** the building of the mosque; the institution of the **adhan** (call to prayer), seen in a dream by Abdullah ibn Zayd and approved by the Prophet ﷺ.

## Historical / Theological Context
- The **mosque-first** approach shows that a community's foundation is its bond with Allah.
- The **Ansar's generosity** (59:9) is one of the most beautiful examples of brotherhood in human history.
- The **Constitution of Madinah** established a pluralistic, treaty-based society under one law — early evidence of justice and coexistence.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Minor details of the Constitution's exact clauses vary in transmission; its substance is well-documented (Ibn Ishaq).

## The Lessons
1. **Build your life around the remembrance of Allah** — the mosque came first.
2. **Brotherhood over tribalism** — Islam dissolved blood-feuds into a brotherhood of faith.
3. **Generosity that prefers others over yourself** (59:9) — the highest social ethic.

**→ Next: Makkah is not finished with them — the Battle of Badr.**

# EPISODE 50 — THE BATTLE OF BADR

## The Story
In **2 AH**, the first major battle came. About **313** Muslims — poorly armed, with few horses and camels — faced a Quraysh army of around **1,000.** The night before, the Prophet ﷺ stood in prayer through the night, raising his hands until his cloak fell, begging: *"O Allah, if this band is destroyed today, You will not be worshipped on earth."* Allah answered: He sent **angels** to support the believers, cast fear into the enemy, and granted a decisive victory. The Prophet ﷺ threw a handful of dust toward the enemy — and the Qur'an says, *"You did not throw when you threw, but Allah threw."* The tyrants of Quraysh, including **Abu Jahl**, were killed. The Qur'an named this day **"the Day of the Criterion" (Yawm al-Furqan)** — the day truth and falsehood were distinguished. It was the turning point that announced Islam to all of Arabia.

## The Qur'an Says
- **3:123** — *"Allah had already given you victory at Badr while you were few in number, so fear Allah that you may be grateful."*
- **8:9** — *"…I will reinforce you with a thousand angels, following one another."* (also 3:124–125: 3,000 then 5,000)
- **8:17** — *"You did not kill them, but Allah killed them. And you (Prophet) did not throw when you threw, but Allah threw…"*
- **8:41** — the spoils, and the mention of *"the day of the Criterion, the day the two armies met."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Muslim:** the Prophet's ﷺ intense **du'a** the night before Badr; Abu Bakr comforting him.
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** the angels at Badr; the killing of Abu Jahl (by two young Ansari boys); the aftermath.

## Historical / Theological Context
- Badr proved that **victory is from Allah, not numbers** — a small, sincere force overcame a much larger one.
- The Prophet's ﷺ **du'a + full preparation** together is the model of *tawakkul* (reliance with effort).

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Exact angel-numbers and how they fought — narrations describe their presence; precise mechanics vary.

## The Lessons
1. **Rely on Allah AND prepare fully** — the Prophet ﷺ organized the army *and* prayed all night.
2. **Victory is by Allah's will, not by might** — 313 defeated 1,000.
3. **Sincere du'a moves the heavens** — angels descended in answer to his prayer.

**→ Next: Victory breeds overconfidence — the painful lesson of Uhud, and the siege of the Trench.**

# EPISODE 51 — UHUD & THE TRENCH

## The Story
In **3 AH**, Quraysh returned for revenge at **Uhud** with ~3,000. The Prophet ﷺ placed **archers** on a hill with a strict order: *do not leave your posts, whatever happens.* The Muslims were winning — but when the enemy fled and spoils appeared, **most of the archers abandoned their positions** to collect them, disobeying the command. The cavalry commander **Khalid ibn al-Walid** (then still a disbeliever) seized the gap, attacked from behind, and turned victory into devastating loss. Many companions were martyred — including the Prophet's ﷺ beloved uncle **Hamza** — and the Prophet ﷺ himself was wounded (his tooth broken, his face bloodied). The Qur'an gently diagnosed the cause: disobedience and the love of spoils. Two years later (**5 AH**), Quraysh returned with a **confederate army of ~10,000** to wipe out Madinah. On the advice of **Salman al-Farisi**, the Muslims dug a **trench (Khandaq)** around the exposed parts of the city. Through bitter cold, hunger, and the betrayal of a tribe within, they held. Then Allah sent a **fierce wind and unseen forces** that scattered the confederates — and they withdrew without victory.

## The Qur'an Says
- **3:152** — *"…until you lost courage and disputed about the order and disobeyed after He had shown you what you love (victory)…"*
- **3:153** — the chaos of Uhud; *"…so He repaid you with distress upon distress so you would not grieve over what escaped you…"*
- **3:165–166** — *"…Say, 'It is from yourselves' …"* (the loss was a consequence of the lapse).
- **33:9–25** (al-Ahzab — the Trench) — *"O you who believe, remember the favor of Allah upon you when armies came to you and We sent against them a wind and forces you did not see…"* (33:9); the trial, the believers' steadfastness, and the enemy's retreat (33:25).

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** the events of Uhud — the archers, Hamza's martyrdom, the Prophet's ﷺ wounds.
- **Bukhari:** the digging of the trench; the hunger; the miracles of food being blessed; the Prophet ﷺ digging alongside the companions.

## Historical / Theological Context
- **Uhud's lesson** is searing: a single act of **disobedience to the Prophet's ﷺ command** — driven by the desire for spoils — turned a winning battle into a disaster. Discipline matters most *at the moment of apparent success.*
- The **Trench** showed innovation (Salman's idea), unity, patience under siege — and that ultimate deliverance is from Allah (the wind).

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Some emotional details of the battlefield and named individual duels vary across seerah sources.

## The Lessons
1. **Your biggest mistakes come near victory** — Uhud fell apart at the moment of winning, through greed and broken discipline.
2. **Obedience over personal opinion** — the archers "knew better" and left their posts; obedience to the Prophet's ﷺ command was the test.
3. **Do your part, then trust the unseen** — they dug the trench (effort); Allah sent the wind (His help).

## ✅ SEASON 13 COMPLETE
A miraculous escape, the birth of the first Muslim nation built on brotherhood and justice, and three defining battles — Badr (trust → victory), Uhud (disobedience → painful lesson), the Trench (patience → deliverance). The thread: **victory is from Allah, through sincere effort, obedience, and trust — and even defeats are teachers.**
