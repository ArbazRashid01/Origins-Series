# SEASON 7 — MUSA (A.S.): THE PRINCE WHO DEFIED A GOD-KING
### *The most-mentioned prophet in the Qur'an — a baby in a river, a fugitive shepherd, the one Allah spoke to, and the man who broke an empire*
*(✅ Qur'an/Sahih · ⚠️ Weak/disputed · ❌ Isra'iliyyat · ❓ Unknown)*

---

# EPISODE 26 — BORN UNDER A DEATH SENTENCE

## The Story
**Pharaoh** had made himself a god-king over Egypt, enslaving the **Children of Israel** (descendants of Ya'qub/Yusuf). Fearing a prophecy that a boy from them would end his reign, he ordered every newborn Israelite **boy killed.** Into this terror, Musa was born. Allah inspired his mother to **nurse him, then place him in a basket in the river** — promising, *"Do not fear or grieve; We will return him to you and make him a messenger."* The basket floated to **Pharaoh's own palace.** Pharaoh's righteous wife (**Asiya**) saved him: *"A comfort of the eye for me and you — do not kill him."* The baby refused every wet-nurse, until his own sister "happened" to suggest a woman — his **own mother** — and so Allah returned him to her, *"that her eye might be comforted and she would not grieve."*

## The Qur'an Says
- **28:3–6** — Pharaoh's tyranny; *"We wished to favor those oppressed and make them leaders and inheritors."*
- **28:7** — the inspiration to his mother: *"…and if you fear for him, cast him into the river… We will return him to you and make him one of the messengers."*
- **28:8–9** — the palace finds him; Asiya: *"A comfort of the eye for me and for you…"*
- **28:10–13** — the mother's anxious heart; the sister watches; he refuses wet-nurses; returned to his mother.
- **20:38–40** — the same account; *"…that My eye might be raised over you (cared for)."*
- **66:11** — Pharaoh's wife (Asiya) held up as an example of faith: *"My Lord, build for me a house near You in Paradise…"*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** Among the perfect/complete women is **Asiya, the wife of Pharaoh** (and Maryam) — the Qur'an names her role; her *name* is from authentic tradition.

## Historical / Theological Context
- The Children of Israel had gone from Yusuf's honored family to enslaved people — fortunes turn; only faith endures.
- The exquisite irony: the child meant to topple Pharaoh was **raised in Pharaoh's house, on Pharaoh's wealth.**
- *"Pharaoh"* is a title; the Qur'an does **not** give his personal name.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The specific "dream of Pharaoh" / advisors' prophecy details — tafsir, varying strength (the broad fear is implied by 28:6).
- ⚠️ "Asiya" as the name — authentic via hadith tradition (not in the Qur'an text itself).

## The Lessons
1. **Allah opens the road from the very place you fear** — the river (a death-threat) carried Musa to safety and power.
2. **A mother's trust + Allah's promise** — she let go of her baby into a river *because* she trusted Allah's word.
3. **Tyranny has an expiry date** — no Pharaoh, however godlike, outlasts Allah's decree.

# EPISODE 27 — THE PRINCE WHO RAN

## The Story
Musa grew up in the palace, given strength, wisdom, and knowledge. One day he came upon two men fighting — one of his people, one of Pharaoh's. He intervened and **struck the Egyptian with his fist — and the man died.** Musa had not intended to kill; he immediately turned to Allah: *"My Lord, I have wronged myself, so forgive me"* — and Allah forgave him. (This was before prophethood.) Warned that the chiefs were plotting to kill him, Musa **fled to Madyan.** Exhausted and alone, he helped two women water their flock — asking nothing — then prayed, *"My Lord, I am in need of whatever good You send me."* The women's father, a righteous elder, offered Musa marriage to his daughter in exchange for working for him for eight to ten years. Musa became a shepherd in exile.

## The Qur'an Says
- **28:14–16** — *"…he struck him and killed him. He said, 'This is from Shaytan's doing…'"*; *"My Lord, I have wronged myself, so forgive me."*; Allah forgave him.
- **28:18–21** — the warning to flee; *"My Lord, save me from the wrongdoing people."*
- **28:22–24** — Madyan; watering the flock; *"My Lord, I am, for whatever good You send me, in need."*
- **28:25–28** — the elder's daughters; the marriage offer (work 8–10 years).
- **20:40** — *"…and you killed someone, but We saved you from retaliation and tried you with a [severe] trial…"*

## The Authentic Hadith
- The Madyan elder is traditionally identified as **Shu'ayb (A.S.)**, though the Qur'an does **not** name him in Musa's story; scholars differ (some say a righteous man of Shu'ayb's people).

## Historical / Theological Context
- The killing was **accidental/unintended** and **before prophethood**; Musa repented sincerely and was forgiven — a prophet's pre-prophetic slip, handled honestly by the Qur'an.
- The "wilderness years" in Madyan were Allah's **training ground** — humility, patience, shepherding — preparing him for the mission.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Whether the elder was Shu'ayb himself — **scholarly difference**, not certain.
- ⚠️ Detailed identities of the two women / which daughter he married — tafsir.

## The Lessons
1. **Take responsibility for your mistakes** — Musa blamed himself ("I have wronged myself"), never made excuses; the door to forgiveness opened immediately.
2. **Do good even when your own life is falling apart** — broke and hunted, he still helped strangers.
3. **Your "stuck" season is preparation** — Allah was shaping a shepherd into a liberator.

**→ Next: A fire on a mountain — and Allah speaks to him directly.**

# EPISODE 28 — THE BURNING BUSH

## The Story
Years later, traveling with his family on a cold night, Musa saw a **fire** on the side of a mountain (**Mount Tur**, in the sacred valley of **Tuwa**). He went to fetch a burning branch for warmth — and instead found the greatest moment of his life. Allah called to him directly: *"Indeed, I am Allah. There is no deity except Me, so worship Me and establish prayer for My remembrance."* Musa is **Kalimullah** — the one Allah *spoke to.* Allah gave him two miraculous signs: his **staff turned into a serpent**, and his **hand emerged shining white** (without disease). Then came the mission: *"Go to Pharaoh; indeed, he has transgressed."* Musa, afraid and aware of his speech difficulty, prayed for his chest to be expanded and for his brother **Harun** to support him — and Allah granted it all.

## The Qur'an Says
- **20:9–16** — the fire; *"Indeed, I am your Lord, so remove your sandals; you are in the sacred valley of Tuwa."*; *"Indeed, I am Allah… so worship Me and establish prayer for My remembrance."*
- **20:17–23** — the staff/serpent; the white hand — *"that We may show you Our greater signs."*
- **27:7–12 / 28:29–35** — the same call; the two signs; *"Go to Pharaoh; he has transgressed."*
- **20:25–36** — *"My Lord, expand for me my chest, ease my task, untie the knot from my tongue… and appoint for me a minister from my family — Harun, my brother."* — granted.
- **4:164** — *"…and Allah spoke to Musa with [direct] speech."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- Musa's special status as **Kalimullah** is affirmed; he is among the five *Ulul-'Azm* (the resolute messengers).

## Historical / Theological Context
- The first command to Musa was **tawhid + salah** — worship and prayer; the foundation before any mission.
- Musa's fear and his du'a show that **even great prophets feel inadequate** — and turn that fear into reliance on Allah.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Over-detailed descriptions of the bush/light — tafsir; keep to the Qur'an.

## The Lessons
1. **Allah meets you in the ordinary** — Musa went for firewood and received prophethood. Stay open.
2. **Fear is human; faith is the response** — Musa didn't deny his fear; he asked Allah for help and obeyed.
3. **Worship and prayer come first** — before changing the world, anchor your bond with Allah.

**→ Next: The showdown — Musa returns to the god-king who claimed divinity.**

# EPISODE 29 — THE MAGICIANS' SHOWDOWN

## The Story
Musa and Harun went to **Pharaoh** and called him — *gently* (as Allah commanded: *"speak to him a gentle word"*) — to worship Allah and free the Children of Israel. Pharaoh, drunk on power, sneered: *"I am your lord, the most high,"* and *"I know of no god for you other than me."* He ordered his minister **Haman** to build a tower to "climb up to Musa's God." He accused Musa of magic and arranged a grand public contest with the empire's greatest **magicians** on a festival day. The magicians threw their ropes and staffs, which appeared to slither as snakes. Then Musa threw his staff — and it became a real serpent that **swallowed their illusions whole.** In that instant, the magicians — Pharaoh's own experts — saw undeniable truth and fell into **prostration: "We believe in the Lord of the worlds, the Lord of Musa and Harun."** Pharaoh, enraged, threatened to crucify them and cut off their hands and feet. But they had tasted real faith and no longer feared death: *"…we only desire that our Lord forgive us… Our Lord, pour patience upon us and let us die as Muslims."* (Among the believers was also a **secret believer from Pharaoh's own household** who courageously defended Musa.)

## The Qur'an Says
- **20:43–44** — *"Go, both of you, to Pharaoh… and speak to him gently…"*
- **79:24** — Pharaoh: *"I am your most exalted lord."* **28:38** — *"I have not known a god for you other than me."*; the tower for Haman.
- **7:103–126** — the contest; the staff swallows their magic; the magicians prostrate and believe; Pharaoh's threats; *"…and make us die as Muslims."*
- **20:56–73 / 26:10–51** — the full showdown.
- **40:28–45** — the **believing man of Pharaoh's family** who concealed his faith and defended Musa: *"Would you kill a man for saying, 'My Lord is Allah'?"*

## The Authentic Hadith
- The Qur'an's account is the primary source; the magicians' instant martyr-faith is a celebrated lesson in tafsir and khutbahs.

## Historical / Theological Context
- **Pharaoh's claim of godhood** is the ultimate arrogance — the human echo of Iblis.
- The magicians began the day seeking reward from Pharaoh and ended it as **martyrs** — proof that *truth recognized by a sincere heart outweighs the fear of death.*
- The difference between Pharaoh and the magicians was **the heart, not the knowledge** — Pharaoh *knew* the truth (27:14: *"they rejected them out of injustice and arrogance, while their souls were convinced"*) yet refused.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Exact numbers of magicians (tens/hundreds of thousands in some reports) — tafsir, varied.

## The Lessons
1. **Call to truth with gentleness — even to tyrants** (20:44). Harshness wasn't Musa's opening move.
2. **The heart, not the IQ, accepts truth** — Pharaoh knew and refused; the magicians saw and surrendered.
3. **Faith can erase the fear of death in an instant** — yesterday's hired magicians became today's martyrs.

**→ Next: Pharaoh's refusal brings the plagues — and a midnight escape.**

# EPISODE 30 — THE PLAGUES & THE ESCAPE

## The Story
Despite the magicians' conversion, Pharaoh hardened and intensified the persecution. So Allah sent a series of **signs and punishments** upon Pharaoh's people: years of drought and crop failure, then the **flood, locusts, lice, frogs, and blood** — clear, distinct signs. Each time disaster struck, they begged Musa to pray for relief, promising to believe — and each time it lifted, **they broke their promise.** Finally, Allah commanded Musa to **lead the Children of Israel out by night.** Pharaoh, discovering their flight, pursued them at dawn with his entire army. The Israelites reached the **sea** — trapped, the army closing in behind. They cried, *"We are surely caught!"* But Musa, with unshakable certainty, said: *"No! Indeed, with me is my Lord; He will guide me."*

## The Qur'an Says
- **7:130** — years of drought and shortage of fruits.
- **7:133** — *"So We sent upon them the flood and locusts and lice and frogs and blood as distinct signs, but they were arrogant…"*
- **7:134–135** — they beg, promise, then break the promise each time.
- **17:101** — *"We gave Musa nine clear signs…"*
- **26:52–59** — the command to travel by night; Pharaoh's pursuit.
- **26:61–62** — *"…the companions of Musa said, 'Indeed, we are to be overtaken!' [Musa] said, 'No! Indeed, with me is my Lord; He will guide me.'"*
- **44:23 / 20:77** — *"Travel with My servants by night… strike for them a dry path through the sea."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** The Prophet ﷺ found the Jews of Madinah fasting **'Ashura** (10th of Muharram) because *"it is the day Allah saved Musa and his people and drowned Pharaoh."* The Prophet ﷺ said, *"We are more deserving of Musa than you,"* and fasted it and ordered fasting it.

## Historical / Theological Context
- The plagues were **mercy-warnings** — chances to repent — not just punishment.
- The repeated **broken promises** are a mirror of human nature: we turn to Allah in crisis, then forget in comfort.
- *"With me is my Lord; He will guide me"* — the summit of *yaqeen* (certainty) at the moment of zero options.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The exact "nine signs" enumeration differs slightly among scholars (the five in 7:133 + staff + hand + drought + crop loss).

## The Lessons
1. **Don't be a "foxhole believer"** — promising Allah in crisis then forgetting in ease is the disease of Pharaoh's people.
2. **Certainty in the impossible moment** — when trapped with no way out, the believer says *"my Lord is with me."*
3. **Gratitude through 'Ashura** — the salvation of Musa is honored by Muslims to this day.

**→ Next: The sea splits — and even after the miracle, the people build a golden calf.**

# EPISODE 31 — THE SEA SPLITS

## The Story
Allah commanded Musa: *"Strike the sea with your staff."* The sea **split into two**, each side standing like a great mountain, leaving a dry path. The Children of Israel crossed to safety. Pharaoh and his army charged in after them — and as they were in the midst of the sea, the walls of water **collapsed**, drowning them all. As he drowned, Pharaoh — who had claimed godhood — screamed, *"I believe that there is no deity except the One in whom the Children of Israel believe!"* But it was too late: *"Now? When you had disobeyed before…?"* Allah preserved Pharaoh's **body as a sign** for later generations. Yet — astonishingly — soon after this colossal miracle, while Musa went to commune with his Lord at Mount Tur for forty nights, the Children of Israel made and worshipped a **golden calf** (crafted by the **Samiri**). Musa returned in grief and anger; the people repented. (It was also here that Musa, in his love, asked, *"My Lord, show me Yourself" — and the mountain crumbled* at Allah's manifestation, teaching that He cannot be seen in this world.)

## The Qur'an Says
- **26:63–66** — *"Strike the sea with your staff." And it parted, each portion like a great mountain… then We drowned the others."*
- **20:77–79** — the dry path; Pharaoh and his soldiers were covered by the sea.
- **2:50** — *"…We parted the sea and saved you and drowned the people of Pharaoh while you looked on."*
- **10:90–92** — Pharaoh's drowning confession; *"Now? When you had disobeyed before…? So today We save you in body, that you may be a sign…"*
- **20:83–98** — the golden calf; the **Samiri**; Musa's anger; *"…we were made to carry burdens of the people's ornaments…"*; the calf destroyed.
- **7:142–143** — Musa's forty nights; *"My Lord, show me Yourself…"* — the mountain crushed to dust; Musa fell unconscious.
- **7:148–156** — the calf; repentance; Allah's mercy.

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** 'Ashura — the day Allah drowned Pharaoh and saved Musa.
- The preservation of Pharaoh's body (10:92) is often connected by modern discussion to mummified remains — but the Qur'anic point is the **sign**, not any specific identification (which is speculative).

## Historical / Theological Context
- **"Show me Yourself" (7:143):** Allah told Musa he could not bear to see Him *in this life*; the believers will see Allah in the Hereafter (a core Sunni belief). No depiction of Allah — only a wave of light shattering the mountain.
- The **golden calf** after the sea-splitting is the season's most sobering lesson: **witnessing miracles does not guarantee faith — the heart must truly change.**
- The **Samiri** is named in the Qur'an (20:85) as the calf's maker.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Detailed identification of Pharaoh's mummy / "which dynasty" — modern speculation, not established by revelation.
- ⚠️ Elaborate Samiri backstory (that he took dust from Jibreel's horse's hoofprint to animate the calf) — based on 20:96 but the *details* are tafsir of varying strength.

## What We Don't Know
- ❓ The exact location of the crossing; Pharaoh's identity; the precise nature of the Samiri's trick.

## The Lessons
1. **Seeing isn't believing — the heart must change.** People who watched the sea split worshipped a calf weeks later. Faith is a daily, living choice.
2. **Repentance has a deadline** — Pharaoh's faith at the moment of death was rejected; don't postpone returning to Allah.
3. **Allah is beyond our sight in this world** — humility before His majesty (7:143); we'll be honored with His vision in Paradise.

## ✅ SEASON 7 COMPLETE
The largest prophetic arc in the Qur'an: a baby saved from genocide, a fugitive trained in exile, the one Allah spoke to, the confronter of a god-king, and the witness to the sea splitting — and to how quickly people forget. The thread: **all the power on earth is nothing before Allah; true safety is only in trusting Him.**
