# SEASON 11 — THE WORLD BEFORE MUHAMMAD ﷺ
### *A world of idols and buried daughters — and the orphan everyone called "the Trustworthy"*
*(✅ Qur'an/Sahih · ⚠️ Weak/disputed · ❌ Isra'iliyyat · ❓ Unknown · Seerah: Ar-Raheeq al-Makhtum, Ibn Hisham)*

> The Seerah begins. By the deen's ethics, we never depict the Prophet ﷺ, his family, or the companions — we honor them in words. Sources: Qur'an + Sahih hadith + verified Seerah.

# EPISODE 41 — A WORLD IN DARKNESS

## The Story
Centuries after Isa was raised, his message had been distorted and the world slid back into darkness. Nowhere was this clearer than in **Arabia**, in an age the Arabs themselves later called **al-Jahiliyyah** — "the Age of Ignorance." The **Kaaba** — the very house Ibrahim built for the worship of the One God — now stood surrounded by **360 idols.** Tribes raided and slaughtered one another over generations-long blood feuds. The strong devoured the weak. Women were inherited like property. And in the most chilling cruelty of all, some fathers **buried their newborn daughters alive**, out of shame or fear of poverty. Yet Allah preserved His house: when the Abyssinian governor **Abraha** marched on Makkah with an army and an **elephant** to destroy the Kaaba, Allah sent flocks of birds (*ababil*) that pelted them with stones of baked clay, annihilating them. This was the **Year of the Elephant** — and, by the strongest reports, the year the final Prophet ﷺ was born. The world was waiting, in its deepest night, for a dawn.

## The Qur'an Says
- **105 (al-Fil)** — *"Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with the companions of the elephant? Did He not make their plan go astray? And He sent against them birds in flocks, striking them with stones of hard clay, making them like eaten straw."*
- **106 (Quraysh)** — the security and provision Allah gave Quraysh, caretakers of the Kaaba — *"So let them worship the Lord of this House."*
- **81:8–9** — *"And when the buried infant girl is asked, for what sin she was killed."* (condemning female infanticide)
- **16:58–59** — the disgrace they felt at a baby girl, hiding from people — *"…Unquestionably, evil is what they decide."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari:** On the day of the Conquest, there were **360 idols** around the Kaaba; the Prophet ﷺ struck them, reciting, *"Truth has come and falsehood has vanished"* (17:81).
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** The Prophet ﷺ described the age and praised the few who retained goodness; the general moral condition of Jahiliyyah is well-documented in seerah.

## Historical / Theological Context
- *Jahiliyyah* was not a lack of literacy or sophistication (the Arabs had rich poetry, trade, and lineage-pride) — it was *moral and spiritual* ignorance: shirk, injustice, tribalism.
- It wasn't *all* darkness — there were noble customs (hospitality, courage, keeping one's word) that Islam would later refine and elevate.
- The **Year of the Elephant** (~570 CE) framed the Prophet's ﷺ birth with a divine sign protecting the Kaaba.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The exact year of the Elephant event vs. the Prophet's ﷺ birth — strongly associated, widely held, with minor scholarly discussion.
- ⚠️ Vivid Abraha legends beyond the Qur'an's account — seerah details vary.

## The Lessons
1. **The darkest hour precedes the dawn** — when the world seemed beyond saving, Allah's light was about to be born.
2. **Allah protects His symbols** — the Kaaba was defended by birds against an empire's army.
3. **Recognize your era's "Jahiliyyah"** — every age has its idols and injustices; the believer's job is not to drift with them.

# EPISODE 42 — THE ORPHAN OF MAKKAH

## The Story
He was born into the noble tribe of **Quraysh**, of the clan of **Banu Hashim** — but into hardship. His father, **Abdullah**, died *before he was even born.* His mother, **Amina**, named him **Muhammad** ("the Praised One"). As was the custom, he was sent to the desert to be nursed by **Halimah as-Sa'diyyah**, whose household was visibly blessed by his presence. When he was about **six**, his mother Amina died — and he became a complete orphan. His loving grandfather **Abdul-Muttalib** took him in, but died just two years later. Then his uncle **Abu Talib** raised him with deep love. Through all this loss, the young Muhammad ﷺ grew into a person of such honesty, integrity, and trustworthiness that all of Makkah — a city of idolaters — called him **"Al-Amin" (the Trustworthy)** and **"As-Sadiq" (the Truthful).** People left their valuables with him for safekeeping. His character was his testimony, long before a single verse was revealed.

## The Qur'an Says
- **93:6–8** — *"Did He not find you an orphan and give [you] refuge? And He found you lost and guided [you]. And He found you poor and made [you] self-sufficient."*
- **94:1–4** — *"Did We not expand for you your breast?… and raised high for you your repute."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Muslim** (the splitting of the chest, *shaqq as-sadr*): angels opened the young Muhammad's ﷺ chest, purified his heart, and removed *"the portion of Shaytan"* — preparing him from childhood. (Occurred in his early years with Halimah, and again before the Mi'raj.)
- **Bukhari:** before prophethood he was known for truthfulness — the Quraysh themselves affirmed at the Safa gathering, *"We have never known you to lie."*
- **Seerah (Ibn Hisham):** the **Black Stone arbitration** — when the Quraysh rebuilt the Kaaba (he was ~35) and nearly came to war over who would place the Black Stone, they agreed the first to enter would judge. It was Muhammad ﷺ. He placed the stone on a cloak and had each tribal chief lift a corner together — a solution of brilliant wisdom that averted bloodshed.

## Historical / Theological Context
- His orphanhood and hardship were part of Allah's shaping — *"Did He not find you an orphan and give refuge?"* (93:6).
- His pre-prophetic reputation (**Al-Amin**) is crucial: when he later proclaimed prophethood, his lifelong honesty was undeniable — they could call him many things, but never a liar (until it suited them).

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The **monk Bahira** story (recognizing signs of prophethood in the boy during a trade caravan) — narrated in at-Tirmidhi and seerah; **scholars discuss its chain** (some grade parts weak). Often retold, but treat with caution.
- ⚠️ Various childhood miracle-tales — many are seerah details of differing strength.

## The Lessons
1. **Character is the greatest proof** — Muhammad ﷺ won hearts through honesty *before* he ever preached.
2. **Allah elevates through hardship** — orphanhood, poverty, and loss were the soil of greatness.
3. **Wisdom prevents bloodshed** — the Black Stone episode shows leadership that unites rather than divides.

# EPISODE 43 — KHADIJAH & THE CAVE

## The Story
Muhammad's ﷺ honesty caught the attention of **Khadijah bint Khuwaylid** — a noble, wealthy, and respected businesswoman of Makkah. She hired him to lead her trade caravan to Syria, and was so impressed by both his profits and his **character** that she proposed marriage. He was about **25**, she about **40.** It was a marriage of deep love, loyalty, and tranquility — she would become his greatest support and the **first to believe** in him. As the years passed, Muhammad ﷺ grew increasingly troubled by the idolatry and injustice of his society. He began withdrawing — sometimes for days — to a cave on **Mount Hira (Jabal an-Nur)**, devoting himself to reflection and worship (*tahannuth*), searching for the truth a broken world had forgotten. It was here, in the solitude and silence, that everything was about to change.

## The Qur'an Says
- **93:3** — *"Your Lord has not forsaken you, nor does He hate [you]."* (later comfort, but reflects the divine care surrounding him)
- The cave retreats set the stage for **96:1–5** (next season).

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim** ('Aisha — the beginning of revelation): *"The commencement of revelation was in the form of true dreams… Then solitude became dear to him, and he would go to the cave of Hira to worship for many nights…"*
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** the immense virtues of **Khadijah** — *"Allah never gave me anyone better than her… she believed in me when people disbelieved, she affirmed my truthfulness when people called me a liar, and she comforted me with her wealth when people deprived me."* Jibreel conveyed Allah's greeting and glad tidings of a house in Paradise for her.

## Historical / Theological Context
- Khadijah's role is monumental: the **first Muslim**, the Prophet's ﷺ rock through the hardest years. Their marriage is a model of partnership built on character and faith.
- The **retreats to Hira** show that the greatest mission was preceded by stillness, reflection, and worship — a turning toward Allah even before revelation.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Exact ages (25/40) come from seerah; widely reported, with minor variation in some accounts.
- ⚠️ Detailed accounts of the Syria trade journeys — seerah, varying detail.

## The Lessons
1. **Marry on character and faith** — Khadijah chose Muhammad ﷺ for his integrity; theirs became the greatest of marriages.
2. **A righteous spouse is a mercy in hardship** — Khadijah's support carried the Prophet ﷺ through his darkest trials.
3. **Seek stillness with Allah** — in a noisy, distracted world, the cave of Hira reminds us that truth and clarity are found in solitude and worship.
