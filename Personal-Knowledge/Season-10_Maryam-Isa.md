# SEASON 10 — A MIRACLE IS BORN: ZAKARIYA, MARYAM & ISA (A.S.)
### *Two impossible births, and the true Islamic account of the Messiah*
*(✅ Qur'an/Sahih · ⚠️ Weak/disputed · ❌ Isra'iliyyat · ❓ Unknown)*

> ⚠️ The most theologically sensitive season. In Islam, Isa (A.S.) is a mighty **servant and messenger of Allah** — NOT divine, NOT the son of God, NOT part of a trinity — and (per the Qur'an) **was not crucified, but raised by Allah.** Stated with respect, never mockery.

---

# EPISODE 37 — THE PRAYER IN THE TEMPLE (ZAKARIYA & YAHYA)

## The Story
The prophet **Zakariya (A.S.)** was old, his bones weakened, his hair white — and he and his wife had no children. He was the guardian of **Maryam**, a pure girl devoted to worship in the temple. Whenever he entered her prayer-chamber, he found **out-of-season provision** beside her. When he asked, she said, *"It is from Allah; Allah provides without measure for whom He wills."* This filled Zakariya with hope: the God who sends fruit out of season can give a child to the old. He prayed — privately, from the depths of his heart — for a righteous heir. Allah answered with glad tidings of a son named **Yahya (John)** — a name no one had borne before. Astonished, Zakariya asked how, given his age and barren wife; Allah gave him a **sign**: he would not speak to people for three days, except by gesture. Yahya grew into a prophet of remarkable purity, devotion, and kindness.

## The Qur'an Says
- **3:37** — Maryam's miraculous provision; *"Allah provides for whom He wills without account."*
- **3:38–41** — Zakariya's prayer; glad tidings of Yahya; *"My sign is that you will not speak to people for three days except by gesture."*
- **19:2–11** — *"…my bones have weakened, and my head has filled with white… I fear the successors after me… so give me an heir."*; the sign of silence.
- **19:12–15** — *"O Yahya, take the Scripture with determination." …kindness, purity, dutiful to his parents… "Peace be upon him the day he was born, the day he dies, and the day he is raised alive."*
- **21:89–90** — Zakariya's call; *"…they used to hasten to good deeds and supplicate Us in hope and fear."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- Yahya (John the Baptist) is among the noble prophets; the Prophet ﷺ met him in the heavens during the Mi'raj (Bukhari & Muslim).

## Historical / Theological Context
- This unfolds among the **Children of Israel** under Roman rule, in an age of religious legalism — Allah sent renewal through these prophets.
- Zakariya's *private, persistent du'a* despite "impossible" odds is the model of never giving up on Allah.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Yahya's martyrdom (beheading) — recorded in Isra'iliyyat/biblical tradition and some reports; the *details* are not firmly established in sahih sources, though his high status is certain.
- ⚠️ Elaborate details of Maryam's upbringing/guardianship (drawing lots with pens, 3:44) — the casting of lots is Qur'anic; surrounding details vary.

## The Lessons
1. **Never stop making du'a** — Zakariya was old and "past hope," yet Allah answered. Your circumstances don't limit Allah's power.
2. **Provision is from Allah, by means you can't predict** — out-of-season fruit, a child to the barren.
3. **Raise the next generation in devotion** — Zakariya's worry was a *righteous* heir, not just an heir.

**→ Next: If Allah can give a child to the old, He can give one with no father at all.**

# EPISODE 38 — THE CHOSEN WOMAN (MARYAM)

## The Story
**Maryam (Mary)** — daughter of 'Imran — was pure, devout, and chosen by Allah above all the women of the worlds. One day, in her seclusion, an **angel (Jibreel) appeared to her in the form of a perfect man.** Startled, she said, *"I seek refuge in the Most Merciful from you, if you fear Allah."* He replied that he was a messenger from her Lord, sent *"to give you [news of] a pure boy."* Bewildered, she asked, *"How can I have a boy, when no man has touched me, and I am not unchaste?"* The answer is the foundation of Islamic belief about Isa: *"Thus it is; your Lord says, 'It is easy for Me… and We will make him a sign to the people and a mercy from Us.'"* For Allah, creating a child without a father is no harder than creating Adam **with no parents at all** — He simply says *"Be," and it is.*

## The Qur'an Says
- **3:42** — *"O Maryam, indeed Allah has chosen you and purified you and chosen you above the women of the worlds."*
- **3:45–47** — *"…glad tidings of a word from Him, whose name is the Messiah, Isa, son of Maryam, distinguished in this world and the Hereafter…"*; *"How can I have a child when no man has touched me?"*; *"…when He decrees a matter, He only says to it, 'Be,' and it is."*
- **19:16–21** — the angel as a man; *"I seek refuge in the Most Merciful from you…"*; *"I am only the messenger of your Lord to give you a pure boy."*; *"…it is easy for Me… a sign for the people and a mercy from Us."*
- **3:59** — *"Indeed, the example of Isa to Allah is like that of Adam. He created him from dust; then He said, 'Be,' and he was."* — the key verse refuting his divinity.
- **66:12** — *"And Maryam, who guarded her chastity… and believed in the words of her Lord… and was of the devoutly obedient."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** *"The best of the women of the world are four: Maryam bint 'Imran, Asiya, Khadijah, and Fatimah."* (wordings vary).
- **Bukhari:** *"Every child is touched by Shaytan at birth… except Maryam and her son,"* (connected to the prayer of Maryam's mother for protection — 3:36).

## Historical / Theological Context
- **"A word from Allah" (kalimah):** This means Isa was *created by* Allah's word *"Be"* — **NOT** that he is divine or "the Word made flesh." 3:59 makes the parallel explicit: like **Adam**, created by *"Be."*
- Maryam is the **only woman named in the Qur'an**, and an entire surah (19) bears her name — a position of immense honor.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Detailed Isra'iliyyat about Maryam's daily miracles and conversations — keep to the Qur'an.

## The Lessons
1. **"Be, and it is"** — nothing is impossible for Allah. A child with no father is as easy for Him as Adam with no parents.
2. **Purity and worship raise a person's rank** — Maryam was *chosen* through her devotion.
3. **Honor Maryam as Islam does** — with deep love and respect, while affirming she is a righteous servant, not divine.

**→ Next: Unmarried and pregnant, she faces her people — and a newborn speaks.**

# EPISODE 39 — THE BABY WHO SPOKE (THE BIRTH OF ISA)

## The Story
Maryam withdrew to a far, lonely place. The pains of childbirth drove her to the trunk of a **palm tree**, and in her anguish she cried, *"Oh, I wish I had died before this and been forgotten!"* A voice comforted her from below: *"Do not grieve; your Lord has placed a stream beneath you. And shake the trunk of the palm — fresh, ripe dates will fall. So eat and drink and be comforted."* She was told to vow a **fast of silence** — to let the miracle speak for itself. When she returned to her people carrying the baby, they were scandalized: *"O sister of Harun! Your father was not an evil man, nor your mother unchaste!"* Maryam simply **pointed to the baby.** They scoffed: *"How can we speak to one in the cradle?"* — and then the newborn **Isa spoke**, declaring the very first words of his mission: ***"Indeed, I am the servant of Allah. He gave me the Scripture and made me a prophet… and dutiful to my mother…"***

## The Qur'an Says
- **19:22–26** — the palm; *"I wish I had died before this…"*; the comforting voice; the stream; the dates.
- **19:26** — *"…say, 'I have vowed to the Most Merciful a fast (of silence), so I will not speak today to any human.'"*
- **19:27–28** — the accusation; *"O sister of Harun…"*
- **19:29** — she points to the baby; *"How can we speak to one who is in the cradle a child?"*
- **19:30–33** — *"He (the baby) said, 'Indeed, I am the servant of Allah. He has given me the Scripture and made me a prophet. He has made me blessed wherever I am, and has enjoined upon me prayer and zakah as long as I remain alive — and [made me] dutiful to my mother… And peace is on me the day I was born, the day I will die, and the day I am raised alive.'"*

## The Authentic Hadith
- Isa speaking in the cradle is among the proofs of his prophethood and Maryam's innocence; the Prophet ﷺ mentioned only three (or a few) who spoke in the cradle as infants.

## Historical / Theological Context
- **"Sister of Harun" (19:28):** This does **NOT** mean Prophet Harun (brother of Musa, who lived ~1,200 years earlier). Scholars explain it refers either to a righteous man named Harun of her own time/lineage, or it's an expression of her belonging to a pious line. (Supported by a hadith in Muslim where the Prophet ﷺ clarified people of that era named themselves after prophets and righteous people.)
- **Isa's first recorded words — *"I am the servant of Allah"*** — are the cornerstone: he declared his *servanthood*, not divinity, at the very start.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Details of how many days/years between events, the exact location — tafsir/unknown.

## The Lessons
1. **When you're truthful and wronged, let Allah defend you** — Maryam said nothing; Allah made a newborn speak in her defense.
2. **Comfort comes in hardship** — at her most desperate, Allah gave her water, food, and reassurance.
3. **Isa's mission from word one: servanthood to Allah** — the foundation of who he truly is.

**→ Next: His miracles, his message, the plot — and the truth about the crucifixion.**

# EPISODE 40 — MIRACLES & THE PLOT

## The Story
Isa grew and was sent as a messenger to the **Children of Israel**, given the **Injil (Gospel)** and astonishing miracles — *all by Allah's permission.* He fashioned a bird from clay and breathed into it, and it became a living bird **by Allah's leave.** He healed the **blind** and the **leper**, and even **raised the dead** — *by Allah's leave.* His message never wavered: *"Indeed, Allah is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him. This is the straight path."* A loyal group, the **Hawariyyun (disciples)**, believed and supported him; for them Allah even sent down a **table spread with food (al-Ma'idah)** from heaven as a sign. But the leaders plotted to kill him. They schemed — and Allah out-planned them. The Qur'an states plainly: **they did not kill him, nor did they crucify him; it was made to appear so to them.** Instead, **Allah raised Isa up to Himself.** Isa is a servant and messenger of Allah — and he will **return** before the end of time. On the Day of Judgment, Isa himself will testify that he never told people to worship him.

## The Qur'an Says
- **3:48–51** — taught the Scripture, wisdom, the Torah and Gospel; the miracles *"by permission of Allah"* (clay bird, healing the blind & leper, raising the dead); *"Indeed, Allah is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him."*
- **3:52–53** — the **Hawariyyun**: *"We are the helpers of Allah; we believe in Allah…"*
- **5:110** — Allah recounts His favors upon Isa, repeating *"by My permission"* for each miracle.
- **5:112–115** — the **table (al-Ma'idah)** sent from heaven.
- **4:157–158** — *"…they did not kill him, nor did they crucify him, but it was made to appear so to them… Rather, Allah raised him to Himself. And Allah is Mighty, Wise."*
- **4:171–172** — *"…the Messiah, Isa, son of Maryam, was but a messenger of Allah and His word… So believe in Allah and His messengers. Do not say 'Three'… Allah is but One God…"*; *"The Messiah would never disdain to be a servant of Allah…"*
- **5:72–75** — refutation of his divinity; *"The Messiah, son of Maryam, was not but a messenger… his mother was a supporter of truth. They both ate food…"*
- **5:116–118** — on the Day of Judgment, Isa is asked if he told people to worship him and his mother; he replies, *"Glory be to You! It was not for me to say what I had no right to… I only said what You commanded me: 'Worship Allah, my Lord and your Lord.'"*
- **19:36** — *"And indeed, Allah is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him. That is a straight path."*
- **43:61** — *"And indeed, he (Isa) will be a sign for [the coming of] the Hour…"* (his return).
- **61:6** — Isa foretold a messenger to come after him *"whose name is Ahmad"* (the Prophet ﷺ).

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** The Prophet ﷺ foretold the **return (descent) of Isa** near the end of times — he will *"break the cross, kill the swine, abolish the jizyah,"* rule justly by the Shari'ah of Muhammad ﷺ, and the false messiah (Dajjal) will be destroyed in his time.
- **Bukhari:** *"Whoever testifies that there is no god but Allah… and that Isa is the servant of Allah, His messenger, and His word which He bestowed on Maryam and a spirit from Him… Allah will admit him to Paradise."*

## Historical / Theological Context
- **Every miracle is "by Allah's permission"** — this is repeated deliberately (3:49; 5:110) to show Isa's power was *granted*, not inherent; he is a servant, not a deity.
- **The crucifixion (4:157):** The Qur'an categorically denies that Isa was killed or crucified; Allah raised him bodily. Scholars differ on the *details* of who was made to resemble him, but agree on the core: **Isa was not crucified.** Present this with respect toward Christians, not mockery.
- **The trinity and divinity are explicitly rejected** (4:171; 5:72–75) — yet Isa is honored as one of the greatest messengers (Ulul-'Azm).

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ The identity of the one made to resemble Isa (a disciple? the betrayer?) — **tafsir speculation**, not definitively established. The Qur'an only says *"it was made to appear so."*
- ❌ Detailed biographies/sayings attributed to Isa from non-Islamic sources — not authoritative in Islam.

## The Lessons
1. **All power is "by Allah's permission"** — even raising the dead. Never attribute divine power to creation.
2. **Every prophet's message is one: worship Allah alone** — from Adam to Isa to Muhammad ﷺ.
3. **Love and honor Isa truly** — by affirming what he affirmed about himself: *"I am the servant of Allah."* To deify him is to contradict his own words.
