# EPISODE 4 — THE ONE WHO REFUSED
### Season 1: Before Humanity · *Iblis, the fall of the greatest worshipper*

---

## 1. THE STORY

He had everything.

Iblis was a jinn — created from fire — but his worship of Allah was so intense, so constant, that he had been raised up to live among the **angels** themselves. He was counted in their ranks. He prostrated when they prostrated. To any onlooker, he seemed like one of the most devoted servants in all of creation.

Then came the test that would expose what was really inside him.

Allah created the first human — Adam — and shaped him with His own hands, and breathed into him a soul. Then He commanded *everyone* present: **"Prostrate to Adam."**

Understand this carefully: it was not a prostration of *worship* — worship belongs to Allah alone. It was a prostration of *honor and respect*, commanded by Allah, the way one might bow in greeting. And it was, above all, a direct order from the Lord of the worlds.

The angels — beings who never disobey — fell into prostration immediately. Every single one.

Except Iblis.

He stood there. Rigid. Refusing.

And Allah, who knows all things, gave him the chance to reconsider. He asked him directly: *"What prevented you from prostrating when I commanded you?"*

And out of Iblis's mouth came the sentence that would echo through all of history — the first words of arrogance ever spoken:

***"I am better than him. You created me from fire, and You created him from clay."***

There it was. Not ignorance. Not confusion. Not "I didn't understand the command." Iblis understood perfectly. He simply decided that *his own judgment* was superior to Allah's command. He looked at his origin (fire), looked at Adam's origin (clay), and concluded: *I am better, so I will not bow.*

This was the first time in existence that a creature used "logic" to justify disobeying a clear command of Allah. And it was built on a lie — the lie that worth comes from what you're *made of*, rather than from obedience and God-consciousness.

In that single moment, a lifetime — perhaps ages — of worship turned to ash. The Qur'an describes his fall in three devastating words: *"He refused, was arrogant, and became one of the disbelievers."* (2:34)

Refusal. Then pride. Then disbelief.

The greatest worshipper became the greatest rebel — not because he stopped believing Allah *existed*, but because his ego would not let him *submit.*

## 2. THE QUR'AN SAYS

**He was of the jinn (the #1 correction):**
- **18:50** — *"And [mention] when We said to the angels, 'Prostrate to Adam,' and they prostrated, except Iblis. He was of the jinn and departed from the command of his Lord…"*
- **15:31** — *"…except Iblis. He refused to be with those who prostrated."* (he was *counted among* them)

**The command and the refusal (appears 7+ times — showing its weight):**
- **2:34** — *"And [mention] when We said to the angels, 'Prostrate before Adam'; so they prostrated, except Iblis. He refused and was arrogant and became of the disbelievers."*
- **7:11–12** — *"…We said to the angels, 'Prostrate to Adam'; so they prostrated, except Iblis... [Allah] said, 'What prevented you from prostrating when I commanded you?' [Iblis] said, 'I am better than him. You created me from fire and created him from clay.'"*
- **38:75–76** — *"[Allah] said, 'O Iblis, what prevented you from prostrating to that which I created with My hands?... ' He said, 'I am better than him. You created me from fire and created him from clay.'"*
- **17:61** · **20:116** — same command, same refusal by Iblis.

**The consequence:**
- **7:13** — *"[Allah] said, 'Descend from it, for it is not for you to be arrogant therein. So get out; indeed, you are of the debased.'"*
- **38:77–78** — *"[Allah] said, 'Then get out of it, for indeed you are expelled. And indeed, upon you is My curse until the Day of Recompense.'"*
- **7:18** — *"…Whoever follows you among them — I will surely fill Hell with you, all together."*

**The principle that destroys Iblis's "logic":**
- **49:13** — *"…Indeed, the most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous of you (atqakum)…"* — honor is by **taqwa**, NOT by what you're made of, your race, or your status.

## 3. THE AUTHENTIC HADITH

- **Sahih Muslim** (the definition of arrogance): A man asked about liking nice clothes and shoes. The Prophet ﷺ said: *"Indeed Allah is Beautiful and loves beauty. **Pride (kibr) is rejecting the truth (batar al-haqq) and looking down on people (ghamt an-nas).**"* — Iblis had BOTH: he rejected Allah's command (rejecting truth) and despised Adam (looking down on people).

- **Sahih Muslim:** *"No one who has an atom's weight of pride (kibr) in his heart will enter Paradise."* — A chilling verdict, given that Iblis's *entire* fall was pride.

- **Sahih al-Bukhari** (Hadith Qudsi): Allah says: *"Pride is My cloak and greatness is My garment. Whoever competes with Me regarding either of them, I will cast him into the Fire."*

- **Sahih Muslim:** *"When the son of Adam recites a verse of prostration and prostrates, the devil withdraws weeping, saying: 'Woe to me! The son of Adam was commanded to prostrate and he prostrated, so Paradise is his; and I was commanded to prostrate but I refused, so the Fire is mine.'"* — Iblis himself confirms the lesson.

## 4. THE HISTORICAL / THEOLOGICAL CONTEXT

**Why was a jinn among the angels?** Scholars explain Iblis was elevated to the company and rank of the angels because of his immense worship — so when the command "prostrate" was given to the angels, he was included in it. His **creation** was jinn (fire, 18:50); his **company** was angelic. This resolves the apparent puzzle of "was he an angel or a jinn?" — he was a *jinn* who lived among angels.

**The "first false analogy" (qiyas):** Iblis reasoned: *fire is higher than clay → I am higher than Adam → I won't bow.* This was the first time a creature used personal reasoning to *override* a direct command of Allah. Scholars note two fatal errors:
1. He assumed worth comes from *material* — but Allah says worth comes from *taqwa* (49:13).
2. He placed his own logic *above* revelation — the root of all misguidance.

**The three-stage fall (2:34):** *abaa* (refused) → *istakbara* (was arrogant) → *kaana min al-kafirin* (became a disbeliever). It was a chain reaction: disobedience fed by pride, hardening into outright kufr.

**His kufr was NOT denial of Allah:** Iblis spoke *to* Allah, acknowledged Him as Creator ("You created me from fire"), and believed in the Day of Judgment (he asked for respite "until the Day they are resurrected"). His disbelief was ***kufr al-istikbar*** — disbelief of *arrogance* (refusing to submit), not ***kufr al-juhud*** (denying Allah exists). **This is the most terrifying lesson of the episode:** you can "believe" in Allah and still be ruined — if your ego refuses to obey.

**The Adam contrast (the moral spine of the whole series):** Both Iblis and (later) Adam disobeyed. But Iblis, when confronted, *justified himself* — Adam, when he erred, *repented* (7:23). Iblis ran *from* Allah; Adam ran *to* Him. Same fall, opposite response, opposite destiny.

## 5. WEAK & DISPUTED NARRATIONS (labeled — for awareness)

⚠️ **"Iblis worshipped Allah for thousands of years (e.g., 80,000 years) before his fall"** — Specific durations come from **Isra'iliyyat and weak reports**, NOT sahih hadith. The authentic point is simply that his worship was great enough to place him among the angels — without a confirmed number.

❌ **"Iblis's original name was Azazeel" / "he was the peacock of the angels" / "he was the teacher of the angels"** — All **Isra'iliyyat** (traceable to sources like Ka'b al-Ahbar), NOT established in the Qur'an or sahih hadith. Do not present as fact.

❌ **Detailed dialogues between Iblis and the angels, or elaborate backstories of his rebellion** — Beyond what the Qur'an records (his actual words ARE in the Qur'an), the extra "scenes" are folklore.

⚠️ **"Iblis led an army of angels against the jinn before Adam"** — As noted in Episode 3, this is from weak/Isra'iliyyat sources and contradicts 18:50 (he was *of the jinn*).

---

## 6. WHAT WE DON'T KNOW
- How long Iblis worshipped before the fall — **unknown** (numbers are Isra'iliyyat).
- His "original name" — **not established** (Azazeel = Isra'iliyyat).
- The exact nature of how a jinn was placed among angels — **scholarly explanation, not detailed in texts.**
- Whether he had a specific rank/role among the angels — **unknown.**

## 7. THE LESSONS

1. **Knowledge and worship without humility can destroy you.** Iblis had more worship than anyone — and it didn't save him. Pride turned all of it to ash in one moment. Worship without humility is a building with no foundation.

2. **Arrogance is the original sin — and it has a precise definition.** The Prophet ﷺ defined *kibr* as *"rejecting truth and looking down on people"* (Muslim). Check your heart against both: Do I reject truth when it bruises my ego? Do I look down on others? That is the disease that felled Iblis.

3. **"I believe in God" is not enough.** Iblis believed Allah exists, spoke to Him, knew the Day of Judgment was real — and is still the most cursed creature. Faith is not just *acknowledging* Allah; it is *submitting* to Him. Submission is the whole game.

4. **Your worth is not your "material."** Iblis's fatal lie was "I'm better because of what I'm made of." Allah destroyed that logic forever: *"the most noble of you is the most righteous"* (49:13). Not your race, lineage, wealth, looks, or background — only taqwa.

5. **The most dangerous moment is when you "have a good reason" to disobey.** Iblis didn't reject Allah randomly; he had a *justification* that felt logical to him. Beware the clever excuse your ego builds to avoid submitting. That voice — "but in my case it's different" — is the voice of Iblis.

> *"He refused, was arrogant, and became of the disbelievers."* (2:34)
