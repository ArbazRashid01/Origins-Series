# EPISODE 1 — BEFORE TIME EXISTED
### Season 1: Before Humanity · *Who is Allah, and what was there before everything?*

---

## 1. THE STORY

Close your eyes for a moment and try to do something almost impossible.

Remove the people around you. Remove the room. Remove the city, the country, the earth beneath it. Now remove the sun, the moon, every star in every galaxy. Remove light itself — and remove darkness too, because darkness is just the absence of light, and there is nothing yet for light to be absent *from*.

Now remove space — there is no "up," no "down," no "here," no "there."

And now remove the hardest thing of all: remove **time**. No past. No future. No "before" and no "after." No ticking, no waiting, no moments passing.

What is left?

Only **Allah**.

Alone. Eternal. Needing nothing and no one. He did not come into existence, because to "come into existence" you need a *before* — and for Allah, there was no before. He simply *is*, and always *was*, and always *will be*. The First — with nothing before Him. The Last — with nothing after Him.

Our minds short-circuit here, and that is okay. We are creatures of time; we think in "first this, then that." But Allah is beyond time, beyond our categories entirely. The Prophet ﷺ told us plainly: *"Allah existed, and there was nothing besides Him."* (Sahih al-Bukhari)

Then — by His will alone — He **chose** to create. He needed nothing. He gained nothing. He was not lonely, not incomplete, not in want. Out of His wisdom and His mercy, He spoke creation into being. And the very first realities He brought forth were not stars or planets — they were things our eyes have never seen: a mighty **Throne** over **water**, and a **Pen** that wrote the destiny of everything that would ever happen.

This is where our entire story begins. Not with humans. Not with angels. Not even with the universe. It begins with the One who has no beginning — and the breathtaking truth that *you*, reading this right now, were brought into being by a God who never needed you… and made you anyway.

## 2. THE QUR'AN SAYS

**On Allah's eternality:**
- **57:3** — *"He is the First (al-Awwal) and the Last (al-Akhir), the Ascendant (az-Zahir) and the Intimate (al-Batin), and He is Knowing of all things."*
- **112 (al-Ikhlas)** — *"Say: He is Allah, [who is] One. Allah, the Eternal Refuge (as-Samad). He neither begets nor is born, nor is there to Him any equivalent."* — The Prophet ﷺ said this surah equals a third of the Qur'an (Bukhari).

**On His self-sufficiency:**
- **35:15** — *"O mankind, you are those in need of Allah, while Allah is the Free of need, the Praiseworthy."*
- **51:56–57** — *"I did not create the jinn and mankind except to worship Me. I do not want from them any provision, nor do I want them to feed Me."*

**On nothing being like Him:**
- **42:11** — *"There is nothing like unto Him, and He is the Hearing, the Seeing."* (The master verse of belief in Allah's attributes.)
- **19:65** — *"…Do you know of any similarity to Him?"*

**On His names:**
- **7:180** — *"And to Allah belong the best names, so invoke Him by them."*
- **59:22–24** — A cluster of His names: the Knower of the unseen and witnessed, the Most Merciful, the Sovereign, the Pure, the Source of Peace, the Granter of Security, the Overseer, the Mighty, the Compeller, the Supreme, the Creator, the Originator, the Fashioner.

**On the Throne over water (before creation):**
- **11:7** — *"And it is He who created the heavens and the earth in six days — and His Throne had been upon water…"*

**On the Pen and the Preserved Tablet:**
- **68:1** — *"Nun. By the pen and what they inscribe."*
- **85:21–22** — *"But this is an honored Qur'an, [inscribed] in a Preserved Tablet (Lawh Mahfuz)."*
- **57:22** — *"No disaster strikes upon the earth or among yourselves except that it is in a register before We bring it into being…"* (the foundation of belief in Qadar/decree).

**On the purpose — the bridge to everything:**
- **51:56** — *"I did not create jinn and mankind except to worship Me."*
- **67:2** — *"[He] who created death and life to test you as to which of you is best in deed."*

## 3. THE AUTHENTIC HADITH

- **Sahih al-Bukhari** (Imran ibn Husayn): *"Allah existed, and there was nothing besides Him, and His Throne was upon the water. He wrote in the Preserved Tablet everything (that would be), and He created the heavens and the earth."* — The bedrock narration for this episode.

- **Sahih Muslim** ('Abdullah ibn 'Amr): *"Allah decreed the measures (of creation) fifty thousand years before He created the heavens and the earth, and His Throne was upon the water."* — Authentic. (Scholars discuss the meaning of "fifty thousand years," since time as we know it is itself a creation.)

- **Abu Dawud & at-Tirmidhi** (graded *sahih* by some, e.g., al-Albani; *discussed* by others): *"The first thing Allah created was the Pen. He said to it: 'Write.' It said: 'My Lord, what shall I write?' He said: 'Write the decree of all things until the Hour comes.'"*

- **at-Tirmidhi:** *"The Pen has dried with what is to be."* (The decree is finalized; nothing escapes Allah's knowledge and will.)

- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim:** *"Allah has ninety-nine names; whoever comprehends/preserves them (ahsaha) will enter Paradise."* — The *concept* of 99 names is authentic.

- **Sahih Muslim** (the night du'a): *"O Allah, You are the First, so there is nothing before You; You are the Last, so there is nothing after You; You are the Most High (az-Zahir), so there is nothing above You; You are the Most Near (al-Batin), so there is nothing nearer than You."*

## 4. THE HISTORICAL / THEOLOGICAL CONTEXT

**The three categories of Tawhid (the lens for everything that follows):**
1. **Tawhid ar-Rububiyyah** — Oneness of Lordship: Allah alone creates, sustains, owns, and controls everything.
2. **Tawhid al-Uluhiyyah** — Oneness of Worship: only Allah deserves worship, love, hope, and devotion.
3. **Tawhid al-Asma was-Sifat** — Oneness of Names and Attributes: He alone possesses His perfect names and attributes, like none of His creation.

Notice: even the Makkan idol-worshippers (whom we meet in Season 11) *admitted* Rububiyyah — they agreed Allah created them (43:87). Their shirk was in *Uluhiyyah* — they worshipped others alongside Him. This distinction is the heartbeat of the entire ORIGINS series.

**The order of the first creations (a real scholarly discussion):**
- The Qur'an + Bukhari establish the **Throne was over water** before the heavens and earth.
- Hadith establish the **Pen** was commanded to write the decree.
- **Did the Throne come before the Pen, or the Pen before the Throne?** Scholars genuinely differ:
  - Ibn Taymiyyah and others lean toward the **Throne** being first (it pre-existed the Pen).
  - Others, taking "the first thing Allah created was the Pen" literally, place the **Pen** first.
  - A common reconciliation: the Throne and water existed, *then* the Pen was the first thing created to write the decree.
- **The honest position: we do not know the exact sequence with certainty, and it is not a matter we are required to settle.**

**Belief in Qadar (Divine Decree):** This episode plants one of the six pillars of faith. The Pen wrote everything before it happened (57:22). This is not fatalism — humans still have real choice (we'll see this with the jinn in Episode 3). It means nothing is outside Allah's knowledge and will.

**The approach to Allah's attributes (Imam Malik's golden rule):** When asked *how* Allah "rose over the Throne" (istawa, 7:54), Imam Malik replied: *"The 'rising' is known, the 'how' is unknown, believing in it is obligatory, and asking about it (the 'how') is an innovation."* This is the balanced creed: affirm what Allah said about Himself, without imagining a form (tashbih) and without denying it (ta'til).

## 5. WEAK & DISPUTED NARRATIONS (labeled — for awareness)

⚠️ **"The first thing Allah created was my light / the light of Muhammad ﷺ"** — This narration ("Awwalu ma khalaqAllahu nuru Nabiyyik") is **weak / not authentically established** (graded weak by major hadith scholars). It is widely circulated but should not be presented as fact. The authentic narrations mention the **Pen** and the **Throne over water** — not a pre-existent "light of Muhammad."

⚠️ **Detailed "creation timelines"** (e.g., "Allah created the Throne, then the Pen, then the Tablet, then such-and-such on Sunday, Monday…") — Some of this comes from a hadith in Sahih Muslim (the "days of creation") that **many scholars, including some early authorities, critique** because parts trace back to Ka'b al-Ahbar (a Jewish convert) rather than the Prophet ﷺ. Treat detailed day-by-day creation schedules with caution.

❌ **Stories of "what Allah was doing before creation"** — There is NO authentic text describing this. Any such claim is baseless speculation. The correct answer is: *we are not told.*

❌ **Philosophical claims about Allah's "nature" beyond revelation** — The Prophet ﷺ warned: *"Reflect on the creation of Allah, but do not reflect on (the essence of) Allah."* (reported in various wordings; the meaning is sound) — because the human mind cannot encompass Him.

---

## 6. WHAT WE DON'T KNOW (and that's part of the beauty)
- What "existed" or "happened" before creation — **unknown** (and the question itself is flawed, since "before" is a time-concept).
- The exact order: Throne first or Pen first — **debated, no certainty.**
- The exact nature/appearance of the Throne, the Pen, the Tablet — **unknown** beyond what the texts say.
- The meaning of "50,000 years" relative to our time — **discussed, not definitive.**
- The "how" of Allah's attributes — **deliberately beyond us** (and admitting this *is* correct belief).

---

## 7. THE LESSONS

1. **You were not an accident.** A God who needed absolutely nothing — no worship, no company, no provision (51:57) — chose to create you anyway. Your existence is a pure gift, not a transaction.

2. **Purpose precedes everything.** Before you ask "how should I live?", Islam answers "*why* do you exist?" — to know and worship the One who made you (51:56). Every episode after this is humanity either remembering or forgetting that one purpose.

3. **Humility before the Unseen.** The very first lesson is that our minds have limits. A believer is comfortable saying "I don't know" about what Allah didn't reveal — and finds peace, not frustration, in that.

4. **Everything is written, yet you still choose.** Belief in Qadar brings calm (nothing is random or outside His control) without removing responsibility (your choices are real). This balance returns again and again.

5. **He is *as-Samad*** — the One everything leans on, who leans on nothing. When the whole world feels unstable, this name is an anchor: the One you depend on never weakens, never tires, never needs.

> *"He is the First and the Last, the Ascendant and the Intimate, and He is Knowing of all things."* (57:3)
