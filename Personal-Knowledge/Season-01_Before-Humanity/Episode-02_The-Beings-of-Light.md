# EPISODE 2 — THE BEINGS OF LIGHT
### Season 1: Before Humanity · *The angels — Allah's first creation, made of pure light*

---

## 1. THE STORY

Long before the first human took a breath, long before the earth was spread out or the stars were lit, the heavens were already full of life.

Not human life. Not animal life. A creation made of pure **light** — the **angels** (al-Malaika).

Imagine beings so vast that one of them could fill the entire horizon. Beings who have never once, in all of existence, disobeyed their Lord. They do not eat. They do not sleep. They do not grow tired or bored or distracted. They have no ego, no jealousy, no hidden agenda. From the moment of their creation until this very second, they are doing one thing: glorifying Allah — *"they glorify Him night and day and do not slacken"* (21:20).

Their numbers are beyond imagination. The Prophet ﷺ described a house in the seventh heaven, the *Bayt al-Ma'mur*, where **seventy thousand angels** enter to worship every single day — and once they leave, they never get another turn, because there are so many waiting (Bukhari). The angels are an ocean without a shore.

And among them are names you should know. **Jibreel** (Gabriel) — the greatest of them — the one who carried Allah's words down to every prophet. **Mikail** (Michael) — entrusted with rain and provision. The one who will blow the **Trumpet** that ends the world. The **Angel of Death**, who collects every soul when its time is complete. The eight who will carry Allah's Throne on the Last Day. And countless others assigned to mountains, clouds, wombs, graves, and gardens.

But here is what should stop you in your tracks. Right now — as you read this — **two angels are with you.** One on your right, one on your left. They are writing down everything you do: every word, every deed, every secret kindness, every quiet sin. You are never, not for a single heartbeat, truly alone.

And there are angels who do something even more beautiful: they *pray for you.* The angels who carry the Throne, and those around it, *"ask forgiveness for those who have believed"* (40:7). Somewhere in the unseen, beings of light you have never met are begging Allah to forgive *you*.

This is the world that existed before us. A world of perfect obedience, infinite worship, and quiet love for the children of Adam who hadn't even been created yet.

## 2. THE QUR'AN SAYS

**Belief in angels is a pillar of faith:**
- **2:285** — *"The Messenger believes... and the believers. All of them have believed in Allah and His angels and His books and His messengers."*
- **2:177** — Righteousness includes belief in *"Allah, the Last Day, the angels, the Book, and the prophets."*

**Their creation and nature:**
- **35:1** — *"…who made the angels messengers having wings, two or three or four. He increases in creation what He wills."*
- **66:6** — *"…[angels] harsh and severe; they do not disobey Allah in what He commands them but do what they are commanded."*
- **21:19–20** — *"…those near Him are not too proud to worship Him, nor do they tire. They glorify [Him] night and day [and] do not slacken."*
- **21:26–27** — *"They are but honored servants. They cannot precede Him in word, and they act by His command."*

**Named angels & roles:**
- **2:97–98** — *Jibreel* (brought the revelation down to the heart of the Prophet ﷺ) and *Mikail* are named.
- **39:68 / 69:13** — the *Trumpet* (the blower is traditionally Israfil — name from Sunnah).
- **32:11** — *"Say: the Angel of Death, who has been entrusted with you, will take you…"*
- **69:17** — *"…and will bear the Throne of your Lord above them, that Day, eight [angels]."*
- **40:7** — *"Those who carry the Throne and those around it… ask forgiveness for those who have believed."*
- **42:5** — *"…and the angels exalt [Allah] with praise of their Lord and ask forgiveness for those on earth."*

**The recording angels:**
- **82:10–12** — *"And indeed, [appointed] over you are keepers, noble and recording; they know whatever you do."*
- **50:17–18** — *"When the two receivers receive, seated on the right and on the left. He utters no word except that with him is an observer prepared."*
- **13:11** — *"For each one are successive [angels] before and behind him who protect him by the decree of Allah."*

**They are not Allah's "daughters" (a Makkan slander):**
- **53:27** — *"Those who do not believe in the Hereafter name the angels female names."*
- **43:19** — rebukes those who made the angels females.

## 3. THE AUTHENTIC HADITH

- **Sahih Muslim** ('Aisha): *"The angels were created from light, the jinn were created from a smokeless flame of fire, and Adam was created from what has been described to you."* — The foundational hadith.

- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim:** The Prophet ﷺ saw **Jibreel** in his true form — he had **600 wings**, filling the horizon. (Connected to Qur'an 53:5–14.)

- **Sahih al-Bukhari** (Night Journey): *"Then I was taken up to the Bayt al-Ma'mur; every day seventy thousand angels enter it, never to return to it again."*

- **Sunan Abi Dawud** (sound): describing a Throne-bearing angel — *"between his earlobe and his shoulder is the distance of seven hundred years' travel."* (Conveys their immense scale.)

- **Sahih al-Bukhari:** *"Angels take turns among you by night and by day, and they all assemble at the dawn and afternoon prayers. Those who stayed with you ascend, and Allah asks them — though He knows best — 'How did you leave My servants?' They reply: 'We left them while they were praying, and we came to them while they were praying.'"*

- **Sahih Muslim:** *"Allah has angels who travel the roads seeking gatherings of dhikr. When they find a people remembering Allah, they call out, 'Come to what you seek!' and they envelop them with their wings up to the lowest heaven."*

- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** (the womb): *"…then Allah sends an angel who is commanded to write four things: his provision, his lifespan, his deeds, and whether he will be wretched or blessed…"*

- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim:** When Allah loves a servant, He calls Jibreel, who loves him, then calls to the inhabitants of heaven to love him, *"then acceptance is placed for him on the earth."*

## 4. THE HISTORICAL / THEOLOGICAL CONTEXT

**The major named angels and their roles:**
| Angel | Role | Source |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Jibreel** (Gabriel) | Brings revelation (*ar-Ruh al-Amin*, the Trustworthy Spirit) | Qur'an 2:97; 26:193 |
| **Mikail** (Michael) | Rain & provision | Qur'an 2:98 (named); role from scholarly tradition |
| **Israfil** | Will blow the Trumpet | Trumpet is Qur'anic (39:68); the *name* is from Sunnah |
| **Malak al-Mawt** (Angel of Death) | Takes souls | Qur'an 32:11 (the name "Azrael" is NOT established) |
| **Malik** | Keeper/guardian of Hellfire | Qur'an 43:77 (named in Qur'an) |
| **Ridwan** | Keeper of Paradise | name from hadith tradition (not Qur'an) |
| **Munkar & Nakir** | Question the soul in the grave | the concept is in sound hadith (at-Tirmidhi) |
| **Kiraman Katibin** | The two recording angels | Qur'an 82:10–12 |
| **Throne-bearers** | Carry Allah's Throne | Qur'an 69:17; 40:7 |
| **Guardians of Hell** | 19 angels, fierce | Qur'an 74:30; 66:6 |

**The key difference between angels and us:** Angels have **no free will to disobey** (66:6). They are not "tested" the way we are — obedience is their nature. This makes the human (and jinn) capacity to *choose* obedience extraordinary, which is exactly why Adam was honored over the angels in knowledge (Episode 6).

**Their question about Adam was NOT objection:** In 2:30, the angels asked, *"Will You place upon it one who causes corruption and sheds blood, while we glorify You?"* Scholars stress this was **respectful inquiry seeking wisdom** (adab), not protest. They immediately submitted when Allah said, *"Indeed, I know that which you do not know."* This is the perfect contrast to Iblis's arrogant *refusal* (Episode 4).

**What angels do NOT have:** no gender, no need to eat/drink/sleep, no fatigue, no ego, no capacity to rebel. Affirming this protects us from the mythologies other cultures built around "angels."

## 5. WEAK & DISPUTED NARRATIONS (labeled — for awareness)

⚠️ **"Azrael" as the name of the Angel of Death** — The Qur'an only says *"Malak al-Mawt"* (the Angel of Death, 32:11). The name "Azrael" comes from **Isra'iliyyat** (earlier traditions), not from the Qur'an or any sahih hadith. Use "the Angel of Death."

⚠️ **Detailed physical descriptions of specific angels** beyond the texts (e.g., elaborate descriptions of their faces, colors, exact appearances) — mostly **unverified or weak**. Stick to what's authentic: light, wings, immense size.

⚠️ **"Ridwan" as keeper of Paradise** — widely accepted by scholars, but the *name* comes from hadith of varying strength, not from the Qur'an (unlike "Malik," who IS named in 43:77). Acceptable to mention, with awareness.

⚠️ **Long folk-tales about angels' daily lives, conversations, and "ranks"** — much of this circulating content is **Isra'iliyyat or fabricated**. Be cautious: affirm the authentic framework, avoid the embellished stories.

❌ **"Harut and Marut were angels who sinned"** in the sensational way folklore tells it — The Qur'an mentions Harut and Marut (2:102) as a *test* in Babylon, but the lurid story (that they drank wine, committed sin, etc.) is **Isra'iliyyat**. Scholars differ even on whether they were angels or righteous men; the dramatic narrative is not authentically established.

---

## 6. WHAT WE DON'T KNOW
- The total number of angels — **known only to Allah** (74:31).
- Their exact default appearance (beyond "light" + wings) — **unknown.**
- The precise roles of most angels — **unknown** (only a handful are detailed for us).
- Whether "Israfil" is definitively the Trumpet-blower's name — **from Sunnah, widely held, but the Qur'an just says "the Trumpet."**

## 7. THE LESSONS

1. **You are watched — and accompanied — with love.** Two angels record your every move (82:10–12); others pray for your forgiveness (40:7). In your loneliest moment, in a dark room at 3am, you are *seen* and *prayed for.* Let that change how you behave when "no one is looking."

2. **The unseen is real.** A believer's worldview is bigger than the material. Belief in angels trains the heart to take the unseen — including Paradise, Hell, and the Day of Judgment — seriously.

3. **Obedience is beautiful, not weak.** The mightiest creatures in the cosmos — beings that fill horizons — spend eternity in humble worship and *"are not too proud to worship Him"* (21:19). If they aren't too proud, who are we to be?

4. **Sit in gatherings of dhikr.** The Prophet ﷺ told us angels literally surround circles of remembrance (Muslim). Your dhikr is not lost into the air — it is witnessed by beings of light.

5. **Guard your tongue and deeds.** The recording angels don't take breaks. The awareness that everything is written is one of the most practical motivators in Islam to live well.

> *"They glorify Him night and day and do not slacken."* (21:20)
