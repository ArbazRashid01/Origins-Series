# SEASON 9 — TESTED BY GOD: AYYUB & YUNUS (A.S.)
### *Two prophets, two trials — and the two doors Allah always leaves open: patience (sabr) and prayer (du'a)*
*(✅ Qur'an/Sahih · ⚠️ Weak/disputed · ❌ Isra'iliyyat · ❓ Unknown — grouped by THEME, not strict chronology)*

> Note: This season is grouped thematically. Ayyub's exact era is uncertain; Yunus came much later (Nineveh). The link between them is the *meaning* of their tests, not a timeline.

---

# EPISODE 35 — THE MAN WHO LOST EVERYTHING (AYYUB)

## The Story
**Ayyub (A.S.)** was a prophet blessed with abundant wealth, a large family, and good health — and he was deeply grateful and devoted. Then came the test. One by one, Allah took it all: his **wealth** vanished, his **children** were lost, and his **health** collapsed into a long, painful illness that left him weak for years. Through all of it, Ayyub never complained against his Lord, never lost hope in Him. When he finally turned to Allah, his words held no bitterness — only humility and trust: *"Indeed, adversity has touched me, and You are the Most Merciful of the merciful."* Allah answered: *"Strike [the ground] with your foot"* — and a cool spring gushed forth for him to wash and drink, healing him. Allah **restored his family and doubled it**, as a mercy and a reminder. Allah testified: *"Indeed, We found him patient — an excellent servant. Indeed, he was one repeatedly turning back [to Allah]."*

## The Qur'an Says
- **21:83–84** — *"And [mention] Ayyub, when he called to his Lord, 'Indeed, adversity has touched me, and You are the Most Merciful of the merciful.' So We responded to him and removed what afflicted him, and We gave him [back] his family and the like with them…"*
- **38:41** — *"Remember Our servant Ayyub, when he called to his Lord, 'Shaytan has touched me with hardship and torment.'"*
- **38:42** — *"Strike [the ground] with your foot; this is a [spring for] washing — cool and a drink."*
- **38:43** — *"We granted him his family and a like number with them, as mercy from Us…"*
- **38:44** — *"…Indeed, We found him patient. What an excellent servant! Indeed, he was one repeatedly turning back."*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **Bukhari:** While Ayyub was bathing (after his cure), gold locusts fell upon him; he began gathering them in his garment, and his Lord called him: *"O Ayyub, did I not enrich you beyond what you see?"* He said, *"Yes, my Lord, but I cannot do without Your blessing."* — A lesson on never being indifferent to Allah's bounty.

## Historical / Theological Context
- **38:44 (the bundle of grass / the oath):** *"Take in your hand a bundle [of grass] and strike with it, and do not break your oath."* — Ayyub had sworn (in a moment of distress, in some accounts regarding his wife). Allah gave him a gentle way to fulfill the oath without harm — teaching mercy and the keeping of vows.
- Ayyub is the **archetype of sabr** (patience) in Islam — patience that keeps a *good opinion of Allah (husn al-zann)* through devastation.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ❌ **The graphic "worms ate his flesh," his body rotting, everyone abandoning him except his faithful wife, 18 years of illness, sitting on a dunghill** — these vivid details are **Isra'iliyyat**, NOT in the Qur'an or Sahih hadith. The Qur'an dignifiedly says only that *"adversity touched him."* Avoid the gory embellishments.
- ⚠️ The exact nature of his illness and its duration — **unknown** (numbers like "7 years / 18 years" are Isra'iliyyat).

## What We Don't Know
- ❓ Ayyub's era, his illness's exact nature/duration, the full context of his oath.

## The Lessons
1. **Sabr is not silence — it's keeping faith in Allah's mercy *through* the pain.** Ayyub felt and expressed his hardship, but never accused Allah.
2. **Husn al-zann (good opinion of Allah)** — even at rock bottom, he addressed Allah as *"the Most Merciful of the merciful."*
3. **Never be indifferent to Allah's blessing** (the gold-locusts hadith) — gratitude even in restoration.
4. **What you lose for Allah's sake, He can return doubled** — but the real reward is His pleasure.

# EPISODE 36 — SWALLOWED BY THE SEA (YUNUS)

## The Story
**Yunus (A.S.)** — also called **Dhun-Nun** ("the man of the whale") — was sent to the people of **Nineveh.** He called them for a long time, but they refused. In frustration, he **left them — before Allah had permitted him to** — assuming Allah would not constrain him. He boarded a ship; a storm threatened it; lots were cast to lighten the load, and Yunus's name came up repeatedly. He was cast into the sea — and a great **fish (whale) swallowed him whole.** There, in layers of **darkness** (the night, the sea, the belly of the fish), Yunus called out the prayer that has rescued countless souls since: *"There is no deity except You; glory be to You; indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers."* He took full responsibility — blaming no one but himself. Allah **rescued him**, cast him onto a barren shore, and caused a **gourd plant** to grow over him for shade as he recovered. And his people? When they saw the punishment approaching, they **all believed** — the *only* town in the Qur'an that believed in time and was spared.

## The Qur'an Says
- **21:87** — *"And [mention] the man of the fish (Dhun-Nun), when he went off in anger and thought We would not decree [anything] upon him. Then he called out within the darknesses, 'There is no deity except You; glory be to You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers.'"*
- **21:88** — *"So We responded to him and saved him from the distress. And thus do We save the believers."*
- **37:139–144** — *"…he fled to the laden ship… and the fish swallowed him while he was blameworthy. Had he not been of those who glorify [Allah], he would have remained in its belly until the Day of Resurrection."*
- **37:145–146** — *"…We cast him onto the open shore while he was ill, and We caused a gourd vine to grow over him."*
- **10:98** — *"…except the people of Yunus. When they believed, We removed from them the punishment of disgrace… and gave them enjoyment for a time."*
- **68:48** — *"Be patient for the decision of your Lord, and be not like the companion of the fish…"*

## The Authentic Hadith
- **at-Tirmidhi (sahih):** *"The supplication of Dhun-Nun when he called out in the belly of the fish — 'La ilaha illa Anta, subhanaka, inni kuntu min az-zalimin' — no Muslim ever supplicates with it for anything except that Allah answers him."*
- **Bukhari & Muslim:** *"No one should say that I am better than Yunus ibn Matta."* — affirming his high status as a noble prophet despite his lapse.

## Historical / Theological Context
- Yunus's leaving was a **slip** (he left before Allah's permission, out of frustration) — for which he repented; he remained a great prophet (hence *"none better than Yunus"*).
- The **"darknesses"** (21:87) are commonly explained as the darkness of night + sea + the fish's belly — total helplessness, where the only way out was *upward* to Allah.
- His people (Nineveh) are **unique**: the only nation that believed *before* the punishment fell and were saved (10:98) — proving repentance can avert disaster, even at the last moment.

## Weak & Disputed Narrations
- ⚠️ Exact details of how long he stayed in the fish (3 days, 7 days, 40 days — various tafsir reports), the type of fish/whale — **not firmly established.**
- ❌ Elaborate "inside the whale" scenes/dialogues — Isra'iliyyat.

## What We Don't Know
- ❓ The duration inside the fish, the exact location/era beyond Nineveh.

## The Lessons
1. **No darkness can block your call to Allah.** Whatever "whale" you're trapped in — debt, illness, sin, despair — His response reaches you. *"And thus do We save the believers"* (21:88).
2. **Take responsibility — don't blame others.** Yunus's prayer blamed only himself (*"I have been of the wrongdoers"*). Owning your fault is the doorway to mercy.
3. **It's never too late to repent** — Nineveh believed at the last moment and was saved. Hope is never lost.
4. **Patience in da'wah** — *"do not be like the companion of the fish"* (68:48): don't abandon your post in frustration.

---

## ✅ SEASON 9 COMPLETE
Two tests, two doors: when hardship comes from *outside* (Ayyub) — answer with **sabr** and good thoughts of Allah; when you fall through your *own* fault (Yunus) — answer with **du'a** and owning your mistake. Either way, the road always leads back to Allah. And being tested is often a sign of His love, not His anger.
