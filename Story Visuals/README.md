# Story Visuals — The Director's Bible
### Cinematic Visual Treatments for *Origins: The Story of Islam*

> *"My goal is not to create beautiful AI images. My goal is to create a cinematic documentary. Every image should feel like a movie scene."*

This folder is the **visual companion** to the *Personal Knowledge For Deen* encyclopedia. For every one of the 55 episodes, there is a **director's treatment** — a scene-by-scene visual plan that translates the *meaning* of the narration into the *language of film*, so the story becomes easy to **see, shoot, and tell.**

These documents do not illustrate words. They **illustrate ideas.** The guiding question for every scene is never *"What image matches this sentence?"* — it is *"What scene would a Netflix documentary create to communicate this idea?"*

---

## ⚠️ The First Rule: Halal Visual Compliance (Non-Negotiable)

This is sacred content. Every visual choice in this folder obeys these limits **without exception:**

| ❌ NEVER depict | ✅ Represent instead with |
|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Allah** (in any form whatsoever) | Light, cosmos, calligraphy, the unseen suggested by scale and atmosphere |
| **The Prophets** (Adam, Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, Muhammad ﷺ, all of them) | Silhouettes (back-lit, never facial features), implied presence, POV shots, hands/feet/objects only, light where they stand, an empty seat, footprints |
| **The Angels** (Jibril, etc.) | Light, radiance, wind, feathers of light, glowing geometry, presence felt not seen |
| **The Sahaba (Companions)** | Silhouettes, crowds shot from behind/afar, hands, objects, environmental storytelling |
| **The face of Iblis / the unseen** | Shadow, smoke, a dark void, a cold colour-shift, a whisper of darkness at the frame's edge |

**Core technique:** *Suggest, don't show.* Cinema can convey a person powerfully through a **silhouette against light, a shadow on a wall, a hand entering frame, a POV camera, an object they touch, or the reaction of the environment around them** — never their face. When a prophet "speaks," we may show the *listener's reaction*, the *landscape*, the *calligraphy of the words*, or *light*, never the speaker's face.

> Anything depicting a sanctified figure is **rejected and re-designed.** This rule overrides every other consideration in this folder.

## How To Read Each Episode Treatment

Each episode file contains:

1. **Story Analysis** — *Before any visual is chosen.* What is this episode? Its **type** (historical / scientific / philosophical / emotional / dialogue / Islamic narration / abstract / reflection / action / mystery / revelation), its **core emotion**, and its **purpose** (what it teaches).
2. **Cinematic Identity** — the *unique signature* of this episode, so no two episodes look alike. Its palette, lighting key, lens/camera personality, pace, and recurring motif.
3. **Sound & Music Design** — the sonic identity (since sound is half of cinema).
4. **Scene-by-Scene Breakdown** — the episode divided into cinematic **scenes of ~3–10 seconds**, each with: the narration beat it covers, the **visual treatment**, the **style** (from the toolkit below), **camera movement**, and **lighting/sound notes.** Scenes change only on a real shift of *location, emotion, topic, time, perspective, or purpose* — never mechanically.
5. **Visual Variety Check** — confirming the episode alternates styles like a premium documentary.

## The Visual Style Toolkit

A premium documentary **never repeats one style.** Every episode draws from a mix of these:

| Code | Style | Best for |
|:----:|-------|----------|
| **REC** | Cinematic recreation (environments, period architecture, crowds-as-silhouettes) | Historical narration |
| **ENV** | AI-generated / matte environment (vast landscape, city, desert, sea) | Establishing, scale, atmosphere |
| **MG** | Motion graphics / animated diagram / infographic | Explanation, timelines, lineage, concepts |
| **MACRO** | Macro cinematography (sand, water droplet, ember, ink, fabric) | Detail, intimacy, texture |
| **SLOW** | Slow-motion detail | Emotional weight, turning points |
| **SYM** | Symbolic / abstract composition | Unseen (*ghayb*), spiritual ideas |
| **LIGHT** | Pure light, mist, cosmos, void | Allah's majesty, the unseen, revelation |
| **CALLIG** | Arabic calligraphy / typography on screen | Qur'an verses, key words, divine speech |
| **SILH** | Silhouette / shadow / POV (the halal way to "show" a figure) | Any prophet, angel, or person |
| **MAP** | Animated map / geography | Journeys, locations, battles |
| **NAT** | Nature (rain, storm, mountains, dawn, stars) | Emotion, reflection, transitions |
| **TH** | Talking head (narrator/scholar, minimal cinematic backdrop) | Direct address, framing, reflection |
| **ARTIFACT** | Ancient objects, ruins, manuscripts, textures | Authenticity, history made tangible |

## The Emotional Colour Language (House Style)

To give the whole series unity *while* letting each episode have its own identity, colours carry meaning:

- **Cool blues / cosmic blacks / pure white light** → the unseen, the divine, the eternal, *tawhid*.
- **Warm golds / ambers / soft dawn** → mercy, revelation, hope, guidance, Paradise.
- **Cold deep shadow / desaturated greys / a sickly edge** → arrogance, *shirk*, the whisper of Shaytan, the doomed.
- **Earthy clay browns / ochres** → humanity, the human story, the prophets among their people.
- **Storm greys, harsh contrast** → judgement, trial, battle.
- **Soft greens** → life, renewal, Paradise hints, mercy after hardship.

Each episode bends this palette toward its own mood.

---

## The Pacing Principle

- Scenes run **~3–10 seconds.** Reflection scenes breathe longer; action and revelation cut faster.
- **Camera always has intention** — every push-in, pull-out, tilt, or drift should *reveal* something, never move for decoration.
- **Silence is a tool.** Some of the most powerful beats (a name, a death, a revelation) are best held on a near-still frame with only ambient sound or a single sustained note.
