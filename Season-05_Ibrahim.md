# PART A — REEL SCRIPTS
---

## 🎬 S5 • E16 — "THE BOY WHO QUESTIONED EVERYTHING"
**Citations:** Qur'an 6:74/19:42/21:52 (questioning his father Azar) · 21:51 (early guidance) · 6:75–78 (star/moon/sun) · 6:79 (turns to the Creator) · 21:53 (forefathers).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Uske aas-paas har koi pathar ke buton ko poojta tha."* (pause) *"Aur wo akela tha — jisne poocha: 'Inhe banaya kisne?'"*
**🎙️ Narration:** Born in an idol-soaked city (his father Azar made idols). He questioned: why worship what can't hear/see/help? Reasoning with his star-worshipping people, he pointed to a star, moon, sun — each *set* — "what disappears can't be God"; he turned to the Creator. *Reflection:* real faith isn't blind inheritance; it's sincere seeking — don't fear the big questions.
**🔄 Open loop:** He waited for the city to empty and entered the temple with an axe. → E17.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Star/moon/sun = his *reasoning with people* (prophets don't doubt Allah). Azar named in Qur'an (relationship discussed).

## 🎬 S5 • E17 — "THE IDOL SMASHER"
**Citations:** Qur'an 21:57–58 (the plan; breaking idols) · 21:62–63 ("ask the big one") · 21:64–65 (they admit, then revert) · 21:66–68 (his argument + "burn him") · Bukhari/Muslim (the "three statements").
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Usne but-khaane ke saare buut tod diye — sirf ek ko chhod kar. Aur kulhaadi sabse bade but ke kandhe par tika di."*
**🎙️ Narration:** He smashed all but the largest, leaning the axe on it. Asked "did you do this?" — "ask the big one, if it can speak." *Climax:* truth briefly hit them ("we are the wrongdoers"), then pride snapped back; Ibrahim: "why worship what can't help or harm? Won't you reason?" *Reflection:* he held up a mirror — sometimes the strongest da'wah is one question; but cornered pride chooses anger over reflection.
**🔄 Open loop:** They built the biggest fire ever to throw him in. → E18.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** "Ask the big one" = rhetorical device; one of the three statements (Bukhari/Muslim), not a sinful lie.

## 🎬 S5 • E18 — "THE FIRE THAT WOULDN'T BURN"
**Citations:** Qur'an 21:68 (burn him) · 21:69 ("be cool & safe") · 21:70 / 37:98 (they were made the losers) · 2:258 (the king debate).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Unhone itni badi aag jalaayi ke parinde uske ooper se nahi udd sakte the."* (pause) *"Aur jab Ibrahim ko usmein phenka gaya… Allah ne aag se baat ki."*
**🎙️ Narration:** "O fire, be coolness and safety upon Ibrahim" — the blaze became a garden; he emerged unharmed. Then he silenced the tyrant king: "My Lord brings the sun from the east — bring it from the west." The king was dumbfounded. *Reflection:* fire burns only when Allah wills; when you fear Allah alone, every worldly "fire" loses its power.
**🔄 Open loop:** Migrating for Allah, angelic guests arrived with news and a warning about a nearby city. → E19.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** **King unnamed in Qur'an** ("Nimrod" = tradition). Catapult/Jibreel details = Isra'iliyyat (avoid).

## 🎬 S5 • E19 — "THE CITY THAT WAS TURNED UPSIDE DOWN" *(Lut)*
**Citations:** Qur'an 11:69–71/51:24–30 (angels' glad tidings) · 7:80–81/26:165–166 (Lut's people) · 11:77–79 (guests; the people) · 11:81/66:10 (saved except his wife) · 11:82–83/15:74 (overturned, stoned).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Mehmaan ban kar farishtey ek aisे shehar mein aaye…"* (pause) *"…jo itna gira hua tha ke mehmaan bhi mehfooz nahi the."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Angels gave Ibrahim glad tidings of a son, then went to Lut's people — who committed an unprecedented immorality. Lut warned for years. The angels (as guests) came; the people came demanding them; the family escaped by night, except Lut's wife (who sided with the wrongdoers). *Climax:* the cities were overturned and rained with stones. *Reflection:* Lut's wife (a prophet's wife) wasn't saved; Ibrahim (from an idol-maker's home) led — home doesn't save you, your own faith does.
**🔄 Open loop:** The son Ibrahim was given would become his hardest test. → E20.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Dignified, beginner-appropriate language. "Sodom" = tradition. Wife perished for betrayal in faith (66:10).

## 🎬 S5 • E20 — "THE SACRIFICE"
**Citations:** Qur'an 37:100–101 (the forbearing boy) · 37:102 (the dream + the son's submission) · 37:103 · 37:104–107 (fulfilled; ransomed) · 37:108.
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Ek poori zindagi aulaad ke liye tadapne ke baad, aakhir use ek beta mila."* (pause) *"Aur phir hukum aaya — us bête ko Allah ke liye qurbaan kar do."*
**🎙️ Narration:** After long longing, he was given Ismail. A true dream (revelation) commanded the sacrifice; he told the boy, whose reply: "Father, do as you are commanded; you'll find me patient." *Climax:* both submitted; as he laid him down, Allah called "you have fulfilled the vision" — it was a test of obedience; a great sacrifice ransomed the boy. *Reflection:* Allah wants our hearts above all — that we put Him before our dearest; remembered every Eid al-Adha.
**🔄 Open loop:** Father & son would build a house the whole world still turns to. → E21.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Strong position: the son was **Ismail** (note some early scholars said Ishaq). A prophet's dream = revelation.

## 🎬 S5 • E21 — "BUILDING THE KAABA" *(Finale)*
**Citations:** Sahih al-Bukhari (Hajar/Zamzam; the Sa'i) · Qur'an 14:37 (the dua) · 2:127 (raising the foundations) · 2:128–129 · 22:27 (the call to Hajj).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Duniya ki sabse zyada visit ki jaane wali jagah — ek baap, ek beta, aur ek maa ke yaqeen se bani. Aur shuruaat hui ek soonsaan registaan mein."*
**🎙️ Narration:** By Allah's command, Ibrahim left Hajar & baby Ismail in a barren valley; Hajar: "Then Allah will not abandon us." Water ran out; she ran between Safa & Marwah; Allah brought forth Zamzam. Years later, Ibrahim & grown Ismail raised the Kaaba, praying "our Lord, accept from us." *Climax:* a mother's run is now Hajj; a baby's spring quenches millions; their walls are the world's qiblah. *Reflection:* Hajar had no guarantee — only trust — and it changed history.
**🔄 Open loop (→ S6):** From his line — Ishaq → Yaqub → a boy whose own brothers threw him in a well: Yusuf.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Hajar/Zamzam = authentic Bukhari; no invented dialogue.

# PART B — YOUTUBE DOCUMENTARY
---

## 📌 Title: "Ibrahim: The Friend of God" · ⏱️ ~36–40 min *(the series' first epic — six major arcs)*

**Overview:** One man against the world — honored by Allah with a title given to no one before: *Khalilullah*. Search for the Creator, smashing idols, the fire, the tyrant king, Lut, the ultimate sacrifice, and the founding of the Kaaba & Hajj. Spine: *total trust in Allah, no matter the cost.*

### 🎥 COLD OPEN
A raging fire cooling to a calm light; flashes (stars/moon/sun; an axe on an idol; a shrinking throne; an overturned city; a ram in light; a mother running, water bursting). *"Allah ne use 'Khalilullah' — apna dost — kaha. Ek insaan kaise khuda ka dost ban jaata hai?"* TITLE.

### 🎙️ INTRODUCTION
Recap S1–S4; shift from nations to one righteous man in a broken world. Timeline → "ONE MAN, ALONE."

### CHAPTER 1 — The Mind That Wouldn't Settle *(E16)* — the idol-soaked world; the fitrah; the star/moon/sun *as reasoning with people*; gentle da'wah to his father (19:42–47). **Why it matters:** seek sincerely; don't fear questions.
### CHAPTER 2 — The Mirror He Held Up *(E17)* — sparing the largest idol; the trial; the "three statements" hadith. **Why it matters:** pride cornered turns to violence — guard your own ego.
### CHAPTER 3 — The Fire and the King *(E18)* — "be cool & safe"; the Nimrod debate (2:258); *king unnamed*. **Why it matters:** fear Allah alone and every "fire" loses power.
### CHAPTER 4 — The Overturned City *(E19)* — the honored guests; Lut's people; the destruction; the wife's fate. **Why it matters:** home/relationship doesn't save you; faith does; "normal" sin is still sin.
### CHAPTER 5 — The Test of Love *(E20)* — the long wait; the dream; Ismail's willing submission; the ransom; Eid al-Adha. **Why it matters:** put Allah before your dearest; He wants hearts, not loss.
### CHAPTER 6 — A Family's Trust *(E21)* — Hajar's tawakkul; Sa'i; Zamzam; the Kaaba; the call to Hajj (22:27). **Why it matters:** trust-driven obedience yields unimaginable barakah.

### 🏁 FINALE
*Khalilullah* explained — friendship built on trust (4:125). Main lesson: never let go of trust in Allah. The Prophet ﷺ comes from Ibrahim's line (via Ismail). → Season 6 (Yusuf): the dream of 11 stars.

### 🎞️ POST-CREDIT TEASER (Season 6)
11 stars; a dark well. *"Tum apne sabse pyaron par bharosa karo… aur wahi tumhe dhoka dein. Ek ladka, ek khwaab, gyaarah jaltе bhai. Quran ki sabse khoobsoorat kahani."*

Signature: star/moon/sun setting; shattered idols + axe; fire cooling; overturning city; the ram; Safa-Marwah + Zamzam + Kaaba walls. The Ibrahim lineage tree (finale). Honesty cards: reasoning (not doubt) / king unnamed / son = Ismail.
