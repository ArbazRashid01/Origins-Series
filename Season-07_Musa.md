# ORIGINS — SEASON 7: MUSA — THE PRINCE WHO DEFIED A GOD-KING
**6 Reels + 1 Documentary (~44 min — the series' largest arc)**
*Theme: All the power on earth is nothing before Allah; true safety is only in trusting Him.*

---
# PART A — REEL SCRIPTS
---

## 🎬 S7 • E26 — "BORN UNDER A DEATH SENTENCE"
**Citations:** Qur'an 28:4 (Pharaoh's tyranny) · 28:5–6 · 28:7 (the river inspiration) · 28:8–9 (Asiya) · 28:11 (the sister) · 28:12–13 (returned to his mother).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Ek baadshah ne hukum diya — har naya ladka maar do. To ek maa ne apne bête ko dariya ke hawaale kar diya."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Pharaoh enslaved Bani Israel, killed their baby sons. Allah inspired Musa's mother to place him in the river (promise: "We'll return him and make him a messenger"). The basket reached Pharaoh's palace; his wife (Asiya) said "do not kill him." The baby refused all wet-nurses; the sister led them to his own mother. *Reflection:* the river (the feared danger) carried him to the palace; Allah opens roads from where you fear.
**🔄 Open loop:** The child destined to destroy Pharaoh was raised by Pharaoh. → E27.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Pharaoh unnamed in Qur'an. Asiya named in hadith (Bukhari), not Qur'an.

## 🎬 S7 • E27 — "THE PRINCE WHO RAN"
**Citations:** Qur'an 28:14 · 28:15–16 (the killing + repentance) · 28:20 (the warning) · 28:21–22 (flight) · 28:23–24 (the well) · 28:27 (marriage).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Misr ka shehzaada. Aish mein pala. Lekin ek hi mukkа use bhagoda bana gaya."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Intervening in a fight, he struck a man who died (unintended; before prophethood); he repented — "I have wronged myself, forgive me" — and Allah forgave. Warned of a plot, he fled to Madyan; helped two women at a well; a righteous old man married him to his daughter for years of work. *Reflection:* he didn't excuse his error — he turned to Allah; and kindness while broken defines character; Allah was preparing him through humble shepherding.
**🔄 Open loop:** One night, a fire on a mountain — and God spoke to him. → E28.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** The killing was unintended + repented. The Madyan elder traditionally Shu'ayb (not named here).

## 🎬 S7 • E28 — "THE BURNING BUSH"
**Citations:** Qur'an 20:10 (the fire) · 20:11–12 (Tuwa) · 20:13–14 ("I am Allah… worship Me") · 20:17–22/27:10–12 (staff + hand) · 20:24 (go to Pharaoh) · 20:25–36/28:34 (his dua + Harun).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Wo apne ghar ke liye aag laane gaye the. Aur waapas aaye — ek Nabi ban kar."*
**🎙️ Narration:** In the sacred valley he was called: "I am Allah; worship Me; establish prayer." Two signs: the staff→serpent, the shining hand. Commanded to face Pharaoh "who has transgressed." Fearful, he turned fear into dua — ease, and his brother Harun's support — granted. *Reflection:* he sought fire, got light; fear before a great task is human — turn it into dua and begin.
**🔄 Open loop:** Return to the palace he fled, to tell a "god-king" he was wrong; Pharaoh summoned his magicians. → E29.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Allah *spoke to* Musa (Kalimullah, 4:164) — affirm reverently, no "how."

## 🎬 S7 • E29 — "THE MAGICIANS' SHOWDOWN"
**Citations:** Qur'an 20:43–44 (gentle call) · 28:38 (Pharaoh's claim) · 20:59 (the contest) · 7:116–117 (the staff swallows illusions) · 7:120–122 (the magicians prostrate) · 7:123–126 ("make us die as Muslims") · 40:28 (the believing man).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Firaun ne apne sabse bade jaadugar bulaaye. Lekin din ke khatam hone tak, wahi jaadugar Allah ko sajda kar rahe the."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Pharaoh claimed godhood; gathered magicians on a festival. Their illusory snakes were swallowed by Musa's staff. *Climax:* the magicians fell in prostration — "we believe in the Lord of Musa & Harun." Pharaoh threatened crucifixion; they stood firm — "make us die as Muslims." *Reflection:* Pharaoh knew the truth but pride refused; the magicians saw it and gave their lives — the difference was the heart, not knowledge.
**🔄 Open loop:** His response was torture — and the plagues began. → E30.

## 🎬 S7 • E30 — "THE PLAGUES & THE ESCAPE"
**Citations:** Qur'an 7:130 · 7:133 (the signs) · 7:134–135 (broken promises) · 26:52/20:77 (travel by night) · 26:60 (pursuit) · 26:61–62 ("my Lord is with me").
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Khoon, tiddiyaan, maut — ek ke baad ek nishaani aati gayi. Aur phir bhi wo zaalim nahi jhuka."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Years of drought, then distinct signs (flood, locusts, lice, frogs, blood); each time they begged and broke their promise. Allah commanded the night exodus; Pharaoh pursued at dawn; trapped at the sea, the people cried "we're caught!" — Musa: "No! My Lord is with me; He will guide me." *Reflection:* same situation, two responses — "we're dead" vs. "my Lord is with me"; yaqeen changes everything.
**🔄 Open loop:** Trapped — then Allah told Musa to strike the sea. → E31.
**⚠️ Doc note:** The Prophet ﷺ fasted Ashura — Musa was saved on that day (Bukhari/Muslim).

## 🎬 S7 • E31 — "THE SEA SPLITS" *(Finale)*
**Citations:** Qur'an 26:63/20:77 (the parting) · 2:50 (Pharaoh drowned) · 10:90–92 (his too-late faith; body preserved) · 20:85–88/7:148 (the golden calf) · 7:149–151 (repentance).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Samandar do hisson mein phat gaya. Aur jo log ye mojza dekhenge — kuch hafton baad ek sone ka but poojेंge."*
**🎙️ Narration:** The sea parted into mountain-like walls; the believers crossed; Pharaoh's army followed and drowned. Drowning, Pharaoh said "I believe" — too late ("Now?!"); his body preserved as a sign. *Climax:* while Musa met his Lord, the people worshipped a golden calf (the Samiri); Musa returned in grief; they repented. *Reflection:* seeing isn't enough — the heart must change; faith is a daily choice, not a one-time miracle.
**🔄 Open loop (→ S8):** Even after the sea, people forgot. Later came the age of kings — a shepherd boy facing a giant.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Pharaoh's preserved body is Qur'anic (10:92) — no specific mummy claim. The Samiri is named in Qur'an.

# PART B — YOUTUBE DOCUMENTARY

## 📌 Title: "Musa vs Pharaoh: The Greatest Showdown" · ⏱️ ~42–46 min *(the series' longest)*

**Overview:** A baby saved from genocide, raised by his enemy, who returns as a prophet to confront a king who claimed to be god. Spine: *all power on earth is nothing before Allah.*

### 🎥 COLD OPEN
A basket on a moonlit river; flashes (palace, staff→serpent, burning bush, magicians prostrating, walls of water, a sinking crown). *"Ek baadshah ne khud ko khuda keh diya… jab duniya ki sabse badi taaqat ek akele insaan ke khilaaf ho — to kaun jeetta hai?"* TITLE.

### 🎙️ INTRODUCTION
Recap S6; Yusuf's descendants enslaved in Egypt. Timeline → "EGYPT — CENTURIES LATER."

### CHAPTER 1 — The River and the Palace *(E26)* — tyranny; the river; Asiya; returned to his mother. **Why it matters:** Allah's plan works through what we fear.
### CHAPTER 2 — The Fugitive *(E27)* — the unintended killing + repentance; flight; Madyan; the "wilderness" as preparation. **Why it matters:** don't excuse error — return; your "stuck" time is preparation.
### CHAPTER 3 — The Sacred Valley *(E28)* — the call (Kalimullah); two signs; fear turned to dua. **Why it matters:** fear is human — turn it into dua and act.
### CHAPTER 4 — The Showdown *(E29)* — Pharaoh's claim; Haman; the contest; the magicians' martyrdom; the believing man of Pharaoh's household (40:28). **Why it matters:** truth is recognized by the heart; speak truth even in a tyrant's court.
### CHAPTER 5 — The Signs & The Night *(E30)* — the signs; broken promises; the exodus; yaqeen at the sea; *Ashura* (Bukhari/Muslim). **Why it matters:** we make and break promises in hardship — certainty is the difference.
### CHAPTER 6 — The Sea & The Calf *(E31)* — the parting; Pharaoh's end + preserved body; "show me Yourself" at Tur (7:143); the golden calf. **Why it matters:** seeing isn't enough — the heart must change; faith is a daily choice.

### 🏁 FINALE
Recap. Main lesson: power vs. trust — Pharaoh had everything but Allah; Musa had only Allah, which was everything. → Season 8 (the age of kings).

### 🎞️ POST-CREDIT TEASER (Season 8)
A war-drum; a giant's shadow; a crown, wind, birds, a palace. *"Ek teenager ek ghulel ke saath aage badhe… Wo baadshah, jo Nabi the — Dawud aur Sulayman."*

### 🎨 Visual Direction
Navy/black + gold; **cyan + gold** accent. Signature: basket on the river; burning bush + staff-serpent; magicians' prostration; trapped at the sea; the parting + sinking crown + crumbling mountain. **No depiction of Allah** at 7:143 — only a wave of light shattering the mountain. Honesty cards: Asiya (hadith); the elder (Shu'ayb); preserved body (no mummy claim).
### 🔊 Sound Direction
Vocals-only nasheed + ambient. Oppressive Egyptian drone; sacred resonance at the call; emotional prostration swell; a thunderous split → silence; the immense roar of the crumbling mountain.
