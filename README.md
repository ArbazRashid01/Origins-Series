## 🎬 SCRIPT BIBLE (Governs Every Script)

**Language:** Conversational Urdu / Hinglish — extremely simple (12-year-old understands, adults appreciate). Every Arabic term explained naturally.

**Tone:** Emotional but not melodramatic · Humanized · Respectful · Authentic · Cinematic · Never theatrical toward Prophets.

**Influences:** Netflix documentary · Nas Daily · Johnny Harris — kept fully halal.

### The Reel Formula (60–90s, every episode)
1. **Pattern-Interrupt Hook (0–5s)** — honest, curiosity-driven, never clickbait
2. **Emotional Setup (5–20s)**
3. **Story Development (20–60s)**
4. **Climax / Revelation (60–80s)**
5. **Reflection (80–90s)** — "why this matters to your life today"
6. **Open Loop** — sincere reason to watch next episode

### The Documentary Formula (1 per season)
Cold Open → Introduction (recap + timeline) → Chapters (each reel = 1 expanded chapter with: context, Qur'an refs, hadith refs, visuals, sound, "Why does this matter today?") → Finale → Post-Credit Teaser

**Runtime Rule:** *"As short as possible, as long as necessary."* Never forced, never padded.
**Priority:** (1) Authenticity (2) Clarity (3) Storytelling (4) Emotional Impact (5) Runtime

## ✅ AUTHENTICITY RULES
- **Qur'an first**, then **authentic Hadith** (Sahih/Hasan, graded), then trusted scholars
- **Never invent dialogue, emotions, or events**
- **Clearly label** weak narrations, Isra'iliyyat, and scholarly differences
- **If unknown — say it is unknown**
- **No depiction** of Allah, Prophets, angels, or Sahaba — light, calligraphy, landscapes, silhouettes only
- **Audio:** vocals-only nasheed / ambient / sound design (no instrumental music)
