# PART A — REEL SCRIPTS

## 🎬 S2 • E6 — "THE CREATION OF ADAM"
**Citations:** Qur'an 2:30 (khalifa) · 38:71/15:29 (clay + the spirit) · 38:75 ("two Hands") · 2:31–33 (taught the names) · 2:34 (angels prostrate) · Sahih al-Bukhari (Adam's height).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Allah ne poori kaaynaat sirf ek lafz se banayi — 'Ho ja.'"* (pause) *"Lekin ek makhlooq ko… usne apne haathon se banaya."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Allah told the angels He'd place a khalifa; they questioned, He said "I know what you do not" (2:30). He formed Adam from clay, breathed of His spirit, and taught him the names — knowledge angels lacked. He commanded the angels to prostrate (honor, not worship); all obeyed. *Climax:* beings of light bowed to a being of clay — because Allah gave him knowledge and a soul. *Reflection:* humble clay + honored soul = humility and dignity together.
**🔄 Open loop:** Adam placed in Jannah; one tree forbidden; a waiting enemy. → E7.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Affirm "two Hands"/"spirit" reverently, no "how" (42:11). Avoid Isra'iliyyat (clay color/type). Angels' question + reply are Qur'anic.

## 🎬 S2 • E7 — "PARADISE & THE FORBIDDEN TREE"
**Citations:** Qur'an 2:35 · 7:20–21 (false oath) · 20:120 (false promise) · 7:22 (they ate, covered themselves) · 20:121.
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Unke paas Jannat thi. Har cheez. Na dukh, na dard, na maut."* (pause) *"Aur sirf ek whisper… ne sab cheen liya."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Adam & Hawwa in Jannah; one rule. Iblis didn't force — he whispered, swore he was a sincere advisor, lied that the tree gave eternity/angelhood. The whisper worked; they ate. *Climax:* instantly shame, covering with leaves — the promise was a lie. *Reflection:* Shaytan dresses wrong as right ("just once," "you deserve it") — it always sounds reasonable.
**🔄 Open loop:** Sent down to earth — but the real lesson was what Adam did next. → E8.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** **Tree type UNKNOWN** (wheat/fig = Isra'iliyyat). **No Hawwa-blame** (no Qur'anic basis; addresses both / mentions Adam, 20:121).

## 🎬 S2 • E8 — "THE FALL TO EARTH"
**Citations:** Qur'an 2:36 · 2:37 (repentance accepted; At-Tawwab) · 7:23 (the dua) · 2:38 (guidance will come) · 7:12–13 (Iblis's self-justification — callback).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Do ne galti ki. Ek hamesha ke liye barbaad. Doosra Jannat ka waaris. Farq sirf ek cheez ka tha."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Iblis justified himself ("I am better") — never repented. Adam did the opposite: turned to Allah, admitted the wrong; Allah taught him the words — *"Rabbana zalamna anfusana…"* (7:23). *Climax:* Allah forgave immediately (At-Tawwab). The difference wasn't the sin — Iblis ran *from* Allah; Adam ran *to* Him. *Reflection:* everyone falls; what defines you is whether you make excuses (Iblis) or return (Adam). The door never closes.
**🔄 Open loop:** Humanity began; among Adam's sons came the first jealousy and the first blood. → E9.

## 🎬 S2 • E9 — "THE FIRST MURDER" *(Finale)*
**Citations:** Qur'an 5:27 (two offerings + "muttaqin") · 5:28 (non-retaliation) · 5:30 · 5:31 (the crow) · Sahih al-Bukhari (first son's share of every unjust killing).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Is zameen par pehli maut budhaape se nahi aayi. Wo ek qatl tha — qaatil tha apna hi sagaa bhai."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Two sons offered a sacrifice; one accepted. Envy ignited; the rejected one threatened murder. The righteous brother: "Allah accepts from the God-conscious… even if you raise your hand, I won't — I fear Allah" (5:27–28). *Climax:* he killed him — history's first murder. A crow showed how to bury the body (5:31); too-late regret. *Reflection:* the same envy that felled Iblis spilled the first blood; jealousy burns its carrier first; true strength is God-consciousness over revenge.
**🔄 Open loop (→ S3):** Generations drifted into idolatry — until a man warned them for 950 years.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** The two sons are **unnamed in the Qur'an** (Habil/Qabil = tradition). The "sister/marriage" motive = **Isra'iliyyat** — avoid. Murder implied, never shown.

## 📌 Title: "Adam: The First Human & The First Sin" · ⏱️ ~21–24 min

**Overview:** The human story begins — dignity (honored clay, taught knowledge), a single boundary, the defining lesson of how to fall and rise again, and the first blood. Spine: *the door of repentance never closes.*

### 🎥 COLD OPEN
A handful of glowing clay; *"Allah ne sab kuch ek lafz se banaya… lekin ek cheez apne haathon se."* Flashes: light entering clay, a garden + one tree, a whisper, gold draining to grey, a red drop. Compelling question: *"Agar tumhe sab mile aur sirf ek shart todni pade — tum kya karte? Aur galti ke baad, karte kya?"* TITLE.

### 🎙️ INTRODUCTION
Recap S1 (eternal Allah → angels → jinn → Iblis's vow). Placement: the human against whom Iblis swore is now created. Timeline → "HUMANITY BEGINS."

### CHAPTER 1 — The Honored Clay *(E6)* — khalifa; "I know what you do not"; clay + spirit (reverent, no "how"); taught the names; the prostration of honor; Adam's height (Bukhari). **Why it matters:** humility (dust) + dignity (chosen by Allah) together.
### CHAPTER 2 — The One Rule *(E7)* — the test of freedom + one boundary; how waswasa works; *tree-type unknown*; *Qur'an doesn't blame Hawwa*; the birth of conscience (shame). **Why it matters:** the whisper always sounds logical — recognize the voice.
### CHAPTER 3 — The First Return *(E8)* — arrogance (Iblis) vs. repentance (Adam) as the moral spine; *At-Tawwab*; the dua (7:23) believers still use; earth as the start of purpose. **Why it matters:** Iblis ran from Allah, Adam ran to Him — the door is always open, no barrier, no intermediary.
### CHAPTER 4 — The First Blood *(E9)* — envy (same sin as Iblis); the righteous brother's restraint; the crow & first burial; *sons unnamed; motive = Isra'iliyyat*. **Why it matters:** envy burns its carrier first; real strength is fearing Allah over revenge.

### 🏁 FINALE
Recap. Main lesson: erring is human; the *response* to error is everything — Iblis's path (pride) vs. Adam's path (repentance). Generations slide toward idolatry → tease Nuh (950 years, an ark). → Season 3 card.
