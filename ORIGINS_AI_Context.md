# EPISODE 1 TOPICS — BEFORE TIME, ALLAH'S ETERNALITY, THE FIRST CREATIONS


## 1A — WHO IS ALLAH? (His Eternality & Self-Sufficiency)

### ✅ Qur'anic
- **57:3** — "He is al-Awwal (the First) — nothing before Him; al-Akhir (the Last) — nothing after Him; az-Zahir (the Manifest) — nothing above Him; al-Batin (the Hidden) — nothing closer than Him."
- **112 (al-Ikhlas)** — "Say: He is Allah, One. Allah as-Samad (the Self-Sufficient, upon whom all depend). He begets not, nor was He begotten. And there is none comparable to Him."
- **42:11** — "There is nothing like unto Him, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing." (The master verse on attributes.)
- **2:255 (Ayat al-Kursi)** — "Allah — no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Self-Sustaining. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and the earth…"
- **59:22–24** — A cluster of Names: al-Malik, al-Quddus, as-Salam, al-Mu'min, al-Muhaymin, al-'Aziz, al-Jabbar, al-Mutakabbir, al-Khaliq, al-Bari', al-Musawwir.
- **20:8** — "Allah — there is no deity except Him. To Him belong the most beautiful names."
- **7:180** — "The most beautiful names belong to Allah, so call upon Him by them."
- **6:103** — "Vision perceives Him not, but He perceives all vision."
- **55:26–27** — "Everyone upon the earth will perish, and there will remain the Face of your Lord, Owner of Majesty and Honor."
- **28:88** — "Everything will be destroyed except His Face."
- **51:56** — "I did not create jinn and mankind except to worship Me."

### ✅ Sahih Hadith
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — Imran ibn Husayn: "Allah existed and there was nothing else besides Him, and His Throne was over the water. Then He wrote in the Preserved Tablet everything, and He created the heavens and the earth."
- **Sahih Muslim** — The du'a: "You are al-Awwal, so there is nothing before You. You are al-Akhir, so there is nothing after You. You are az-Zahir, so there is nothing above You. You are al-Batin, so there is nothing closer than You."
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — "Allah has ninety-nine names — whoever comprehends/preserves (ahsaha) them will enter Paradise."
- **Sahih Muslim** — "Allah was, when there was nothing other than Him, and His Throne was upon water, and He wrote in the Dhikr (the Preserved Tablet) everything, and He created the heavens and the earth." (variant wording)

### ⚠️ Discussed / Needs Verification
- **The list of 99 names:** The *concept* (99 names) is Sahih (Bukhari/Muslim). But the **specific enumerated list** that circulates comes from a narration through al-Walid ibn Muslim — scholars like Ibn Hajar and Ibn Taymiyyah note this specific list may be an insertion by the narrator, not the Prophet's ﷺ words. The concept is authentic; the particular list's chain is debated.
- **"Before creating the heavens and the earth by 50,000 years"** — Sahih Muslim: "Allah wrote the decrees of creation 50,000 years before He created the heavens and the earth, and His Throne was upon water." (This is sahih but scholars discuss what "50,000 years" means relative to earthly time.)

### ❌ What you CANNOT assert
- What "Allah was doing" before creation — no authentic text tells us; speculation is forbidden.
- Any specific "timeline" of creation (first this, then that, then this) beyond what the texts explicitly state.
- That the Throne was "the first creation" vs. the Pen — this is a scholarly debate (see below).


## 1B — THE THRONE (al-'Arsh)

### ✅ Qur'anic
- **11:7** — "His Throne was upon the water" (before creation of heavens & earth).
- **7:54** — "Then He rose over (istawa 'ala) the Throne."
- **69:17** — "The angels will be on its sides, and eight will bear the Throne of your Lord above them that Day."
- **40:7** — "Those who carry the Throne and those around it glorify the praises of their Lord and believe in Him and ask forgiveness for the believers."
- **9:129** — "He is the Lord of the Mighty Throne."
- **23:86** — "Who is the Lord of the seven heavens, and Lord of the Great Throne?"
- **2:255** — "His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth."

### ✅ Sahih Hadith
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — "Allah existed, and nothing was with Him, and His Throne was over the water."
- **Sahih Muslim** — Abu Dharr asked about the Kursi; the Prophet ﷺ said: "The seven heavens compared to the Kursi are like a ring thrown in a desert. And the Kursi compared to the Throne is like that ring in that desert." (shows immense scale)
- **Sahih Muslim** — "The Throne is above the water, and Allah is above the Throne, and He knows what you are doing." (Note: scholars differ on whether this is marfu' or mawquf; handle with care.)

### Scholarly notes
- **Istawa 'ala al-'Arsh** (rose over the Throne): Imam Malik was asked about it and said: "The istawa is known, the 'how' is unknown, belief in it is obligatory, and asking about it is an innovation." This is the mainstream aqidah approach.
- The Throne is the greatest of all creations in size.
- Was the Throne created before the Pen or after? — **debated** (see below).

### ⚠️ The Throne vs. The Pen — which was first?
- **Position 1:** The Throne was first (based on Bukhari: "His Throne was over water" before creation of heavens/earth). Ibn Taymiyyah held this.
- **Position 2:** The Pen was first (based on Abu Dawud/Tirmidhi: "the first thing Allah created was the Pen"). Some scholars reconcile: the Pen was the first thing created *after* the Throne + water.
- **Our position:** State both honestly; don't assert one over the other.


## 1C — THE PEN (al-Qalam) & THE PRESERVED TABLET (al-Lawh al-Mahfuz)

### ✅ Qur'anic
- **68:1** — "Nun. By the Pen and what they inscribe."
- **85:21–22** — "It is a Glorious Qur'an, in a Preserved Tablet (Lawh Mahfuz)."
- **57:22** — "No calamity befalls on earth or in yourselves but it is inscribed in a Book before We bring it into existence."
- **54:52–53** — "Everything they did is in the records (zubur). Every matter, small and great, is inscribed."

### ✅ / ⚠️ Hadith
- **Abu Dawud & at-Tirmidhi** (graded sahih by al-Albani; others debate): "The first thing Allah created was the Pen. He said to it: 'Write.' It said: 'What shall I write?' He said: 'Write the decree (maqadir) of everything until the Hour.'"
- **Sahih Muslim** — "Allah wrote the decrees of creation 50,000 years before He created the heavens and the earth, and His Throne was upon water."
- **at-Tirmidhi** — "The Pen has dried with what will be." (The decree is complete; nothing changes.)

### Scholarly notes
- The Pen wrote everything that will happen until the Day of Judgment — all qadar/destiny.
- The Lawh al-Mahfuz is where it is all recorded.
- This is the foundation of belief in **Qadar** (divine decree) — one of the six pillars of faith.
- The full "chain" as some scholars present it: Throne (over water) → Pen → Lawh al-Mahfuz → then the heavens and earth. But this exact sequence is **assembled by scholars**, not stated in one single hadith; treat it as scholarly synthesis.

### ❌ What you CANNOT assert
- The exact material/color of the Pen or Tablet.
- The exact location of the Tablet relative to the Throne (some reports exist but chains are weak).
- Any "visual" beyond what the text says.

## 1D — THE WATER

### ✅ Qur'anic + Sahih
- **Qur'an 11:7** — "His Throne was upon the water."
- **Qur'an 21:30** — "We made from water every living thing." (Water = the origin of biological life.)
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — "His Throne was over the water" (confirms water existed before the heavens/earth).

### Scholarly notes
- Water existed before the heavens and earth — this is established.
- Beyond that (what the water was on, how vast it was), the texts are silent — don't speculate.

## 1E — THE CREATION OF THE HEAVENS & EARTH

### ✅ Qur'anic
- **41:9–12** — Created the earth in two "days" (periods), set mountains, blessed it, determined provisions in four periods, then turned to the heaven (smoke) and made seven heavens in two periods.
- **21:30** — "The heavens and the earth were joined together, and We separated them."
- **51:47** — "We built the heaven with might, and We are expanding it."
- **67:3** — "He created seven heavens in layers."
- **71:15** — "Do you not see how Allah created seven heavens in layers?"
- **2:29** — "He it is who created for you all that is on earth, then He turned to the heaven and made them seven heavens."
- **79:27–33** — The sequence: heaven raised, night/day, earth spread, water/pasture, mountains.

### ⚠️ Scholarly discussion
- "Six days" of creation (7:54, 50:38) — most scholars say "days" here means **periods/stages**, not 24-hour days; al-Tabari & Ibn Kathir discuss this.
- The "smoke" (41:11) — some scholars see it as a gaseous/nebular state.
- Whether the earth or heavens were created first — scholars reconcile the verses differently (see Ibn Kathir's discussion).
- **None of this is in conflict with modern cosmology if understood as periods** — but this is a scholarly opinion, not a certainty. Don't force science onto the text.

### ❌ Popular fabrications about creation order
- The viral "creation-on-each-day" chart (Saturday = earth, Sunday = mountains, etc.) — **this comes from a hadith in Sahih Muslim that many scholars INCLUDING Muslim himself note has issues (some chains go back to Ka'b al-Ahbar, a Jewish convert).** It's disputed. Don't use it as definitive.

# EPISODE 2 TOPICS — THE ANGELS (al-Malaika)

## 2A — THEIR CREATION & NATURE

### ✅ Sahih
- **Sahih Muslim** — Aisha (R.A.): "The angels were created from light, the jinn from smokeless fire, and Adam from what has been described to you (clay)."
- This is THE foundational hadith for all three — one single narration covers them all.

### ✅ Qur'anic nature
- **21:20** — "They glorify [Allah] night and day and do not slacken." (never tire)
- **66:6** — "They do not disobey Allah in what He commands them and they do what they are commanded." (no free will to rebel)
- **21:26–27** — "They are but honored servants. They do not precede Him in speech, and they act by His command."
- **16:49–50** — "Whatever is in the heavens and whatever creatures are on earth prostrate to Allah, and the angels — they are not arrogant. They fear their Lord above them, and they do what they are commanded."
- **35:1** — "Praise be to Allah, Creator of the heavens and earth, who made the angels messengers having wings — two, three, or four. He increases in creation what He wills."

### ✅ Their immense scale
- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** — The Prophet ﷺ saw Jibreel in his true form: he had **600 wings**, and he **filled the entire horizon** between the east and west.
- **Qur'an 53:5–9** — "Taught to him by one of mighty power, possessing strength. He appeared in his true form while on the highest horizon."
- **Qur'an 81:19–21** — "It is the word of a noble messenger, possessing power, secure in position with the Owner of the Throne, obeyed and trustworthy."
- **Sahih Muslim** — The Prophet ﷺ described an angel: "I was given permission to speak about one of the angels who bears the Throne: the distance between his earlobe and his shoulder is a journey of 700 years." (Some scholars discuss this narration's exact wording.)
- **Abu Dawud (sahih chain)** — "I was given permission to describe one of the Throne-bearing angels: from his earlobe to his shoulder is the distance of 700 years' travel."

### ✅ Their numbers are beyond counting
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — Night Journey narration: "I was shown al-Bayt al-Ma'mur (the Frequented House in the seventh heaven). Seventy thousand angels pray in it every day, and when they leave, they never return to it — (such is their number that each gets only one chance)."
- **Qur'an 74:31** — "And none knows the soldiers of your Lord except Him."

### ⚠️ Do they have a form we can describe?
- They can appear in human form (Jibreel appeared to Maryam as a man — 19:17; appeared to the Prophet ﷺ as a man — the hadith of Jibreel in Muslim). But their TRUE form is described only as immense, with wings.
- **We do NOT know** their default appearance beyond "light" + "wings." Any depiction is speculative.

## 2B — NAMED ANGELS & THEIR ROLES

### ✅ Jibreel (Gabriel)
- **Qur'an 2:97** — "Whoever is an enemy to Jibreel — then indeed he brought it (the Qur'an) down upon your heart by Allah's permission."
- **Qur'an 2:98** — "Whoever is an enemy to Allah, His angels, His messengers, Jibreel and Mikail — then indeed Allah is an enemy to the disbelievers."
- **Qur'an 66:4** — "Allah is his protector, and Jibreel, and the righteous believers, and the angels after that are his helpers."
- **Qur'an 26:193** — "The Trustworthy Spirit (ar-Ruh al-Amin) brought it down."
- **Qur'an 81:19–21** — Described as noble, mighty, obeyed in the heavens, trustworthy.
- **Role:** Bringing revelation (wahi) to the prophets. He is the greatest of all angels.
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — He brought the first revelation (Iqra).

### ✅ Mikail (Michael)
- **Qur'an 2:98** — Named alongside Jibreel.
- **Role (from scholars/tradition):** Responsible for rain and provision (rizq). This is widely accepted by scholars but the specific hadith assigning this role is discussed.

### ⚠️ Israfil
- **NOT named in the Qur'an.** His name comes from hadith traditions.
- The Trumpet (as-Sur) is mentioned in the Qur'an: **39:68** — "The Trumpet will be blown, and whoever is in the heavens and earth will fall dead except whom Allah wills. Then it will be blown again, and they will stand."
- **Role:** Blowing the Trumpet on the Day of Judgment. Widely accepted in scholarship.
- **Abu Dawud, at-Tirmidhi, an-Nasa'i** — narrations mentioning Israfil by name; some chains are strong, others discussed.
- **Our position:** Mention him as "widely known from hadith tradition" — the Trumpet-blower is Qur'anic (39:68, 69:13), the *name* Israfil is from Sunnah.

### ✅ The Angel of Death (Malak al-Mawt)
- **Qur'an 32:11** — "Say: the Angel of Death, who has been put in charge of you, will take your souls."
- **The name "Azrael"** — NOT in the Qur'an, NOT in any sahih hadith from the Prophet ﷺ. It comes from Isra'iliyyat/Jewish tradition. **Do not use it.**

### ✅ The Throne-Bearers
- **Qur'an 69:17** — "And the angels will be on its sides, and eight will bear the Throne of your Lord above them that Day."
- **Qur'an 40:7** — "Those who carry the Throne and those around it glorify their Lord, believe in Him, and ask forgiveness for the believers."

### ✅ The Recording Angels (Kiraman Katibin)
- **Qur'an 82:10–12** — "And indeed over you are keepers (hafizin), noble recorders (kiraman katibin), who know whatever you do."
- **Qur'an 50:17–18** — "When the two receivers (angels) receive, seated on the right and on the left. He does not utter a word except that with him is an observer prepared."
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — "The angels of the night and the angels of the day succeed one another, and they meet at Fajr prayer and Asr prayer." Then when the angels who were with you during the night ascend, Allah asks them (though He knows): "How did you leave My servants?" They say: "We left them while they were praying, and we came to them while they were praying."

### ✅ Angels who seek forgiveness for believers
- **Qur'an 40:7–9** — "Those who carry the Throne and those around it glorify the praises of their Lord and believe in Him, and ask forgiveness for those who believe: 'Our Lord, You have encompassed all things in mercy and knowledge, so forgive those who repented and followed Your way…'"
- **Qur'an 42:5** — "The angels glorify the praises of their Lord and ask forgiveness for those on earth."

### ✅ Angels at gatherings of dhikr
- **Sahih Muslim** — "Allah has angels who roam the roads seeking out people of dhikr. When they find a group remembering Allah, they call to one another: 'Come to what you seek!' and they surround them with their wings up to the lowest heaven."
- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** — "No group gathers in a house of the houses of Allah (a mosque), reciting the Book of Allah and studying it among themselves, except that tranquility descends upon them, mercy covers them, the angels surround them, and Allah mentions them to those near Him."

### ✅ The guardians of Hellfire
- **Qur'an 66:6** — "Over it (Hellfire) are angels fierce and severe; they do not disobey Allah in what He commands them, and they do what they are ordered."
- **Qur'an 74:30–31** — "Over it are nineteen (angels)."

### ✅ Angels assigned to the womb
- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** — "An angel is sent to the womb after 40 days (or 40 nights) and writes: his provision, his lifespan, his deeds, and whether he will be wretched or blessed."

### ✅ Munkar & Nakir (the questioners in the grave)
- **at-Tirmidhi (sahih)** — The Prophet ﷺ mentioned two angels who come to question in the grave. Their names "Munkar & Nakir" appear in some narrations; scholars accept the concept with these names widely.

### ⚠️ Ridwan (keeper of Paradise) & Malik (keeper of Hell)
- **Malik** is named in the Qur'an: **43:77** — "They will call: 'O Malik! Let your Lord put an end to us!' He will say: 'You are to remain.'"
- **Ridwan** — the keeper of Paradise — his name is NOT in the Qur'an; it comes from hadith traditions of varying strength. Widely accepted by scholars but technically not as firmly established as Malik.

## 2C — WHAT ANGELS DO NOT HAVE
- ❌ Free will to disobey (66:6; 21:27)
- ❌ Desires/needs (they don't eat, drink, sleep, marry — this is scholarly consensus from the texts)
- ❌ Gender (they are not male or female — Qur'an rebukes those who called them "daughters of Allah," e.g., 53:27)
- ❌ Boredom or fatigue (21:20)

## 2D — CAN ANGELS HAVE OPINIONS/QUESTIONS?
- ✅ YES — they asked about Adam (2:30) — but this was adab (respectful seeking of wisdom), NOT arrogance or objection. They submitted immediately when Allah answered.
- This is the KEY contrast with Iblis: the angels *asked* out of humility, then obeyed; Iblis *refused* out of pride.

# EPISODE 3 TOPICS — THE JINN

## 3A — THEIR CREATION & NATURE

### ✅ Qur'anic + Sahih
- **Qur'an 55:15** — "And He created the jinn from a smokeless flame of fire."
- **Qur'an 15:27** — "And the jinn We created before (mankind) from scorching fire."
- **Sahih Muslim** — "…the jinn from smokeless fire (marij min nar)…"
- **Qur'an 7:27** — "He (Shaytan) and his tribe see you from where you do not see them."
- **Qur'an 51:56** — "I did not create jinn and mankind except to worship Me."

### ✅ They have free will & accountability
- **Qur'an 72:11** — "Among us are the righteous, and among us are others; we are of divided ways."
- **Qur'an 72:14–15** — "Among us are Muslims, and among us are the unjust. Whoever accepts Islam has sought out the right course. But as for the unjust — they will be firewood for Hell."
- **Qur'an 6:130** — On Judgment Day: "O assembly of jinn and mankind, did there not come to you messengers from among you, relating to you My verses…?"
- **Qur'an 46:29–31** — Jinn listened to the Qur'an and went back to warn their people.
- **Qur'an 55:39** — "On that Day neither man nor jinn will be asked about his sin." (i.e., sins will be known; both are accountable)

### ✅ They eat, drink, marry, reproduce, die
- **Sahih Muslim** — The Prophet ﷺ said: "Do not clean yourselves with dung or bones, for they are food for your brothers among the jinn." (They eat.)
- **Scholarly consensus** — Jinn have offspring (Qur'an 18:50 — "Will you then take him and his descendants as allies rather than Me?"); they die (otherwise there'd be no accountability/judgment).
- **Qur'an 55:56** — "In them (gardens) are women limiting their glances, untouched before by man or jinn." (Implies jinn have relations.)

### ⚠️ Three types of jinn
- **Hadith (at-Tabarani; graded by some as sahih/hasan via al-Hakim, also al-Albani authenticated a similar chain):** "The jinn are three types: one type flies in the air, one type consists of snakes and dogs, and one type halts for rest then resumes travel."
- **Status:** Acceptable to mention if labeled as "from hadith tradition; scholars discuss grading."

### ✅ The Qarin (your jinn companion)
- **Sahih Muslim** — "There is none of you who does not have a companion (qarin) from among the jinn appointed for him." They said: "Even you, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "Even me, but Allah helped me against him, and he submitted (aslama) — so he only commands me to do good." (Or: "so I am safe from him" — two readings of the Arabic.)
- This is established — every human has a qarin from the jinn who tries to lead them astray.

### ✅ Shaytan's access to humans
- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** — "Shaytan runs through the son of Adam as blood runs through his veins."
- **Qur'an 114:4–6** — "The retreating whisperer, who whispers into the hearts of people, from among jinn and mankind."
- **Qur'an 7:200–201** — "If an evil suggestion comes to you from Shaytan, seek refuge in Allah."
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — "Shaytan sits in the paths of the son of Adam…" (long hadith about Shaytan discouraging at every step: hijrah, jihad, etc.)

### ✅ Protection from jinn/Shaytan
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — "Whoever recites Ayat al-Kursi (2:255) at night, a guardian angel is appointed for him, and Shaytan cannot approach him until morning."
- **Sahih Muslim** — "Whoever recites the last two verses of Surah al-Baqarah at night, they will suffice him."
- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** — The Mu'awwidhat (113, 114) and morning/evening adhkar are protection.
- **Qur'an 23:97–98** — "Say: My Lord, I seek refuge in You from the prompting of the devils."

## 3B — JINN & THE QUR'AN (Surah al-Jinn)

### ✅ The full event
- **Qur'an 72:1–2** — "Say: it has been revealed to me that a group of jinn listened and said: 'We have heard a wondrous Qur'an — it guides to the right course, and we have believed in it.'"
- **Qur'an 46:29–31** — "And when We directed to you a few of the jinn listening to the Qur'an — when they attended it, they said: 'Listen.' When it was concluded, they went back to their people as warners. They said: 'O our people, we have heard a Book revealed after Musa, confirming what came before it. It guides to the truth.'"
- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** — The Prophet ﷺ was reciting at Nakhlah during the night when a group of jinn heard and believed. (This is the context of Surah al-Jinn.)
- **Qur'an 72:11** — The jinn themselves said: "Among us are the righteous, and among us are others. We are on divided ways."

## 3C — JINN ON EARTH BEFORE ADAM (the scholarly discussion)

### ⚠️ Tafsir/Athar (NOT sahih marfu' hadith)
- **Ibn Kathir** (tafsir of 2:30) cites reports from Ibn Abbas, Qatadah, and ar-Rabi' ibn Anas that before Adam, jinn inhabited the earth and caused corruption and bloodshed.
- **at-Tabari** cites similar.
- **The angels' question** (2:30 — "Will You place therein one who causes corruption and sheds blood?") implies they had prior knowledge of this — where did they get it? Scholars say: from what they witnessed with the jinn before Adam.
- **Status:** This is scholarly *interpretation* (tafsir), not a direct Qur'anic statement or sahih hadith from the Prophet ﷺ. It's widely accepted but should be presented as "scholars say" — not "it happened exactly like this."

### ❌ The "jinn-angel war" narrative
- **at-Tabari and others** narrate: Allah sent an army of angels (some say Iblis was among them / leading them) to fight the corrupt jinn on earth and drive them to mountains/islands/seas.
- **Status:** These come from **athar** (companion/tabi'in reports) and some from **Ka'b al-Ahbar** (a Jewish convert whose narrations are often Isra'iliyyat). The chains are weak or untraceable to the Prophet ﷺ.
- **The claim that Iblis "led" the angel-army contradicts** 18:50 (he was OF the jinn, not an angel commanding them).
- **Our verdict:** ❌ Do NOT present as fact. If mentioned at all, clearly label as "from tafsir traditions; not established; chains are weak."

## 3D — IMPORTANT MISCONCEPTIONS TO CORRECT
- ❌ **Jinn are NOT inherently evil.** Among them are believers and disbelievers — just like humans (72:11).
- ❌ **Jinn are NOT "demons."** "Shaytan" (devils) refers specifically to the rebellious disbelieving jinn (and evil humans — 6:112). Not all jinn are shayateen.
- ❌ **Jinn are NOT folklore/horror creatures.** They are a separate creation with free will, accountable before Allah.
- ❌ **Shaytan cannot FORCE you** — only suggest/whisper (14:22; 114:4–6).
- ✅ **But they are REAL** — belief in the unseen (including jinn) is part of faith (2:3).

# EPISODE 4 TOPICS — IBLIS & HIS REFUSAL

## 4A — WHO IS IBLIS?

### ✅ He is a JINN, not an angel
- **Qur'an 18:50** — "And when We said to the angels: 'Prostrate to Adam,' they prostrated, EXCEPT Iblis. He was of the jinn, and he departed from the command of his Lord."
- This single verse settles the debate — he was NOT a "fallen angel." The Islamic position differs from some other traditions.

### ✅ He was among the angels (elevated by worship)
- **Qur'an 15:31** — "Except Iblis — he refused to be among those who prostrated." (He was *counted* among them.)
- **Qur'an 2:34** — "And when We said to the angels: 'Prostrate to Adam,' they prostrated, except Iblis."
- **Scholars explain:** He was a jinn by creation (from fire), but was elevated to the rank/company of the angels due to his intense worship of Allah. When the command came, he was included in it.

### ⚠️ How long did he worship?
- Popular claim: "6,000 years" / "60,000 years" / various numbers.
- **Status:** ❌ These come from **Isra'iliyyat and weak narrations.** No sahih hadith gives a specific duration. Keep it vague: "his worship was so much that he was elevated among the angels" — this is the safe scholarly framing.

### ⚠️ Was he the "best" of the jinn? Did he have a special name?
- Some tafsir traditions say his original name was "Azazeel" and he was the "peacock of the angels."
- **Status:** ❌ These are from Isra'iliyyat (Ka'b al-Ahbar and similar). NOT established. Do not use.

## 4B — THE COMMAND & THE REFUSAL

### ✅ The command to prostrate
- **Qur'an 2:34 · 7:11 · 15:28–29 · 17:61 · 18:50 · 20:116 · 38:71–72** — All mention the command. It appears in at least 7 places in the Qur'an — showing its immense importance.
- **Nature of the prostration:** It was a prostration of *honor/greeting* (tahiyyah), NOT worship (ibadah). Worship-prostration is for Allah alone. This was a command from Allah to honor Adam — and disobeying the *command* was the sin, not the prostration itself.
- **Scholarly consensus:** Prostration to other than Allah was permitted in earlier laws as a form of greeting/honor (see Yusuf 12:100 — his family "prostrated" to him). In our Shari'ah it is forbidden, but in the time of the angels/Adam it was permitted *by Allah's command.*

### ✅ Allah's question — the chance to repent
- **Qur'an 7:12** — "Allah said: 'What prevented you from prostrating when I commanded you?'"
- **Qur'an 38:75** — "He said: 'O Iblis, what prevented you from prostrating to that which I created with My hands? Were you arrogant, or are you among the exalted?'"
- Note: Allah gave him a chance — asked him. This was a door to say "I'm sorry." He chose pride instead.

### ✅ Iblis's answer — the "first false qiyas"
- **Qur'an 7:12** — "He said: 'I am better than him. You created me from fire, and You created him from clay.'"
- **Qur'an 38:76** — Same: "I am better than him — You created me from fire and created him from clay."
- **Scholarly analysis:** This is the first recorded instance of using personal *reasoning* (qiyas) to override a direct divine command. He judged that fire > clay, therefore he > Adam. But:
  - He assumed material determines worth — FALSE (49:13 — honor is by taqwa).
  - He assumed his own judgment overrides Allah's command — the root of his sin.
  - He was asked "What prevented you?" — implying the command should have been enough, regardless of any "reason."

### ✅ The three-step fall (2:34)
- **Qur'an 2:34** — "…except Iblis: (1) he refused (abaa), (2) he was arrogant (wastakbara), (3) and was among the disbelievers (wa kaana min al-kaafirin)."
- Scholars note the chain: refusal → pride → kufr. It escalated.
- Some scholars note "kaana min al-kaafirin" — "WAS among the disbelievers" — as if this was always his potential/destiny, now revealed.

### ✅ The consequence
- **Qur'an 7:13** — "Descend from it (your rank/Paradise). It is not for you to be arrogant here. Get out — you are among the debased."
- **Qur'an 38:77–78** — "Get out of it, for you are expelled, accursed. And upon you is My curse until the Day of Judgment."
- **Qur'an 7:18** — "Get out of it, disgraced and expelled. Whoever of them follows you — I will fill Hellfire with you, all together."
- **Qur'an 38:85** — "I will surely fill Hellfire with you and with those of them that follow you, all together."

## 4C — THE DEFINITION OF ARROGANCE (KIBR)

### ✅ Sahih hadith — the complete definition
- **Sahih Muslim** — Abdullah ibn Mas'ud reported: The Prophet ﷺ said: "No one who has an atom's weight of arrogance (kibr) in his heart will enter Paradise." A man said: "But a man likes his clothes to be nice and his shoes to be nice." He said: "Indeed Allah is Beautiful and loves beauty. Kibr is rejecting the truth (batar al-haqq) and looking down on people (ghamt an-nas)."
- **Two components:**
  1. **Batar al-haqq** — refusing/rejecting truth when it's presented to you (because your ego won't accept it)
  2. **Ghamt an-nas** — despising/belittling people (seeing yourself as above them)
- Iblis had BOTH: he rejected Allah's command (batar al-haqq) AND despised Adam (ghamt an-nas).

### ✅ Supporting texts on arrogance
- **Qur'an 40:35** — "Thus does Allah seal over every arrogant, tyrannical heart."
- **Qur'an 39:72** — "Enter the gates of Hell to abide eternally therein — wretched is the abode of the arrogant."
- **Qur'an 16:23** — "Indeed He does not like the arrogant."
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — "Allah says: 'Pride (al-kibriya) is My cloak and greatness (al-'azamah) is My garment. Whoever competes with Me in either of them, I will throw into the Fire.'" (Hadith Qudsi)

## 4D — THE ADAM CONTRAST (the moral spine of the series)

### ✅ Both erred — different responses
- **Iblis's error:** Refused a command → justified himself → never repented → vowed to corrupt others.
- **Adam's error (coming in S2):** Ate from the tree → felt shame immediately → turned to Allah → said "we have wronged ourselves" (7:23) → Allah forgave him.
- **The lesson:** The sin itself didn't determine their fate — the RESPONSE did.
  - Iblis: ran FROM Allah (pride → self-justification → enmity).
  - Adam: ran TO Allah (shame → admission → repentance → forgiveness).
- **Qur'an 49:13** destroys Iblis's logic: honor isn't by origin (fire/clay/color/race) — it's by taqwa.

## 4E — DOES IBLIS BELIEVE IN ALLAH?

### ✅ Yes — and this is the terrifying point
- Iblis acknowledged Allah directly — he was speaking TO Him.
- He acknowledged Allah as Creator ("You created me from fire").
- He acknowledged the Day of Judgment ("Give me respite until the Day they are resurrected" — 7:14).
- He acknowledged Allah's power.
- **His kufr was not denial of Allah's existence** — it was arrogant refusal to OBEY. This is *kufr al-istikbar* (disbelief of arrogance), not *kufr al-juhud* (disbelief of denial).
- **Lesson:** Knowing Allah exists is not enough. Even Iblis "believes" Allah exists. What matters is submission, obedience, and humility.

# EPISODE 5 TOPICS — THE VOW AGAINST HUMANITY (Iblis's Respite & Mission)

## 5A — THE REQUEST FOR RESPITE

### ✅ Qur'anic
- **Qur'an 7:14** — "He said: 'Give me respite until the Day they are resurrected.'"
- **Qur'an 15:36** — "He said: 'My Lord, then reprieve me until the Day they are resurrected.'"
- **Qur'an 38:79** — "He said: 'My Lord, then reprieve me until the Day they are resurrected.'"

### ✅ Allah's response — the respite granted
- **Qur'an 7:15** — "He (Allah) said: 'You are of those reprieved.'"
- **Qur'an 15:37–38** — "He said: 'Then indeed, you are of those reprieved, until the Day of the time well-known.'"
- **Qur'an 38:80–81** — "He said: 'Then indeed you are of those reprieved, until the Day of the time well-known.'"

### Why did Allah grant it?
- **It is part of the TEST of humanity.** Without a tempter, there is no test; without a test, there is no virtue in choosing right.
- **It is NOT a "defeat" for Allah** — Allah is in full control. He allowed it because it serves His wisdom and plan.
- **Scholars note:** The respite shows that even Iblis's continued existence serves Allah's purpose — and his eventual punishment is guaranteed.

## 5B — THE VOW (his declared mission)

### ✅ Qur'anic (Iblis's own words, recorded)
- **Qur'an 7:16–17** — "He said: 'Because You have put me in error, I will surely sit in wait for them on Your straight path. Then I will come to them from before them and from behind them, and on their right and on their left, and You will not find most of them grateful.'"
- **Qur'an 15:39–40** — "He said: 'My Lord, because You have put me in error, I will surely make (disobedience) attractive to them on earth, and I will mislead them all — except Your chosen servants among them.'"
- **Qur'an 17:62** — "He said: 'Do You see this one whom You have honored above me? If You reprieve me until the Day of Resurrection, I will surely destroy his descendants — except a few.'"
- **Qur'an 38:82–83** — "He said: 'By Your might, I will surely mislead them all — except among them Your chosen servants.'"

### Key details from the vow:
1. He blames Allah for his own fall ("because You put me in error") — the arrogant never take responsibility.
2. He targets humans from ALL directions — front, back, right, left (7:17).
3. He intends to make sin *attractive* (15:39) — not force, but beautify evil.
4. His goal: make them *ungrateful* (7:17 — "you will not find most of them grateful").
5. He admits his own limit: **"except Your chosen servants"** (15:40; 38:83) — the sincere are beyond his reach.

## 5C — THE CRUCIAL LIMIT — HE HAS NO POWER TO COMPEL

### ✅ Qur'anic (the most important point)
- **Qur'an 14:22** — "And Shaytan will say when the matter is decided (on Judgment Day): 'Indeed Allah promised you the promise of truth. And I promised you, but I betrayed you. I had no authority (sultan) over you except that I invited you, and you responded to me. So do not blame me, but blame yourselves.'"
- **Qur'an 15:42** — "Indeed, My servants — you have no authority over them, except those who follow you of the deviators."
- **Qur'an 16:99** — "Indeed, he has no authority over those who believe and rely upon their Lord."
- **Qur'an 17:65** — "Indeed, over My servants, there is for you no authority. And sufficient is your Lord as Disposer of affairs."
- **Qur'an 34:20–21** — About Iblis and his influence: "He had no authority over them — except that We might distinguish those who believe in the Hereafter from those who are in doubt."
- **Qur'an 58:10** — "Secret conversation is only from Shaytan, that he may grieve those who believe. But he will not harm them at all, except by Allah's permission."

### The clear conclusion:
- Shaytan **whispers** (waswasa).
- Shaytan **invites** and **suggests**.
- Shaytan **beautifies** evil.
- Shaytan **CANNOT force, control, or compel.**
- The CHOICE always remains with the human.
- On Judgment Day, he will himself *disown* his followers (14:22).

## 5D — HIS METHODS (how he works — from the texts)

### ✅ Waswasa (whispering)
- **Qur'an 114:4–6** — "From the evil of the retreating whisperer, who whispers into the hearts of people, from among jinn and mankind."
- **Qur'an 7:20** — "Then Shaytan whispered to them (Adam & Eve)…"
- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** — "Shaytan flows through the son of Adam as blood flows through veins."

### ✅ Making evil attractive (tazyin)
- **Qur'an 15:39** — "I will surely make [disobedience] attractive to them on earth."
- **Qur'an 6:43** — "Shaytan made their deeds attractive to them."
- **Qur'an 8:48** — "Shaytan made their deeds seem fair to them."
- He doesn't say "do something evil" — he makes it LOOK good, logical, justified.

### ✅ False promises & creating fear
- **Qur'an 4:120** — "He promises them and arouses desire in them. But Shaytan promises them nothing except delusion."
- **Qur'an 2:268** — "Shaytan threatens you with poverty and orders you to immorality, while Allah promises you forgiveness and bounty."
- He creates fear of loss (poverty, missing out) to push toward sin.

### ✅ Step-by-step escalation
- **Qur'an 24:21** — "O you who believe, do not follow the footsteps of Shaytan." (He leads step by step, not all at once.)
- **Qur'an 2:208** — "Enter into Islam completely, and do not follow the footsteps of Shaytan."
- Scholars note: he doesn't push you from 0 to 100. He takes you one small step at a time — each one seeming "not that bad."

### ✅ Creating division & hatred
- **Qur'an 5:91** — "Shaytan only wants to cause between you animosity and hatred through intoxicants and gambling."
- **Sahih Muslim** — "Iblis sends out his troops; the nearest to him in rank is the one who causes the most fitnah (discord). One of them says: 'I did not leave him until I separated between him and his wife.' Iblis brings him close and says: 'You are the best!'"

### ✅ Distracting from remembrance of Allah
- **Qur'an 58:19** — "Shaytan has overcome them and made them forget the remembrance of Allah."
- **Qur'an 12:42** — "Shaytan made him forget to mention (Yusuf) to his master." (The man in prison forgot Yusuf.)

## 5E — PROTECTION FROM SHAYTAN (comprehensive)

### ✅ From Qur'an
- **Seeking refuge:** 7:200 — "If an evil suggestion comes from Shaytan, seek refuge in Allah."
- **The Mu'awwidhat:** 113:1–5 and 114:1–6 — the two surahs of protection.
- **Ayat al-Kursi:** 2:255.
- **Bismillah** before eating, entering the house, relations — each one shuts a door on Shaytan.

### ✅ From Sahih hadith
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — Ayat al-Kursi at night: "a guardian is appointed and Shaytan cannot approach until morning."
- **Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim** — "When a person enters his house and mentions the name of Allah upon entering and upon eating, Shaytan says: 'There is no lodging for you tonight and no meal for you.'"
- **Sahih Muslim** — "When the adhan is called, Shaytan turns and flees."
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — "Yawning is from Shaytan; if one of you yawns, let him suppress it."
- **Sahih Muslim** — "When one of you wakes from sleep, let him wash his hands before putting them in a vessel (of water), for he does not know where his hand spent the night." (And Shaytan ties knots on the sleeping person, which break with dhikr, wudu, and prayer.)

## 5F — THE SINCERE ARE PROTECTED

### ✅ Qur'anic
- **Qur'an 15:39–40** — Iblis himself admitted: "except among them Your chosen (mukhlas) servants."
- **Qur'an 15:42** — Allah confirmed: "Indeed, over My servants, you have no authority — except those who follow you of the deviators."
- **Qur'an 38:83** — Iblis: "except among them Your chosen servants."
- The *mukhlasin* — those sincere in their devotion to Allah — are beyond Shaytan's reach.

### Who are the mukhlasin?
- Those who worship Allah with sincerity (ikhlas).
- Those who are constant in dhikr, prayer, and seeking refuge.
- Those who maintain tawbah — who keep returning to Allah every time they slip.
- **It doesn't mean "perfect people"** — it means people whose DIRECTION is always toward Allah, even when they fall.
