# Personal Knowledge For Deen
### A Complete Chronological Encyclopedia of Islam and Islamic History

> *"My Lord, increase me in knowledge."* — **Qur'an 20:114**

This is a **private, personal knowledge base** — a living encyclopedia built to teach Islam and Islamic history from *before the beginning of creation* until the present day, exactly as events happened, in chronological order.

It is **not** a collection of YouTube scripts, and **not** short-form content. Each episode is written as a **full research chapter** — detailed enough to stand as a chapter in a published history book, and complete enough that the topic never needs to be researched again from scratch.

---

## How To Read This Encyclopedia

Read in order. Each chapter assumes you know **nothing** and builds the picture brick by brick. The narration style blends the classical historians (Ibn Kathir, at-Tabari, Ibn Hisham) with modern researchers (Martin Lings, Dr. Yasir Qadhi, Dr. Ali as-Sallabi) — beautiful to read, but academically careful.

Every chapter follows the same structure so you always know where to find things:

1. Episode Summary
2. Chronological Timeline
3. Background (political / religious / social / economic / civilizational context)
4. The Story (told in full)
5. Qur'anic Revelation (Arabic · transliteration · translation · tafsir · context)
6. Authentic Hadith (with collection, number, and grading)
7. Historical Sources
8. Important People (biographies)
9. Important Places (geography, maps, modern locations)
10. Important Objects (material culture)
11. Lessons
12. Misconceptions
13. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)
14. Timeline Recap
15. Connections (to previous / next / later history)
16. References

## The Hierarchy of Trust (used in every chapter)

Knowledge is only as good as its source. Throughout this encyclopedia, evidence is ranked and **labelled** so you always know how firm a claim is:

| Rank | Source | Symbol | How to treat it |
|:----:|--------|:------:|-----------------|
| 1 | The Qur'an | ✅ | Absolute, undeniable |
| 2 | Sahih Hadith (Bukhari / Muslim & authenticated others) | ✅ | Authentic, reliable |
| 3 | Hasan Hadith | ☑️ | Acceptable; note the level |
| 4 | Scholarly tafsir / consensus (ijma') | 📖 | Trusted interpretation, but human |
| 5 | Athar (reports of Companions / Tabi'in) | 🕰️ | Valuable context, not binding |
| 6 | Isra'iliyyat / weak (da'if) chains | ⚠️ | Mention **only** with a label; never as fact |
| 7 | Fabricated (mawdu') / pop-culture / viral claims | ❌ | Rejected; corrected as misconceptions |

**Golden rule:** Weak and authentic reports are **never** mixed. When something is genuinely unknown, the chapter says so plainly — *"this is unknown"* — rather than inventing an answer.

## Citation Format

**Qur'an** is cited as `Surah Name (chapter:verse)` and presented with Arabic, transliteration, a translation, and short tafsir.

**Hadith** is cited with collection, book/chapter where useful, hadith number when available, and a grading, e.g.:

> **Sahih al-Bukhari 3208** — *Authentic (Sahih)*
> **Jami' at-Tirmidhi 2516** — *Good (Hasan)*
> **Musnad Ahmad** — *Weak (Da'if)* — labelled, not relied upon

> ⚠️ **Verification note:** Exact hadith numbers vary slightly between print and online editions. Before treating any number as final, confirm Qur'an references on [quran.com](https://quran.com) and hadith text + grading on [sunnah.com](https://sunnah.com). Numbers here are given as study pointers.
