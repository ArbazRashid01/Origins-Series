# Season 07 · Episode 28
# The Burning Bush
### The Voice in the Valley, and the Calling of Kalim-Allah

> *"Indeed, I am Allah. There is no deity except Me, so worship Me and establish prayer for My remembrance."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Ta-Ha (20:14)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

After about a decade in Madyan, Musa set out with his family back toward Egypt. On a dark, cold night near **Mount Tur (Sinai)**, he saw a fire and went to fetch it — and instead met **Allah, who spoke to him directly.** This chapter covers Musa's prophethood, the two great signs (the staff and the hand), the divine command to confront Pharaoh, Musa's humble requests (including his brother Harun as a helper), and the unique honour by which Musa is called **Kalim-Allah** — "the one to whom Allah spoke." It is the moment a shepherd became a messenger.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Travelling at night, lost and cold, Musa glimpsed a **fire** on the side of **Mount Tur**. He told his family to wait while he went to bring back a burning brand for warmth and guidance. But when he reached it, he was called from the **blessed valley of Tuwa**: he was told to **remove his sandals**, for he stood on sacred ground. Then came the overwhelming declaration: **"Indeed, I am Allah, the Lord of the worlds... There is no deity except Me, so worship Me and establish prayer for My remembrance."**

Musa was granted prophethood and given **two clear signs (*ayat*)**: (1) his **staff**, which Allah turned into a real, moving serpent (then back into a staff), and (2) his **hand**, which drew forth radiant white (without disease) when he placed it in his garment. He was then given his terrifying commission: **"Go to Pharaoh; indeed, he has transgressed."**

Musa, with beautiful humility, made several requests: that Allah **expand his chest** (give him courage and calm), **ease his task**, **untie the knot in his tongue** (he had a speech difficulty) so people would understand him, and — most movingly — that Allah appoint his elder brother **Harun (Aaron)** as a helper and fellow messenger, "to strengthen me and share my task." Allah **granted all of it**, and even reminded Musa of His past favours upon him (the river, the palace, Madyan). With his signs, his brother, and his Lord's promise of support — *"Indeed, I am with you both; I hear and I see"* — Musa turned toward the greatest confrontation in prophetic history. This chapter explores the calling, the signs, the humility of the prophet, and the speech that established pure *tawhid* and the command to prayer.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Fire and the Sacred Valley

> ✅ **Surah Ta-Ha (20:9-14)**
>
> **Translation:** "Has the story of Musa reached you? When he saw a fire and said to his family, 'Stay here; I have perceived a fire; perhaps I can bring you a torch from it or find at the fire some guidance.' And when he came to it, he was called, 'O Musa! Indeed, I am your Lord, so remove your sandals; you are in the sacred valley of Tuwa. And I have chosen you, so listen to what is revealed. **Indeed, I am Allah. There is no deity except Me, so worship Me and establish prayer for My remembrance.**'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** Musa went seeking a small fire for warmth and direction — and found the **greatest light of all**: the direct speech of Allah and the gift of prophethood. The first command, "remove your sandals," marked the sanctity of the moment and place. Then the foundation of *all* prophethood is declared in one breathtaking verse (20:14): **tawhid** ("I am Allah, no deity but Me"), **worship** ("so worship Me"), and **prayer** ("establish prayer for My remembrance"). Note that **salah (prayer)** was commanded to Musa as it is the heart of every prophet's message — and that its purpose is *the remembrance of Allah.*

### 3.2 The Two Signs

> ✅ **Surah Ta-Ha (20:17-23)** & **Surah An-Naml (27:10-12):**
> - **The Staff:** Allah asked, "What is in your right hand, O Musa?" He described his staff (for leaning, herding, etc.). Allah commanded him to throw it — and it became a **serpent, moving swiftly.** Musa recoiled in fear, but Allah told him to take hold of it: it returned to a staff. This staff would become the instrument of mighty miracles (the magicians, the sea — Episodes 29-31).
> - **The Hand:** Allah commanded Musa to draw his hand into his garment (at the side/collar); it came out **shining white, radiant, without disease** — a second clear sign.
>
> **Tafsir:** These were Musa's *mu'jizat* (prophetic miracles) — undeniable signs to authenticate him before Pharaoh. The staff-into-serpent especially would matter against Egypt's magicians (Episode 29). Note Allah's gentle pedagogy: He asked Musa about the staff (engaging him), then transformed it, then reassured him through his fear.

### 3.3 The Commission and Musa's Humility

> ✅ **Surah Ta-Ha (20:24-35)**
>
> **Translation:** "'Go to Pharaoh. Indeed, he has transgressed.' [Musa] said, 'My Lord, expand for me my chest, and ease for me my task, and untie the knot from my tongue that they may understand my speech. And appoint for me a minister/helper from my family — Harun, my brother. Increase through him my strength and let him share my task, that we may exalt You much and remember You much. Indeed, You are ever Seeing of us.'"
>
> **Tafsir (as-Sa'di):** Musa's response to the most daunting mission imaginable — confronting a god-claiming tyrant — is a **masterclass in humble du'a.** He didn't boast of readiness; he asked Allah for:
> 1. **Sharh as-sadr** — expansion of the chest: courage, calm, and capacity. (This famous phrase is also used for the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in Surah Ash-Sharh.)
> 2. **Ease of the task** — for Allah to smooth the difficulty.
> 3. **Loosening his tongue** — Musa had a speech impediment (a "knot" in his tongue, traditionally from an incident in childhood), and he asked for clarity so people would understand.
> 4. **Harun as a helper** — selfless: he asked that his *brother* be made a fellow prophet to share the burden, "increase my strength through him."
> This du'a (20:25-28) is beloved by Muslims, recited especially before public speaking or daunting tasks. Allah responded: **"You have been granted your request, O Musa"** (20:36).

### 3.4 "I Am With You Both"

> ✅ **Surah Ta-Ha (20:46):** "[Allah] said, 'Fear not. Indeed, I am with you both; I hear and I see.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Before sending two men to confront the mightiest empire and its god-claiming king, Allah gave the ultimate reassurance: **His own presence, hearing, and sight.** This *ma'iyyah* (companionship) of Allah — "I am with you" — is the believer's deepest source of courage in any trial. Two men, armed with nothing but a staff and the truth, were *unconquerable* because Allah was with them.

## 4. Musa as Kalim-Allah — The Unique Honour

Musa holds a distinction shared by no other prophet in quite the same way:

> ✅ **Surah An-Nisa (4:164):** "...and Allah spoke to Musa with [direct] speech (*taklima*)."
> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:143):** Later, Musa asked to *see* Allah; Allah said "You will not see Me," and manifested to the mountain, which crumbled to dust, and Musa fell unconscious — teaching that no one can bear the sight of Allah in this world.
>
> **Tafsir:** Because Allah spoke to Musa *directly* (without intermediary, in a manner befitting Allah), Musa is titled **Kalim-Allah** — "the one Allah spoke to." This is among the greatest honours given to any human. It is affirmed as real speech, *without* asking "how" (the methodology of S1E1). Musa is one of the five **Ulu al-'Azm** (Messengers of firm resolve) and is mentioned more than any other prophet in the Qur'an.

## 6. Lessons

1. **You may set out seeking something small and find your true purpose.** Musa went for a torch and received prophethood. Stay open — Allah's plans for you may dwarf your own.
2. **Tawhid, worship, and prayer are the core of the message** (20:14) — the same for every prophet. And prayer's purpose is *"My remembrance"* — to keep the heart connected to Allah.
3. **Humility is the posture of the great.** Facing the hardest mission, Musa *asked Allah for help* rather than relying on himself — courage, ease, eloquence, and a brother to share the load (20:25-32).
4. **Ask Allah to "expand your chest."** Before any daunting task, Musa's du'a (20:25-28) is the believer's go-to — for calm, courage, ease, and clear speech.
5. **"I am with you both; I hear and I see" (20:46).** The consciousness that Allah is *with* you — hearing, seeing, supporting — is the ultimate antidote to fear of any tyrant or trial.
6. **Allah equips those He calls.** Musa was given signs, a helper, and divine companionship. When Allah assigns a mission, He provides the means.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Musa saw Allah at the burning bush."**
> ✅ Musa *heard* Allah's direct **speech** (hence *Kalim-Allah*), but did **not** see Him. Later, when Musa asked to see Allah, he was told "You will not see Me" (7:143) — no one can bear that in this world.

> ❌ **"The staff-serpent and the radiant hand were magic tricks."**
> ✅ They were **real miracles (*mu'jizat*)** from Allah — genuine transformations, not illusions (unlike the magicians' tricks in Episode 29, which the Qur'an explicitly contrasts).

> ❌ **"Musa's speech impediment made him a weak prophet."**
> ✅ He asked Allah to ease it (20:27) and was granted help; his "weakness" was met by Allah's support and by Harun's eloquence — showing Allah's strength works *through* human limitation.

> ❌ **"'Remove your sandals' has a hidden mystical meaning we must decode."**
> ✅ The clear meaning is reverence for the **sacred valley** (20:12). Speculative mystical interpretations beyond the text are not established.

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — The origin of the tongue-knot, the staff's detailed history, the exact fire**

- **Narration:** That Musa burned his tongue on a coal as a baby (testing whether he'd grab a jewel or a coal before Pharaoh), the detailed lineage of the staff (from Adam, passed through prophets), the precise nature of the fire.
- **Source:** Isra'iliyyat and tradition.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'an confirms the "knot in the tongue" (20:27) but not the coal story. The staff's elaborate history is unverified. Stick to the Qur'anic essentials.

## 9. Important People

**Musa (Kalim-Allah), peace be upon him:** Now a prophet and messenger; granted two signs, divine speech, and the mission to confront Pharaoh. One of the five *Ulu al-'Azm*; the most-mentioned prophet in the Qur'an.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Did Musa see Allah at the bush?**
A: No — he *heard* Allah's direct speech. Seeing Allah in this world is impossible; when Musa later asked, he was told "You will not see Me" (7:143).

**Q: Why is Musa called Kalim-Allah?**
A: Because Allah spoke to him **directly** (4:164) — a unique honour among the prophets.

**Q: What were Musa's two signs?**
A: The **staff** that became a serpent, and his **hand** that shone radiant white — clear miracles to authenticate him before Pharaoh (20:17-22).

**Q: Why did Musa ask for his brother Harun?**
A: Out of humility and selflessness — to share the burden of the mission and strengthen the da'wah; Harun was eloquent and Allah granted the request (20:29-36).

**Q: What is the du'a "expand my chest"?**
A: Musa's prayer (20:25-28) for courage, ease, and clear speech before a daunting task — a beloved supplication Muslims recite before challenges.
