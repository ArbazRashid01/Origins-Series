# Season 07 · Episode 31
# The Sea Splits
### The Drowning of the Tyrant, and the Sign Preserved in His Body

> *"Indeed, with us is my Lord; He will guide me."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:62)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This is the climactic miracle of the Exodus and one of the most famous events in scripture: trapped between Pharaoh's army and the sea, the Children of Israel are saved when Allah **parts the sea** for them — and drowns Pharaoh and his army as they pursue. This chapter covers the moment of trapped despair and Musa's unshakable trust, the splitting of the sea, the drowning of the tyrant, Pharaoh's rejected death-bed "faith," and the remarkable Qur'anic claim that his **body would be preserved as a sign** — a claim that intersects with modern discoveries. It is the definitive lesson that **Allah's help comes at the moment of seeming impossibility.**

---

## 1. Episode Summary

The fleeing Israelites reached the shore of the sea with **Pharaoh's army closing in behind them.** Trapped — water ahead, soldiers behind — the people panicked: *"Indeed, we are to be overtaken!"* (26:61). But Musa, in one of the greatest expressions of *tawakkul* in the Qur'an, replied with total certainty: **"No! Indeed, with me is my Lord; He will guide me."** (26:62).

Allah commanded Musa: **"Strike the sea with your staff."** He did — and the sea **split into towering walls of water with a dry path between them**, through which the Children of Israel crossed to safety. When Pharaoh and his army reached the parted sea and charged in after them, Allah let the waters return, and the entire army — with Pharaoh at its head — **was drowned.**

In his final moments, as the water engulfed him, **Pharaoh cried out that he believed** in "the God of the Children of Israel." But it was **too late** — faith declared only when death is certain and escape is impossible is not accepted. Allah declared: *"Now? When you had disobeyed before and were of the corrupters?"* (10:91). Then came an astonishing statement: **"So today We will preserve your body that you may be a sign for those who come after you"** (10:92). This chapter explores the trapped moment, the splitting, the drowning, the rejected deathbed faith, and the preserved body — closing the saga of Musa and Pharaoh with the eternal lesson that **no tyrant escapes Allah, and His help arrives precisely when hope seems lost.**

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 Trapped — and the Triumph of Trust

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:61-62)**
>
> **Arabic:** فَلَمَّا تَرَاءَى الْجَمْعَانِ قَالَ أَصْحَابُ مُوسَىٰ إِنَّا لَمُدْرَكُونَ ۝ قَالَ كَلَّا ۖ إِنَّ مَعِيَ رَبِّي سَيَهْدِينِ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Falamma tara'a al-jam'ani qala ashabu Musa inna lamudrakun. Qala kalla, inna ma'iya Rabbi sayahdin.*
>
> **Translation:** "And when the two companies saw one another, the companions of Musa said, 'Indeed, we are to be overtaken!' [Musa] said, 'No! Indeed, with me is my Lord; He will guide me.'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** This is the **summit of *tawakkul*** (reliance on Allah). Every visible fact said the Israelites were doomed: an impassable sea ahead, a heavily-armed army behind, no escape. Musa's people saw only the trap. But Musa saw only Allah: **"Indeed, with me is my Lord; He will guide me."** He didn't know *how* deliverance would come — he simply *knew Allah would not abandon him.* This is the lesson of the entire episode: **when human means are exhausted, Allah's help is nearest.** Relief comes at the moment of seeming impossibility, for those who truly trust. (Compare Ibrahim in the fire, S5E18, and the Prophet ﷺ in the cave, Season 13 — the same unbreakable certainty.)

### 3.2 The Splitting

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:63)** & **Surah Ta-Ha (20:77)**
>
> **Translation:** "Then We inspired to Musa, 'Strike with your staff the sea,' and it parted, and each portion was like a great towering mountain." (26:63)
>
> **Tafsir:** At Allah's command, Musa struck the sea with the same staff that had become a serpent (Episode 28). The water **split into two massive walls** — "like great mountains" — with a **dry path** through the middle (20:77: "a dry path... fearing neither overtaking nor drowning"). The Children of Israel crossed in safety between the towering walls of water — one of the most awe-inspiring miracles in all of revelation, a direct, visible demonstration that the very sea obeys its Creator.

### 3.3 The Drowning of Pharaoh

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:64-66)** & **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:50)**
>
> **Translation:** "And We brought near the others (Pharaoh's army) [to the sea]. And We saved Musa and those with him, all together. Then We drowned the others." (26:64-66)
>
> **Tafsir:** Seeing the parted sea, Pharaoh — blinded by arrogance and rage — charged in after the Israelites with his entire army, perhaps assuming the path was his to take too. Once the believers were safely across and the army was in the midst of the sea, **Allah let the walls of water collapse**, drowning Pharaoh and his entire host. The tyrant who **claimed to be a god** and who **drowned Israelite babies** was himself **drowned** — a perfectly fitting end (recall S4E14: Allah humbles the arrogant, often through a fitting means). The Israelites watched their oppressor of generations destroyed in moments.

### 3.4 The Faith That Came Too Late

> ✅ **Surah Yunus (10:90-91)**
>
> **Translation:** "...until, when drowning overtook him, [Pharaoh] said, 'I believe that there is no deity except that in whom the Children of Israel believe, and I am of the Muslims.' [It was said,] 'Now? When you had disobeyed before and were of the corrupters?'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** As the water filled his lungs and death was certain, Pharaoh finally declared belief. But it was **rejected** — for faith is only meaningful when one *could still choose otherwise.* Faith declared at the moment of certain death, when all escape is gone and the unseen has become seen, is **not accepted** (this is a foundational principle: the "door of repentance" closes when death's reality arrives — cf. 4:18, "repentance is not for those who... when death comes to one of them, says 'now I repent'"). Pharaoh's lifetime of arrogance, tyranny, and rejection could not be undone by a coerced confession in his final seconds.

### 3.5 The Body Preserved as a Sign

> ✅ **Surah Yunus (10:92)**
>
> **Arabic:** فَالْيَوْمَ نُنَجِّيكَ بِبَدَنِكَ لِتَكُونَ لِمَنْ خَلْفَكَ آيَةً
>
> **Transliteration:** *Fal-yawma nunajjika bibadanika litakuna liman khalfaka ayah.*
>
> **Translation:** "So today We will save you in your body (preserve your corpse) that you may be a sign for those who come after you. And indeed, many among the people are heedless of Our signs."
>
> **Tafsir & modern discussion:** Allah declared that Pharaoh's **body would be preserved** as a **sign** (*ayah*) for later generations — so that the tyrant who claimed divinity would become a mute exhibit of Allah's power. 
> 📖 **The modern resonance:** Among the mummified remains of Egyptian pharaohs (today displayed in Cairo's museum) are kings proposed as candidates for "the Pharaoh of Musa" (e.g., **Merneptah**, son of Ramesses II). That the **physical body of a pharaoh survives to be examined by "those who come after"** — millennia later, viewable by millions — strikingly fits the verse. 
> ⚠️ **Balanced note:** We should *not* overclaim a specific scientific "proof" (the identification of *which* pharaoh, and famous stories like a French scientist's conversion, are **not verified** and should be cited with great caution). But the *general* fact — that pharaohs' bodies are uniquely preserved and displayed for posterity — is a genuine and remarkable correspondence with 10:92. State the verse, note the resonance, avoid sensationalism. *(Methodology balance.)*

## 5. The Themes — The Climax of the Musa Saga

1. **Allah's help comes at the moment of impossibility.** Trapped between sea and army, with zero human options, deliverance arrived. *"Indeed, with me is my Lord; He will guide me."* (26:62). The darkest moment is often the threshold of rescue.
2. **No tyrant escapes Allah.** Pharaoh — god-claimer, baby-killer, master of the world's greatest empire — was drowned like the weakest man. *All* worldly power is nothing before Allah (the lesson of Nimrod, 'Aad, Thamud — now Pharaoh).
3. **Faith has a deadline.** Belief declared only when death is certain and escape impossible is **not accepted** (10:91; 4:18). Repent and submit *while you still have a choice* — that is the entire point of the test.
4. **Allah turns tyrants into lessons.** Pharaoh's preserved body (10:92) makes the would-be god an eternal *exhibit* of Allah's power — a warning "for those who come after."
5. **Reliance + action.** Musa trusted *and* struck the sea with his staff (26:63). *Tawakkul* is trust paired with the action Allah commands — not passive waiting.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **When all doors close, Allah opens the sea.** Never despair at the moment of seeming impossibility — that is precisely when Allah's help is nearest. Trust like Musa: "My Lord is with me; He will guide me."
2. **Repent now, not later.** Pharaoh's deathbed faith was worthless because it came when choice was gone. Don't postpone returning to Allah — the door closes at death (4:18).
3. **Worldly power is an illusion of security.** The mightiest tyrant in history drowned in moments. Never fear oppressors more than Allah, and never let power make you arrogant.
4. **Trust *and* act.** Musa relied on Allah *and* struck the sea. Do what Allah commands, then trust Him with the outcome.
5. **Allah's signs surround us.** From the splitting sea to the preserved body, Allah leaves signs "for those who come after" (10:92) — yet "many are heedless." Reflect, and don't be among the heedless.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The sea-splitting is a myth / just a low-tide phenomenon."**
> ✅ The Qur'an describes the water standing in **towering walls "like great mountains"** with a **dry path** (26:63; 20:77) — an explicit, deliberate **miracle** by Allah's command, not a natural tide.

> ❌ **"Pharaoh repented and was saved at the end."**
> ✅ His last-second "faith" was **explicitly rejected** — "Now? When you had disobeyed before?" (10:91). Faith at the point of certain death is not accepted (4:18).

> ❌ **"The preserved-body verse proves a specific pharaoh / a famous scientist's conversion."**
> ✅ The *general* correspondence (pharaohs' bodies uniquely preserved and displayed) is striking and worth noting; but the **specific identification** and viral conversion stories are **unverified** — cite with caution, avoid overclaiming (§3.5).

> ❌ **"Tawakkul means Musa just waited passively."**
> ✅ He **struck the sea** at Allah's command (26:63). True reliance *acts* on Allah's instruction and trusts Him for the result.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: How did the sea split?**
A: By Allah's command — Musa struck it with his staff, and it parted into towering walls of water with a dry path between (26:63; 20:77). A deliberate miracle.

**Q: Why wasn't Pharaoh's final "faith" accepted?**
A: Because it came at the moment of **certain death**, when choice was gone and the unseen had become visible. Faith/repentance is only accepted while one can still freely choose (10:91; 4:18).

**Q: Is Pharaoh's body really preserved today?**
A: The Qur'an says his body would be preserved as a sign (10:92). Pharaonic mummies are indeed displayed today, which is a striking resonance — but the *specific* identification of "Musa's Pharaoh" is uncertain, and sensational claims should be avoided (§3.5).

**Q: What is the connection to 'Ashura?**
A: This deliverance happened on the 10th of Muharram; the Prophet ﷺ fasted it in gratitude and encouraged Muslims to do so (Bukhari 2004).

**Q: What's the single greatest lesson?**
A: That Allah's help arrives at the moment of seeming impossibility for those who truly trust — "Indeed, with me is my Lord; He will guide me" (26:62) — and that no tyrant escapes Allah's justice.
