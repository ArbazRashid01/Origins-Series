# Season 07 · Episode 30
# The Plagues & The Escape
### The Signs Upon Egypt, and the Night of Departure

> *"So We sent upon them the flood and the locusts and the lice and the frogs and the blood — distinct signs. But they were arrogant and were a criminal people."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-A'raf (7:133)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

After the magicians' conversion, Pharaoh hardened further, intensifying the oppression of the Israelites. Allah responded with a series of **escalating signs (plagues)** upon Egypt — each a mercy-warning meant to break Pharaoh's arrogance and each met with broken promises. This chapter covers the plagues, the steadfast believers (including a secret believer in Pharaoh's own court and the example of Asiyah), the command to prepare for departure, and the night Allah ordered Musa to **lead the Children of Israel out of Egypt** — setting up the climactic splitting of the sea (Episode 31).

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Pharaoh's defeat at the contest did not humble him; it hardened him. He escalated the persecution of the Israelites and clung to power. So Allah sent a series of **signs and plagues** upon Egypt — the Qur'an names: **the flood, the locusts, the lice, the frogs, and blood** (7:133), along with years of **drought and shortage of crops** (7:130). Each plague was a measured warning. Each time, Pharaoh and his people, in desperation, would **beg Musa to pray for relief, promising to believe and free the Israelites** — and each time, once relief came, they **broke their word** and returned to arrogance. This cycle exposed that their "faith" was mere bargaining under pressure, not true submission.

Throughout, the Qur'an highlights the **believers** amid the tyranny: **Asiyah**, Pharaoh's wife (Episode 26), and a remarkable figure — **a believing man from Pharaoh's own family who had concealed his faith** ("the believer of the family of Pharaoh," Surah Ghafir 40:28), who courageously defended Musa before the court, warning them with wisdom and reminding them of the fate of past nations.

Finally, when Pharaoh's rejection was complete, Allah commanded Musa to **lead the Israelites out by night.** They were to prepare and depart secretly. As dawn approached and the escape was discovered, **Pharaoh mustered his entire army** and pursued them to the shore of the sea — where the Israelites, trapped between the water and the approaching army, faced the moment that would become the greatest miracle of the Exodus (Episode 31). This chapter explores the plagues, the broken promises, the hidden believers, and the night of departure.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Plagues as Escalating Warnings

> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:130-133)**
>
> **Translation:** "And We certainly seized the people of Pharaoh with years of drought and a shortage of fruits, that perhaps they would be reminded... So We sent upon them the flood and the locusts and the lice and the frogs and the blood as distinct signs, but they were arrogant and were a criminal people."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** The plagues were not random cruelty but **escalating mercy-warnings** ("that perhaps they would be reminded," 7:130). Each was a *sign* (*ayah*) calling Pharaoh's people to humility and faith. The progression — from economic hardship (drought, crop failure) to overwhelming natural afflictions (flood, locusts, lice, frogs, blood) — gave repeated chances to submit. Their response, "arrogance," is the recurring disease of every doomed people. Allah's justice is never hasty: warning upon warning precedes judgment.

### 3.2 The Cycle of Broken Promises

> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:134-135)**
>
> **Translation:** "And when the punishment descended upon them, they said, 'O Musa, invoke for us your Lord by what He has promised you. If you remove the punishment from us, we will surely believe you and send with you the Children of Israel.' But when We removed the punishment from them until a term they were to reach, at once they broke their word."
>
> **Tafsir:** This cycle repeated with each plague: **affliction → desperate promise of faith → relief → betrayal.** It exposes a crucial truth about the human heart: **"foxhole faith"** — turning to God only in crisis and abandoning Him in ease — is *not* real faith. Pharaoh's people "believed" only as a bargaining chip to escape pain; the moment the pain lifted, their arrogance returned. (Contrast the magicians, whose faith *cost* them everything yet held firm — Episode 29.) True faith is constant in hardship *and* ease.

### 3.3 The Hidden Believer of Pharaoh's Court

One of the most remarkable figures in the Musa narrative appears here:

> ✅ **Surah Ghafir / Al-Mu'min (40:28)**
>
> **Translation:** "And a believing man from the family of Pharaoh, who concealed his faith, said, 'Do you kill a man [merely] because he says, "My Lord is Allah," while he has brought you clear proofs from your Lord? If he is a liar, then upon him is his lie; but if he is truthful, some of what he promises you will strike you. Indeed, Allah does not guide one who is a transgressor and a liar.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** This unnamed believer — a man of *Pharaoh's own family/court* who had kept his faith secret out of necessity — **risked his life to defend Musa** with brilliant, measured reasoning (40:28-44). His argument is a model of wisdom: *at minimum, leave the man alone; at most, he's telling the truth and you're in grave danger.* He reminded them of the destroyed nations and called them to the Hereafter. Surah Ghafir preserves his beautiful speech. He stands alongside Asiyah as proof that **faith can survive, and even flourish, in the heart of tyranny** — and that a single courageous voice for truth matters, even when surrounded by oppressors.

### 3.4 The Command to Leave by Night

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:52)** & **Surah Ta-Ha (20:77)** & **Surah Ad-Dukhan (44:23)**
>
> **Translation:** "And We inspired Musa, 'Travel by night with My servants; indeed, you will be pursued.'" (26:52) ... "And strike for them a dry path through the sea, fearing neither overtaking [by Pharaoh] nor [drowning]." (20:77)
>
> **Tafsir:** When Pharaoh's rejection was final, Allah commanded the **Exodus** — a secret departure by night with the entire community of believers (the Children of Israel). Allah forewarned them they would be **pursued** — and that Allah's plan was already prepared (the dry path through the sea). The night departure of a whole oppressed people, leaving the only land they had known, toward freedom and the unknown, required immense trust.

### 3.5 Pharaoh's Pursuit

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:53-60)**
>
> **Translation:** "Then Pharaoh sent among the cities gatherers, [saying], 'Indeed, these are a small band, and indeed, they are enraging us, and we are a cautious society.'... So they pursued them at sunrise."
>
> **Tafsir:** Discovering the escape, Pharaoh — unable to let his slave-labour force go and his pride wounded — **mobilized his entire army** to recapture them. He dismissed the Israelites as "a small band" while admitting they "enraged" him (the contradiction of a threatened tyrant). The chase brought both peoples to the shore of the sea, setting the stage for the climax (Episode 31).

This authentic practice ties the Muslim ummah directly to the deliverance of Musa — a living link between this episode and Islamic worship today.

## 5. The Themes

1. **"Foxhole faith" is not faith.** Pharaoh's people promised belief under every plague and broke it under every relief (7:135). Faith that appears only in crisis and vanishes in comfort is hollow. Real iman is steadfast in both.
2. **Allah warns before He punishes — repeatedly.** The escalating plagues were chance after chance to submit. Allah's justice is preceded by abundant mercy (cf. 17:15).
3. **One believer in a tyrant's court can shine.** The hidden believer of Pharaoh's family (40:28) and Asiyah prove that environment never forces disbelief — and that courageous truth-telling matters even when outnumbered.
4. **Liberation requires trust and movement.** The Exodus demanded the Israelites leave everything familiar, by night, toward danger — trusting Allah's promise. Freedom from oppression often requires a leap of faith.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Don't be a "crisis-only" believer.** Turn to Allah in ease as in hardship. The one who remembers Allah in good times finds Him in bad times.
2. **Allah's warnings are mercies — heed them early.** Each plague was a chance to repent. Recognize life's "warnings" (setbacks, losses) as calls to turn back to Allah before greater consequences come.
3. **Speak truth courageously, even when alone.** The believer of Pharaoh's family risked everything for one righteous stand. A single voice for truth in a hostile place is precious to Allah.
4. **Conceal faith when necessary, but never abandon it.** The hidden believer kept his faith secret for survival yet acted on it decisively when it mattered — a model of wisdom under persecution (relevant to the early Muslims, Season 12).
5. **Trust Allah's plan when He commands you to move.** The night departure required leaving security for the unknown. Sometimes obedience means stepping out in trust before the path is visible (the sea wasn't split *yet*).

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The plagues prove Allah is cruel."**
> ✅ They were **escalating warnings**, each followed by relief when the people begged — "that perhaps they would be reminded" (7:130). Judgment came only after relentless arrogance and broken promises. This is justice preceded by repeated mercy.

> ❌ **"Pharaoh's people genuinely believed during the plagues."**
> ✅ Their "belief" was **bargaining under pain** (7:134), abandoned the moment relief came (7:135). It was not sincere faith.

> ❌ **"No one in Pharaoh's court believed in Allah."**
> ✅ At least two did — **Asiyah** (his wife) and **the hidden believer of his family** (40:28). Faith survived even there.

> ❌ **"The Exodus is purely a Jewish/Biblical story unrelated to Islam."**
> ✅ It is central to the Qur'an, and Muslims commemorate Musa's deliverance by **fasting 'Ashura** to this day (Bukhari 2004). The Prophet ﷺ said, "We have more right to Musa than you."

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — The order, duration, and added plagues; the name of the believer of Pharaoh's family; the number of Israelites**

- **Narration:** Detailed sequencing and durations of each plague, additional plagues beyond the five named, the believer's name (some say "Habib" or others), exact population figures (e.g., 600,000+).
- **Source:** Isra'iliyyat (paralleling the Biblical ten plagues) and tradition.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'an names **specific signs** (flood, locusts, lice, frogs, blood — 7:133) plus drought; it does **not** give the Biblical "ten plagues" framework, exact numbers, or the believer's name. Stick to the Qur'anic account.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What plagues does the Qur'an mention?**
A: Drought and crop shortage (7:130), then **the flood, locusts, lice, frogs, and blood** (7:133) — "distinct signs." The Qur'an does not list the Biblical "ten plagues" framework.

**Q: Did Pharaoh ever believe during the plagues?**
A: He and his people *promised* to believe under each plague to get relief, then broke their word every time (7:134-135) — bargaining, not belief.

**Q: Who was the believer in Pharaoh's family?**
A: An unnamed man of Pharaoh's court who **hid his faith** and courageously defended Musa (Surah Ghafir 40:28-44). His name isn't given authentically.

**Q: Why fast 'Ashura?**
A: To commemorate Allah saving Musa and the Israelites from Pharaoh; the Prophet ﷺ fasted it and said Muslims "have more right to Musa" (Bukhari 2004).

**Q: Why did Allah have them leave by night?**
A: For a secret departure ahead of Pharaoh's pursuit — with Allah's plan (the sea) already prepared (20:77; 26:52).
