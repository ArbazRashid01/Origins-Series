# Season 07 · Episode 29
# The Magicians' Showdown
### The Day Pharaoh's Own Sorcerers Believed

> *"So the magicians fell down in prostration. They said, 'We have believed in the Lord of the worlds — the Lord of Musa and Harun.'"*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-A'raf (7:120-122)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Musa, now a prophet, returns to Egypt to confront the god-claiming Pharaoh. This chapter covers the first encounter at court, Pharaoh's arrogance, and the dramatic **public contest with Egypt's greatest magicians** — which ends with the magicians themselves recognizing the truth, **falling in prostration to Allah**, and accepting horrific torture and death rather than abandon their new faith. It is one of the most stirring stories of sudden, total conversion and courage in the Qur'an.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Musa and Harun came to **Pharaoh** with the message: worship Allah alone, and **let the Children of Israel go.** They delivered it with the gentleness Allah had commanded — "speak to him a gentle word, that perhaps he may be reminded or fear" (20:44). Pharaoh responded with arrogance and mockery, reminding Musa that he had raised him in the palace, and questioning "who is the Lord of the worlds?" Musa answered with clear *tawhid*. Pharaoh, threatened, accused Musa of being a **magician** trying to seize Egypt — and resolved to defeat him publicly with Egypt's finest sorcerers.

A grand contest was arranged on a festival day before the assembled people. The magicians threw down their ropes and staffs, which — through powerful sorcery — *appeared* to the eyes as slithering serpents, striking fear. Then Musa, at Allah's command, threw his staff — and it became a **real serpent that swallowed up all their illusions.** The magicians, who *knew magic better than anyone*, instantly recognized that **this was no magic — it was a true sign from God.** In a stunning reversal, **they fell down in prostration**, declaring: *"We believe in the Lord of the worlds, the Lord of Musa and Harun."*

Enraged, Pharaoh threatened them with **crucifixion and the cutting off of their hands and feet** for believing without his permission. But the magicians — believers for only minutes — displayed breathtaking courage: *"We will never prefer you over the clear proofs that have come to us... decree whatever you will; you can only decree for this worldly life... we have believed in our Lord that He may forgive us."* They chose death with faith over life with a tyrant. This chapter explores the court confrontation, the contest, and the magicians' extraordinary conversion and martyrdom — a lesson that **truth, once truly seen, is worth any price.**

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Gentle Word to a Tyrant

> ✅ **Surah Ta-Ha (20:43-44)**
>
> **Translation:** "Go, both of you, to Pharaoh. Indeed, he has transgressed. And speak to him a gentle word (*qawlan layyinan*), that perhaps he may be reminded or fear [Allah]."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** A profound divine instruction: even to **Pharaoh** — history's archetypal tyrant, a man who claimed godhood and slaughtered children — Allah commanded Musa to speak **gently.** Why? Because the goal of da'wah is *to reach the heart*, not to vent or provoke. Harshness hardens; gentleness may open a door. This is a foundational principle of Islamic calling: lead with wisdom and kindness, leaving the result to Allah. (Pharaoh still refused — but the *method* was honoured regardless of his response.)

### 3.2 Pharaoh's Arrogance

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:18-29):** Pharaoh tried to belittle Musa — "Did we not raise you among us as a child? And you committed your deed (the killing) and were ungrateful." Musa acknowledged his past, explained it, and credited Allah for guiding him. When Musa described "the Lord of the worlds," Pharaoh mockingly questioned it, then threatened: *"If you take a god other than me, I will surely place you among those imprisoned"* (26:29).
>
> **Tafsir:** Pharaoh's defining trait is **arrogance masquerading as divinity.** He could not argue with Musa's clear *tawhid*, so he resorted to mockery, manipulation (reminding Musa of past "debts"), and threats — the tools of every tyrant who has lost the argument (echoing Nimrod, S5E18, and Ibrahim's people, S5E17).

### 3.3 The Contest

> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:113-122) & Ta-Ha (20:65-70):** The magicians gathered, confident, asking Pharaoh for reward if they won. They invited Musa to throw first; he let them go first. They cast their ropes and staffs, which — by powerful illusion — *appeared* to writhe as serpents, and "**bewitched the eyes of the people and terrified them, producing a great magic**" (7:116). Musa felt a flash of apprehension, but Allah reassured him: *"Fear not; indeed, it is you who are superior. And throw what is in your right hand; it will swallow up what they have made. What they have made is only the trick of a magician, and the magician will not succeed wherever he is"* (20:68-69). Musa threw his staff — it became a **real serpent that devoured all their illusions** (7:117).
>
> **Tafsir:** The Qur'an is precise: the magicians' work was **illusion** — it *appeared* real to the eyes but had no substance (20:66). Musa's staff was a **real transformation** — a true miracle. The contrast is the difference between *magic* (deceiving perception) and a *sign from Allah* (actual reality). And note the principle stated: **"the magician will not succeed"** (20:69) — falsehood, however impressive, ultimately fails before truth.

### 3.4 The Magicians Recognize the Truth

> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:120-122)**
>
> **Translation:** "And the magicians fell down in prostration. They said, 'We have believed in the Lord of the worlds — the Lord of Musa and Harun.'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** This is the dramatic heart of the story. **The magicians were the experts** — no one knew the limits of illusion better than they did. So when they saw Musa's staff perform what *no magic* could, they were the *first* to realize, with certainty, that **this was from God, not sorcery.** Their expertise, meant to defeat Musa, became the very thing that *convinced* them. They fell into prostration on the spot — converting from Pharaoh's paid sorcerers to believers in Allah in a single moment of clarity.

### 3.5 Faith Under the Threat of Death

> ✅ **Surah Ta-Ha (20:71-73)**
>
> **Translation:** "[Pharaoh] said, 'You believed him before I gave you permission! Indeed, he is your leader who taught you magic. So I will surely cut off your hands and feet on opposite sides, and I will crucify you on the trunks of palm trees, and you will surely know which of us is more severe in punishment and more enduring.' They said, 'We will never prefer you over what has come to us of clear proofs, and [over] He who created us. So decree whatever you will to decree; you can only decree for this worldly life. Indeed, we have believed in our Lord that He may forgive us our sins and the magic you compelled us to do. And Allah is better and more enduring.'"
>
> **Tafsir (as-Sa'di):** The courage here is *staggering*. These people had been believers for **only minutes** — yet faced with the most gruesome torture and death, they did not waver for an instant. Their reasoning is the essence of faith: *this life is temporary ("you can only decree for this worldly life"); Allah and the Hereafter are "better and more enduring."* They even hoped their martyrdom would be expiation for "the magic you compelled us to do." **The Qur'an immortalizes them**: people who entered the day as Pharaoh's hired magicians and left it (by tradition) as **martyrs** — *"they began the day as magicians and ended it as martyrs"* (a well-known scholarly description). True faith, once it enters a sincere heart, can produce in *minutes* what a lifetime of habit could not.

## 5. The Themes

1. **Magic deceives perception; miracles change reality.** The Qur'an explicitly says the magicians' serpents *appeared* to move by illusion (20:66), while Musa's was real. Truth is not about being *impressive* but about being *real* — and reality always devours illusion.
2. **The experts knew first.** Those most knowledgeable about magic were the quickest to recognize a divine sign. *Knowledge, when honest, leads to truth.* (A lesson for every field: real expertise, free of ego, recognizes truth.)
3. **Sincere faith can transform a person in an instant.** The magicians went from greed-driven sorcerers to fearless martyrs in one afternoon. No one is beyond guidance; the heart can turn completely the moment truth is truly seen.
4. **Truth is worth any price.** They chose mutilation and death over abandoning faith — "Allah is better and more enduring" (20:73). The believer values the eternal over the temporary.
5. **Gentleness even with tyrants** (20:44). The method of da'wah is wisdom and kindness, regardless of how the listener responds.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Lead with gentleness, even toward the worst.** If Musa was told to speak gently to *Pharaoh*, our harshness toward lesser opponents is rarely justified.
2. **Don't be intimidated by impressive falsehood.** The magicians' display "terrified the people" — but it was empty. Allah reassured Musa, "you are superior." Truth needs no fear of falsehood's theatrics.
3. **It's never too late to believe.** The magicians converted at the very end, after a life of sorcery — and earned martyrdom and (we hope) Paradise. The door of guidance is open until the last moment.
4. **Real faith fears Allah more than any tyrant.** "Decree whatever you will" (20:72). When the heart truly grasps that Allah is "better and more enduring," no earthly threat can break it.
5. **Honest expertise recognizes truth.** The magicians' very knowledge convicted them. Seek knowledge honestly, and it will point you to the truth.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Musa's staff was just better magic than the magicians'."**
> ✅ The Qur'an sharply distinguishes them: the magicians' was **illusion** (it *appeared* to move, 20:66); Musa's was a **real serpent** that **consumed** their illusions (7:117). Miracle ≠ magic.

> ❌ **"The magicians converted out of fear of Musa's serpent."**
> ✅ They converted out of **certainty** — as *experts*, they knew Musa's sign was beyond any magic, hence from God (7:120-122). They then faced death *fearlessly* for that faith.

> ❌ **"Magic isn't real in Islam."**
> ✅ Islam affirms *sihr* (magic) is **real** but works only by Allah's permission and is **forbidden and harmful** (2:102; Bukhari 5764). The magicians' craft was real illusion-magic — which is precisely why their recognition of Musa's *non-magic* was so decisive.

> ❌ **"Speaking gently to Pharaoh means Islam is soft on tyranny."**
> ✅ Gentle *speech* (the method of calling) coexisted with **uncompromising truth** and the demand to free the oppressed. Gentleness of manner ≠ compromise of message.

- **Narration:** Specific counts (e.g., 70, or thousands), named leaders, the precise rewards, and detailed martyrdom accounts.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What's the difference between Musa's miracle and the magicians' magic?**
A: The magicians' serpents were **illusions** that only *appeared* to move (20:66); Musa's staff became a **real** serpent that consumed their illusions (7:117). Miracle = real; magic = deception of perception.

**Q: Why did the magicians believe so quickly?**
A: As **experts in magic**, they instantly recognized that Musa's sign was beyond any sorcery — therefore from God. Their expertise convicted them (7:120-122).

**Q: What happened to the magicians?**
A: Pharaoh threatened them with crucifixion and mutilation for believing; they refused to recant, choosing faith and (by tradition) martyrdom — "Allah is better and more enduring" (20:73).

**Q: Why did Allah tell Musa to speak gently to Pharaoh?**
A: Because the aim of da'wah is to reach the heart (20:44). Gentle method, uncompromising message.

**Q: Is magic real in Islam?**
A: Yes — *sihr* is real but works only by Allah's permission, and it is a grave, forbidden sin (2:102). The magicians' craft was real illusion-magic.
