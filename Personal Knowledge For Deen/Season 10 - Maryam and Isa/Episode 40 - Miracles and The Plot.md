## How To Use This Chapter

This final chapter of Season 10 covers the **prophetic mission of Isa (Jesus)**: his message of pure *tawhid*, his miracles by Allah's permission, his loyal **disciples (Hawariyyun)**, the miracle of the **table from heaven (Ma'idah)**, his prophecy of the coming of **Ahmad (Muhammad ﷺ)**, and the climactic Islamic teaching on his end — that he was **not killed or crucified, but raised up by Allah**, and will **return** before the end of time. It presents the Islamic view clearly and respectfully, completing the story of the Messiah.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

As an adult, **Isa** carried out his mission to the **Children of Israel**: calling them to worship **Allah alone** ("Indeed, Allah is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him; this is a straight path" — 3:51), confirming the **Torah**, and bringing the **Injil (Gospel)** as guidance and light. Allah granted him remarkable **miracles — all "by Allah's permission"**: he healed the blind and the leper, **gave life to the dead**, **fashioned a bird from clay and breathed life into it**, and told people what they ate and stored in their homes (3:49; 5:110). Every miracle was explicitly *by Allah's leave* — signs pointing to Allah, never to Isa's own divinity.

Most of the Children of Israel's leaders rejected him. A loyal group, the **Hawariyyun (disciples)**, believed and supported him, declaring: *"We are the helpers of Allah"* (3:52). They once asked Isa to bring down a **table spread with food from heaven (al-Ma'idah)** as a sign; Allah sent it, with a stern warning against disbelief after such proof (5:112-115) — this gave **Surah Al-Ma'idah** ("The Table") its name.

Isa also delivered a momentous prophecy: he foretold **a messenger to come after him whose name is Ahmad** (61:6) — the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. As opposition hardened, his enemies **plotted to kill him.** But the Qur'an is emphatic: **"They did not kill him, nor crucify him"** — Allah **raised Isa up to Himself**, and one made to *resemble* him was taken instead. Isa did not die on a cross; he was **raised alive**, and authentic teachings affirm he will **return near the end of time** as a just ruler who will break the cross, affirm Islam, and the world will know peace under true *tawhid*. He will then die a natural death and be buried like other humans. This chapter completes the Islamic portrait of the Messiah — a great prophet, miracle-worker by Allah's leave, raised up and returning, always a servant of the One God.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Message: Pure Tawhid

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:51):** "[Isa said,] 'Indeed, Allah is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him. This is a straight path.'"
> ✅ **Surah Az-Zukhruf (43:64):** "[Isa said,] 'Indeed, Allah is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him. This is a straight path.'"
> ✅ **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:72):** Isa said, "O Children of Israel, worship Allah, my Lord and your Lord."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Isa's core message was **identical to every prophet's**: worship Allah *alone*. He never called people to worship *himself* — he called them to worship his Lord *and theirs.* The Qur'an records Isa explicitly disavowing, on the Day of Judgement, any claim that he asked to be worshipped (5:116-117): "I only told them what You commanded me — to worship Allah, my Lord and your Lord." Isa's own words are the clearest refutation of his deification.

### 3.2 The Miracles — By Allah's Permission

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:49)**
>
> **Translation:** "[Isa, a messenger to the Children of Israel, said,] 'I have come to you with a sign from your Lord: I design for you from clay [something] like the form of a bird, then I breathe into it and it becomes a bird **by permission of Allah**. And I cure the blind and the leper, and I give life to the dead — **by permission of Allah**. And I inform you of what you eat and store in your houses...'"
>
> **Tafsir (as-Sa'di):** Isa's miracles were extraordinary — even *giving life* (to a clay bird, to the dead). But notice the Qur'an's **relentless repetition: "by permission of Allah" (*bi-idhnillah*)** — stated again and again. This is deliberate and crucial: Isa's miracles were **not his own power** but Allah's, granted *through* him as signs. The greatest of his miracles (raising the dead) is precisely the kind that could be misread as divine — so Allah emphasizes "*by My permission*" each time, leaving no room to deify the prophet. The miracles authenticated his prophethood and pointed to the power of **Allah**, not Isa.

### 3.3 The Disciples (Hawariyyun) and the Table

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:52):** "But when Isa felt [persistent] disbelief from them, he said, 'Who are my supporters for [the cause of] Allah?' The disciples said, 'We are the supporters of Allah. We have believed in Allah and testify that we are Muslims [submitting to Him].'"
> ✅ **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:112-115):** The disciples asked if Allah could send down a **table spread with food (al-Ma'idah)** from heaven, to reassure their hearts and bear witness. Isa prayed for it; Allah sent it — with a warning: *"whoever disbelieves afterward among you — I will punish him with a punishment by which I have not punished anyone among the worlds"* (5:115).
>
> **Tafsir:** The **Hawariyyun** were Isa's loyal core of believers who declared themselves "**Muslims**" (submitters to Allah) — showing that the true followers of Isa were monotheists submitting to the One God, the same *islam* of all prophets. The **table from heaven** (which named Surah 5, *Al-Ma'idah*) was a great sign granted on request — with the sobering principle that **greater proof brings greater accountability**: to disbelieve after witnessing such a miracle invites severe punishment.

### 3.4 The Prophecy of Ahmad (Muhammad ﷺ)

> ✅ **Surah As-Saff (61:6)**
>
> **Translation:** "And [mention] when Isa, son of Maryam, said, 'O Children of Israel, indeed I am the messenger of Allah to you, confirming what came before me of the Torah and bringing good tidings of a messenger to come after me, whose name is **Ahmad**.'..."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Isa was not the final prophet — he **foretold the coming of Muhammad ﷺ** (here called "*Ahmad*," a name of the Prophet meaning "the most praised"). This connects Season 10 directly to Seasons 11-14: the Messiah himself announced the final Messenger. The Prophet ﷺ said: *"I am the supplication of my father Ibrahim and the glad tidings of [my brother] Isa"* (referencing 2:129 and 61:6) — the two great links of the prophetic chain pointing to him.

### 3.5 The Plot and the Raising-Up — The Decisive Islamic Teaching

> ✅ **Surah An-Nisa (4:157-158)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَقَوْلِهِمْ إِنَّا قَتَلْنَا الْمَسِيحَ عِيسَى ابْنَ مَرْيَمَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ وَمَا قَتَلُوهُ وَمَا صَلَبُوهُ وَلَٰكِن شُبِّهَ لَهُمْ... بَل رَّفَعَهُ اللَّهُ إِلَيْهِ
>
> **Translation:** "...And [for] their saying, 'Indeed, we have killed the Messiah, Isa son of Maryam, the messenger of Allah' — and they did not kill him, nor did they crucify him; but [another] was made to resemble him to them. And those who differ over it are in doubt about it... they did not kill him, for certain. Rather, Allah raised him to Himself. And ever is Allah Exalted in Might and Wise."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** This is the **definitive Islamic teaching on Isa's end**, differing from the Christian belief in the crucifixion:
> - The enemies plotted to kill Isa, and *believed* they crucified him — but **they did not.**
> - Allah **raised Isa up to Himself** (alive, body and soul), and **made another resemble him** (one was crucified in his place, while the people thought it was Isa).
> - "They did not kill him, **for certain** (*yaqinan*)" — the Qur'an is emphatic.
>
> So in Islam: **Isa was neither killed nor crucified.** He was honoured by being raised to Allah. (Note: this is a point where Islam and Christianity clearly differ; the encyclopedia states the Islamic position plainly and respectfully.)

### 3.6 The Return of Isa

> ✅ **Authentic hadith — Sahih al-Bukhari 3448 / Sahih Muslim 155:** The Prophet ﷺ foretold that **Isa son of Maryam will descend** near the end of time as a **just judge**: he will *"break the cross, kill the swine, abolish the jizyah, and wealth will be so abundant that none will accept it."* He will rule by the Shari'ah of Muhammad ﷺ, affirm Islam, defeat the Dajjal (the false messiah), and the earth will be filled with justice and *tawhid*.
> ✅ **Surah Az-Zukhruf (43:61):** Isa is "a sign/knowledge of the Hour."
>
> **Tafsir:** Isa's story is not over. He was raised alive and will **return** before the Day of Judgement — not as a new lawgiver, but as a follower and affirmer of the final Prophet's message, establishing pure monotheism and justice. After his mission, he will **die a natural death** (since every soul tastes death) and be buried among humanity. His return is a major sign of the end times in authentic Islamic belief.

## 5. The Theology: Completing the Islamic Portrait of Isa

Drawing the season together, the Islamic belief about Isa (stated clearly and respectfully):

- **A great prophet and Messenger** — one of the five *Ulu al-'Azm*; faith in him is **obligatory** for every Muslim.
- **Born of a virgin** by Allah's miracle (Episodes 38-39).
- **Worked real miracles** — but always *"by the permission of Allah"* (3:49); the power was Allah's.
- **Called only to worship Allah alone** (3:51; 5:72, 116-117) — he never claimed divinity.
- **Was not killed or crucified** — Allah **raised him up** alive (4:157-158).
- **Will return** before the end of time as a just ruler affirming Islam, then die naturally (Bukhari 3448).
- **Foretold the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ** (61:6).

> 📖 **Respectful framing:** Islam and Christianity share deep reverence for Jesus and Mary, and agree on his virgin birth, his miracles, and his messiahship. They differ on his **nature** (servant-prophet vs. divine son), the **trinity** (Islam affirms strict Oneness, 4:171; 5:73), and the **crucifixion** (Islam: he was raised, not killed). The encyclopedia presents the Islamic position with evidence and without hostility — honouring Isa as Islam commands.

## 6. Lessons

1. **Every miracle points to Allah, not the miracle-worker.** Isa's "by Allah's permission" (3:49) is the key — signs magnify the Creator. Never let admiration for a person slide into worshipping them.
2. **The prophets' religion is one.** "Their religion is one" (Bukhari 3442) — *tawhid* and submission to Allah, from Adam to Isa to Muhammad ﷺ. The whole encyclopedia is one continuous message.
3. **Be a "helper of Allah" like the disciples.** "We are the supporters of Allah" (3:52) — support the truth and the people of faith.
4. **Greater proof, greater responsibility** (5:115). The more clearly Allah shows us the truth, the more accountable we are to live by it.
5. **Hold the balanced truth about Isa** — neither diminish him (he is a mighty prophet) nor deify him (he is Allah's servant). This balance is itself a key to Paradise (Bukhari 3435).
6. **The story isn't over.** Isa was raised alive and will return — a reminder that Allah's plan spans far beyond what we see.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Isa's miracles (raising the dead) prove he is God."**
> ✅ The Qur'an stresses each was **"by the permission of Allah"** (3:49). The power was **Allah's**, channelled through His prophet as a sign. Other prophets also worked mighty miracles by Allah's leave.

> ❌ **"Jesus was crucified and died on the cross."**
> ✅ The Qur'an states clearly: **"they did not kill him, nor crucify him... rather, Allah raised him to Himself"** (4:157-158). This is the Islamic belief (differing respectfully from Christian doctrine).

> ❌ **"Isa claimed to be God or asked to be worshipped."**
> ✅ He called only to **worship Allah alone** (3:51; 5:72) and will disavow any claim to divinity on the Day of Judgement (5:116-117). His first words as a baby were "I am the **servant** of Allah" (19:30).

> ❌ **"Isa is dead / his story is finished."**
> ✅ He was **raised alive** and will **return** before the end of time as a just ruler affirming Islam (Bukhari 3448), then die naturally. His role in history continues.

> ❌ **"Islam and Christianity have nothing in common on Jesus."**
> ✅ They share much — his **virgin birth, miracles, messiahship, and honour.** The differences are about his *nature* and *end*. Islam reveres him as a beloved prophet.

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — Details of Isa's life, disciples' names, the substitute on the cross, the table's exact food**

- **Narration:** Naming all disciples, identifying *who* was made to resemble Isa (various reports: a disciple who volunteered, the betrayer, etc.), the precise menu of the heavenly table, detailed accounts of his daily miracles.
- **Source:** Isra'iliyyat/Christian tradition and tafsir reports without firm chains.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'anic essentials (the mission, miracles "by Allah's leave," the disciples, the table, the raising-up) are firm; the specifics (who was substituted, names, menus) are **unverified** — the Qur'an deliberately leaves them open ("those who differ over it are in doubt," 4:157).

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was Isa's message?**
A: Pure *tawhid* — "worship Allah, my Lord and your Lord" (3:51) — confirming the Torah and bringing the Injil. He never called people to worship himself.

**Q: Were Isa's miracles his own power?**
A: No — each was **"by the permission of Allah"** (3:49). They were Allah's signs through His prophet.

**Q: Was Jesus crucified, according to Islam?**
A: **No** — "they did not kill him, nor crucify him... Allah raised him to Himself" (4:157-158). He was raised alive.

**Q: Will Isa return?**
A: Yes — he will **descend before the end of time** as a just ruler, affirm Islam, defeat the Dajjal, then die a natural death (Bukhari 3448).

**Q: Did Isa predict the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ?**
A: Yes — he foretold "a messenger to come after me whose name is **Ahmad**" (61:6).

**Q: How should a Muslim view Jesus?**
A: As a **beloved, mighty prophet and the servant of Allah** (Bukhari 3435) — revered, believed in, but not worshipped. Neither diminished nor deified.
