# Season 10 · Episode 37
# The Prayer in the Temple
### The Family of 'Imran, the Vowed Child, and the Care of Zakariyya

> *"My Lord, indeed I have vowed to You what is in my womb, consecrated [for Your service], so accept this from me. Indeed, You are the Hearing, the Knowing."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Aal 'Imran (3:35)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Season 10 tells one of the Qur'an's most beloved stories — that of **Maryam (Mary)** and her son **Isa (Jesus), peace be upon them.** This first chapter sets the stage: the righteous **family of 'Imran**, the mother who vowed her unborn child to Allah's service, the miraculous childhood of **Maryam** in the Temple under the care of the Prophet **Zakariyya**, and Zakariyya's own miracle — a son (**Yahya/John**) in old age. It is a story of devotion, divine provision, and answered prayers, introducing the chosen woman who would bear the Messiah.

> 📖 **Tone note:** This season concerns figures central to Christianity. The encyclopedia presents the **Islamic view** — clearly, respectfully, and with full evidence — noting where it differs from Christian belief without hostility. Maryam and Isa are deeply honoured in Islam; Isa is one of the five greatest Messengers.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

The story begins with the **family of 'Imran** — a noble, righteous lineage among the Children of Israel. **'Imran's wife** (Maryam's mother, traditionally named **Hannah**), longing for a child, **vowed her unborn baby to the service of Allah** at the Temple (Bayt al-Maqdis in Jerusalem). When she gave birth, it was a **girl** — and she named her **Maryam** ("Mary"), praying for Allah to protect her and her offspring from Shaytan. (The Prophet ﷺ noted that Maryam and her son were among the few never "touched" by Shaytan at birth — see §5.)

Maryam grew up in the Temple under the guardianship of the Prophet **Zakariyya (Zechariah)**, who was amazed to find she had **provision (food) with her out of season** — and when he asked, she said simply: *"It is from Allah. Indeed, Allah provides for whom He wills without account"* (3:37). Witnessing this divine provision for Maryam, the elderly and childless Zakariyya was inspired to **pray for a child of his own** — and Allah granted him **Yahya (John the Baptist)**, despite his old age and his wife's barrenness, with the sign that he would not speak to people for three days except by gesture. This chapter explores the family of 'Imran, the vow, Maryam's blessed upbringing, and the miracle of Yahya — the prelude to the even greater miracle of Isa.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Chosen Family

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:33-34):** "Indeed, Allah chose Adam and Nuh and the family of Ibrahim and the family of 'Imran over the worlds — descendants, some of them from others..."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Allah names the **family of 'Imran** alongside Adam, Nuh, and the family of Ibrahim as chosen lineages — a mark of their honour. From this family would come Maryam and Isa. (Note: Surah 3 of the Qur'an is itself named *"Aal 'Imran"* — "The Family of 'Imran" — reflecting their importance.)

### 3.2 The Vow and the Birth of Maryam

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:35-36)**
>
> **Translation:** "[Mention] when the wife of 'Imran said, 'My Lord, indeed I have vowed to You what is in my womb, consecrated [for Your service], so accept this from me...' But when she delivered her, she said, 'My Lord, I have delivered a female' — and Allah was most knowing of what she delivered — 'and the male is not like the female. And I have named her Maryam, and I seek refuge for her in You and her descendants from Shaytan, the expelled.'"
>
> **Tafsir (as-Sa'di):** 'Imran's wife had vowed her child to **Temple service** (typically a role for males). When she bore a *girl*, she was momentarily concerned ("the male is not like the female" — for such service) — but Allah, "most knowing of what she delivered," had a far greater plan: this girl, **Maryam**, would be honoured above all women and become the mother of a great Messenger. Her mother's prayer — seeking Allah's **protection from Shaytan** for Maryam and her offspring — was beautifully answered (§5). This teaches: what *we* may see as a disappointment, Allah may be shaping into something far greater than we imagined.

### 3.3 Maryam's Blessed Upbringing and Divine Provision

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:37)**
>
> **Arabic:** ...كُلَّمَا دَخَلَ عَلَيْهَا زَكَرِيَّا الْمِحْرَابَ وَجَدَ عِندَهَا رِزْقًا ۖ قَالَ يَا مَرْيَمُ أَنَّىٰ لَكِ هَٰذَا ۖ قَالَتْ هُوَ مِنْ عِندِ اللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَرْزُقُ مَن يَشَاءُ بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍ
>
> **Translation:** "...So her Lord accepted her with good acceptance and made her grow in a good manner and put her in the care of Zakariyya. Every time Zakariyya entered upon her in the prayer chamber (*mihrab*), he found with her provision. He said, 'O Maryam, from where is this [coming] to you?' She said, 'It is from Allah. Indeed, Allah provides for whom He wills without account.'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Maryam was raised in devotion in the Temple, under the guardianship of the Prophet **Zakariyya** (her relative). The miracle: Zakariyya repeatedly found **provision (fruit) with her out of season** — summer fruit in winter and vice versa. Her response — *"It is from Allah; He provides for whom He wills without account"* — reveals her deep faith and *tawhid* even as a young girl. This phrase became a treasured statement of trust in Allah's provision (*rizq*).

### 3.4 Zakariyya's Prayer and the Miracle of Yahya

> ✅ **Surah Maryam (19:2-11) & Aal 'Imran (3:38-41):** Witnessing Allah's provision for Maryam, the elderly **Zakariyya** — whose wife was barren and who feared for the future of his lineage and the faith — turned to Allah in a private, heartfelt prayer: *"My Lord, grant me from Yourself a good offspring. Indeed, You are the Hearer of supplication"* (3:38). His du'a in Surah Maryam is especially moving: *"My Lord, indeed my bones have weakened, and my head has filled with white, and never have I been unhappy in my supplication to You... so give me from Yourself an heir"* (19:4-5). Allah answered: he would have a son, **Yahya (John)** — a name "no one before had been given" — righteous, honourable, and chaste. As a sign, Zakariyya would **not speak to people for three days/nights except by gesture**, devoting himself to Allah's remembrance.
>
> **Tafsir:** Zakariyya's prayer is a model of *du'a*: private, humble, persistent, full of hope, acknowledging his own weakness and Allah's power. Despite old age and barrenness — *humanly impossible* — Allah granted him a son, just as He would soon grant Maryam a son *without any father at all.* The miracle of Yahya (a child to the very old) is the **stepping-stone** to the greater miracle of Isa (a child to a virgin) — Allah preparing hearts to accept that **He creates as He wills, beyond all natural means.**

A direct, authentic confirmation that 'Imran's wife's prayer was answered, and a mark of the special honour of Maryam and Isa.

## 5. The Themes

1. **Sincere vows and prayers are heard.** From 'Imran's wife's vow to Zakariyya's plea for a child, this chapter is full of **answered du'a** — Allah hears the sincere, even when the request seems impossible.
2. **Allah's plan exceeds our expectations.** Maryam's mother wanted a son for Temple service; Allah gave a daughter who would be honoured above all women and mother the Messiah. What seems "less" may be Allah's "more."
3. **Provision is from Allah, without account** (3:37). Maryam's *rizq* came from where she did not expect — teaching trust in Allah's provision through unseen means.
4. **Allah creates beyond natural means.** Yahya (to the very old) prepares us for Isa (to a virgin) — proof that Allah's "Be, and it is" transcends all natural limits.
5. **Devotion and purity are honoured.** Maryam's upbringing in worship and her protected purity set the stage for her unique role.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Make du'a with hope, however impossible the request.** Zakariyya, old and childless, prayed earnestly — and was answered (19:4-7). Never consider any sincere prayer "too much" for Allah.
2. **Trust Allah's plan over your own expectations.** What looks like a disappointment (a daughter instead of a son) may be the seed of something far greater. "Allah was most knowing" (3:36).
3. **"It is from Allah" — trust your provision to Him.** Maryam's words (3:37) model reliance: Allah provides "for whom He wills without account."
4. **Seek Allah's protection for your children** — as Maryam's mother did (3:36); it was answered (Bukhari 3431). Du'a for one's offspring is powerful.
5. **Devotion in youth bears fruit.** Maryam's early life of worship prepared her for her extraordinary destiny.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Islam disrespects Mary."**
> ✅ The opposite — **Maryam is the most honoured woman in the Qur'an**, the only woman named (over 30 times), with a surah named after her (Surah Maryam, ch. 19), and declared the **best of women** (Bukhari 3432). Islam reveres her deeply.

> ❌ **"'Imran (Maryam's father) is the same as 'Imran the father of Musa/Harun."**
> ✅ Different individuals across different eras (the name recurs). Maryam is called "sister of Harun" (19:28) in the sense of lineage/piety or a contemporary named Harun — not Musa's brother Harun (see Episode 38 §7).

> ❌ **"The miracle of Yahya (a son to the elderly) is just legend."**
> ✅ It was a **deliberate miracle** (19:8-9: "How can I have a son when my wife is barren and I have reached extreme old age?" — Allah: "Thus; it is easy for Me"), preparing for the greater miracle of Isa. Allah's creative power has no limits.

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — The mother's name, the casting of lots for Maryam's guardianship, Temple details**

- **Narration:** Naming Maryam's mother "Hannah" and father details, the elaborate account of priests casting lots (pens in a river) to decide who would care for Maryam (alluded to in 3:44), specific Temple customs.
- **Source:** Tradition/Isra'iliyyat; the Qur'an mentions the dispute over her guardianship (3:44) but not the names/full details.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ "Hannah" is a traditional name (commonly used). The Qur'anic essentials (the vow, the upbringing, Zakariyya's care) are firm; the elaborations are unverified.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Who was the family of 'Imran?**
A: A righteous lineage among the Children of Israel, chosen by Allah (3:33), from whom came Maryam and Isa. Surah 3 is named after them.

**Q: How is Maryam honoured in Islam?**
A: She is the most honoured woman in the Qur'an — the only woman named, with a surah named after her, declared the best of women (Bukhari 3432), and protected from Shaytan (Bukhari 3431).

**Q: How did Zakariyya have a son in old age?**
A: By a miracle answering his earnest du'a — Allah said "it is easy for Me" (19:9), granting him Yahya despite his age and his wife's barrenness.

**Q: What was Maryam's miraculous provision?**
A: Zakariyya found out-of-season fruit with her in the *mihrab*; she said it was "from Allah, who provides without account" (3:37).

**Q: Why is the Yahya miracle told before Isa's?**
A: To prepare hearts to accept Allah's limitless creative power — a child to the very old, before the greater sign of a child to a virgin.
