# Season 10 · Episode 38
# The Chosen Woman
### The Annunciation and the Miracle of "Be"

> *"O Maryam, indeed Allah has chosen you and purified you and chosen you above the women of the worlds."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Aal 'Imran (3:42)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter covers the **annunciation** to Maryam — when the angel (Jibril) appeared and announced that she, a chaste virgin, would bear a son by the will of Allah — and the conception of **Isa (Jesus)** through the divine command **"Be."** It establishes the central Islamic teaching about Isa: that his miraculous, fatherless birth is a **sign of Allah's power**, comparable to the creation of Adam (from no parents at all) — emphatically **not** evidence of divinity or sonship of God. It is the theological heart of the season.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

As Maryam devoted herself to worship, having withdrawn to a private place, the angel (**Jibril**) appeared to her **in the form of a well-proportioned man.** Startled and fearing for her chastity, she sought refuge in Allah: *"Indeed, I seek refuge in the Most Merciful from you, [so leave] if you should be fearing of Allah"* (19:18). The angel reassured her: he was a **messenger from her Lord**, sent to announce the gift of **a pure son.**

Maryam, astonished, asked the natural question: *"How can I have a son when no man has touched me, and I have not been unchaste?"* (19:20). The answer is the foundation of Islamic Christology: **"Thus [it will be]; your Lord says, 'It is easy for Me'... and it is a matter [already] decreed"** (19:21). And in Surah Aal 'Imran, the principle is stated with crystal clarity: **the creation of Isa is like the creation of Adam** — *"Indeed, the example of Isa to Allah is like that of Adam. He created him from dust; then He said to him, 'Be,' and he was"* (3:59). Just as Adam had **no father and no mother**, Isa having **no father** is no stranger to Allah's power — it is simply His command "Be."

So Maryam conceived Isa — not through any human father, but by Allah's word breathed by the angel. The angel called him *"a word from Allah"* and *"the Messiah, Isa son of Maryam,"* and foretold he would be a **prophet, speak in the cradle and in maturity, work miracles by Allah's leave, and be honoured in this world and the next.** This chapter explores the annunciation, Maryam's chastity and fear of Allah, the conception by "Be," and the decisive teaching that Isa is a created servant and prophet — not a son of God.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 "Allah Has Chosen You Above the Women of the Worlds"

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:42-43):** "And [mention] when the angels said, 'O Maryam, indeed Allah has chosen you and purified you and chosen you above the women of the worlds. O Maryam, be devoutly obedient to your Lord and prostrate and bow with those who bow [in prayer].'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Maryam was given a unique, twice-stated honour: **"chosen"** (selected by Allah), **"purified"** (of sin and slander), and **"chosen above the women of the worlds"** (the most honoured of women — confirmed by the Prophet ﷺ, Bukhari 3432). This honour was tied to her devotion — "be devoutly obedient, prostrate, and bow." Her elevation was both Allah's grace *and* the fruit of her worship.

### 3.2 The Annunciation

> ✅ **Surah Maryam (19:17-21)**
>
> **Translation:** "...and We sent to her Our Angel, and he represented himself to her as a well-proportioned man. She said, 'Indeed, I seek refuge in the Most Merciful from you, [so leave] if you should be fearing of Allah.' He said, 'I am only the messenger of your Lord to give you [news of] a pure boy.' She said, 'How can I have a boy while no man has touched me and I have not been unchaste?' He said, 'Thus [it will be]; your Lord says, "It is easy for Me, and We will make him a sign to the people and a mercy from Us. And it is a matter [already] decreed."'"
>
> **Tafsir (as-Sa'di):** Note Maryam's first reaction to a strange man appearing in her private place: she **immediately sought refuge in Allah** and appealed to *his* fear of Allah — a model of a chaste woman's vigilance. The angel reassured her of his divine mission. Her question — "how, when no man has touched me?" — was not doubt but natural wonder at the impossible. The answer establishes the core truth: this is **Allah's power** ("It is easy for Me"), Isa will be **"a sign to the people and a mercy,"** and it is **already decreed.** Isa's birth is fundamentally a *sign (ayah)* of Allah's might — that is its purpose.

### 3.3 "Be, and It Is" — The Conception of Isa

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:45-47)**
>
> **Translation:** "[Mention] when the angels said, 'O Maryam, indeed Allah gives you good tidings of a word from Him, whose name will be the Messiah, Isa, the son of Maryam — distinguished in this world and the Hereafter and among those brought near [to Allah]. He will speak to the people in the cradle and in maturity and will be of the righteous.' She said, 'My Lord, how will I have a child when no man has touched me?' [The angel] said, 'Such is Allah; He creates what He wills. When He decrees a matter, He only says to it, "Be," and it is.'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Several foundational points:
> - Isa is **"a word from Allah" (*kalimatun minhu*)** — meaning he was created by Allah's command/word "Be," *not* that he is the eternal divine being. (This title honours the *miraculous manner* of his creation.)
> - **"The Messiah (*al-Masih*), Isa son of Maryam"** — note he is always "son of *Maryam*," emphasizing his human mother and his humanity.
> - He will **speak in the cradle** (Episode 39), be a prophet, and be among the righteous and the near to Allah.
> - The decisive theology: **"He creates what He wills... 'Be,' and it is."** Isa's fatherless creation is simply Allah's *creative command* — the same way Allah creates anything. It demonstrates Allah's *power*, not Isa's divinity.

### 3.4 The Decisive Verse: Isa Is Like Adam

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:59)**
>
> **Arabic:** إِنَّ مَثَلَ عِيسَىٰ عِندَ اللَّهِ كَمَثَلِ آدَمَ ۖ خَلَقَهُ مِن تُرَابٍ ثُمَّ قَالَ لَهُ كُن فَيَكُونُ
>
> **Translation:** "Indeed, the example of Isa to Allah is like that of Adam. He created him from dust; then He said to him, 'Be,' and he was."
>
> **Tafsir:** This single verse is the Qur'an's definitive answer to those who argue Isa's miraculous birth proves he is divine or the "son of God." The logic is unanswerable: if a **fatherless** birth made Isa a "son of God," then **Adam** — who had **neither father nor mother** — would have an even greater claim! Yet no one calls Adam divine. Both were created by Allah's command "Be." **A miraculous *manner* of creation does not make the created being divine; it magnifies the *Creator*.** Isa is a noble human prophet, a servant of Allah, born by a miracle that points to Allah's power alone.

Believing this correctly is a key to Paradise. It carefully **affirms Isa's honour** while **denying his divinity.**

## 5. The Theology: The Islamic View of Isa (Clear & Respectful)

This is the doctrinal heart of the season. The Qur'an's position on Isa is precise and consistent:

**What Islam AFFIRMS about Isa:**
- He is a **great prophet and messenger**, one of the five *Ulu al-'Azm*.
- He was **born miraculously of a virgin** (Maryam) by Allah's command — a true miracle.
- He is **"the Messiah" (*al-Masih*)** and **"a word from Allah"** and **"a spirit from Him."**
- He performed **real miracles by Allah's permission** (Episode 39).
- He was **raised up by Allah** (not crucified) and will **return** before the end of time (Episode 40).
- Honouring him is **obligatory** — to insult or deny him is disbelief.

**What Islam DENIES about Isa (clearly, without hostility):**
- That he is **God** or **part of God** or the **literal "son of God."** *"It is not befitting for Allah to take a son; exalted is He!"* (19:35).
- That God is a **trinity**. *"Do not say 'Three'; desist — it is better for you. Indeed, Allah is but One God"* (4:171).
- That his miraculous birth implies divinity — it is like **Adam's** creation (3:59), a sign of Allah's *power*.

> 📖 **The respectful framing:** Islam holds Isa in the **highest honour** as a beloved prophet — Muslims cannot even be Muslim without believing in him. The difference with Christian doctrine is on his *nature* (created servant vs. divine son) — and the Qur'an states this difference clearly but always within reverence for Isa and his mother. *"O People of the Scripture, do not commit excess in your religion... The Messiah, Isa son of Maryam, was but a messenger of Allah and His word..."* (4:171).

## 6. Lessons

1. **A miraculous origin magnifies the Creator, not the creature.** Isa's virgin birth — like Adam's parentless creation — points to Allah's limitless power, not to any divinity in the created being (3:59).
2. **"Be, and it is" — nothing is impossible for Allah.** The God who creates from nothing by a word can do anything. This frees the heart from despair and from limiting Allah by "natural" expectations.
3. **Chastity and vigilance are honoured.** Maryam's first instinct before a strange man was to seek refuge in Allah (19:18) — a model of purity and God-consciousness.
4. **Honour is from Allah and through devotion.** Maryam was "chosen above the women of the worlds" (3:42) — by Allah's grace and her own worship.
5. **Hold the correct, balanced belief about Isa** (Bukhari 3435): a beloved prophet and servant of Allah — neither diminished nor deified.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Isa's virgin birth proves he is the son of God / divine."**
> ✅ The Qur'an answers directly: Isa is **like Adam** (3:59), who had *no parents at all*. A miraculous birth shows Allah's **power**, not the creature's divinity. Isa is Allah's created servant and messenger (Bukhari 3435).

> ❌ **"Calling Isa 'a word from Allah' and 'a spirit from Him' means he is part of God."**
> ✅ These are **titles of honour** for the *manner* of his creation (by Allah's word "Be") — not that he is literally a piece of the divine. The same Qur'an insists Allah is **One** and "does not take a son" (19:35; 4:171).

> ❌ **"Islam denigrates Jesus."**
> ✅ Islam **reveres** Isa as one of the greatest prophets; believing in him is *required* for faith. Islam simply affirms he is a **prophet, not God** — an honour, not an insult.

> ❌ **"Maryam was unchaste / there was a human father."**
> ✅ Absolutely **not.** The Qur'an emphatically affirms her **purity and virginity** (19:20; 66:12: "who guarded her chastity"); the conception was by Allah's command alone. Slandering Maryam is a grave sin the Qur'an condemns (4:156).

## 8. Weak / Disputed Points (Labelled)

---
**📖 Clarification 1 — "Sister of Harun" (19:28)**

- When Maryam returned with baby Isa, her people called her "O sister of Harun." Scholars explain: (a) "Harun" was a righteous man of her time, and she was called his "sister" as a fellow-pious person; or (b) it refers to lineage from the tribe/house associated with Harun (Aaron); or (c) it was an idiom for descent. It does **NOT** mean she was the literal sister of Musa's brother Harun (who lived centuries earlier) — the Prophet ﷺ clarified that earlier peoples named after the prophets and righteous (cf. Sahih Muslim 2135 area). *A known point, resolved by the Sunnah.*

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: How was Isa conceived?**
A: By Allah's command "Be" (3:47), without any human father — a true miracle, conveyed through the angel. Maryam remained chaste and pure (66:12).

**Q: Does the virgin birth make Isa divine?**
A: No — the Qur'an compares him to **Adam**, created with *no parents at all* (3:59). A miraculous birth magnifies Allah's power; it doesn't make the creature God.

**Q: Why is Isa called "a word from Allah"?**
A: Because he was created by Allah's **word "Be"** — a title of honour for the miraculous manner of his creation, not a claim of divinity (Bukhari 3435).

**Q: What is the Islamic belief about Jesus in one line?**
A: He is **the slave of Allah and His Messenger, His word cast to Maryam, and a spirit from Him** (Bukhari 3435) — a beloved prophet, not God.

**Q: Does Islam respect Mary and Jesus?**
A: Profoundly — Maryam is the best of women and the only woman named in the Qur'an; Isa is one of the greatest prophets, and faith in him is required. Islam honours them while affirming Allah's Oneness.
