# The First Muslim City
### The Mosque, the Brotherhood, and the Constitution of Madinah

> *"The believers are but brothers, so make settlement between your brothers."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Hujurat (49:10)**

## How To Use This Chapter

The Prophet ﷺ has arrived in Madinah (Episode 48). This chapter covers how he **built the first Islamic society** from scratch: the construction of the **Prophet's Mosque (Al-Masjid an-Nabawi)**, the founding of **brotherhood (mu'akhah)** between the Makkan emigrants (**Muhajirun**) and the Madinan helpers (**Ansar**), and the **Constitution of Madinah (Sahifah)** — a pioneering document of pluralistic governance uniting Muslims, Jews, and others into one community. It is the blueprint of Islamic civilization: faith translated into a just, organized society.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Upon arriving in Madinah, the Prophet ﷺ immediately set about building a **community**, not just a congregation. His first major act was constructing the **Prophet's Mosque (Al-Masjid an-Nabawi)** — a simple structure of palm trunks and mud that became the **center of everything**: worship, learning, governance, community gatherings, the welfare of the poor, and the reception of delegations. (Islam's "mosque" was never just a prayer hall; it was the heart of society.)

His second masterstroke addressed the most pressing human problem: the **Muhajirun** (emigrants from Makkah) had arrived with **nothing** — they had abandoned homes, wealth, and businesses for their faith. So the Prophet ﷺ established the ***mu'akhah*** (**brotherhood**) — pairing each emigrant with a Madinan helper (**Ansari**), who **shared their home, wealth, and livelihood** with their new "brother." The generosity of the Ansar was so extraordinary that the Qur'an itself immortalized it: they *"give [others] preference over themselves, even though they are in need"* (59:9). This bond of faith **transcended blood and tribe** — a revolutionary social order.

Third, the Prophet ﷺ — now the leader of a diverse city — drafted the ***Sahifat al-Madinah* (Constitution of Madinah)**, a written covenant uniting the Muslims (Muhajirun and Ansar), the Jewish tribes, and other groups into a single ***ummah* (community/polity)** with mutual rights and obligations: common defense, freedom of religion for the Jews, justice, and the Prophet ﷺ as the final arbiter of disputes. It is regarded by historians as one of the **first written constitutions** in history. Together — the mosque, the brotherhood, and the constitution — the Prophet ﷺ transformed a feuding, fractured oasis into a **unified, just society**, the model of Islamic civilization. This chapter explores how faith was built into a functioning state.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Mosque — Heart of the Community

On arrival, the Prophet ﷺ let his camel roam freely, and where it knelt, he purchased the land and built the **Prophet's Mosque (Al-Masjid an-Nabawi)** — a humble structure of mud-brick walls, palm-trunk pillars, and a palm-frond roof, with his own modest living quarters attached. This mosque was **far more than a prayer hall** — it was the **nucleus of the entire society**:
- **Worship** — the five daily prayers, Jumu'ah, the spiritual center.
- **Education** — the Prophet ﷺ taught the Qur'an and Islam here; the *Suffah* (a shaded platform) housed poor students of knowledge.
- **Governance** — councils, decisions, and the reception of tribal delegations.
- **Welfare** — care of the poor, distribution of charity, shelter for the needy.
- **Community** — gatherings, announcements, and social life.

> **Tafsir/lesson:** The Prophet ﷺ built the **mosque first** — establishing that the community's life revolves around its connection to Allah. And he built it as a **center of integrated life**, not isolation — Islam unites worship and worldly affairs. The mosque remains the heart of Muslim communities to this day.

### 3.2 The Brotherhood (Mu'akhah) — and the Generosity of the Ansar

The **Muhajirun** had sacrificed everything for their faith — leaving homes, wealth, and family in Makkah. The Prophet's ﷺ solution was unprecedented: he formally **paired each emigrant with a Madinan helper (Ansari)** in a bond of **brotherhood** stronger than blood. The Ansar's response is one of the most beautiful chapters of human generosity:

> ✅ **Surah Al-Hashr (59:9)**
>
> **Translation:** "And [also for] those who were settled in the home (Madinah) and [adopted] the faith before them. They love those who emigrated to them and find not any want in their breasts of what they (the emigrants) were given, but **give [them] preference over themselves, even though they are in privation (need). And whoever is protected from the stinginess of his soul — it is those who are successful.**"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** The Ansar didn't merely *tolerate* the refugees — they **loved** them and gave them **preference over themselves**, sharing their homes, wealth, and even (in famous reports) offering to divide their property in half. This *ithar* (selfless preference of others) is the pinnacle of generosity, immortalized by Allah. The Muhajirun, in turn, were dignified — many, like 'Abd ar-Rahman ibn 'Awf, asked only to be shown the *marketplace* so they could earn their own living rather than depend on charity. **The bond of faith created a brotherhood that dissolved the barriers of tribe, wealth, and origin** — the social revolution at the heart of Islam.

> ✅ **The Qur'an on this brotherhood — Surah Al-Hujurat (49:10):** "The believers are but brothers (*ikhwah*)..." — faith makes all believers one family, a bond above blood, race, and nation. The Prophet ﷺ said: *"A Muslim is the brother of a Muslim; he does not wrong him or abandon him"* (**Sahih al-Bukhari 2442**).

### 3.3 The Constitution of Madinah (Sahifah)

As leader of a **diverse city** — Muslim emigrants and helpers, plus the **Jewish tribes** (Banu Qaynuqa', Banu an-Nadir, Banu Qurayzah) and remaining pagan Arabs — the Prophet ﷺ established a **written covenant**, the ***Sahifat al-Madinah* (Constitution of Madinah)**. Its provisions (preserved in the Seerah) included:
- The Muslims (Muhajirun and Ansar) and their allies form **one ummah (community/polity).**
- The **Jewish tribes** are part of the polity, with **freedom to practice their religion**, their own law in their affairs, and equal protection.
- **Common defense**: all parties defend Madinah together against external attack.
- **Justice and mutual obligation**: disputes referred to Allah and the Prophet ﷺ as the final arbiter; no separate alliances with Quraysh against the community.
- Protection of life and property; an end to the old blood-feuds.

> 📖 **Significance:** The Constitution of Madinah is recognized by many scholars and historians as **one of the earliest written constitutions in human history** — establishing a **pluralistic civil society** based on agreed rights and duties, religious freedom for non-Muslims, collective security, and the rule of law (with the Prophet ﷺ as arbiter). It transformed the feuding tribes of Yathrib — who had been locked in generations of war (Aws vs. Khazraj) — into a single, ordered community. It is a foundational document of Islamic political and social thought, showing that Islam came not only to reform hearts but to **organize a just society.**

> *(Note: The later conflicts with specific Jewish tribes arose from breaches of this covenant — treachery during war — and are matters of those tribes' political betrayal, not of the religious freedom the Constitution guaranteed. These events belong to the detailed Seerah of the battles.)*

## 5. The Themes

1. **Faith must be built into society.** The Prophet ﷺ didn't just preach belief — he **organized a community**: a mosque, a brotherhood, a constitution. Islam is a complete way of life — worship *and* social justice, individual faith *and* communal order.
2. **The mosque is the heart of the community.** Worship, learning, governance, and welfare all flowed from it. The believer's life centers on connection to Allah.
3. **Brotherhood transcends blood, tribe, and wealth** (49:10). Islam dissolved the divisions of *Jahiliyyah*, creating an ummah where the emigrant and the helper, the rich and the poor, were one family.
4. **Selfless generosity (*ithar*) is the height of faith** (59:9). The Ansar's preference of others over themselves is the gold standard of brotherhood.
5. **Justice and pluralism under one polity.** The Constitution of Madinah united Muslims and non-Muslims with rights, religious freedom, and the rule of law — Islam organizing a *just* society for all its members.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Build community, not just personal piety.** The Prophet ﷺ modeled that faith creates *society* — mosques, brotherhood, justice, welfare. Be a builder of righteous community, not an isolated worshipper.
2. **Center your life on the connection to Allah** (the mosque). Let your "first construction" — in your home, your day, your community — be your bond with Allah.
3. **Treat fellow believers as true brothers/sisters** (49:10; Bukhari 6011). Care for them as one body cares for its limbs — their pain is your pain.
4. **Practice *ithar* — prefer others over yourself.** The Ansar's selflessness (59:9) is the antidote to the "stinginess of the soul." Generosity, especially when you yourself are in need, is the path of the successful.
5. **Establish justice for all in your care** — Muslim and non-Muslim alike. The Constitution of Madinah shows Islam's commitment to rights, religious freedom, and the rule of law.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Islam is only about personal worship, not society."**
> ✅ The Madinan period proves Islam is a **complete system** — the Prophet ﷺ built a mosque, a brotherhood, *and* a constitution, organizing a just society. Faith and social order are inseparable in Islam.

> ❌ **"Islam was intolerant of Jews and others in Madinah."**
> ✅ The **Constitution of Madinah granted the Jewish tribes religious freedom and equal protection** as part of one polity. Later conflicts arose from specific tribes' **treachery during war** (breaking the covenant), not from religious persecution.

> ❌ **"The brotherhood (mu'akhah) was just symbolic."**
> ✅ It was **deeply practical** — the Ansar shared homes, wealth, and livelihood with the destitute emigrants (59:9). It solved a real humanitarian crisis through extraordinary generosity.

> ❌ **"The mosque is only for prayer."**
> ✅ The Prophet's ﷺ Mosque was the **center of all community life** — worship, education, governance, welfare, and gathering. The mosque's role in Islam is holistic.

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was the first thing the Prophet ﷺ did in Madinah?**
A: He built the **Prophet's Mosque (Al-Masjid an-Nabawi)** — the center of worship, learning, governance, and community — and established brotherhood among the believers.

**Q: What was the brotherhood (mu'akhah)?**
A: The Prophet ﷺ paired each Makkan emigrant with a Madinan helper, who **shared their home and wealth** — solving the refugees' destitution through the Ansar's extraordinary generosity (59:9).

**Q: What was the Constitution of Madinah?**
A: A **written covenant** uniting Muslims, Jews, and others into one polity (*ummah*) with common defense, religious freedom, justice, and the Prophet ﷺ as arbiter — one of the earliest written constitutions in history.

**Q: Did the Constitution give rights to non-Muslims?**
A: Yes — the **Jewish tribes** had religious freedom, their own law in their affairs, and equal protection as members of the polity. Later conflicts came from specific tribes' treachery, not religious intolerance.

**Q: Why is the mosque so central?**
A: Because the Prophet's ﷺ Mosque was the **hub of all community life** — showing that Islam centers society on the connection to Allah and unites worship with worldly affairs.
