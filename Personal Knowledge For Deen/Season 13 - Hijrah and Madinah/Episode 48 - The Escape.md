# Season 13 · Episode 48
# The Escape
### The Pledges of 'Aqabah, the Plot, and the Hijrah to Madinah

> *"Do not grieve; indeed, Allah is with us."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah At-Tawbah (9:40)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter covers the **turning point of Islamic history**: the **Hijrah** (migration) from Makkah to **Yathrib (Madinah)**. It includes the pledges of the people of Yathrib at **'Aqabah**, the Quraysh **assassination plot** against the Prophet ﷺ, the dramatic **escape with Abu Bakr** and the night in the **cave of Thawr**, and the arrival in Madinah that began the **Islamic calendar (Year 1 AH)**. It is the moment Islam went from a persecuted faith to an established community with a homeland.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

After years of Makkan persecution, the message found fertile ground in **Yathrib** (later **Madinah**), a city ~450 km north of Makkah. Groups of its people — from the tribes of **Aws and Khazraj** — met the Prophet ﷺ during the pilgrimage seasons and embraced Islam, taking two historic **pledges at al-'Aqabah**: the **First Pledge** (to worship Allah alone and live righteously) and the **Second Pledge** (in which ~73 men and 2 women pledged to protect the Prophet ﷺ as their own families, inviting him and the Muslims to migrate to their city). Allah then permitted the Muslims to emigrate, and they began leaving Makkah secretly in groups.

Quraysh, alarmed that Islam now had a base and protectors, convened and resolved to **assassinate the Prophet ﷺ** — selecting a young man from *each clan* to strike him together, so his blood would be spread among all tribes and his clan could not seek revenge. But Allah revealed the plot to His Messenger. On the night of the planned murder, **'Ali ibn Abi Talib** courageously slept in the Prophet's ﷺ bed (to deceive the killers), while the Prophet ﷺ left with **Abu Bakr.** They hid in the **cave of Thawr** for three nights as Quraysh search parties came to its very mouth. When Abu Bakr feared they would be seen, the Prophet ﷺ uttered the immortal words: *"Do not grieve; indeed, Allah is with us"* (9:40) — and Allah protected them (by tradition, a spider's web and nesting dove at the cave entrance suggested no one had entered). They then took an unexpected route to Madinah. The Prophet ﷺ arrived to a **jubilant welcome**, founding the first Muslim community. This migration — the **Hijrah** — became **Year 1 of the Islamic calendar (AH)**, marking the birth of the Muslim ummah as a society. This chapter explores the pledges, the plot, the cave, and the arrival.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Pledges of al-'Aqabah

Yathrib was unique: it had Arab tribes (Aws and Khazraj, long feuding) and Jewish tribes who spoke of an awaited prophet. When men of Yathrib heard the Prophet ﷺ during Hajj, many believed, seeing in Islam both truth and the means to end their tribal wars:
- **The First Pledge of al-'Aqabah** (~12 men): they pledged to worship Allah alone, not to steal, commit immorality, kill their children, or disobey in what is right. The Prophet ﷺ sent **Mus'ab ibn 'Umayr** to teach them the Qur'an — Islam's first "ambassador," through whom Islam spread rapidly in Yathrib.
- **The Second Pledge of al-'Aqabah** (~73 men and 2 women, incl. Nusaybah bint Ka'b): a pledge of **protection** — to defend the Prophet ﷺ as they would their own women and children — and an invitation for him and the Muslims to migrate to Yathrib. This created the **safe homeland** the mission needed.

> **Tafsir/lesson:** The pledges show Islam's appeal as both **truth and justice** — the people of Yathrib embraced *tawhid* and saw in it the brotherhood that could heal their divisions (as it did — Episode 49). Mus'ab's mission shows the power of *teaching the Qur'an* to spread faith.

### 3.2 The Assassination Plot

> ✅ **Surah Al-Anfal (8:30)**
>
> **Translation:** "And [remember] when those who disbelieved plotted against you to restrain you or kill you or expel you. They plan, and Allah plans. And Allah is the best of planners."
>
> **The story (Seerah):** Alarmed that Islam now had a fortified base in Yathrib, Quraysh's council (the *Dar an-Nadwah*) debated how to stop the Prophet ﷺ — imprisonment, exile, or murder. They settled on a cunning plan: **a strong young man from each clan** would strike him *together*, so the **blood-guilt would be shared among all tribes**, and his clan (Banu Hashim) could not avenge him against everyone. Allah informed His Messenger of the plot (8:30).

### 3.3 'Ali in the Bed and the Departure

On the night of the planned assassination, with the killers surrounding his house, the Prophet ﷺ asked **'Ali ibn Abi Talib** to **sleep in his bed** wrapped in his cloak — a courageous act, risking 'Ali's own life to cover the Prophet's ﷺ escape and to return the valuables people had entrusted to the Prophet ﷺ (even his enemies trusted *Al-Amin* with their goods!). The Prophet ﷺ left, reciting Qur'an, and (by Allah's protection) passed through the assassins unseen. He went to **Abu Bakr**, who had prepared two mounts, and the two set out — but *southward* (toward Yemen), the opposite direction from Madinah, to throw off pursuers.

### 3.4 The Cave of Thawr — "Do Not Grieve; Allah Is With Us"

> ✅ **Surah At-Tawbah (9:40)**
>
> **Arabic:** ...ثَانِيَ اثْنَيْنِ إِذْ هُمَا فِي الْغَارِ إِذْ يَقُولُ لِصَاحِبِهِ لَا تَحْزَنْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَنَا...
>
> **Translation:** "...the second of two, when they were in the cave, and he said to his companion, 'Do not grieve; indeed, Allah is with us.' And Allah sent down His tranquillity (*sakinah*) upon him and supported him with soldiers (angels) you did not see..."
>
> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3653 / Muslim 2381:** The Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr hid in the **cave of Thawr** (south of Makkah) for **three nights.** Quraysh's search parties tracked them to the very **mouth of the cave.** Abu Bakr, terrified — not for himself but for the Prophet ﷺ — whispered, "If one of them just looked down at his feet, he would see us!" The Prophet ﷺ replied with perfect calm: **"O Abu Bakr, what do you think of two, the third of whom is Allah?"** — the meaning of *"Do not grieve; Allah is with us."*
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** This is one of the most powerful moments of *tawakkul* in the Seerah — directly echoing **Musa at the sea** ("Indeed, my Lord is with me," Season 7) and **Ibrahim in the fire** (Season 5). With the enemy at the cave's mouth and no human escape, the Prophet's ﷺ certainty in Allah was absolute. Allah sent down ***sakinah*** (tranquillity) and unseen angelic support, and the searchers turned away. (By tradition, a spider had woven a web and a dove had nested at the entrance, suggesting no one had entered — though the *strength* of these specific details is debated; the Qur'anic protection is certain.) Verse 9:40 is also a definitive proof of **Abu Bakr's** unique rank — Allah Himself calls him the Prophet's ﷺ "companion" in the cave.

### 3.5 The Journey and the Joyful Arrival

After three nights, they took an **unfamiliar coastal route** north toward Yathrib, guided by a hired (non-Muslim) guide, evading pursuit (including the famous incident of **Suraqah ibn Malik**, whose horse sank into the sand as he chased them, leading him to give up — and the Prophet ﷺ prophesied he would one day wear the bracelets of the Persian emperor, fulfilled years later). They arrived first at **Quba** (on Madinah's outskirts), where the Prophet ﷺ built the **first mosque** (Masjid Quba). His entry into **Madinah** was met with overwhelming **joy** — the people singing his welcome — and the city, formerly Yathrib, became ***Al-Madinah*** ("the City [of the Prophet]"). The **Hijrah** marked the founding of the Muslim community and became **Year 1 of the Islamic (Hijri) calendar.**

> ✅ **On Abu Bakr (Bukhari 3653 / 9:40):** Verse 9:40 — naming Abu Bakr as the Prophet's ﷺ companion in the cave, upon whom Allah sent tranquillity — is one of the strongest Qur'anic proofs of his exalted virtue and his unique closeness to the Prophet ﷺ. The Prophet ﷺ later said: *"If I were to take a *khalil* (intimate friend) other than my Lord, I would have taken Abu Bakr"* (**Sahih al-Bukhari 3656**). Abu Bakr's loyalty during the Hijrah — risking everything, weeping only out of fear for the Prophet ﷺ — sealed his rank as the greatest of the Companions.

## 5. The Significance of the Hijrah

The Hijrah was not a retreat — it was the **strategic and spiritual turning point** of Islamic history:
1. **It established a homeland.** For 13 years Islam had no safe ground; now it had a city to live, govern, and grow.
2. **It began the Islamic calendar.** 'Umar (during his caliphate) later chose the **Hijrah** — not the Prophet's ﷺ birth or the first revelation — as **Year 1 (AH, *Anno Hegirae*)**, recognizing it as the moment the ummah became a **community and a state.**
3. **It transformed the mission.** From *individual faith under persecution* (Makkah) to *building a just society* (Madinah) — with its laws, governance, and the practical application of Islam.
4. **It proved Allah's promise.** The "sun and moon" resolve (Season 12) and the patience through persecution were now vindicated: Allah opened a door no one could close.

> 📖 **The lesson of Hijrah:** Sometimes faith requires *leaving* — abandoning home, wealth, and comfort for the freedom to worship and build. The Prophet ﷺ said: *"Actions are by intentions... whoever's migration was for Allah and His Messenger, his migration is for Allah and His Messenger"* (**Sahih al-Bukhari 1**) — the foundational hadith of Islam, revealed in the context of Hijrah, teaching that the *value of any act* is in its intention.

## 6. The Themes

1. **"Do not grieve; Allah is with us" (9:40).** The summit of *tawakkul* — with death at the cave's mouth, certainty in Allah brought *sakinah*. The believer's ultimate security is Allah's companionship.
2. **"They plan, and Allah plans" (8:30).** Quraysh's elaborate assassination plot was effortlessly overturned. No human scheme defeats Allah's plan (echoing Pharaoh, Yusuf's brothers).
3. **Loyalty and sacrifice.** Abu Bakr's companionship and 'Ali's risking his life in the bed exemplify the devotion the mission inspired.
4. **Hijrah as transformation.** Leaving everything for Allah opened the door to building the first Islamic society. Sometimes you must leave to grow.
5. **Intentions define actions** (Bukhari 1). The Hijrah's lesson: the worth of a deed is in *why* you do it.

---

## 7. Lessons

1. **Trust Allah completely in your hardest moment.** "Allah is with us" (9:40) — when human options run out, the believer's certainty in Allah is the true refuge.
2. **No plot against the righteous succeeds against Allah's plan** (8:30). Don't fear the schemes of others; fear only Allah and rely on Him.
3. **Sometimes faith requires leaving.** The Hijrah teaches that abandoning comfort for the freedom to obey Allah is honoured, not defeat — it can be the doorway to your greatest growth.
4. **Loyalty is precious.** Be an Abu Bakr to those on the path of truth — steadfast, sacrificing, fearing for others before yourself.
5. **Check your intention** (Bukhari 1). Before any act, ask: *for whom am I doing this?* The value of your deeds depends entirely on the answer.

## 8. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The Hijrah was a cowardly flight."**
> ✅ It was a **divinely-commanded strategic migration** to establish a homeland for the faith — the turning point that transformed Islam from a persecuted group into a thriving community. It required immense courage and sacrifice (leaving home, wealth, family).

> ❌ **"The Islamic calendar starts from the Prophet's ﷺ birth."**
> ✅ It starts from the **Hijrah** (Year 1 AH) — chosen by 'Umar as the moment the ummah became a community, marking the start of a new era.

> ❌ **"The spider web and dove at the cave are definitely authentic."**
> ✅ The **Qur'anic protection** (9:40) is certain; the *specific* spider-web/dove details are from the Seerah with **debated authenticity** — don't build doctrine on them, but the divine protection is firm.

> ❌ **"The Prophet ﷺ abandoned his trusts when fleeing."**
> ✅ The opposite — he left **'Ali to return the valuables** people (including his enemies!) had entrusted to him. Even fleeing for his life, *Al-Amin* ensured every trust was returned.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was the Hijrah?**
A: The **migration** of the Prophet ﷺ and the Muslims from Makkah to **Madinah** (Yathrib) — the turning point that established the first Muslim community and began the Islamic calendar.

**Q: Why did the Muslims migrate to Madinah?**
A: Because its people (Aws and Khazraj) embraced Islam and pledged at al-'Aqabah to protect the Prophet ﷺ — providing a safe homeland after years of Makkan persecution.

**Q: What happened in the cave of Thawr?**
A: The Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr hid there for three nights as Quraysh searched. When Abu Bakr feared discovery, the Prophet ﷺ said "Do not grieve; Allah is with us" (9:40), and Allah protected them.

**Q: Why does the Islamic calendar begin with the Hijrah?**
A: 'Umar chose it as Year 1 (AH) because the Hijrah marked the ummah becoming a **community and state** — a new era for Islam.

**Q: What is the lesson of "actions are by intentions"?**
A: The value of any deed depends on its **intention** — the Hijrah for Allah is rewarded; an action for worldly gain gets only that (Bukhari 1).
