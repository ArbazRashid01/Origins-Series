# Season 01 · Episode 04
# The One Who Refused
### Iblis — The First Act of Arrogance in Creation

> *"He refused and was arrogant, and became of the disbelievers."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Baqarah (2:34)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This is the climax of Season 01 — the moment the entire stage we have built (Allah, the Throne, the decree, the angels of light, the jinn of fire) collides in a single command and a single refusal. It is the **first sin** in the history of creation, and it is not committed by a human. It is committed by **Iblis**.

Everything in Episodes 01-03 was preparation for understanding *this* event correctly. We learned that angels **cannot** disobey (so the refuser cannot have been a true angel), and that jinn **can** (so the refuser must have been a jinn). Now we watch a being who worshipped Allah for ages throw it all away over a single word: *"I am better than him."*

This chapter examines exactly **what happened**, **why** it happened (the anatomy of arrogance, *kibr*), **who** Iblis was, and the **theology** of his disbelief — including the deep questions: *Was he forced? Did he have free will? Why did Allah create him? Is he in Hell now?*

> 📖 **Method reminder:** This event is *ghayb*, known only through the Qur'an, which narrates it in **seven** different places — each adding a detail. We will gather them all. Where storytellers added drama (Iblis's "biography," his name before the fall, the war with the jinn), we label it.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

When Allah announced He would create a *khalifah* (vicegerent) on earth — Adam — and fashioned him from clay and breathed into him the soul, He commanded all the angels to **prostrate** to Adam (a prostration of honour, not worship). **Every single angel prostrated. One being did not: Iblis.**

Iblis was **a jinn** (Qur'an 18:50), but through long and intense worship he had risen to dwell among the angels in the heavens — so much so that he was *included* in the command. When the moment came, his hidden disease surfaced. He refused, and gave his reason: *"I am better than him. You created me from fire and created him from clay."* (7:12). This was the first **qiyas** (analogical reasoning) used to oppose a direct command of Allah — and it was rooted in **arrogance (*kibr*)** and **envy (*hasad*)**.

For this, Allah expelled him, cursed him, and stripped him of his lofty station. Iblis did not, however, deny Allah's existence — he *spoke directly with Allah*, accepted resurrection as real, and still chose rebellion. His was not the disbelief of ignorance but the disbelief of **pride**: knowing the truth and refusing to submit to it.

This single event defines the spiritual battle of all human history. The lesson is razor-sharp: **knowledge without humility destroys; worship without submission is worthless; and the deadliest sin is not weakness of the flesh but arrogance of the heart.**

## 3. Background: The Being Who Stood Among the Angels

To feel the weight of this moment, picture the heavens before the command. The angels — countless, luminous, tireless — fill the unseen with worship. And among them stands a being who *looks* like he belongs: he has worshipped Allah so long and so intensely that he dwells in their ranks. The classical scholars report he was given names reflecting his devotion and was among the most worshipful of Allah's servants.

But there is a fatal difference invisible to the eye: the angels are made of *light* and have *no will to disobey*; this one is made of *fire* and has *free will*. His worship was real, but his heart harbored a hidden seed — pride. As long as no command challenged that pride, it stayed buried. Allah's command to prostrate to a being "lesser" in his own eyes was the test that exposed what was always there.

> 💡 **The deep principle:** Iblis's downfall teaches that **long worship does not guarantee a sound heart.** A person can pray, fast, and serve for years while nursing arrogance — and a single test can reveal that the foundation was rotten. The heart's hidden state is what matters to Allah.

## 4. The Story: Told in Full From the Qur'an

The Qur'an narrates this in seven passages: **2:30-39, 7:11-25, 15:26-43, 17:61-65, 18:50, 20:115-123, 38:71-85.** Together they form a complete account.

### 4.1 The Announcement (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:30)

> ✅ **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:30)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ إِنِّي جَاعِلٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةً ۖ قَالُوا أَتَجْعَلُ فِيهَا مَن يُفْسِدُ فِيهَا وَيَسْفِكُ الدِّمَاءَ وَنَحْنُ نُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِكَ وَنُقَدِّسُ لَكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّي أَعْلَمُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Wa idh qala Rabbuka lil-mala'ikati inni ja'ilun fi al-ardi khalifah. Qalu ataj'alu fiha man yufsidu fiha wa yasfiku ad-dima'a wa nahnu nusabbihu bihamdika wa nuqaddisu lak. Qala inni a'lamu ma la ta'lamun.*
>
> **Translation:** "And [mention] when your Lord said to the angels, 'Indeed, I will make upon the earth a successive authority (*khalifah*).' They said, 'Will You place upon it one who causes corruption therein and sheds blood, while we exalt You with praise and declare Your sanctity?' He said, 'Indeed, I know that which you do not know.'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** The angels' question was **not** an objection or arrogance — it was a request to understand (their knowledge that this creation would shed blood likely came from witnessing the earlier corruption of the jinn on earth, or from Allah's informing them). Allah's answer — *"I know what you do not know"* — is the master-statement of the whole episode: Allah's wisdom in creating humanity (with its prophets, martyrs, and sincere worshippers) far exceeds what any creature can perceive. Note the contrast already forming: the *angels* ask humbly and accept; *Iblis* will argue and refuse.

### 4.2 The Command and the Universal Prostration (Surah Sad 38:71-73)

> ✅ **Surah Sad (38:71-73)**
>
> **Translation:** "[Mention] when your Lord said to the angels, 'Indeed, I am going to create a human being from clay. So when I have proportioned him and breathed into him of My [created] soul, then fall down to him in prostration.' So the angels prostrated — **all of them entirely.**"
>
> **Tafsir:** The phrase *kulluhum ajma'un* ("all of them, entirely") is emphatic — it shuts the door on any exception among the angels. This is critical: it proves that the one who refused was **not** part of the obedient angelic body by nature. The prostration here was a **prostration of honour/greeting** to Adam (and ultimately of obedience to Allah's command), **not** an act of worship to Adam — worship is for Allah alone.

### 4.3 The Refusal (Surah Al-A'raf 7:11-12)

> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:11-12)**
>
> **Arabic:** ...فَسَجَدُوا إِلَّا إِبْلِيسَ لَمْ يَكُن مِّنَ السَّاجِدِينَ ۝ قَالَ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَلَّا تَسْجُدَ إِذْ أَمَرْتُكَ ۖ قَالَ أَنَا خَيْرٌ مِّنْهُ خَلَقْتَنِي مِن نَّارٍ وَخَلَقْتَهُ مِن طِينٍ
>
> **Transliteration:** *...fasajadu illa Iblis, lam yakun mina as-sajidin. Qala ma mana'aka alla tasjuda idh amartuk? Qala ana khayrun minhu, khalaqtani min narin wa khalaqtahu min tin.*
>
> **Translation:** "...So they prostrated, **except Iblis. He was not among those who prostrated.** [Allah] said, 'What prevented you from prostrating when I commanded you?' [Iblis] said, **'I am better than him. You created me from fire and created him from clay.'**"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Here is the first sin, fully exposed. Note its three layers:
> 1. **Disobedience** — he did not do what he was commanded.
> 2. **Arrogance (*kibr*)** — "I am better than him."
> 3. **Objecting to Allah's wisdom with his own logic (*qiyas*)** — "fire is better than clay." This was the *first false analogy* in history, placing his own reasoning above a direct divine command. (And his premise was even wrong: clay is humble, stable, fertile, and growth-bearing, while fire is rash and destructive — but the deeper error was *arguing at all* against Allah's command.)

### 4.4 Why "He Refused and Was Arrogant" (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:34)

> ✅ **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:34)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَإِذْ قُلْنَا لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ اسْجُدُوا لِآدَمَ فَسَجَدُوا إِلَّا إِبْلِيسَ أَبَىٰ وَاسْتَكْبَرَ وَكَانَ مِنَ الْكَافِرِينَ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Wa idh qulna lil-mala'ikati usjudu li-Adama fasajadu illa Iblisa aba wastakbara wa kana mina al-kafirin.*
>
> **Translation:** "And when We said to the angels, 'Prostrate to Adam,' they prostrated, **except Iblis. He refused and was arrogant and became of the disbelievers.**"
>
> **Tafsir:** Three words diagnose him: *aba* (he refused — the act), *istakbara* (he was arrogant — the motive), *kana mina al-kafirin* (he became / was of the disbelievers — the result). The phrasing *kana* ("was/became") indicates that this refusal *revealed* and sealed a disbelief his arrogance had been brewing. His sin was **not** an error of weakness or appetite; it was a deliberate, knowing rejection.

### 4.5 The Crucial Clarification: Iblis Was a Jinn (Surah Al-Kahf 18:50)

> ✅ **Surah Al-Kahf (18:50)**
>
> **Arabic:** ...فَسَجَدُوا إِلَّا إِبْلِيسَ كَانَ مِنَ الْجِنِّ فَفَسَقَ عَنْ أَمْرِ رَبِّهِ
>
> **Transliteration:** *...fasajadu illa Iblisa kana mina al-jinni fafasaqa 'an amri Rabbih.*
>
> **Translation:** "...So they prostrated, except Iblis. **He was of the jinn, and he departed from (disobeyed) the command of his Lord.**"
>
> **Tafsir & creed (the decisive point):** This verse settles a major theological question. Iblis was **of the jinn** — made from fire, possessing free will. He was *included* in the command to the angels because he was *living among them* through his worship, **not** because he was an angel. This harmonizes everything: angels cannot disobey (66:6), so the disobeyer had to be a being *with* the capacity to disobey — a jinn. (See §15 for the minority view and why the majority position is correct.)

### 4.6 The Expulsion and the Curse (Surah Al-Hijr 15:34-35; Surah Sad 38:77-78)

> ✅ **Surah Al-Hijr (15:34-35):** "[Allah] said, 'Then get out of it, for indeed you are expelled (*rajim*). And indeed, upon you is the curse until the Day of Recompense.'"

> ✅ **Surah Sad (38:77-78):** "[Allah] said, 'Then get out of Paradise/heaven, for indeed you are expelled. And indeed, My curse is upon you until the Day of Recompense.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** In an instant, ages of worship were undone. The being who stood among the angels became *Ash-Shaytan ar-Rajim* — the accursed, expelled Satan. His title from this point forward, the one we seek refuge from before reciting Qur'an, was *earned* in this moment. The lesson screams: **no amount of past worship protects a heart that refuses to submit.**

## 5. The Anatomy of Arrogance (*Kibr*) — The Theology of His Fall

Why did Iblis really fall? The Qur'an and Sunnah let us dissect it precisely.

### 5.1 The Definition of *Kibr* (Arrogance)

> ✅ **Sahih Muslim 91** — *Authentic.* The Prophet ﷺ said: **"No one who has an atom's weight of arrogance (*kibr*) in his heart will enter Paradise."** A man asked, "What if a person likes his clothes and shoes to be nice?" He replied: **"Allah is beautiful and loves beauty. Arrogance (*kibr*) is rejecting the truth (*batar al-haqq*) and looking down on people (*ghamt an-nas*)."**
>
> **Explanation:** This hadith *is* the diagnosis of Iblis. He did exactly both halves: he **rejected the truth** (the command to prostrate) and **looked down on** Adam ("I am better than him"). Iblis is the eternal case study of the sin that bars Paradise. Note the warning in it: even an *atom's weight* of this disease is deadly.

### 5.2 Envy (*Hasad*) Beneath the Pride

Beneath the arrogance lay **envy**: Adam had been honoured — fashioned by Allah's hand, given the soul, taught the names of all things (2:31), and now angels bowed to him. Iblis could not bear that this "younger," clay-made creature was elevated above him. Arrogance and envy are twin diseases, and Iblis carried both. (This is why *hasad* is so condemned — it is the very sin of Iblis, and the first sin committed in the heavens.)

### 5.3 The Danger of *Qiyas* Against Revelation

Iblis reasoned: *fire > clay, therefore I > Adam, therefore I should not bow.* This is the archetype of **placing personal logic above clear divine command.** Scholars note this is the root of much misguidance: when a person says "this command of Allah doesn't make sense *to me*, so I'll follow my own reasoning," they walk the path of Iblis. Submission (*islam*) means trusting the Commander even when the wisdom is not fully visible — exactly what the angels did and Iblis refused.

### 5.4 He Believed in Allah — Yet Disbelieved

Here is the most chilling theological point. Iblis was not an atheist. He:
- **spoke directly to Allah** and acknowledged Him as "Lord" (*Rabbi*, 15:39),
- **affirmed the Day of Resurrection** as real (15:36; 7:14),
- **knew** Allah created him.

So how is he a *kafir* (disbeliever)? Because **iman (faith) is not mere belief-that-God-exists; it is submission, obedience, and love.** Iblis had knowledge *about* Allah but refused to *submit to* Allah. This defines the Sunni understanding of *kufr*: disbelief can be the **disbelief of rejection (*kufr al-istikbar / al-iba'*)** — knowing the truth and arrogantly refusing it. Iblis is the prototype. *(This refutes the idea that "as long as you believe in God in your heart, actions don't matter.")*

It frames the human-Shaytan relationship perfectly: where *we* are offered the very thing he forfeited (submission → Paradise), and he is consumed by the fact that we can still take the path he refused.

## 7. Weak, Disputed & Non-Authentic Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Athar / Isra'iliyyat 1 — Iblis's "biography" before the fall**

- **Narration:** That Iblis's name before the fall was *al-Harith* or *'Azazil*; that he was a noble angel, a "peacock of the angels," the treasurer of Paradise, who governed the lowest heaven and earth; the leader who fought the earlier jinn.
- **Source:** Reported from **Ibn 'Abbas, Ibn Mas'ud and others in tafsir**, but largely **Isra'iliyyat**; no marfu' sahih chain.
- **Why disputed:** These details cannot be known except by revelation, and there is no sound prophetic report for them. They also feed the false "fallen angel" framing.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ **NO for the details** (names like 'Azazil, "peacock of angels"). What's **certain** from the Qur'an: he was a jinn (18:50) who worshipped enough to be among the angels, then refused.
---
**⚠️ Disputed 2 — "Iblis was originally an angel"**

- **Narration:** A minority of early scholars held Iblis *was* an angel who was transformed/punished.
- **Why minority/weak:** It contradicts the explicit 18:50 ("he was of the jinn"), the nature of angels (66:6: they cannot disobey), and the substance distinction (Muslim 2996: angels=light, Iblis argued he was made of fire).
- **Rely on it?** ❌ The **majority and stronger view** (Ibn Kathir, the verse 18:50) is that he was a **jinn**. The "angel" view is a weak minority reading, often based on his *inclusion* in the command, which 18:50 already explains.
---
**❌ Fabricated 3 — Detailed dialogues / "Iblis's advice" stories**

- **Narration:** Long fabricated "conversations" where Iblis reveals all his secrets, lists who he fears, names his children and their jobs (e.g., a demon for the marketplace, one for the bathroom), with precise detail.
- **Source:** Fabricated hadith (e.g., the long "Hadith of Iblis" with Mu'adh) and folklore.
- **Why rejected:** No sound chains; classic fabrication.
- **Rely on it?** ❌ **NO.** Use only the authentic texts above.

## 9. Important "People" (Beings) Profiled

**Iblis (Ash-Shaytan ar-Rajim):**
- **Substance:** Fire (a jinn) — Qur'an 18:50; 7:12.
- **Status before the fall:** An intense worshipper who dwelt among the angels in the heavens.
- **The sin:** Arrogance (*kibr*) + envy (*hasad*) + objecting to Allah's command with his own logic.
- **The fall:** Expelled, cursed, named *Ash-Shaytan ar-Rajim* (the accursed/expelled Satan).
- **His nature now:** The sworn enemy of mankind (2:208; 35:6), the whisperer (*al-Waswas*), the leader of the *shayatin*. He has *descendants* (18:50) who continue his work.
- **His end:** Granted respite till the appointed Day (Episode 05), after which Hell is his eternal abode (7:18; 38:85).

**Adam (introduced here, profiled fully in Season 02):** The honoured creation — fashioned by Allah's hand, given the soul, taught "the names of all things" (2:31), the one before whom angels bowed. The object of Iblis's envy and the father of humanity.

## 11. Important "Objects" / Concepts

- **The prostration of honour (*sujud at-tahiyyah*):** A bow of greeting/respect permitted to honour Adam by Allah's command — distinct from *sujud al-'ibadah* (worship), which is for Allah alone. (Later, worship-prostration to anyone but Allah was forbidden entirely.)
- **The two clays vs. fire:** Adam's clay (humble, fertile, stable) vs. Iblis's fire (volatile, proud, destructive) — a symbolic contrast the episode turns on.
- **The title *Ar-Rajim* ("the expelled/accursed"):** Born in this event; embedded in *isti'adhah* (A'udhu billahi min ash-shaytan ir-rajim).

## 12. Lessons

1. **Arrogance is the original sin — and the deadliest.** Not lust, not greed: *kibr*. It barred Iblis from mercy and bars an "atom's weight" of it from Paradise (Muslim 91). Guard the heart first.
2. **Worship without humility is hollow.** Iblis worshipped for ages, yet one proud refusal erased it. The *state of the heart* outweighs the *quantity of deeds*.
3. **Never argue with Allah's command using your own logic.** "It doesn't make sense to me" was Iblis's exact reasoning. Submission means trusting the Wise Commander. *Sami'na wa ata'na* — "we hear and we obey" (2:285) — is the angels' way; *"I am better"* is Iblis's way.
4. **Envy destroys the envier.** Iblis's *hasad* of Adam consumed *him*, not Adam. Envy is the fire that burns its own owner.
5. **Belief in God is not enough — submission is.** Iblis believed in Allah and the Last Day, yet died (will die) a *kafir*, because he refused to obey. Faith is surrender, not mere acknowledgment.
6. **Know your enemy.** The being who envied your father Adam, and who forfeited Paradise, now seeks to drag you down with him (out of that same envy). Recognizing his strategy is the first defense (developed in Episode 05).

## 13. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Iblis (Satan) is a fallen angel."**
> ✅ He was **a jinn** (18:50), made of fire, with free will. Angels *cannot* disobey (66:6). The "fallen angel" idea is a Judeo-Christian/Western frame, not the Qur'anic one. He was *among* the angels by worship, not *of* them by nature.

> ❌ **"Iblis sinned out of weakness / a moment of passion."**
> ✅ No — his sin was **deliberate, knowing arrogance** (2:34: "he refused and was arrogant"). He argued *with* Allah. This is the most dangerous kind of sin: rebellion with full knowledge.

> ❌ **"Iblis didn't believe in God."**
> ✅ He spoke with Allah, called Him "Lord," and affirmed the Resurrection (15:36-39). His *kufr* was the disbelief of **refusal**, not of ignorance — proving that knowing about God without obeying Him is not faith.

> ❌ **"Allah created Iblis evil / forced him to sin."**
> ✅ Allah created him with **free will**; the evil was Iblis's *choice*. Allah's foreknowledge of the choice did not compel it. (Qadr, see Episode 01 §5.6.) Allah does not wrong His creation (4:40); Iblis wronged himself.

> ❌ **"Prostrating to Adam means worshipping Adam — so this contradicts tawhid."**
> ✅ It was a prostration of **honour/greeting by Allah's command**, not worship. Worship was, is, and always will be for Allah alone. Obeying Allah's command to honour Adam *was itself* an act of submission to Allah.

> ❌ **"Iblis can be redeemed / will repent eventually."**
> ✅ No. He chose defiance knowingly, then *doubled down* by vowing to misguide humanity (Episode 05). He is cursed until the Day of Recompense and his end is Hell (7:18; 38:85).

## 14. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: If Iblis was a jinn, why was he commanded along with the angels?**
A: Because he had risen, through worship, to dwell among them — so the command included him. 18:50 clarifies his true nature. His refusal then exposed the difference between *being among* the angels and *being one of* them.

**Q: Why did Allah create Iblis if He knew he'd rebel?**
A: For wisdom we partly understand: Iblis is the *test* that gives human choice meaning. Without an enemy and temptation, there is no struggle, no moral achievement, no genuine submission. Allah's wisdom ("I know what you do not know," 2:30) encompasses what we can't fully see. Iblis chose his path freely; the responsibility is his.

**Q: Was prostrating to Adam shirk?**
A: No. It was honour-prostration commanded by Allah, like the prostration of Yusuf's family to him later (12:100). Worship-prostration is only ever for Allah.

**Q: Did Iblis repent or try to?**
A: Never sincerely. Instead of repenting (as Adam later would, 7:23), he blamed Allah ("because You misled me," 7:16) and vowed revenge on humanity. Contrast this with Adam's humility — that contrast is the heart of Season 02.

**Q: Is Iblis the same as "Shaytan"?**
A: Iblis is *the* Shaytan — the chief. "Shaytan/shayatin" also refers to the rebellious jinn (and evil humans) who follow his way. Iblis is the individual; shaytan is also a category.

**Q: Where is Iblis now?**
A: Alive, by the respite Allah granted him until the Day (Episode 05), actively working to misguide. His final, eternal abode is Hell (38:85).

**Q: What exactly is the difference between Iblis's sin and Adam's later sin?**
A: Both disobeyed. But Iblis sinned through **arrogance** and then **justified and persisted**; Adam sinned through **forgetfulness/weakness** and then **repented humbly**. The sin is not what defined their fates — the *response* did. This is the single most important lesson bridging Episode 04 and Season 02.

## 15. The Theological Debate: Was Iblis an Angel or a Jinn?

Because this is historically debated, here is the structured analysis the encyclopedia promises:

**Opinion 1 — Iblis was a JINN (the correct, majority view).**
- **Evidence:** Qur'an 18:50 explicitly: "*he was of the jinn*"; Muslim 2996 (angels=light, jinn=fire) with Iblis's own statement that he was made of *fire* (7:12); angels cannot disobey (66:6).
- **Scholars:** Al-Hasan al-Basri, Ibn Kathir, and the majority of later verifiers.
- **Strength:** **Strongest** — rests on an explicit verse and harmonizes all texts.

**Opinion 2 — Iblis was originally an ANGEL (minority view).**
- **Evidence:** His *inclusion* in the command "to the angels"; some athar from early figures (Ibn 'Abbas via certain chains).
- **Scholars:** Some early exegetes.
- **Weakness:** Contradicts the explicit 18:50; requires explaining away the nature of angels; the inclusion is already explained by his dwelling among them.

> 📖 **Conclusion (strongest by evidence):** Iblis was a **jinn**. The verse 18:50 is decisive (*nass*), and an explicit text overrides inference. He was commanded *with* the angels because of his rank-by-worship, not his nature. This is the position adopted throughout this encyclopedia.
