# Season 01 · Episode 01
# Before Time Existed
### Allah, Eternity, and the Architecture of Reality Before Creation

> *"He is the First and the Last, the Ascendant and the Intimate, and He is, of all things, Knowing."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Hadid (57:3)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This is the foundation stone of the entire encyclopedia. Before we can understand Adam, Nuh, Ibrahim, or the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, we must understand the One who created them — and the reality that existed *before* anything we call "the universe" existed at all.

This chapter is unusual among all the others, because it deals with a time **before time**. There are no maps here, no empires, no tribes, no cities — because none of them existed yet. Instead, our "geography" is the unseen (*al-ghayb*), and our "sources" are exclusively the Qur'an and authentic Sunnah, because **no human being witnessed this and no human reasoning can reach it.** Where we step beyond clear evidence, this chapter will stop and say so.

> 📖 **A note on method:** In matters of the unseen (*ghayb*) and the Divine Names and Attributes, mainstream Sunni belief (the way of the *Salaf*, the first three generations) is to **affirm what Allah affirmed for Himself, without distortion (tahrif), without denial (ta'til), without asking "how" (takyif), and without comparison to creation (tamthil)**. We believe; we do not philosophize beyond the text. This single principle will save us from a thousand errors.

## 1. Episode Summary

Before there was a universe, before there was time, before there was a single atom of creation — there was **Allah**, and nothing alongside Him. He did not come into existence; He simply **is**, eternally, with no beginning and no end. He was not lonely, not incomplete, not in need of anything. Creation added nothing to Him.

Then, by His will and His knowledge and His decree, He chose to create. This chapter explores everything authentic sources tell us about that "moment" and the structures of reality that frame all of existence:

- **Allah's eternal existence** before all things, and the nature of His Names and Attributes.
- **The first written decree** — when Allah commanded the Pen (*Al-Qalam*) to write everything that would ever happen, fifty thousand years before the heavens and earth.
- **The Throne ('Arsh)** above the water, the **Kursi**, the **Preserved Tablet (Al-Lawh al-Mahfuz)**, and **Divine Decree (Al-Qadr)**.
- **The order of creation** — what was made first (the Pen, the Throne, water) and the scholarly differences about it.
- The creation, in their own categories, of **the angels** (from light), **the jinn** (from smokeless fire), and the appearance of **Iblis**.
- **Time itself** as a created thing.

This is the stage being set. The actors — angels, jinn, Iblis, and finally humanity — enter in the chapters that follow.

## 3. Background: What "Existed" Before Creation?

This is the only chapter in the encyclopedia with no political background, no economy, no civilizations — because there was nothing and no one but Allah. So the "background" here is **theological**: who is the Being from whom everything flows?

### 3.1 The Most Important Truth: Allah Has No Beginning

Everything you have ever seen began at some point. You began. Your parents began. The sun, the mountains, the oceans, the galaxies — all began. The deepest truth of Islam is that **the chain of "things that began" must end at a Being who did not begin.** That Being is Allah.

> ✅ **Surah Al-Hadid (57:3)**
>
> **Arabic:** هُوَ الْأَوَّلُ وَالْآخِرُ وَالظَّاهِرُ وَالْبَاطِنُ ۖ وَهُوَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Huwa al-Awwalu wa al-Akhiru wa az-Zahiru wa al-Batinu, wa Huwa bikulli shay'in 'Alim.*
>
> **Translation:** "He is the First and the Last, the Most High and the Most Near, and He has knowledge of all things."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** The Prophet ﷺ himself explained these four names in a famous supplication: *Al-Awwal* (the First) — there is **nothing before** Him; *Al-Akhir* (the Last) — there is **nothing after** Him; *Az-Zahir* (the Most High / Manifest) — there is **nothing above** Him; *Al-Batin* (the Most Near / Hidden) — there is **nothing beyond** Him. (This explanation is from **Sahih Muslim 2713**.) In four words, Allah locates Himself outside of time (no before, no after) and outside of space (nothing above, nothing beyond). He frames all of reality without being contained by any of it.

### 3.2 Allah Was Not Lonely, Not in Need

A common human assumption is that Allah created because He was lonely or needed worship. Islam firmly rejects this. Allah is *Al-Ghani* — the Self-Sufficient, the One free of all need.

> ✅ **Surah Fatir (35:15)**
>
> **Arabic:** يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ أَنتُمُ الْفُقَرَاءُ إِلَى اللَّهِ ۖ وَاللَّهُ هُوَ الْغَنِيُّ الْحَمِيدُ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Ya ayyuha an-nasu antumu al-fuqara'u ila Allah, wa Allahu Huwa al-Ghaniyyu al-Hamid.*
>
> **Translation:** "O mankind, you are those in need of Allah, while Allah is the Free of need, the Praiseworthy."
>
> **Tafsir:** The relationship is entirely one-directional. *We* need Him for our every breath; *He* needs nothing from us. Our worship benefits **us**, not Him. In a sacred narration (hadith qudsi), Allah says that even if the first and the last of mankind and jinn were all as pious as the most righteous heart among them, it would add nothing to His dominion; and if all were as wicked as the worst heart, it would take nothing away (**Sahih Muslim 2577**, *Authentic*). Creation, then, is an act of pure wisdom and generosity, not of need.

### 3.3 So *Why* Did Allah Create?

Allah tells us the purpose plainly:

> ✅ **Surah Adh-Dhariyat (51:56)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَمَا خَلَقْتُ الْجِنَّ وَالْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Wa ma khalaqtu al-jinna wa al-insa illa liya'budun.*
>
> **Translation:** "And I did not create the jinn and mankind except to worship Me."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** "Worship" ('*ibadah*) here means to know Him, single Him out, and obey Him willingly. Ibn 'Abbas (may Allah be pleased with him) explained *liya'budun* as *liya'rifun* — "so that they would **know** Me." The purpose of existence is to know and worship the One who has no need of us — a gift of meaning, offered to beings who began from nothing.

There is also a beautiful, complementary explanation in a hadith qudsi:

> ☑️ **A treasured concept (the "Hidden Treasure"):** A famous saying circulates — *"I was a Hidden Treasure and I loved to be known, so I created the creation that I might be known."* ❌ **Important:** Despite its popularity, **hadith scholars classify this as having no authentic chain (it is not established as a hadith).** It is frequently quoted in Sufi literature. **Do not attribute it to the Prophet ﷺ.** The *authentic* anchor for the same idea is the verse and athar above (51:56). *Labelled here precisely because it is so widely (and wrongly) quoted.*

## 4. The Names and Attributes of Allah (The "Who" of This Chapter)

Since this whole chapter is about Allah-before-creation, we must understand *who* He is, according to mainstream Sunni theology (the creed of the Salaf, as systematized by scholars like at-Tahawi).

### 4.1 Tawhid — The Absolute Oneness

Allah is **One** (*Ahad*) in a way nothing else is one. He is not one-of-a-kind in a category; He is utterly unique with no category at all.

> ✅ **Surah Al-Ikhlas (112:1-4)** — described by the Prophet ﷺ as equal to a third of the Qur'an (**Sahih al-Bukhari 5015**).
>
> **Arabic:** قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ ۝ اللَّهُ الصَّمَدُ ۝ لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ ۝ وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Qul Huwa Allahu Ahad. Allahu as-Samad. Lam yalid wa lam yulad. Wa lam yakun lahu kufuwan ahad.*
>
> **Translation:** "Say: He is Allah, the One. Allah, the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is born. Nor is there to Him any equivalent."
>
> **Tafsir (as-Sa'di):** This surah is the very definition of *tawhid*. *Ahad* — uniquely One. *As-Samad* — the Master upon whom all creation depends while He depends on none; the One who is sought for every need. *Lam yalid wa lam yulad* — He has no son, no father, no offspring; this refutes the claims of those who attributed children to God. *Lam yakun lahu kufuwan ahad* — nothing in existence is comparable, equal, or similar to Him in any way.

Sunni theology distinguishes three dimensions of this Oneness (the categories of *Tawhid*):

| Category | Meaning | Simple example |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Tawhid ar-Rububiyyah** | Oneness of Lordship — He alone creates, sustains, controls | Only Allah gives life and death |
| **Tawhid al-Uluhiyyah** | Oneness of Worship — He alone deserves worship | We pray only to Him |
| **Tawhid al-Asma' wa's-Sifat** | Oneness of Names & Attributes — His names are unique to Him | His "hearing" is unlike our hearing |

### 4.2 The Beautiful Names (Al-Asma' al-Husna)

> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:180):** "And to Allah belong the best names, so invoke Him by them."

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 2736 / Sahih Muslim 2677** — *Authentic:* "Allah has ninety-nine names, one hundred minus one; whoever **enumerates** them (*ahsaha* — knows them, believes in them, and acts by them) will enter Paradise."

A crucial point of belief: the "ninety-nine" is **not** a cap on His names. In an authentic supplication, the Prophet ﷺ asked Allah by *"every name that You have named Yourself, or revealed in Your Book, or taught to any of Your creation, or **kept hidden in the knowledge of the unseen with Yourself**"* (**Musnad Ahmad 3712**, *graded authentic by scholars*). So Allah has names known only to Him — fitting for a chapter about what existed before us.

### 4.3 The Foundational Rule for Attributes

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shura (42:11)**
>
> **Arabic:** لَيْسَ كَمِثْلِهِ شَيْءٌ ۖ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Laysa kamithlihi shay', wa Huwa as-Sami'u al-Basir.*
>
> **Translation:** "There is nothing like unto Him, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing."
>
> **Tafsir & creed:** This single verse is the master-key of Sunni theology on the Attributes. In one breath it both **affirms** that Allah hears and sees, and **negates** any resemblance to creation. So when texts say Allah has a "Hand," "rises over the Throne (istiwa)," "descends," "is pleased," or "is angry" — we **affirm these as real, in a manner befitting His majesty**, while denying that they resemble our hands, our anger, or our motion. Imam Malik's timeless answer about *istiwa'* (Allah's rising over the Throne) summarizes the entire approach: *"The rising-over is known; the 'how' is unknown; belief in it is obligatory; and asking about it is an innovation."* 🕰️ *(Reported from Imam Malik; well-established athar.)*

This is why we do **not** ask *how* Allah existed before time, *how* He created without effort, or *how* He is above the Throne yet near to us. We affirm what He told us and surrender the "how."

## 5. The Architecture of the Unseen (The "Where" of This Chapter)

Before creation of the universe, Allah brought into being several grand realities. These are our "geography." Each is established by clear text.

### 5.1 The Throne (Al-'Arsh) — The Greatest of Created Things

The 'Arsh is the mightiest created object — a real, immense throne above the seven heavens, above which Allah is established in a way that befits Him (*istiwa'*).

> ✅ **Surah Taha (20:5):** *"Ar-Rahmanu 'ala al-'Arshi istawa"* — "The Most Merciful, over the Throne, rose/established Himself."

> ✅ **Surah Al-Mu'minun (23:86):** "Say: Who is the Lord of the seven heavens and Lord of the Great Throne (*al-'Arsh al-'Azim*)?"

The scale is almost unimaginable. The Prophet ﷺ described the relationship of the seven heavens to the Kursi, and the Kursi to the Throne:

> ☑️ **Reported by Abu Dharr (in Ibn Hibban, al-Bayhaqi, etc.):** "The seven heavens compared to the Kursi are like a ring thrown into a vast desert; and the superiority of the Throne over the Kursi is like the superiority of that desert over the ring." — *graded authentic (sahih) by a number of scholars including al-Albani.* ✅/☑️

### 5.2 The Kursi (The Footstool)

The Kursi is a distinct, immense creation — larger than the heavens and earth, yet smaller than the Throne.

> ✅ **Ayat al-Kursi, Surah Al-Baqarah (2:255):** *"...wasi'a kursiyyuhu as-samawati wa al-ard..."* — "His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Ibn 'Abbas reported that the Kursi is the **place of the two Feet** (footstool), while the Throne ('Arsh) is greater still, and none can estimate its size except Allah. 🕰️ *(athar of Ibn 'Abbas)*. The verse's point is overwhelming majesty: the entire cosmos sits within His Kursi, which is itself dwarfed by His Throne — and **guarding all of it does not weary Him in the slightest.**

### 5.3 Water — The Throne Was Upon It

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:7):** *"...wa kana 'arshuhu 'ala al-ma'..."* — "...and His Throne had been upon the water..."

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3191** — *Authentic.* A delegation from Yemen (Banu Tamim) came to the Prophet ﷺ asking about the beginning of creation. He said: **"There was Allah, and there was nothing else besides Him, and His Throne was over the water. He wrote everything in the Book (the Preserved Tablet), and He created the heavens and the earth."**
>
> **Explanation:** This is the single most important hadith in this chapter. It establishes (a) Allah's eternal priority — nothing existed with Him; (b) the Throne over water before the heavens/earth; and (c) the written decree. Note: this water is part of creation; it is not eternal alongside Allah.

### 5.4 The Pen (Al-Qalam) and The First Decree

> ✅ **Jami' at-Tirmidhi 2155 / Sunan Abi Dawud 4700** — *graded authentic (sahih).* The Prophet ﷺ said: **"The first thing Allah created was the Pen. He said to it: 'Write.' It said: 'My Lord, what shall I write?' He said: 'Write the decree of everything until the Hour comes.'"**
>
> **Explanation:** This is the engine of *al-Qadr* (Divine Decree). Before a single galaxy spun, the entire script of all existence — every event, every choice, every leaf that would fall — was already written.

> ✅ **Sahih Muslim 2653** — *Authentic.* "Allah wrote down the decrees of creation **fifty thousand years before He created the heavens and the earth**, and His Throne was upon the water."

> ⚠️ **A famous tension (and how scholars resolve it):** The Tirmidhi hadith says "the first thing Allah created was the **Pen**," while the Bukhari hadith implies the **Throne** (over water) was already there. Which was truly first? Three scholarly positions:
> - **Opinion 1 — The Throne was first.** Held by many, including Ibn Taymiyyah and Ibn al-Qayyim. They read *"the first thing Allah created was the Pen"* as **"the first thing created *after the Throne*,"** or as "the first of *this category* of creation." *Evidence:* the Bukhari hadith and verses describing the Throne over water. *Strength:* strong; preferred by many *muhaqqiqin*.
> - **Opinion 2 — The Pen was first, absolutely.** Held by some, reading the Tirmidhi hadith literally. *Strength:* literal but harder to reconcile with the Throne texts.
> - **Opinion 3 — "First" is relative, not absolute, in each text.** "First" means first *of its kind / role* in each context (the Pen first to *write*, the Throne first as a *created structure*). *Strength:* harmonizes all texts; favored by many.
>
> 📖 **Conclusion:** The **strongest and most reconciling view** is that the **Throne** preceded, and the **Pen** was the first thing commanded to *write the decree*. But the matter is one of the unseen; certainty on the exact micro-order is not required for faith. What is binding: **all of these preceded the heavens, earth, and mankind.**

### 5.5 The Preserved Tablet (Al-Lawh al-Mahfuz)

The Pen wrote, and what it wrote is preserved in *Al-Lawh al-Mahfuz* — the master record of all decree.

> ✅ **Surah Al-Buruj (85:21-22):** *"Bal huwa Qur'anun majid, fi lawhin mahfuz"* — "Rather, it is a glorious Qur'an, [inscribed] in a Preserved Tablet."

> ✅ **Surah Al-Hadid (57:22):** "No disaster strikes upon the earth or among yourselves except that it is in a register (*kitab*) **before We bring it into being** — indeed that, for Allah, is easy."

### 5.6 Divine Decree (Al-Qadr) — The Sixth Pillar of Faith

Everything above culminates in *al-Qadr*: the belief that Allah knew, wrote, willed, and created all that happens. It is the **sixth pillar of iman**.

> ✅ **The Hadith of Jibril, Sahih Muslim 8** — *Authentic.* When the angel Jibril asked the Prophet ﷺ about faith (*iman*), he replied: "It is to believe in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, **and to believe in the decree (*al-qadar*), both its good and its bad.**"

The four levels of belief in Qadr (a precise framework scholars derive from the texts):
1. **'Ilm** — Allah's eternal **knowledge** of all things.
2. **Kitabah** — His **writing** of it (the Pen / the Tablet).
3. **Mashi'ah** — His **will**: nothing happens except by it.
4. **Khalq** — His **creating** of all things, including our actions.

> 📖 **The balance to hold:** Belief in Qadr does **not** cancel human responsibility. Allah created us with real choice (*kasb*) and will judge us by it. We are commanded to *act*, then trust the decree — not to use the decree as an excuse for sin (the Prophet ﷺ rebuked that reasoning). This is fully unpacked in Season 02 (Adam) and later in the human story. For now: the script was written, **and** our pen of accountability writes within it.

## 6. Time Itself Was Created

A subtle but profound point: **time is a creation.** Allah is not *inside* time waiting for events; He created time along with the universe. Concepts like "before," "after," "fifty thousand years," and "six days" are measures meaningful *to creation*. Allah is *Al-Awwal* and *Al-Akhir* — beyond the very sequence He authored.

> 📖 This is why questions like *"What was Allah doing before He created the universe?"* are, strictly, malformed — "before" is a feature of the created timeline. The honest, text-based answer is the hadith of Bukhari: *"There was Allah, and nothing else with Him."* The Prophet ﷺ also warned against probing endlessly into "what was before" until one is led to doubt; he directed us instead to **seek refuge in Allah and move on** (meaning of **Sahih al-Bukhari 3276 / Sahih Muslim 134**, *Authentic*).

## 7. The Order of Creation (Six "Days") — Scholarly Discussion

After the Throne, Pen, Tablet, and water, Allah created the heavens and the earth in **six "days"** (*ayyam*).

> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:54):** "Indeed your Lord is Allah, who created the heavens and the earth in six days, then established Himself above the Throne."
> ✅ **Surah Fussilat (41:9-12)** details the phases: the earth in two "days," its provisions and mountains, then turning to the heaven (which was *smoke* — *dukhan*) and forming seven heavens.

**What is a "day" (yawm) here?**
- **Opinion 1:** Ordinary 24-hour days. *Scholars:* some classical exegetes. *Strength:* literal, but "day" is hard to define before the sun existed.
- **Opinion 2:** "Day" = a phase/era of unknown length, since the **sun (the measure of our day) was created within the process.** *Scholars:* many, noting Allah elsewhere mentions "days" of differing lengths (e.g., 50,000 years in 70:4; 1,000 years in 22:47, 32:5). *Strength:* strong and widely held.

📖 **Conclusion:** The exact length is *ghayb*; both views are within orthodoxy. The certainty is **six divinely ordered phases**, ending with *istiwa'* over the Throne. (Note also: the **order** in 41:9-12 (earth then heaven) vs. 79:27-30 (heaven then earth's "spreading, *dahaha*") is reconciled by scholars as creation vs. *preparation/spreading* — not a contradiction.)

Other scholars defended it. **Verdict for our encyclopedia:** treat the *Qur'anic "six days"* as the firm anchor; treat the day-by-day weekly breakdown as **disputed**, not a basis for doctrine. *This is exactly the kind of labelling this project requires.*

## 9. Weak & Non-Authentic Narrations (Clearly Labelled)

These appear constantly in popular books and online posts about "before creation." They are **not** reliable. We list them precisely so you can recognize and avoid them.

---
**⚠️ Weak Narration 1 — "The Hadith of the Veils / The Light of Muhammad created first" (Nur Muhammadi)**

- **Narration:** That the very first thing Allah created was the *light of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ* from His own light, and everything else was made from that light.
- **Source:** Attributed via Jabir (the "Hadith of Jabir") in late, unreliable compilations.
- **Why scholars consider it weak/fabricated:** It has **no authentic chain**; it contradicts the authentic Tirmidhi hadith ("the first thing created was the Pen") and the Bukhari hadith. Major scholars classify the Jabir version as **baseless (la asla lahu).**
- **Should it be relied upon?** ❌ **NO.** The Prophet ﷺ is the greatest of creation and the best of mankind — but he was a created human (*"Say: I am only a man like you, to whom it is revealed..."* — 18:110), born to 'Abdullah and Aminah. Do not believe he was a pre-eternal light from which the universe was made.
---
**⚠️ Weak Narration 2 — "I was a Hidden Treasure..." (Kuntu kanzan makhfiyyan)**

- **Narration:** "I was a Hidden Treasure and loved to be known, so I created creation to be known."
- **Source:** Widely quoted in Sufi works; **no isnad to the Prophet ﷺ.**
- **Why weak:** Hadith masters (Ibn Taymiyyah, al-'Asqalani, as-Suyuti) state it is **not an authentic hadith.**
- **Should it be relied upon?** ❌ **NO** — not as a hadith. The *meaning* that Allah is to be known is supported by **Qur'an 51:56**, so quote *that* instead.
---
**⚠️ Weak Narration 3 — Detailed "what the Pen wrote" / cosmic dialogues**

- **Narration:** Long elaborate stories of conversations between the Pen, the Tablet, the angels, and detailed descriptions of pre-creation events.
- **Source:** Mostly **Isra'iliyyat** (Jewish/Christian lore via Ka'b al-Ahbar, Wahb ibn Munabbih) and storyteller (*qussas*) embellishments in some tafsir.
- **Why weak:** No sound chains to the Prophet ﷺ; the unseen cannot be known except by revelation.
- **Should it be relied upon?** ❌ **NO.** Accept only what the Qur'an and authentic Sunnah state; for the rest, the correct posture is *"Allah knows best."*
---

> 🧭 **Principle to internalize:** In matters before creation, **the absence of evidence is itself the answer.** When a detailed story has no sound chain, the scholarly and safe response is silence, not speculation.

## 10. Historical & Scholarly Sources

Since no human witnessed this era, our "sources" are **revelation first**, then the scholars who systematized belief from it:

- **Primary (revelation):** The Qur'an; Sahih al-Bukhari; Sahih Muslim; the Sunan (Tirmidhi, Abu Dawud, etc.).
- **Creed (Aqidah):** *Al-'Aqidah at-Tahawiyyah* by Imam at-Tahawi (d. 321 AH) — the gold-standard concise Sunni creed; *Sharh al-'Aqidah at-Tahawiyyah* by Ibn Abi al-'Izz; works of Ibn Taymiyyah (*Al-'Aqidah al-Wasitiyyah*) and Ibn al-Qayyim.
- **Tafsir:** Ibn Kathir, at-Tabari, al-Qurtubi, as-Sa'di.
- **Topical:** *The World of the Angels*, *The World of the Jinn*, and *Divine Decree (al-Qadar)* from the **Islamic Creed Series by Dr. Umar Sulaiman al-Ashqar** — excellent, accessible, evidence-based modern works directly relevant to Season 01.
- **History framing:** Ibn Kathir, *Al-Bidayah wa'n-Nihayah* (Vol. 1 opens precisely with "the beginning of creation").

## 13. Important "Objects" / Concepts of Material(less) Culture

- **The Pen and the Tablet** — the "writing instruments" of destiny (not literal ink and paper as we know them; affirmed without "how").
- **The six days/phases** — the divine schedule of cosmic construction.
- **Light and Fire** — the raw "materials" of angels and jinn respectively.
- **Smoke (*dukhan*)** — the primordial state of the heaven before it was formed (41:11) — a point modern readers sometimes connect to cosmology (see §15).

## 14. Lessons (Spiritual, Theological, Personal)

1. **You are not an accident.** Your existence was written, by name, fifty thousand years before the stars. You were *intended*.
2. **Allah needs nothing from you — yet He invites you.** Worship is a gift to *you*, a way to know the One who authored reality.
3. **Mercy is woven into the foundation.** The first thing Allah wrote above the Throne was: *"My mercy prevails over My wrath"* (Bukhari 3194). The universe runs on mercy, not anger.
4. **Trust the Decree, then act.** Knowing everything is written should produce *peace*, not paralysis. We plan, we strive, we make du'a — and we rest in the knowledge that the Author is the Most Wise and Most Merciful.
5. **Humility before the unseen.** The most learned answer to the deepest questions is often *"Allah knows best."* True knowledge includes knowing the limits of knowledge.
6. **Guard your faith from speculation.** The Prophet ﷺ taught us to seek refuge and move on when the mind spirals into "what was before the before." Faith is anchored in revelation, not in endless conjecture.

## 15. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Islam says God created the universe in six literal 24-hour days."**
> ✅ The Qur'an says six *ayyam* (days/phases). Since the sun was created *within* the process, many scholars hold these are eras of unknown length. The Qur'an itself uses "day" for spans of 1,000 and 50,000 years. So this is *not* a fixed dogma of 144 hours.

> ❌ **"Allah created because He was lonely / needed worship."**
> ✅ Allah is *Al-Ghani* (Self-Sufficient). Creation adds nothing to Him and removes nothing from Him (Muslim 2577). He created out of wisdom and generosity.

> ❌ **"The first thing created was the Light of Muhammad ﷺ."**
> ✅ Baseless narration. The authentic text says the Pen (Tirmidhi 2155); the Throne was already over water (Bukhari 3191). The Prophet ﷺ was a created human (18:110).

> ❌ **"The 'Big Bang' / cosmic smoke proves the Qur'an is a science book."**
> ✅ Be careful and balanced. The Qur'an mentions the heaven was *dukhan* (smoke) before being formed (41:11) and speaks of the heavens and earth being "joined then parted" (21:30). These are *spiritually* striking and worth reflecting on — but the Qur'an is a book of **guidance, not a physics textbook**. Avoid forcing every scientific theory onto verses (and avoid the opposite error of mockery). State the verse, note the resonance, and don't overclaim. *(Balanced scholarly position.)*

> ❌ **"Belief in Qadr means humans have no free will and aren't responsible."**
> ✅ Wrong. Allah created us *with* real choice and commands us to act; using Qadr as an excuse for sin was explicitly rejected by the Prophet ﷺ. Knowledge of an outcome is not coercion of it.

> ❌ **"We can describe *how* Allah existed/created before time."**
> ✅ We cannot, and we shouldn't try. We affirm *that* He did (the texts), and surrender the *how* (Malik's rule on *istiwa'*).

## 16. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was there before Allah created everything?**
A: Only Allah. *"There was Allah, and nothing else besides Him"* (Bukhari 3191). And strictly, "before" is a created concept — Allah is *Al-Awwal*, with no before.

**Q: What was Allah "doing" before creation?**
A: The question assumes time existed, which it didn't. The Prophet ﷺ steered us away from this spiral. The safe answer: Allah is beyond time; He is perfect and self-sufficient eternally.

**Q: If everything is already written, why try?**
A: Because *trying* is also written, and you are judged by your effort and intention. Allah hid the decree from you precisely so you would *choose*. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Work, for everyone is facilitated toward that for which he was created" (**Sahih al-Bukhari 4949**, *Authentic*) — and then commanded us to *strive*.

**Q: Why isn't the exact order of creation (Pen vs. Throne) clear?**
A: Because it's *ghayb*, and certainty on it isn't required for faith. The texts give us enough to believe rightly; the rest is "Allah knows best."

**Q: Did angels and jinn exist before humans?**
A: Yes. Both categories were created before Adam. The jinn, in particular, lived on earth before mankind (detailed in Episode 03). Iblis was among them (Episode 04).

**Q: Is the Throne a literal throne? Is Allah literally "sitting"?**
A: The Throne is a real, immense creation. Allah's *istiwa'* (rising/establishing) over it is affirmed as real, *but unlike any created sitting or settling* — "There is nothing like unto Him" (42:11). We affirm without picturing.

**Q: Where was Allah before He created the Throne and the heavens?**
A: This too is *ghayb*. In a hadith, the Prophet ﷺ was asked "Where was our Lord before He created creation?" and answered with the meaning that *He was in a state without anything beside Him* (referencing the report in **Tirmidhi 3109**, debated grading). The firm answer remains Bukhari 3191. We do not assign Him a "place" in the created sense.
