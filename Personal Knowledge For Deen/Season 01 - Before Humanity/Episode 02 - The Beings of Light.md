# Season 01 · Episode 02
# The Beings of Light
### The Angels (Al-Mala'ikah) — Their Creation, Nature, Ranks, and Roles

> *"They do not disobey Allah in what He commands them, and they do whatever they are commanded."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah At-Tahrim (66:6)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

In Episode 01 we stood before creation itself — Allah alone, the Throne, the Pen, the decree. Now the unseen begins to fill with **inhabitants**. The first beings we meet are the **angels** (*al-mala'ikah*): the luminous, obedient servants of Allah who run the machinery of existence — carrying revelation, recording deeds, taking souls, guarding hell and tending paradise, and surrounding the Throne in worship.

Belief in the angels is **the second of the six pillars of faith** (after belief in Allah). To deny them is to step outside Islam. So this chapter is not optional background — it is core creed (*aqidah*).

> 📖 **Method reminder:** Everything about angels is *ghayb* (unseen). We affirm exactly what the Qur'an and authentic Sunnah state — no more, no less. Where popular culture or weak narrations have added details, this chapter labels and removes them.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

The angels are a vast creation of Allah, made **from light (*nur*)**, before humanity. They are not male or female, do not eat or drink, do not tire, and — crucially — **have no free will to disobey.** Obedience is their nature, the way fire's nature is to burn.

They are not a small group; they are **immense in number** — so many that a structure in the heavens called *al-Bayt al-Ma'mur* is entered by seventy thousand angels every single day, never to return. They vary enormously in size, rank, and role. Some carry the Throne; some bring revelation to prophets; some record every word a human speaks; some take souls at death; some are charged with the mountains, the rain, the wombs, paradise, and hell.

This chapter covers their creation, their nature, the **named angels** and their duties, their ranks, their relationship with human beings, the rewards of loving them, and the misconceptions (especially the false idea — held by some pagan Arabs and rejected by the Qur'an — that angels are "daughters of God").

This episode also quietly sets up the great drama to come: among the dwellers of the heavens was one who *looked* like he belonged with the angels but was not one of them — **Iblis** (Episodes 03-04). To understand why his refusal was so shocking, we must first understand how *naturally and completely* the true angels obey.

## 3. Background: Why Angels Matter to Everything That Follows

Every major event in the rest of this entire encyclopedia involves angels:

- **Revelation** to every prophet — from Adam to Muhammad ﷺ — came through the angel **Jibril** (Gabriel).
- The **Night Journey** (Season 12), **Badr** (Season 13, where angels fought), the **death of every human** (the Angel of Death), and the **Day of Judgement** (Israfil's trumpet) all turn on angels.
- Your *own* life is surrounded by them: two angels record your deeds, others protect you, others observe your prayer.

So understanding the angels here is like learning the cast of characters before the play begins. They are not decoration — they are Allah's active agents in history and in your daily life.

## 4. The Story: Who The Angels Are

### 4.1 Created From Light

> ✅ **Sahih Muslim 2996** — *Authentic.* 'A'ishah (may Allah be pleased with her) reported the Prophet ﷺ said: **"The angels were created from light, the jinn were created from a smokeless flame of fire, and Adam was created from what has been described to you (clay)."**
>
> **Explanation:** This is the foundational hadith of all three categories of creation. The angels' substance is *nur* — light — which beautifully matches their nature: pure, luminous, free of the lower desires that fire (jinn) and clay (humans) carry. Their being is wholly oriented toward Allah.

### 4.2 They Have No Free Will To Disobey

This is the single most important thing to understand about angels — and it is the key that unlocks the entire Iblis drama.

> ✅ **Surah At-Tahrim (66:6):** "...over which are angels, harsh and severe; they do not disobey Allah in what He commands them, but do what they are commanded."

> ✅ **Surah Al-Anbiya (21:19-20):** "To Him belongs whoever is in the heavens and the earth. And those near Him are not too proud to worship Him, nor do they tire. They exalt Him night and day, never slackening."

> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** The angels are in *perpetual, effortless worship*. They do not get bored, do not rebel, do not weigh "should I or shouldn't I." Obedience for them is not a struggle (as it is for us) — it is their very design. This is why, when Iblis refused to bow (Episode 04), the contrast is so stunning: in a realm of total obedience, one being chose pride. It also proves Iblis was **never an angel** — because an angel *cannot* disobey, by definition. (More in Episode 04.)

### 4.3 They Are Neither Male Nor Female

> ✅ **Surah As-Saffat (37:150)** and **Surah Az-Zukhruf (43:19):** The Qur'an condemns those who called the angels "females" or "daughters of Allah," asking: "Did they witness their creation?" Angels have **no gender** in the human sense; assigning them femininity (as the pagan Arabs did) is a fabrication Allah rebukes sharply.

### 4.4 They Do Not Eat or Drink

When angels came to Ibrahim (peace be upon him) as guests in human form, he offered them a roasted calf — and grew uneasy when they did not eat (Surah Hud 11:69-70; Adh-Dhariyat 51:24-28). This confirms angels do not require food. (Full story in Season 05.)

### 4.5 They Have Wings — Of Varying Numbers

> ✅ **Surah Fatir (35:1):** "All praise is for Allah, Originator of the heavens and earth, who made the angels messengers having wings — two or three or four. He increases in creation what He wills."

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3232 / Sahih Muslim 174** — *Authentic.* The Prophet ﷺ saw **Jibril in his true form, having six hundred wings, filling the horizon.** This shows the immense scale and power of the great angels.

### 4.6 They Can Take Human Form

Angels can appear as men, by Allah's permission. Jibril came to Maryam as "a well-proportioned man" (Surah Maryam 19:17), and famously appeared in the **Hadith of Jibril** as a stranger in pure white robes who questioned the Prophet ﷺ about Islam, Iman, and Ihsan, then vanished (**Sahih Muslim 8**). They appeared to Ibrahim and to Lut as handsome young men (Season 05).

## 5. The Named Angels and Their Duties

The Qur'an and authentic Sunnah name and describe specific angels with specific charges. These are the "important people" of this chapter.

| Angel | Role | Key Evidence |
|-------|------|--------------|
| **Jibril (Gabriel)** | The angel of **revelation** — brought Allah's words to all prophets; called *Ar-Ruh al-Amin* (the Trustworthy Spirit) and *Ruh al-Qudus* (the Holy Spirit) | Qur'an 2:97-98; 26:193; 16:102 |
| **Mika'il (Michael)** | Charged with **rain and provision (rizq)**, vegetation | Qur'an 2:98 (named); detailed in tafsir |
| **Israfil** | Will **blow the Trumpet (Sur)** to end the world and resurrect creation | Qur'an 39:68 (the Trumpet); named in hadith |
| **Malik** | The keeper/guardian of **Hell (Jahannam)** | Qur'an 43:77 |
| **Ridwan** | Often cited as the keeper of **Paradise** | ⚠️ *Name not in Qur'an/Sahih explicitly — see note* |
| **Munkar & Nakir** | The two angels who **question the dead** in the grave | Jami' at-Tirmidhi 1071 |
| **The Angel of Death** (popularly "Azra'il") | **Takes souls** at death | Qur'an 32:11 — *"Malak al-Mawt"*; name "Azra'il" not authentic, see note |
| **The Bearers of the Throne** (*Hamalat al-'Arsh*) | **Carry the Throne** of Allah; eight on the Day of Judgement | Qur'an 40:7; 69:17 |
| **Kiraman Katibin** | The "noble recorders" — two angels who **write every deed** | Qur'an 50:17-18; 82:10-12 |
| **The Mu'aqqibat** | Angels who **guard** a person, succeeding one another by night and day | Qur'an 13:11 |
| **Ridwan / guardians of Paradise** | Greet the believers entering Jannah | Qur'an 39:73; 13:23-24 |

> ⚠️ **Naming note (important):**
> - The names **Jibril, Mika'il, Israfil, Malik** are firmly established. **Munkar & Nakir** come from an authentic-grade hadith (Tirmidhi 1071).
> - **"Azra'il"** (for the Angel of Death) and **"Ridwan"** (for the keeper of Paradise) are **commonly used but not established in the Qur'an or rigorously authentic hadith.** The Qur'an simply says *Malak al-Mawt* ("the Angel of Death," 32:11). Use these popular names with that awareness — they are not part of firm creed.

### 5.1 Jibril — The Greatest of the Angels

Jibril holds the highest station among angels. He is the channel of all revelation, the one who descended on *Laylat al-Qadr*, who brought the Qur'an "upon the heart" of the Prophet ﷺ:

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:192-194):** "And indeed, it (the Qur'an) is the revelation of the Lord of the worlds. The Trustworthy Spirit (*Ar-Ruh al-Amin*) brought it down upon your heart, that you may be of the warners."

> ✅ **Surah At-Takwir (81:19-21)** describes him as "a noble messenger, possessing power, with the Owner of the Throne, secure in position, obeyed there and trustworthy." The Prophet ﷺ saw him in his true form twice (Surah An-Najm 53:5-14). Jibril will appear again and again across this encyclopedia — at the cave of Hira (Season 12), at Badr, on the Night Journey, and at the Prophet's final review of the Qur'an.

### 5.2 The Bearers of the Throne — Beings of Staggering Scale

> ☑️ **Sunan Abi Dawud 4727** — *graded sound/hasan by many.* The Prophet ﷺ described one of the angels who bear the Throne: the distance between his earlobe and his shoulder is the span of *seven hundred years* of travel. This conveys a scale beyond human imagining — and the majesty of the One whose Throne they carry.

## 6. The Sheer Number of Angels

The angels are beyond counting. Two authentic texts convey it:

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3207 / Sahih Muslim 164** — *Authentic.* On the Night Journey (*Mi'raj*), the Prophet ﷺ was shown **al-Bayt al-Ma'mur** (the "Much-Frequented House" in the seventh heaven, the heavenly counterpart of the Ka'bah). He was told that **seventy thousand angels enter it every day, and once they leave, they never return** (a new seventy thousand come each day). Imagine the count over the lifetime of the universe.

> ✅ **On the Day of Judgement (Sahih Muslim 2842)** — *Authentic.* Hell will be brought forth that day with **seventy thousand reins, each rein held by seventy thousand angels.** The numbers are deliberately overwhelming — a glimpse of Allah's limitless dominion.

## 7. Qur'anic Revelation — Key Passages on the Angels

> ✅ **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:285)** — *the creed statement:*
>
> **Arabic:** آمَنَ الرَّسُولُ بِمَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِ وَالْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Amana ar-Rasulu bima unzila ilayhi min Rabbihi wa al-mu'minun; kullun amana billahi wa mala'ikatihi wa kutubihi wa rusulih.*
>
> **Translation:** "The Messenger believes in what was revealed to him from his Lord, and so do the believers. Each one believes in Allah, **His angels**, His books, and His messengers..."
>
> **Tafsir:** Belief in the angels is placed right beside belief in Allah Himself — proof that it is foundational, not peripheral.

> ✅ **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:97-98):**
>
> **Translation:** "Say: Whoever is an enemy to Jibril — it is he who brings it (the Qur'an) down upon your heart by Allah's permission... Whoever is an enemy to Allah, His angels, His messengers, **Jibril and Mika'il** — then indeed Allah is an enemy to the disbelievers."
>
> **Context (Asbab al-Nuzul):** Revealed when some Jews of Madinah claimed Jibril was "their enemy" (because he brought punishment/news they disliked) and used this as an excuse to reject the Prophet ﷺ. Allah responds: to be an enemy of Jibril is to be an enemy of Allah. *(Reported in tafsir of Ibn Kathir.)*

> ✅ **Surah Az-Zumar (39:75):** "And you will see the angels surrounding the Throne, exalting their Lord with praise..." — a scene of the angels' eternal worship around the 'Arsh.

> ✅ **Surah Fussilat (41:30):** "Indeed, those who say 'Our Lord is Allah' and then remain steadfast — **the angels descend upon them** (saying): 'Do not fear, nor grieve, but receive the good news of Paradise...'" — angels comfort the believers, especially at death.

## 9. Weak & Non-Authentic Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Weak / Baseless 1 — Elaborate physical descriptions of named angels (faces, colors, detailed biographies)**

- **Narration:** Long descriptions of the appearance, "personalities," and life-stories of Israfil, Mika'il, the Angel of Death, etc.
- **Source:** Mostly **Isra'iliyyat** and storyteller embellishment in some tafsir.
- **Why weak:** No authentic chains; the unseen is known only by revelation.
- **Rely on it?** ❌ **NO.** Affirm only the roles the texts give.
---
**⚠️ Weak / Misattributed 2 — "Azra'il" and "Ridwan" as confirmed names**

- **Narration:** That the Angel of Death is named "Azra'il" and the keeper of Paradise "Ridwan."
- **Source:** Popular usage / Isra'iliyyat; not in Qur'an or rigorously authentic hadith.
- **Why weak:** No firm evidence. The Qur'an says only *Malak al-Mawt* (32:11).
- **Rely on it?** ❌ Not as established creed. You may use the names colloquially, but know they are **not** confirmed.
---
**❌ Fabricated 3 — "Harut and Marut" embellished stories**

- **Narration:** Lurid expanded tales about the two angels mentioned in Surah Al-Baqarah (2:102) — that they sinned, drank wine, committed adultery, were punished by hanging in a well in Babylon, etc.
- **Source:** **Isra'iliyyat** that crept into some tafsir.
- **Why weak/rejected:** The Qur'an mentions Harut and Marut only briefly as a *test* sent regarding magic in Babylon; the lurid additions have **no sound basis** and many contradict the established sinlessness of angels.
- **Rely on it?** ❌ **NO.** Stick to the bare Qur'anic text (2:102): they were a trial, and they warned people, saying *"We are only a trial, so do not disbelieve."*

## 11. Important "People" (The Angels Profiled)

**Jibril (Gabriel):** Highest-ranking angel; bearer of revelation to all prophets; titles *Ar-Ruh al-Amin*, *Ruh al-Qudus*. Has 600 wings. Appears throughout the entire encyclopedia.

**Mika'il (Michael):** Charged with rain, vegetation, and provision (rizq). Paired with Jibril in Qur'an 2:98.

**Israfil:** Will blow the Trumpet (*Sur*) twice — once to end creation, once to resurrect it (Qur'an 39:68). Stands ready, awaiting the command.

**Malik:** The stern guardian of Hell (Qur'an 43:77; 66:6 area).

**The Angel of Death (Malak al-Mawt):** Extracts souls by Allah's command (Qur'an 32:11). Comes gently for believers, harshly for disbelievers (Qur'an 79:1-2 area; tafsir).

**Munkar & Nakir:** Question the deceased in the grave: "Who is your Lord? What is your religion? Who is your Prophet?" (Tirmidhi 1071).

**Kiraman Katibin (the Noble Recorders):** Two angels, one on each shoulder, recording good and bad deeds (Qur'an 50:17-18, 82:10-12). They never miss a word.

**The Bearers of the Throne (Hamalat al-'Arsh):** Carry the 'Arsh; eight on the Day of Judgement (Qur'an 69:17); colossal in size; they also *pray for the believers* (Qur'an 40:7-9).

## 14. Lessons

1. **You are never truly alone.** Recording angels are with you always; guardian angels protect you. Live with the awareness of honourable witnesses (50:18).
2. **Obedience can be beautiful, not burdensome.** The angels show that nearness to Allah produces tireless, joyful worship — a model to aspire toward.
3. **Your deeds echo in the heavens.** A loved servant is announced by Jibril to all the heavens (Bukhari 3209). Dhikr gatherings are encircled by angels (Muslim 2675). The unseen responds to what you do.
4. **Death is not abandonment.** For the believer, angels descend with "do not fear, nor grieve" (41:30). The same beings who recorded your life escort your soul.
5. **Humility before scale.** A single angel can fill the horizon (Jibril). The bearers of the Throne are beyond imagining. How great, then, is the One they serve.
6. **Guard the creed from invention.** Loving and believing in angels means accepting *only* what Allah revealed about them — not the embellishments of folklore.

## 15. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Angels are female / daughters of God."**
> ✅ The Qur'an explicitly condemns this (37:150; 43:19; 53:27). Angels have no gender, and Allah has no children.

> ❌ **"Iblis (Satan) was a fallen angel."**
> ✅ **No.** Iblis was a **jinn**, made of fire, not light (Qur'an 18:50). Angels *cannot* disobey (66:6). This is fully proven in Episode 04. The "fallen angel" idea is a Biblical/Western framing, not the Qur'anic one.

> ❌ **"Angels have free will and some chose evil."**
> ✅ Angels have **no capacity to disobey** (66:6; 21:19-20). Evil among the unseen beings comes from the jinn (who *do* have free will), not the angels.

> ❌ **"The Angel of Death is named Azra'il" / "Ridwan guards Paradise" — as established facts.**
> ✅ These names are popular but **not authentically established.** The Qur'an says only *Malak al-Mawt* (32:11). Use with caution.

> ❌ **"Harut and Marut were two angels who sinned and were punished."**
> ✅ The lurid stories are **Isra'iliyyat** with no sound basis. The Qur'an (2:102) mentions them only as a *test* concerning magic; they *warned* people against disbelief.

> ❌ **"We can draw or depict angels."**
> ✅ Islamic visual tradition (and this encyclopedia's halal-imagery rule) avoids depicting angels. We represent them through light, radiance, and described phenomena — never figural images.

## 16. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: When were the angels created — before or after the jinn?**
A: Both were before Adam. The *exact* order between angels and jinn is not specified in authentic texts; we say "Allah knows best."

**Q: Do angels sleep, eat, marry, or have children?**
A: No to all. They do not eat or drink (shown by the guests of Ibrahim), do not tire or sleep (21:20), have no gender, and do not reproduce.

**Q: Can angels make mistakes or sin?**
A: No. Their defining trait is total, effortless obedience (66:6). This is *the* difference between angels and jinn/humans.

**Q: How many angels are there?**
A: Beyond counting. 70,000 enter al-Bayt al-Ma'mur every day, never returning (Bukhari 3207). Only Allah knows their number.

**Q: Can humans see angels?**
A: Not normally in their true form. The Prophet ﷺ saw Jibril in his true form twice (53:5-14). Angels can take human shape and appear to people by Allah's will (e.g., Hadith of Jibril, the guests of Ibrahim and Lut).

**Q: Do the recording angels write down sins immediately?**
A: A reported mercy (in narrations): the angel on the left delays recording a bad deed, giving the person time to repent; a *good* intention is written immediately, and a good *deed* multiplied (cf. Sahih al-Bukhari 6491 / Muslim 131 on the multiplication of deeds).

**Q: Why didn't Allah just make humans like angels — unable to sin?**
A: Because the *worth* of human worship lies in **choice**. An angel obeying is its nature; a human choosing Allah over temptation is a moral achievement. This is the very theme that the Adam–Iblis story (Episode 04, Season 02) explores.
