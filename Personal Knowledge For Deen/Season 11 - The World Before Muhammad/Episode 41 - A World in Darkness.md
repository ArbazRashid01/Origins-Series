# Season 11 · Episode 41
# A World in Darkness
### Arabia, the Empires, and the Age of Jahiliyyah

> *"You were on the edge of a pit of the Fire, and He saved you from it."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Aal 'Imran (3:103)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Six centuries after Isa, the world awaited the final Messenger he had foretold (61:6). This chapter sets the **stage** for the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ: the state of **Arabia** in the *Jahiliyyah* (the "Age of Ignorance"), the great surrounding empires (**Byzantine Rome** and **Sasanian Persia**), the religious and social condition of the world, the city of **Makkah** and the custodianship of the Ka'bah, the tribe of **Quraysh**, and the dramatic **Year of the Elephant** — the year the Prophet ﷺ was born. It is the essential historical and cultural background for everything in Seasons 11-14.

> 📖 **Method note:** We now enter the *Seerah* (prophetic biography), the best-documented period of this encyclopedia. Sources include the Qur'an, Sahih hadith, and the classical *Seerah* works (Ibn Ishaq/Ibn Hisham, al-Mubarakpuri's *Ar-Raheeq al-Makhtum*). Pre-Islamic history is reconstructed from these plus historical/archaeological records.

## 1. Episode Summary

By the **6th century CE**, the message of pure monotheism brought by all the prophets had been almost entirely lost or distorted across the world. The two **superpowers** — the Christian **Byzantine (Eastern Roman) Empire** and the Zoroastrian **Sasanian Persian Empire** — were locked in endless, exhausting wars, oppressing their peoples with heavy taxes and rigid class systems. Religion had largely become corrupted: idolatry, fire-worship, distorted Christianity, and superstition prevailed; the pure *tawhid* of Ibrahim survived only in scattered individuals (the *hunafa'*).

**Arabia** sat between these empires — a vast, largely desert peninsula of fiercely independent **tribes**. Its condition is summed up in the term ***Jahiliyyah*** ("Ignorance"): not ignorance of facts, but ignorance of *guidance* — a society of both noble virtues (generosity, courage, loyalty, eloquence) and terrible vices (idolatry, tribal warfare, the **burying of infant daughters alive**, the oppression of women and slaves, drunkenness, gambling, usury, and blood-feuds). At the heart of Arabia stood **Makkah** and the **Ka'bah** — built by Ibrahim and Isma'il for the worship of Allah alone (Season 05), but now filled with **360 idols**, its pure purpose buried under generations of *shirk*. The Ka'bah's custodianship lay with the powerful tribe of **Quraysh**, descendants of Isma'il.

The chapter culminates in the **Year of the Elephant (c. 570 CE)** — when **Abrahah**, the Abyssinian (Christian) ruler of Yemen, marched on Makkah with a war elephant to **destroy the Ka'bah**, and Allah destroyed his army with **flocks of birds hurling stones of baked clay** (Surah Al-Fil). That same year, in that same city, the **Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born** — as the long-awaited dawn after the world's deepest darkness. This chapter paints the world that was about to be transformed.

## 3. The State of the World

### 3.1 The Two Superpowers

The world was dominated by two exhausted empires:
- **The Byzantine (Eastern Roman) Empire:** Christian, ruling from Constantinople over Syria (Sham), Egypt, and North Africa. Its Christianity had become entangled in political power, theological schisms, and the deification of Isa (Season 10).
- **The Sasanian Persian Empire:** Zoroastrian (worshipping fire and a dualism of light/dark), ruling Iraq and Persia, with a rigid caste system.

The Qur'an itself references their wars:

> ✅ **Surah Ar-Rum (30:2-4):** "The Byzantines have been defeated in the nearest land. But they, after their defeat, will overcome within a few years..." — *a prophecy (fulfilled) about the Roman-Persian wars, revealed in Makkah.*

Both empires were spiritually bankrupt and politically oppressive — the world was, as the Prophet ﷺ later described, in a state where Allah "looked at the people of the earth and detested them, Arabs and non-Arabs alike, except some remnants of the People of the Book" (**Sahih Muslim 2865**).

### 3.2 Arabia and the Jahiliyyah

Arabia was a land of stark contrasts. The Arabs possessed magnificent virtues — **generosity, courage, loyalty to their word, hospitality, and unmatched eloquence** (poetry was their highest art). Yet their society was sunk in *Jahiliyyah*:
- **Idolatry:** Worship of hundreds of idols (al-Lat, al-'Uzza, Manat, Hubal, and the ancient Wadd, Suwa', etc. — the very idols from Nuh's time, Season 03!).
- **Female infanticide:** Burying newborn daughters alive out of shame or fear of poverty — condemned in the Qur'an (16:58-59; 81:8-9: "when the buried infant girl is asked, for what sin she was killed").
- **Oppression of women:** Treated as property, inherited like goods, with no rights.
- **Tribalism and blood-feuds:** Endless wars over honour, with the strong devouring the weak.
- **Slavery, usury (riba), gambling, alcohol** — rampant and unrestrained.

> 📖 **Why "ignorance"?** *Jahiliyyah* doesn't mean the Arabs were unintelligent — they were brilliant poets, traders, and survivors. It means they were ignorant of **divine guidance** — living by tribal custom and desire rather than revelation. Into this darkness, light was about to come.

### 3.3 Makkah, the Ka'bah, and Quraysh

**Makkah** — the barren valley where Ibrahim left Hajar and Isma'il (Season 05) — had grown into a major **trade and pilgrimage hub**, precisely because of the **Ka'bah.** But the House Ibrahim built for *tawhid* now held **360 idols**, and pilgrimage had become a polytheistic, commercialized affair. The dominant tribe was **Quraysh** — descendants of Isma'il through 'Adnan — who held the prestigious roles of **custodians of the Ka'bah** and providers for the pilgrims (the *siqayah* — water, and *rifadah* — food). The Prophet's ﷺ great-grandfather and grandfather (**'Abd al-Muttalib**) were leaders of Quraysh. Despite the idolatry, Quraysh still revered the Ka'bah and traced their honour to Ibrahim and Isma'il — a faint memory of the pure origin.

> ✅ **Surah Quraysh (106:1-4):** Allah reminds Quraysh of their security and provision through the Ka'bah and their trade caravans ("the winter and summer journeys") — and calls them to "worship the Lord of this House, who fed them against hunger and made them safe from fear." *Their entire prosperity flowed from the House of the One God — yet they filled it with idols.*

## 4. The Year of the Elephant (Surah Al-Fil)

The most dramatic event preceding the Prophet's ﷺ birth:

> ✅ **Surah Al-Fil (105:1-5)**
>
> **Arabic:** أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِأَصْحَابِ الْفِيلِ ۝ أَلَمْ يَجْعَلْ كَيْدَهُمْ فِي تَضْلِيلٍ ۝ وَأَرْسَلَ عَلَيْهِمْ طَيْرًا أَبَابِيلَ ۝ تَرْمِيهِم بِحِجَارَةٍ مِّن سِجِّيلٍ ۝ فَجَعَلَهُمْ كَعَصْفٍ مَّأْكُولٍ
>
> **Translation:** "Have you not considered how your Lord dealt with the companions of the elephant? Did He not make their plot go astray? And He sent against them birds in flocks, striking them with stones of hard clay, and made them like eaten straw."
>
> **The story (from Seerah and tafsir):** **Abrahah**, the Abyssinian Christian viceroy of Yemen, had built a grand cathedral and wanted to divert Arabia's pilgrimage from the Ka'bah to it. Enraged that the Arabs still revered Makkah, he marched a large army — led by a **war elephant** — to **demolish the Ka'bah.** When 'Abd al-Muttalib's camels were seized, he famously told Abrahah: *"I am the lord of the camels, and the House has a Lord who will protect it."* The elephant **refused to advance** toward Makkah. Then Allah sent **flocks of birds** carrying small stones of baked clay, which they dropped on the army, destroying it utterly — "like eaten straw."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** This was a **direct divine protection of the Ka'bah** — and a sign, like 'Aad and Pharaoh (Seasons 4, 7), that **Allah humbles the mightiest through the smallest means** (an army with an elephant destroyed by *small birds*). It happened in **c. 570 CE — the "Year of the Elephant" — the very year the Prophet ﷺ was born** (Episode 42). It was as if the world was being prepared: the House was divinely cleansed of its attackers just as the one who would cleanse it of idols was about to arrive.

## 6. The Themes

1. **The deepest darkness precedes the dawn.** The world was at a spiritual low point — which is precisely when Allah sent the greatest light. Despair is never warranted; Allah's mercy arrives when needed most.
2. **Allah protects His sacred symbols.** The Ka'bah, though filled with idols, was divinely defended from Abrahah (105) — preserving it for its true purpose: to be cleansed and restored by the Prophet ﷺ.
3. **The mighty are humbled by the small** (again) — an elephant army destroyed by birds, echoing 'Aad (wind) and Pharaoh (water). Allah's power needs no grand means.
4. **Virtue and vice coexisted in Jahiliyyah.** The Arabs' noble qualities (generosity, courage, loyalty) would, once guided by Islam, become the engine of a world-changing civilization. Islam *refined* their virtues and *removed* their vices.
5. **The idols were a betrayal of their own heritage.** Quraysh descended from Ibrahim and Isma'il and guarded the House of *tawhid* — yet filled it with *shirk*. The Prophet ﷺ would call them back to their own true origin.

## 7. Lessons

1. **No society is beyond reform.** The Jahiliyyah seemed hopeless — yet within a generation, Islam transformed it into the most just society of its age. Never write off a person or a people.
2. **Allah preserves what serves His plan.** The Ka'bah was protected for its destiny. Trust that Allah safeguards the means of guidance.
3. **Light is most needed in darkness.** The worse the times, the greater the need — and the greater Allah's response. Be a source of light wherever you are.
4. **Good qualities are raw material for greatness.** Islam didn't erase the Arabs' identity; it purified and directed their virtues. Your natural strengths, aligned with faith, can serve Allah.
5. **Reflect on the signs of history.** The Year of the Elephant was a sign read by all Arabia. Allah's hand in history is a lesson for "those who reflect."

## 8. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Jahiliyyah means the Arabs were stupid/uncivilized."**
> ✅ It means ignorance of **divine guidance**, not of intellect or culture. The Arabs were sophisticated poets, traders, and survivors — but lacked revelation. Islam guided, not "civilized from scratch."

> ❌ **"The Year of the Elephant is a legend."**
> ✅ It is recorded in the Qur'an (Surah Al-Fil) and the Seerah, dated c. 570 CE — the Prophet's ﷺ birth year. It was a famous, era-defining event the Arabs reckoned dates by.

> ❌ **"Quraysh were always pure idolaters with no link to monotheism."**
> ✅ They descended from **Ibrahim and Isma'il** and guarded the Ka'bah, retaining a faint memory of *tawhid* (and a few *hunafa'* still sought it). Islam called them back to their own roots.

> ❌ **"Pre-Islamic Arabs had no virtues."**
> ✅ They had great virtues (generosity, courage, loyalty, eloquence) **alongside** grave vices. Islam preserved and refined the good, abolished the evil (like female infanticide, 81:8-9).

## 9. Weak / Disputed Points (Labelled)

---
**📖 Note — Pre-Islamic details and dates**

- Much pre-Islamic Arabian history comes from later Arab historians and the Seerah works; specific dates (e.g., 570 CE for the Year of the Elephant) are **traditional approximations**, generally reliable but not precise to the year.
- Some popular stories of the *hunafa'* (pre-Islamic monotheists like Zayd ibn 'Amr, Waraqah ibn Nawfal) are authentic in part (Zayd and Waraqah appear in Sahih hadith); others have weaker chains. Use authentic reports.

## 10. Important People

**Quraysh:** The dominant Makkan tribe, descendants of Isma'il, custodians of the Ka'bah — into which the Prophet ﷺ would be born.

**'Abd al-Muttalib:** The Prophet's ﷺ grandfather, chief of Quraysh, who confronted Abrahah ("the House has a Lord who will protect it") — a man of dignity and a faint monotheistic instinct.

**Abrahah:** The Abyssinian ruler of Yemen who tried to destroy the Ka'bah and was destroyed by Allah (Surah Al-Fil).

**The *hunafa'*:** The scattered pre-Islamic monotheists who rejected idolatry and sought the religion of Ibrahim (e.g., Zayd ibn 'Amr, Waraqah ibn Nawfal — the latter appears at the start of revelation, Season 12).

## 13. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was the world like before Islam?**
A: Dominated by two exhausted, oppressive empires (Byzantine Christian and Sasanian Zoroastrian), with pure monotheism nearly lost; Arabia was in *Jahiliyyah* — idolatry, tribal war, female infanticide, and injustice, amid genuine virtues.

**Q: What does *Jahiliyyah* mean?**
A: The "Age of Ignorance" — ignorance of **divine guidance**, not of intelligence or culture.

**Q: Who guarded the Ka'bah, and why was it full of idols?**
A: The tribe of **Quraysh** (descendants of Isma'il). Over generations since Ibrahim, idolatry had crept in (as it did in Nuh's time), filling the House of *tawhid* with 360 idols.

**Q: What was the Year of the Elephant?**
A: c. 570 CE, when Abrahah's army (with a war elephant) marched to destroy the Ka'bah and was annihilated by Allah via flocks of birds dropping stones (Surah Al-Fil) — the year the Prophet ﷺ was born.

**Q: Did any monotheists exist before the Prophet ﷺ?**
A: Yes — scattered *hunafa'* who rejected idols and sought Ibrahim's religion (e.g., Zayd ibn 'Amr, Waraqah ibn Nawfal).
