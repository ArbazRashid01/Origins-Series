# Season 11 · Episode 43
# Khadijah & The Cave
### The Blessed Marriage and the Night Revelation Began

> *"Read in the name of your Lord who created — created man from a clinging clot. Read, and your Lord is the Most Generous."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-'Alaq (96:1-3)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter completes Season 11 and stands at the threshold of the entire mission: the Prophet's ﷺ blessed marriage to **Khadijah (may Allah be pleased with her)**, his years of reflection and withdrawal to the **cave of Hira**, and the world-changing night when the angel **Jibril** brought the **first words of the Qur'an**: *"Iqra'!"* ("Read!"). It covers Khadijah's pivotal support, the terror and confirmation of the first revelation, and Waraqah's recognition — the dawn of prophethood that Season 12 will carry forward.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

When Muhammad ﷺ was about **25**, his reputation for honesty led a noble, wealthy, and respected widow of Quraysh — **Khadijah bint Khuwaylid** — to entrust him with her trade caravan. Impressed by his integrity and character (and the report of her servant Maysarah), Khadijah **proposed marriage**. He accepted, and theirs became one of the greatest marriages in history: a partnership of deep love, loyalty, and mutual support that lasted **about 25 years until her death.** She was about 40, he about 25; she bore most of his children; and she would become **the first person to believe in him.**

As the years passed, Muhammad ﷺ grew increasingly troubled by the **idolatry and injustice** around him and drawn to solitude and reflection. He took to retreating to the **cave of Hira** on the mountain of **Jabal an-Nur** ("the Mountain of Light") near Makkah, spending nights in worship and contemplation of the Creator. Then, when he was about **40**, on a night in **Ramadan**, the angel **Jibril** appeared and commanded: **"Iqra'!" ("Read!").** Muhammad ﷺ, who was unlettered, replied that he could not read. The angel embraced him powerfully and repeated the command, until he conveyed the **first revelation of the Qur'an**: *"Read in the name of your Lord who created..."* (Surah Al-'Alaq 96:1-5).

Overwhelmed and **trembling with awe**, the Prophet ﷺ rushed home to Khadijah, saying, "Cover me! Cover me!" — fearing for himself. Khadijah's response is immortal: she comforted him and declared with total conviction that **Allah would never disgrace him**, citing his noble character — "you keep good relations, carry the weak, give to the destitute, honour the guest, and help against the calamities of truth." She took him to her cousin **Waraqah ibn Nawfal**, an aged blind monotheist learned in scripture, who recognized that the visitor was ***An-Namus*** (the angel Jibril) **who came to Musa**, and confirmed Muhammad ﷺ was the awaited **Prophet** — warning that his people would oppose and expel him. Prophethood had begun. This chapter explores the marriage, the cave, the first revelation, and the faith of Khadijah — the foundation of all that follows.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Marriage to Khadijah

> Khadijah bint Khuwaylid was a noble, intelligent, and successful businesswoman of Quraysh — twice widowed and highly respected, refusing many prominent suitors. Hearing of Muhammad's ﷺ legendary honesty, she hired him to lead her trade caravan to Syria, and her servant **Maysarah** returned with reports of his integrity and blessed conduct. Recognizing his exceptional character, **Khadijah proposed marriage** to him. He was about **25**; she about **40**. It became a marriage of profound love and partnership.
>
> **Her status:** The Prophet ﷺ loved Khadijah immensely and honoured her memory all his life. She bore him most of his children (including Fatimah). She is among the **greatest women in history** — the Prophet ﷺ said the best women were Maryam, Asiyah, Khadijah, and Fatimah (Bukhari 3432, Season 10). Jibril once conveyed Allah's *greetings of peace* to her and glad tidings of a house in Paradise (**Sahih al-Bukhari 3820**). Most importantly, she would be **the first human being to accept Islam.**

### 3.2 The Cave of Hira

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3 / Muslim 160** (the foundational hadith of revelation): "The commencement of the Divine Revelation to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was in the form of **good dreams** which came true like bright daylight. Then **the love of seclusion was bestowed upon him.** He used to go in seclusion in the **cave of Hira**, worshipping (Allah) continuously for many nights..."
>
> **Tafsir/Seerah:** As Muhammad ﷺ approached 40, Allah prepared him: first through **true dreams**, then through a love of **solitude and worship.** He would withdraw for nights to the **cave of Hira**, high on **Jabal an-Nur**, reflecting on the Creator and recoiling from the idolatry of his people — seeking the truth that the *hunafa'* (like Waraqah) also sought. This was the spiritual preparation immediately before revelation.

### 3.3 "Iqra'!" — The First Revelation

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3 / Muslim 160:** In the cave, "the angel came to him and said, **'Read!' (Iqra')** The Prophet ﷺ replied, 'I do not know how to read.' The angel **caught hold of me and pressed me so hard that I could not bear it any more,** then released me and said, 'Read!' I said, 'I do not know how to read.' [This happened three times,] until the angel released me and said:
>
> ✅ **Surah Al-'Alaq (96:1-5):** *'Read in the name of your Lord who created — created man from a clinging clot. Read, and your Lord is the Most Generous, who taught by the pen — taught man that which he knew not.'*"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** These were the **first words of the Qur'an** ever revealed. Their themes are profound: **"Read" (Iqra')** — the very first command of Islam is to *seek knowledge*; **"in the name of your Lord who created"** — knowledge rooted in *tawhid* and gratitude to the Creator; **"taught by the pen"** — honouring writing and learning; and the reminder that all human knowledge is a *gift* from Allah ("taught man what he knew not"). That the religion's first word is **"Read"** — to an *unlettered* Prophet — is itself a sign: the Qur'an's eloquence could not be his own invention.

### 3.4 "Cover Me!" and Khadijah's Immortal Support

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3 / Muslim 160 (cont.):** The Prophet ﷺ returned home with his **heart trembling**, and said, "**Cover me! Cover me!**" They covered him until his fear subsided. He told Khadijah what happened and said, "**I fear for myself.**" Khadijah replied: **"Never! By Allah, Allah will never disgrace you. You keep good relations with your kin, help the poor and the destitute, serve your guests generously, and assist those stricken by calamity."**
>
> **Tafsir:** This is one of the most beautiful moments in the Seerah — and a model of **what true support looks like.** Terrified and overwhelmed, the Prophet ﷺ turned to his wife, and Khadijah responded not with doubt or panic but with **unshakable conviction and reasoned reassurance**: she pointed to his *character* as proof that Allah would not abandon such a man. Her faith in him — *before* anyone else — was the rock on which the early mission stood. A spouse who believes in and strengthens you in your hardest moment is an immense blessing; Khadijah is the eternal example.

### 3.5 Waraqah ibn Nawfal Confirms the Prophethood

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3 / Muslim 160 (cont.):** Khadijah took him to her cousin **Waraqah ibn Nawfal**, an aged, blind man who had become a *hanif* and Christian, learned in the scriptures. Hearing the account, Waraqah exclaimed: **"This is *An-Namus* (the angel Jibril) whom Allah sent to Musa. I wish I were young and could be alive when your people turn you out."** The Prophet ﷺ asked, "Will they expel me?" Waraqah said: **"Yes. Never has a man come with what you have brought but that he was opposed. If I live to see that day, I will support you strongly."** Waraqah died soon after.
>
> **Tafsir:** Waraqah — one of the few who had sought the true religion of Ibrahim — **immediately recognized** the experience as genuine prophethood (the same angel who came to Musa, Season 7). His testimony, from a learned monotheist with no stake in Islam, was an early confirmation. His warning — that the Prophet ﷺ would be *opposed and expelled*, as every prophet before him — accurately foretold the persecution of Season 12. It also established a sobering truth: **truth is always opposed by the established order**, just as it was for Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, and Isa.

## 5. The Themes

1. **The first command is "Read."** Islam's very first revealed word commands **the pursuit of knowledge** — "in the name of your Lord," knowledge rooted in faith. From the first instant, Islam is a religion of learning and reflection.
2. **An unlettered Prophet, a miraculous Book.** That the Qur'an's matchless eloquence came through an *unlettered* man (who said "I cannot read") is itself proof it was *revealed*, not authored (cf. 29:48).
3. **Behind a great mission, a great support.** Khadijah's instant faith and reasoned reassurance ("Allah will never disgrace you") were the human foundation of the early mission. Marriage and family can be a fortress of faith.
4. **Truth is always opposed.** Waraqah foretold the Prophet ﷺ would be expelled — because every messenger before him was opposed. Conviction must prepare for resistance.
5. **Character is the credential.** Khadijah's argument was his *conduct* — kindness to kin, the poor, guests, and the afflicted. A life of good character is the strongest testimony to one's truthfulness.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Seek knowledge — it is the first command of your faith.** "Read in the name of your Lord" (96:1). Begin and root all learning in Allah.
2. **Be the support your loved ones need in their hardest moment.** Khadijah's response to a terrified husband is the model of faithful partnership: calm, conviction, and reasoned reassurance.
3. **Your character will defend you when words can't.** When the Prophet ﷺ feared for himself, Khadijah's evidence was his *good deeds*. Live so that your conduct testifies for you.
4. **Expect opposition when you stand for truth.** Waraqah's warning is timeless — the established order resists truth. Steadfastness, not surprise, is the believer's stance.
5. **Solitude and reflection prepare the heart.** The Prophet's ﷺ retreats to Hira show the value of withdrawing from distraction to reflect on the Creator.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Muhammad ﷺ claimed prophethood eagerly/ambitiously."**
> ✅ The opposite — the first revelation **terrified** him; he rushed home trembling, fearing for himself ("Cover me!"). This reluctance and awe is the response of a *genuine* recipient, not an impostor.

> ❌ **"He wrote the Qur'an himself."**
> ✅ He was **unlettered** — his first words to Jibril were "I cannot read." The Qur'an's unmatched eloquence from an unlettered man is evidence of revelation (29:48), not authorship.

> ❌ **"Khadijah was a minor figure."**
> ✅ She was **the first believer**, the beloved wife of 25 years, among the greatest women ever (Bukhari 3432), greeted by Allah through Jibril (Bukhari 3820), and the irreplaceable supporter of the mission (Bukhari 3815).

> ❌ **"Waraqah's confirmation was made up to validate Islam."**
> ✅ It is in the **most authentic** hadith of revelation (Bukhari 3) — testimony from a learned monotheist who recognized the angel "who came to Musa," and who died before Islam spread, with no worldly motive.

## 8. Weak / Disputed Points (Labelled)

---
**📖 Note — Khadijah's exact age**

- The famous figure of "40" for Khadijah at marriage comes from Seerah reports; some narrations suggest she was younger (e.g., 28). The exact age is **not certain** and not a matter of doctrine. What's certain is her nobility, her prior widowhood, and her supreme status.
---
**📖 Note — Details of Maysarah's report and the marriage arrangement**

- Various Seerah details (signs Maysarah saw, who arranged the marriage) have differing levels of authenticity. The core (Khadijah hired him, was impressed, and they married) is well-established.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: How did the Prophet ﷺ and Khadijah marry?**
A: She hired him for his honesty to lead her caravan, was impressed by his character, and **proposed marriage**. He was ~25, she ~40 (age debated). It was a blessed 25-year union.

**Q: What was the first revelation?**
A: **Surah Al-'Alaq 96:1-5** — "Read in the name of your Lord who created..." — brought by Jibril in the cave of Hira when the Prophet ﷺ was about 40.

A: **Khadijah** — she instantly believed and reassured him, becoming the first Muslim.

**Q: Who was Waraqah, and what did he say?**
