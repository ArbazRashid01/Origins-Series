# Season 08 · Episode 34
# The Queen of Sheba
### The Hoopoe's Report, the Throne in the Blink of an Eye, and a Queen Who Submitted

> *"My Lord, indeed I have wronged myself, and I submit with Sulayman to Allah, Lord of the worlds."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah An-Naml (27:44)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter completes Season 08 with one of the Qur'an's most elegant narratives: Sulayman's discovery — through a **bird, the hoopoe** — of a powerful, sun-worshipping queen (**Bilqis**, the Queen of **Saba'/Sheba**), and his wise, firm da'wah that leads her from idolatry to submission to Allah. It showcases Sulayman's diplomacy, the miracle of the throne brought instantly, and the gentle intelligence with which a great queen was guided to the truth — a model of *da'wah* through wisdom, not coercion.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

While reviewing his armies of birds, Sulayman noticed the **hoopoe** was absent. When it returned, it brought astonishing news: it had discovered a wealthy kingdom in **Saba' (Sheba**, in **Yemen)** ruled by a **woman** (the queen, traditionally **Bilqis**) with a magnificent throne — but her people **worshipped the sun** instead of Allah, deceived by Shaytan. Sulayman tested the report, then sent a **letter** inviting the queen and her people to submit to Allah and abandon their false worship.

The queen, wise and politically shrewd, consulted her advisors, then — to test Sulayman's intentions — sent a lavish **gift**. Sulayman **rejected the gift**, making clear he sought *faith, not wealth or tribute*: "Do you provide me with wealth? What Allah has given me is better than what He has given you." He warned that he would come with forces they could not resist. Recognizing this was no ordinary king, the queen resolved to come to him herself.

Before her arrival, Sulayman asked who could bring her **throne**; an *'ifrit* offered to fetch it before he rose from his seat, but **one who had knowledge of the Scripture** brought it **"before your glance returns to you"** — instantly. Sulayman recognized this as a test of his gratitude. When the queen arrived, her throne was shown to her (subtly altered), and she was led through a hall whose glass floor she mistook for water — gently dismantling her assumptions. Faced with the wisdom and signs of Sulayman, the queen declared: *"My Lord, I have wronged myself, and I submit with Sulayman to Allah, Lord of the worlds"* (27:44). A queen who ruled an empire **chose faith** — won not by force, but by wisdom. This chapter explores the hoopoe, the letter, the rejected gift, the throne, and the queen's submission.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Missing Hoopoe and Its News

> ✅ **Surah An-Naml (27:20-26):** Sulayman inspected the birds and noticed the **hoopoe** absent, vowing to punish it for unexcused absence unless it brought a clear reason. The hoopoe returned: *"I have encompassed [in knowledge] that which you have not, and I have come to you from Saba' with certain news. Indeed, I found a woman ruling them, and she has been given of all things, and she has a great throne. I found her and her people prostrating to the sun instead of Allah, and Shaytan has made their deeds pleasing to them... Why do they not prostrate to Allah, who brings forth what is hidden in the heavens and earth...?"*
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** Even a **bird** in Sulayman's kingdom was concerned with *tawhid* — distressed that a nation worshipped the sun instead of its Creator. The hoopoe's report is striking: it valued *faith* over the queen's wealth and "great throne." Sulayman's justice is also shown — he would not punish without cause, and accepted the hoopoe's valid excuse. (Note: this is why the hoopoe is protected in Islamic law — the Prophet ﷺ forbade killing it.)

### 3.2 The Letter and the Queen's Wisdom

> ✅ **Surah An-Naml (27:28-35):** Sulayman sent the hoopoe with a **letter**: *"It is from Sulayman, and it is: 'In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Especially Merciful. Be not haughty with me, but come to me in submission [to Allah].'"* The queen (Bilqis) gathered her chiefs and, showing political wisdom, sought their counsel. They offered military strength but deferred to her. She observed shrewdly: *"Indeed, kings, when they enter a city, ruin it and make the honoured of its people humbled"* — and decided to **send a gift** first, to gauge whether Sulayman was a worldly conqueror (who would accept tribute) or a true prophet (who would not).
>
> **Tafsir:** The queen is portrayed as **intelligent, measured, and consultative** — not a tyrant. Her test (the gift) was clever statecraft. Note Sulayman's letter began with *Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Rahim* — the same formula opening the Qur'an's surahs — and called her not to himself but to **submission to Allah** ("come to me as Muslims").

### 3.3 The Rejected Gift

> ✅ **Surah An-Naml (27:36-37)**
>
> **Translation:** "So when they came to Sulayman, he said, 'Do you provide me with wealth? But what Allah has given me is better than what He has given you. Rather, it is you who rejoice in your gift. Return to them, for we will surely come to them with soldiers that they will be powerless to encounter, and we will surely expel them from it in humiliation, and they will be debased.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Sulayman's response cut through the queen's test instantly: he **rejected the wealth**, proving he was no tribute-seeking king but a prophet seeking their *guidance*. "What Allah has given me is better" — the prophet-king, richer than any monarch, had no interest in her gold; he wanted her *faith*. His firm warning (we will come with irresistible forces) was not greed for conquest but a demand that they **abandon false worship** — the seriousness of *tawhid*. This convinced the queen that Sulayman was extraordinary, and she resolved to come herself.

### 3.4 The Throne Brought Instantly

> ✅ **Surah An-Naml (27:38-40):** Sulayman asked his assembly who could bring the queen's throne before she arrived in submission. An **'ifrit** (powerful jinn) offered to bring it "before you rise from your place." But **"one who had knowledge of the Scripture"** said, *"I will bring it to you before your glance returns to you"* — and there it was, settled before him. Sulayman, seeing it, said: *"This is from the favour of my Lord to test me whether I am grateful or ungrateful..."* (27:40).
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** The contrast is meaningful: the jinn's power (fast, but bounded by time — "before you rise") was **surpassed** by the power of a *righteous human with knowledge and faith* who brought it **instantaneously** ("before your glance returns"). Scholars identify this knowledgeable one (traditionally Asif ibn Barkhiya, Sulayman's righteous minister — *unverified name*) as a believer granted a miracle by Allah's leave. The lesson: **the power of faith and divine knowledge exceeds even the might of the jinn** — and Sulayman's response, yet again, was *gratitude*, recognizing it as a test (27:40).

### 3.5 The Queen's Submission

> ✅ **Surah An-Naml (27:41-44):** Sulayman had the throne disguised/altered and asked if she recognized it; she answered with careful wisdom: "It is as though it were it." She had already been given some knowledge and was inclined to submit. Then she was asked to enter the palace hall — its floor of polished glass over water — and, mistaking it for a pool, she **uncovered her shins** to wade through. Sulayman told her it was smooth glass. At this, realizing the depth of the signs and wisdom before her, she declared:
>
> **Arabic:** قَالَتْ رَبِّ إِنِّي ظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِي وَأَسْلَمْتُ مَعَ سُلَيْمَانَ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ
>
> **Translation:** "She said, 'My Lord, indeed I have wronged myself, and I submit with Sulayman to Allah, Lord of the worlds.'" (27:44)
>
> **Tafsir:** The glass floor was a gentle final lesson — her assumptions (about water, about reality, about her own grandeur) were shown to be mistaken; what *seemed* one thing was another. Humbled and enlightened, the great queen **abandoned sun-worship and submitted to Allah** — not under military defeat, but won by **wisdom, signs, and Sulayman's integrity.** Her words mirror the true believer's: ownership of past error ("I have wronged myself") and submission to "the Lord of the worlds." A powerful ruler guided to *tawhid* through intelligence and grace.

## 5. The Themes

1. **Da'wah by wisdom, not coercion.** Though Sulayman *could* have crushed Saba' militarily, the queen was ultimately won by **wisdom, signs, and integrity** — she *chose* to submit (27:44). The Qur'an's "no compulsion in religion" (2:256) is embodied: faith is to be *won*, not forced.
2. **Seek faith, not wealth.** Sulayman rejected the queen's lavish gift — "what Allah has given me is better" (27:37). The sincere caller wants people's *guidance*, not their gold.
3. **Power and knowledge are tests of gratitude.** From the instant-throne to the whole encounter, Sulayman repeatedly framed his gifts as a *test*: "whether I am grateful or ungrateful" (27:40).
4. **The power of faith exceeds the power of jinn.** The believer with "knowledge of the Scripture" outdid the 'ifrit (27:39-40) — righteous knowledge and Allah's leave surpass raw force.
5. **True intelligence submits to truth.** The queen was shrewd, consultative, and wise — and her wisdom led her *to faith*, not away from it. Real intelligence recognizes and submits to the truth.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Win hearts with wisdom and good character.** Sulayman's firmness *and* integrity drew a queen to faith. Harshness and force rarely convert; wisdom, evidence, and sincerity do.
2. **Don't sell your message for worldly gain.** Reject the "gifts" that would compromise your principles. The caller to truth is not for sale (27:37).
3. **Every blessing is a test of gratitude.** Like Sulayman, meet each gift — power, knowledge, success — with "this is to test whether I am grateful" (27:40), not with pride.
4. **Consult and deliberate.** The queen consulted her advisors (27:32) and acted with measured wisdom — a model of *shura* (consultation) even before her faith.
5. **The humble admission "I have wronged myself" opens the door to Allah.** The queen's submission began with owning her error (27:44) — the same humility as Adam, Musa, and every true believer.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The Queen of Sheba was seduced or conquered into submission."**
> ✅ She submitted **by her own reasoned choice** after witnessing Sulayman's wisdom and signs (27:44) — won through da'wah, not force or romance. (The romantic/marriage legends are Isra'iliyyat — §8.)

> ❌ **"Sulayman wanted her wealth/kingdom."**
> ✅ He **rejected her wealth** outright (27:37); he sought her people's *faith*, not tribute.

> ❌ **"The hoopoe story is a children's fable."**
> ✅ It is a Qur'anic account (27:20-28) carrying serious lessons on *tawhid*, justice, and da'wah — and the hoopoe is honoured/protected in Islamic law as a result.

> ❌ **"The one who brought the throne was a magician."**
> ✅ He was "one who had **knowledge of the Scripture**" — a righteous believer granted a miracle by Allah's leave (27:40), explicitly contrasted with the jinn's power. Not magic.

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — The queen's name, lineage, "jinn ancestry," hairy legs, and marriage to Sulayman**

- **Narration:** Naming her "Bilqis," claiming her mother was a jinn, that the glass-floor test was to check rumours she had hairy legs/donkey's hooves, and that Sulayman married her afterward.
- **Source:** **Isra'iliyyat** and folklore; the Qur'an gives **none** of this — neither her name, nor jinn ancestry, nor any marriage.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ "Bilqis" is a traditional name (commonly used). The jinn-ancestry, hooves, and marriage tales are **unverified folklore** — do not present as fact.
---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 2 — The name of the one who brought the throne ("Asif ibn Barkhiya") and how**

- **Source:** Tradition.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'an says only "one who had knowledge of the Scripture" (27:40). The name is traditional, not established.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Who was the Queen of Sheba?**
A: The ruler of **Saba' (Sheba)** in Yemen, a sun-worshipping people. Tradition names her **Bilqis** (not in the Qur'an). She submitted to Allah after encountering Sulayman.

**Q: Did Sulayman marry her?**
A: The Qur'an says **nothing** about a marriage — that's Isra'iliyyat/folklore (§8). The Qur'an's focus is her **submission to Allah** (27:44).

**Q: Who brought her throne instantly?**
A: "One who had knowledge of the Scripture" — a righteous believer empowered by Allah, who outdid the 'ifrit's offer (27:40). Tradition names him Asif, but the Qur'an doesn't.

**Q: Why did Sulayman reject her gift?**
A: To show he was a **prophet seeking faith**, not a king seeking tribute — "what Allah has given me is better" (27:37).

**Q: What's the main lesson of this story?**
A: **Da'wah through wisdom and integrity** — a powerful queen guided to *tawhid* by reasoning and signs, not by force; and every blessing as a test of gratitude.
