# Season 08 · Episode 33
# The King Who Commanded the Wind
### Sulayman — Dominion Over Wind, Jinn, and the Speech of Animals

> *"My Lord, forgive me and grant me a kingdom such as will not belong to anyone after me. Indeed, You are the Bestower."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Sad (38:35)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Dawud's son **Sulayman (Solomon), peace be upon him**, inherited the prophethood and kingdom — and was granted the most extraordinary dominion of any prophet: command over the **wind**, the **jinn**, and the ability to **understand the speech of birds and animals.** This chapter covers Sulayman's unique kingdom, his gratitude and justice, the famous incident with the **ant**, the gathering of his armies, and his death (discovered only when a termite ate his staff) — a profound lesson on power, gratitude, and human mortality. It sets up the story of the Queen of Sheba (Episode 34).

---

## 1. Episode Summary

**Sulayman** inherited Dawud's prophethood and kingdom and prayed for "a kingdom such as will not belong to anyone after me" — a unique, unrepeatable dominion that Allah granted. He was given:
- Command over the **wind**, which carried his transport "a month's journey" in a morning and a month in an evening (34:12);
- Power over the **jinn**, who built and dove for him and did as he commanded by Allah's leave (34:12-13; 38:37-38);
- The **understanding of the speech of birds and animals** ("we have been taught the language of birds," 27:16);
- Molten brass made to flow for him, and structures, fountains, and great works.

Despite this staggering power, Sulayman's defining trait was **gratitude and humility.** When he heard an **ant** warn its colony to flee lest Sulayman's armies crush them unknowingly, he *smiled* and thanked Allah for the favour of understanding it, praying to remain grateful and righteous (27:18-19). He mustered his armies — humans, jinn, and birds — in perfect order, yet never forgot that all of it was a *test* of gratitude (27:40: "this is from the favour of my Lord to test me whether I am grateful or ungrateful").

His death is one of the most striking scenes in the Qur'an: Sulayman died **leaning on his staff**, and the jinn — who were laboring under his command and *believed they knew the unseen* — continued working, not realizing he was dead, until a **termite ate through his staff** and his body fell. This revealed publicly that **the jinn do NOT know the unseen** (34:14) — a direct refutation of those who attributed such knowledge to them. This chapter explores Sulayman's dominion, his gratitude, the ant, and the lesson of his death.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Inheritance and the Unique Kingdom

> ✅ **Surah An-Naml (27:15-16):** "And We had certainly given to Dawud and Sulayman knowledge... And Sulayman inherited Dawud. He said, 'O people, we have been taught the language of birds, and we have been given of all things. Indeed, this is evident bounty.'"
> ✅ **Surah Sad (38:35):** Sulayman prayed: "My Lord, forgive me and grant me a kingdom such as will not belong to anyone after me. Indeed, You are the Bestower."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** Sulayman "inherited" Dawud not in wealth (prophets don't bequeath wealth as inheritance — see the hadith in §5) but in **prophethood, knowledge, and kingship.** His prayer for a unique kingdom "for no one after me" was granted — making his dominion a one-time sign of Allah's power, not a precedent. Note that even at the peak of his request for power, he began with **"forgive me"** — humility before ambition.

### 3.2 Command of the Wind and the Jinn

> ✅ **Surah Saba (34:12-13)**
>
> **Translation:** "And to Sulayman [We subjected] the wind — its morning [journey was that of] a month and its afternoon [journey] a month — and We made flowing for him a spring of molten copper. And among the jinn were those who worked for him by the permission of his Lord... They made for him what he willed of elevated chambers, statues, basins like reservoirs, and cauldrons fixed in place..."
> ✅ **Surah Al-Anbiya (21:81-82):** "And to Sulayman [We subjected] the wind, blowing forcefully, proceeding by his command toward the land which We had blessed... And of the devils (jinn) were those who dived for him and did work other than that; and We were of them a guardian."
>
> **Tafsir:** Sulayman's dominion was unmatched: the **wind** carried him vast distances; the **jinn** built monumental works and dove into the seas at his command — *all "by the permission of his Lord."* The repeated phrase is crucial: Sulayman's power was **delegated by Allah**, not his own. He commanded the jinn, but Allah was their true "guardian" — Sulayman was a servant entrusted with a test, not an independent sorcerer (a vital point, since the Qur'an elsewhere refutes the slander that Sulayman practiced magic — see §7).

### 3.3 The Ant — Power With Humility

> ✅ **Surah An-Naml (27:18-19)**
>
> **Arabic:** حَتَّىٰ إِذَا أَتَوْا عَلَىٰ وَادِ النَّمْلِ قَالَتْ نَمْلَةٌ يَا أَيُّهَا النَّمْلُ ادْخُلُوا مَسَاكِنَكُمْ لَا يَحْطِمَنَّكُمْ سُلَيْمَانُ وَجُنُودُهُ وَهُمْ لَا يَشْعُرُونَ
>
> **Translation:** "Until, when they came upon the valley of the ants, an ant said, 'O ants, enter your dwellings lest Sulayman and his soldiers crush you while they perceive not.' So [Sulayman] smiled, amused at her speech, and said, 'My Lord, enable me to be grateful for Your favour which You have bestowed upon me and upon my parents and to do righteousness of which You approve. And admit me by Your mercy into [the ranks of] Your righteous servants.'"
>
> **Tafsir (as-Sa'di):** A beautiful scene. The mightiest king on earth — commanding wind, jinn, and armies — overhears a tiny **ant** warning its colony, and instead of pride, he **smiles with humility and bursts into gratitude.** His du'a (27:19) is a model: he thanks Allah for the favour, asks to be made *grateful*, to do *righteous* deeds, and — strikingly — to be admitted "among Your righteous servants." A king who, at the height of power, prayed only to be counted among the *righteous*. Note also the ant's mercy: it said the soldiers would crush them "**while they perceive not**" — excusing Sulayman's army of intent, a subtle lesson in good assumption.

### 3.4 The Armies and the Test of Gratitude

> ✅ **Surah An-Naml (27:17, 40):** Sulayman's armies of **jinn, men, and birds** were gathered "set in rows" (27:17) — perfect order over the most diverse forces imaginable. Later, when the throne of the Queen of Sheba was brought before him in the blink of an eye (Episode 34), Sulayman said: *"This is from the favour of my Lord to test me whether I am grateful or ungrateful. And whoever is grateful — his gratitude is only for his own benefit; and whoever is ungrateful — then indeed, my Lord is Free of need and Generous"* (27:40).
>
> **Tafsir:** Sulayman understood his entire kingdom as a **test (*ibtila'*)**: would all this power make him grateful and humble, or arrogant? He chose gratitude at every turn. This is the believer's frame for *any* blessing — wealth, talent, status: it is a test of whether you will thank and use it for Allah, or let it corrupt you.

### 3.5 The Death of Sulayman and the Termite

> ✅ **Surah Saba (34:14)**
>
> **Arabic:** فَلَمَّا قَضَيْنَا عَلَيْهِ الْمَوْتَ مَا دَلَّهُمْ عَلَىٰ مَوْتِهِ إِلَّا دَابَّةُ الْأَرْضِ تَأْكُلُ مِنسَأَتَهُ ۖ فَلَمَّا خَرَّ تَبَيَّنَتِ الْجِنُّ أَن لَّوْ كَانُوا يَعْلَمُونَ الْغَيْبَ مَا لَبِثُوا فِي الْعَذَابِ الْمُهِينِ
>
> **Translation:** "And when We decreed for him death, nothing indicated to them his death except a creature of the earth (a termite) eating his staff. But when he fell, it became clear to the jinn that if they had known the unseen, they would not have remained in humiliating punishment (hard labour)."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** A staggering scene. Sulayman died while **standing, leaning on his staff** — and the jinn, laboring at his command, **kept working for a long time, not realizing he was dead**, until a **termite** slowly ate through the staff and his body collapsed. The point is explicit and doctrinally vital: **the jinn do NOT know the unseen.** Had they known, they wouldn't have toiled on after his death. This single verse demolishes the widespread belief (then and now) that jinn possess knowledge of the unseen or the future (connecting to S1E3 §4.6). Even the great Sulayman's *death* served to teach a lesson about *tawhid* — that knowledge of the unseen belongs to Allah alone.

> ✅ **On "in sha'Allah" (Bukhari 3424):** The Prophet ﷺ said Sulayman once declared he would go around to his wives that night so each would bear a warrior-son for Allah's cause — but he **did not say "in sha'Allah" (if Allah wills)**, and so [the outcome failed]. The Prophet ﷺ said: *"Had he said 'in sha'Allah,' [his wish would have been fulfilled]."* A profound lesson from a prophet's experience: **always attach your plans to the will of Allah** (cf. Qur'an 18:23-24).

## 5. The Themes

1. **Power is a test, not a trophy.** Sulayman, with unmatched dominion, saw it all as "a test of whether I am grateful" (27:40). The greater the blessing, the greater the test of humility and gratitude.
2. **Gratitude is the response of the wise.** From the ant (27:19) to the throne (27:40), Sulayman's reflex was thankfulness — and "whoever is grateful, his gratitude benefits only himself" (the grateful gain; Allah needs nothing).
3. **All authority is delegated by Allah** ("by the permission of his Lord," 34:13). Even commanding wind and jinn, Sulayman was a *servant* under Allah, not an independent power.
4. **The jinn do not know the unseen** (34:14). Sulayman's death exposed this forever — knowledge of the unseen is Allah's alone (refuting fortune-telling and jinn-worship).
5. **Attach plans to Allah's will** ("in sha'Allah," Bukhari 3424). Even a prophet's mighty resolve fails without it.
6. **Death comes to the mightiest.** The king who commanded the wind died leaning on a stick, undone by a termite. No power exempts anyone from mortality.

## 6. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Sulayman was a sorcerer / his power came from magic."**
> ✅ The Qur'an **explicitly refutes** this: *"Sulayman did not disbelieve, but the devils disbelieved, teaching people magic"* (2:102). His power was a **gift from Allah** ("by the permission of his Lord"), not sorcery. Those who attributed magic to him were slandering a prophet.

> ❌ **"The jinn have knowledge of the unseen / the future."**
> ✅ Sulayman's death **disproves this dramatically** — the jinn didn't even know their master had died (34:14). Knowledge of the unseen is Allah's alone.

> ❌ **"Sulayman inherited Dawud's wealth as personal property."**
> ✅ Prophets do not bequeath wealth as inheritance (Bukhari 1470); Sulayman inherited **prophethood, knowledge, and kingship.**

> ❌ **"Sulayman's command of jinn means humans can control jinn today."**
> ✅ It was a **unique gift to Sulayman** ("a kingdom for no one after me," 38:35). Seeking to command jinn is forbidden occultism (S1E3); Sulayman's case is an exception by Allah's special grant, not a model to imitate.

## 7. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — Elaborate magical legends (the "Seal of Solomon," a jinn impersonating him on the throne, controlling demons with a ring)**

- **Narration:** Stories of a magic ring/seal by which Sulayman controlled jinn, a demon (Sakhr) stealing the ring and ruling in his place, etc.
- **Source:** **Isra'iliyyat** and occult legend (popular in folklore and *Arabian Nights*-type literature); the Qur'an gives none of this and 2:102 explicitly clears Sulayman of magic.
- **Rely on it?** ❌ **NO.** These slander a prophet and feed occultism. Sulayman's power was Allah's gift, not a magic ring.
---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 2 — Details of the death (how long the jinn worked, the staff specifics, the termite's "reward")**

- **Source:** Tradition.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'anic essentials (death on the staff, termite, jinn's ignorance of the unseen) are certain; the embellishments are unverified.

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Did Sulayman really control the wind and jinn?**
A: Yes — by Allah's gift and "permission of his Lord" (34:12-13), a **unique** dominion granted to no one after him (38:35). Not magic (2:102).

**Q: Could Sulayman actually talk to animals?**
A: He was given understanding of "the language of birds" and animals (27:16); the Qur'an records his exchange with the ant and the hoopoe (Episode 34).

**Q: How did Sulayman die?**
A: Standing, leaning on his staff; the jinn kept working until a **termite** ate the staff and he fell — proving the jinn don't know the unseen (34:14).

**Q: Was Sulayman a magician?**
A: **No** — the Qur'an clears him explicitly (2:102). His power was Allah's gift; the "magic ring" legends are Isra'iliyyat (§7).

**Q: What's the lesson of his death?**
A: That **knowledge of the unseen belongs to Allah alone** (the jinn didn't know he died), and that **death comes to even the mightiest** — held in perspective by gratitude and humility.
