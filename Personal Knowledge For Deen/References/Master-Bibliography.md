# Master Bibliography
### The Core Library Behind This Encyclopedia

Every chapter draws on this library. Sources are grouped by type and ranked roughly by authority. Primary revelation (Qur'an and Sahih hadith) always outranks human scholarship.

---

## 1. The Primary Source — Revelation

### The Qur'an
- **The Qur'an itself** — the foundation of everything. Recommended verification and translations: **[quran.com](https://quran.com)** (Sahih International, Dr. Mustafa Khattab "The Clear Quran," etc.).

### The Six Books of Hadith (Al-Kutub as-Sittah) + Musnad
- **Sahih al-Bukhari** — the most authentic hadith collection.
- **Sahih Muslim** — the second most authentic.
- **Sunan Abi Dawud**
- **Jami' at-Tirmidhi**
- **Sunan an-Nasa'i**
- **Sunan Ibn Majah**
- **Musnad Ahmad ibn Hanbal** — vast collection, mixed grades.
- **Verification:** **[sunnah.com](https://sunnah.com)** — for Arabic text, English translation, and gradings.

| Work | Author | Note |

| **Taysir al-Karim ar-Rahman** | As-Sa'di (d. 1376 AH) | Clear, concise, faith-centered; excellent for beginners |
| **Tafsir al-Jalalayn** | Al-Mahalli & As-Suyuti | Very concise classical reference |

## A Note on Critical Reading

The great history works (at-Tabari, even parts of Ibn Ishaq) preserve **everything** they received, including weak and Isra'iliyyat reports, *with their chains* — leaving verification to the reader. This is a feature, not a flaw: they trusted scholars to sift. This encyclopedia does that sifting for you, always labelling what is weak. **Never assume a story is authentic merely because it appears in a famous old book.** The chain and the grading decide.
