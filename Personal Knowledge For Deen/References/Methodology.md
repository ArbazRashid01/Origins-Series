# Methodology & Authenticity Standards
### How This Encyclopedia Is Written and Verified

This document is the "constitution" of the entire *Personal Knowledge For Deen* project. Every chapter follows these rules. Read it once, and you will know exactly how much trust to place in any statement anywhere in this encyclopedia.

---

## 1. The Core Principle

> **Authenticity before everything.** A beautiful story that is false has no place here. A plain truth with a sound chain outranks a thousand inspiring legends.

We would rather say *"this is unknown"* than fill a gap with an unverified narration. When the sources are silent, we are silent.

---

## 2. The Hierarchy of Sources (applied in every chapter)

| Rank | Source | Symbol | Treatment |
|:----:|--------|:------:|-----------|
| 1 | **The Qur'an** | ✅ | Absolute, undeniable proof |
| 2 | **Sahih Hadith** (Bukhari, Muslim, & authenticated others) | ✅ | Authentic; reliable for belief and practice |
| 3 | **Hasan Hadith** | ☑️ | Acceptable; the "good" level is noted |
| 4 | **Tafsir & scholarly consensus (ijma')** | 📖 | Trusted human interpretation |
| 5 | **Athar** (reports of Companions / Tabi'in) | 🕰️ | Valuable context; not binding doctrine |
| 6 | **Da'if (weak) / Isra'iliyyat** | ⚠️ | Mentioned **only** with a label; never as fact |
| 7 | **Mawdu' (fabricated) / folklore / viral claims** | ❌ | Rejected; corrected in "Misconceptions" |

**Two narrations are never mixed.** Authentic and weak material is always kept visibly separate.

---

## 3. Hadith Grading Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Sahih** | Authentic — sound chain, reliable narrators, no defects |
| **Hasan** | Good — slightly less strong than Sahih but acceptable |
| **Sahih li-ghayrihi / Hasan li-ghayrihi** | Raised to that level by supporting chains |
| **Da'if** | Weak — a flaw in the chain or narrators; not used as proof |
| **Mawdu'** | Fabricated — invented; falsely attributed to the Prophet ﷺ |
| **Munkar / Shadh** | Rejected / anomalous — contradicts stronger reports |
| **Marfu'** | Attributed to the Prophet ﷺ |
| **Mawquf** | Stopped at a Companion (their own statement) |

## 4. What Is *Isra'iliyyat*?

*Isra'iliyyat* are narrations originating from Jewish and Christian traditions that entered Islamic literature (often through early converts like Ka'b al-Ahbar and Wahb ibn Munabbih). The Prophet ﷺ permitted *relating* them with a crucial caveat. Scholars sort them into three types:

1. **Confirmed** by the Qur'an/Sunnah → **accepted** (because our own sources confirm them).
2. **Contradicted** by the Qur'an/Sunnah → **rejected** outright.
3. **Neither confirmed nor denied** → **related without belief or denial**; we say *"Allah knows best"* and never build doctrine on them.

In this encyclopedia, Isra'iliyyat are always **labelled** (⚠️) so you never mistake them for established fact. Examples flagged in Season 01: the embellished Harut-Marut tales, Iblis's pre-fall "biography" (names like 'Azazil), and the detailed "jinn war before Adam."

## 5. The Creed (Aqidah) Lens

For matters of belief — especially the unseen (*ghayb*): Allah's Names and Attributes, angels, jinn, decree, the Throne — this encyclopedia follows the way of the **Salaf** (the first three generations) as systematized in works like *Al-'Aqidah at-Tahawiyyah*:

> **Affirm what Allah affirmed, without:**
> - **Tahrif** (distorting the meaning),
> - **Ta'til** (denying/emptying the attribute),
> - **Takyif** (asking "how"),
> - **Tamthil** (comparing Him to creation).

Anchored by **Qur'an 42:11**: *"There is nothing like unto Him, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing."* We affirm and believe; we do not philosophize beyond the text.

---

## 6. Handling Scholarly Differences

When scholars differ, this encyclopedia:
1. Presents **each major opinion** with its **evidence** and **proponents**.
2. Notes the **relative strength** of each.
3. States the **majority** and/or **strongest** position by evidence.
4. **Never hides** legitimate disagreement, and never forces a single view where the texts allow more than one.

Where chronology, numbers, or details are genuinely uncertain, the chapter says so plainly.

---

## 7. Citation Standards

- **Qur'an:** `Surah Name (chapter:verse)`, with Arabic, transliteration, translation, and short tafsir. Verify on **[quran.com](https://quran.com)**.
- **Hadith:** Collection + number + grade, e.g. *Sahih al-Bukhari 3191 — Authentic.* Verify text and grading on **[sunnah.com](https://sunnah.com)**.

> ⚠️ **On hadith numbers:** Numbering differs between print and digital editions (and between the Arabic and English on sunnah.com). All numbers in this encyclopedia are **study pointers** — always confirm the *text and grading* on sunnah.com before quoting as final. The grading and meaning matter more than the exact number.

## 8. The Halal-Imagery Rule (for Assets & Maps)

Any visual material referenced in `Assets/` or `Maps/` must be **halal**:
- **NO depiction** of Allah, the Prophets, the angels, or the Sahaba.
- Permitted: light, cosmos, calligraphy, landscapes, silhouettes, objects, architecture, maps, diagrams, and timelines.

---

## 9. Tone & Language

- Written to be understood by a complete beginner ("assume I know nothing"), while remaining academically accurate.
- Narrative and warm — like a history book being read aloud — but never at the expense of precision.
- **Never invents** dialogue, emotions, or details not in the sources. When the texts don't say, we don't say.
- Respectful throughout: *ﷺ* (sallallahu 'alayhi wa sallam) for the Prophet Muhammad; *peace be upon him* for other prophets; *(may Allah be pleased with him/her)* for the Companions.
