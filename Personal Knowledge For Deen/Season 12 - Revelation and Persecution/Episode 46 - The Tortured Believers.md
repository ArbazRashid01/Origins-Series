# Season 12 · Episode 46
# The Tortured Believers
### Bilal, the Persecution, the Migration to Abyssinia, and the Boycott

> *"And there is the [reward of] the Hereafter. And your Lord is going to give you, and you will be satisfied."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Ad-Duha (93:4-5)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

As the message spread, Quraysh's hostility became **brutal violence** — especially against the weak, the poor, and the enslaved who had no tribal protection. This chapter covers the **torture of the early Muslims** (Bilal, the family of Yasir, and others), the patience and steadfastness of the believers, the first **migration to Abyssinia (Habashah)**, and the cruel **social and economic boycott** of the Muslims. It is the story of faith tested by fire — and the endurance that proved its sincerity.

> 📖 **Tone note:** This chapter recounts real persecution and martyrdom with the gravity they deserve, focusing on the believers' faith and patience — not gratuitous detail.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Once Islam was public, Quraysh unleashed **systematic persecution.** Those with strong tribal protection (like the Prophet ﷺ, shielded by Abu Talib, and Abu Bakr) faced mockery, insults, and pressure. But the **weak, poor, and enslaved** believers — who had no clan to protect them — were subjected to **horrific torture** to force them to renounce Islam:
- **Bilal ibn Rabah**, an Abyssinian slave, was dragged across the burning desert sand, a massive rock placed on his chest in the midday heat — yet he repeated only one word: ***"Ahad! Ahad!"*** ("[Allah is] One! One!"). He was eventually **freed by Abu Bakr**, who bought and liberated many tortured slaves.
- The **family of Yasir** — Yasir, his wife **Sumayyah**, and son **'Ammar** — were tortured savagely. **Sumayyah** was killed for refusing to renounce her faith, becoming the **first martyr (shahidah) of Islam.** As the Prophet ﷺ passed them being tortured, he comforted them: *"Patience, O family of Yasir, for your appointed place is Paradise."*

To escape this, the Prophet ﷺ permitted a group of believers to **migrate to Abyssinia (Ethiopia)** — a Christian kingdom ruled by a just king, the **Negus (An-Najashi)**. There, despite Quraysh sending envoys to extradite them, the Negus — moved by the Qur'an's words about Maryam and Isa — **protected them** and refused to hand them over. This was the **first Hijrah** in Islam.

Frustrated, Quraysh imposed a **total social and economic boycott** on the Prophet's ﷺ clan (Banu Hashim), confining them to a valley (Shi'b Abi Talib) for about **three years** — no trade, no marriage, no food — until they were eating leaves and leather from hunger. The boycott eventually collapsed. This chapter explores the torture, the martyrs, the first migration, and the boycott — the crucible in which the believers' faith was proven.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Persecution of the Weak

Quraysh targeted those without protection most savagely, to terrorize others and "prove" Islam could be crushed:

> ✅ **Bilal ibn Rabah (in the Seerah; referenced in authentic narrations):** An Abyssinian slave owned by Umayyah ibn Khalaf, Bilal was tortured in the scorching desert — laid on burning sand with a heavy boulder on his chest — and ordered to renounce Allah and praise the idols. He refused, repeating only: ***"Ahad! Ahad!"*** ("One! One!"). **Abu Bakr** bought and **freed him.** Bilal later became the **first mu'adhdhin (caller to prayer) of Islam** — the once-tortured slave whose voice would call millions to prayer, his elevation a living proof that *tawhid* makes the lowly noble.

> ✅ **The family of Yasir:** Yasir, **Sumayyah** (his wife), and **'Ammar** (his son) were among the most brutally tortured. As the Prophet ﷺ passed them, unable to physically rescue them, he gave them the most precious comfort: **"Patience, O family of Yasir, for your appointed meeting-place is Paradise"** (*Sabran, ya Aal Yasir, fa-inna maw'idakumu al-jannah*). **Sumayyah refused to renounce her faith and was killed** — becoming the **first martyr (the first shahid/shahidah) in Islam.** Her son 'Ammar was tortured so severely that, fearing death, he once uttered words of disbelief under duress, then was wracked with grief — and Allah revealed comfort:

> ✅ **Surah An-Nahl (16:106):** "...except for one who is forced [to renounce faith] while his heart is secure in faith..." — *Allah excused 'Ammar: faith uttered against under torture, with the heart firm, is forgiven. A mercy and a ruling (the basis of the concept that coercion removes sin in such cases).*

### 3.2 The First Migration — Abyssinia

When the persecution became unbearable, the Prophet ﷺ said: *"Go to the land of Abyssinia, for there is a king there under whom no one is wronged"* — and a group of believers (including **'Uthman, his wife Ruqayyah** the Prophet's ﷺ daughter, and **Ja'far ibn Abi Talib**) **migrated to Abyssinia (Habashah)**, a Christian kingdom across the Red Sea, ruled by the just **Negus (An-Najashi).**

Quraysh sent envoys (with gifts) to demand the refugees be handed back. But in the Negus's court, **Ja'far ibn Abi Talib** gave a magnificent speech describing Islam: how they had been in *Jahiliyyah* — worshipping idols, mistreating the weak, severing kinship — until Allah sent a Messenger calling them to *tawhid*, truthfulness, kindness, and worship of the One God. When the Negus asked what the Qur'an said about Isa and Maryam, Ja'far recited from **Surah Maryam** — and the Negus **wept**, saying this and what Isa brought "come from the same source (niche of light)." He **refused to extradite them** and granted them protection. This **first Hijrah** preserved a portion of the community and demonstrated Islam's affinity with the true followers of Isa.

> 📖 The Negus (An-Najashi) is held to have **secretly accepted Islam**; when he died (years later, after the Hijrah to Madinah), the Prophet ﷺ prayed the funeral prayer (*salat al-gha'ib*) for him in absentia (**Sahih al-Bukhari 1320 / Muslim 951**) — an authentic confirmation of his faith and honour.

### 3.3 The Boycott

Unable to stop Islam by torture or extradition, Quraysh escalated to a **total boycott** of the Prophet's ﷺ clan, **Banu Hashim** (and Banu al-Muttalib), who — believers and non-believers alike — protected him:
- **No trade** with them (buying or selling).
- **No intermarriage** with them.
- **No social contact.**
A document of these terms was hung in the Ka'bah. The clan was confined to a valley (**Shi'b Abi Talib**) for about **three years**, enduring **severe famine** — reduced to eating leaves and boiled leather, the cries of hungry children heard outside the valley. Yet the believers **did not abandon the Prophet ﷺ.** The boycott eventually **collapsed** when several fair-minded Quraysh repudiated it, and (per the Seerah) the parchment was found eaten by termites except the part bearing Allah's name — a sign. This chapter of suffering proved that **persecution could not break sincere faith.**

> ✅ **On Khabbab's report (Bukhari 3612):** Khabbab ibn al-Aratt (himself tortured) came to the Prophet ﷺ — who was resting in the shade of the Ka'bah — complaining of the persecution and asking him to pray for help. The Prophet ﷺ reminded him of believers *before* them who were sawn in half or combed with iron combs yet did not abandon their faith, and promised: **"By Allah, Allah will complete this matter until a rider travels from Sana'a to Hadramawt fearing none but Allah... but you are being hasty."** A profound lesson: **victory is certain, but it comes in Allah's time, through patience — not by demanding it instantly.**

## 5. The Themes

1. **Faith is proven under fire.** Bilal's "Ahad! Ahad!" under torture, Sumayyah's martyrdom, the family of Yasir's patience — the believers' endurance proved their faith was *real*, not convenient. Suffering separated the sincere from the rest.
2. **Tawhid ennobles the lowly.** A tortured slave (Bilal) became the first caller to prayer; the weak and enslaved were the vanguard of Islam. Before Allah, the slave who says "One!" outranks the free chieftain who bows to idols.
3. **Coercion removes sin** (16:106). Allah's mercy excused 'Ammar for words forced under torture while his heart stayed firm — a principle of justice and compassion.
4. **Seek refuge with the just, even across faiths.** The Muslims found protection with a *Christian* king (the Negus) who honoured truth — showing Islam's affinity with sincere followers of Isa, and the value of just rulers.
5. **Victory is certain but requires patience** (Bukhari 3612). The Prophet ﷺ promised triumph "but you are hasty" — relief comes in Allah's time, earned through endurance.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Hold to "Ahad! Ahad!" in your trials.** Bilal's single word under torture is the model: whatever the pressure, cling to *tawhid*. No hardship justifies abandoning Allah.
2. **Honour comes from faith, not status.** Islam raised slaves above chieftains. Never measure people by wealth or rank; measure by faith and character.
3. **Allah excuses what is beyond your capacity** (16:106; cf. 2:286). Coercion, genuine inability, and honest mistakes are met with His mercy. Don't despair under duress.
4. **Be patient — victory is coming, in Allah's time.** "You are being hasty" (Bukhari 3612). Demanding instant relief is impatience; trust Allah's perfect timing.
5. **Support the persecuted and stand with the just.** The Negus protected the weak; Abu Bakr freed the tortured. Use your means and standing to defend the oppressed.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The early Muslims fought back violently against persecution."**
> ✅ In Makkah, the Muslims were commanded to **patience and endurance**, not to fight (fighting was not yet permitted). They suffered torture, migration, and boycott **without armed retaliation** — the very opposite of "spread by the sword."

> ❌ **"'Ammar lost his faith by uttering disbelief under torture."**
> ✅ Allah **excused him** (16:106) — words forced under torture, with the heart firm in faith, are forgiven. His faith was intact.

> ❌ **"Muslims and Christians were always enemies."**
> ✅ The first Muslim refugees were **protected by a Christian king** (the Negus), who wept at the Qur'an's account of Isa and Maryam and secretly accepted Islam (Bukhari 1320). Sincere people of earlier faiths recognized the truth.

> ❌ **"The persecution shows Islam failed early on."**
> ✅ It shows the believers' **sincerity and endurance** — and the Prophet ﷺ promised certain victory through patience (Bukhari 3612), which history fulfilled. Trials were the crucible, not the defeat.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Who was the first martyr of Islam?**
A: **Sumayyah bint Khayyat**, mother of 'Ammar, killed for refusing to renounce her faith under torture.

**Q: How did Bilal endure his torture?**
A: By repeating only **"Ahad! Ahad!"** ("Allah is One!") — refusing to praise the idols, no matter the agony. Abu Bakr later freed him.

**Q: Did the early Muslims fight back?**
A: **No** — in Makkah they were commanded to patience and endurance; fighting was not yet permitted. They suffered without armed retaliation.

**Q: Why did Muslims flee to a Christian country?**
A: Because the Negus was a **just ruler** "under whom no one is wronged." He protected them, moved by the Qur'an's reverence for Isa and Maryam, and accepted Islam.

**Q: What was the boycott?**
A: A total social and economic blockade of the Prophet's ﷺ clan (~3 years) — no trade, marriage, or contact — causing severe famine, until it collapsed.
