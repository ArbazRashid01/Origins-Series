# Season 12 · Episode 47
# The Night Journey
### The Year of Sorrow, Al-Isra' wa'l-Mi'raj, and the Gift of Prayer

> *"Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa, whose surroundings We have blessed, to show him of Our signs."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Isra (17:1)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter completes Season 12. After years of persecution came the **Year of Sorrow** — the deaths of the Prophet's ﷺ beloved wife **Khadijah** and his protective uncle **Abu Talib** — followed by the painful rejection at **Ta'if.** Then, in the Prophet's ﷺ darkest hour, Allah honoured and consoled him with the greatest miracle of his life: **Al-Isra' wa'l-Mi'raj** — the Night Journey from Makkah to Jerusalem and the Ascension through the heavens — during which the **five daily prayers** were prescribed. It is a story of divine comfort after grief, and the gift that anchors every Muslim's day.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Around the tenth year of his mission, the Prophet ﷺ suffered two devastating losses in quick succession — **the Year of Sorrow (*'Am al-Huzn*)**: his wife **Khadijah** (his greatest support, the first believer) and his uncle **Abu Talib** (his protector for life) both died. Their deaths left him personally bereaved and politically exposed, as Quraysh intensified their abuse. Seeking new ground for the message, he travelled to **Ta'if** — but was **mocked, rejected, and stoned** by its people until his feet bled. In that moment of utter rejection, his du'a was not for vengeance but a humble plea expressing his reliance on Allah ("if You are not angry with me, I do not care..."). When the **angel of the mountains** offered to crush Ta'if between two mountains, the Prophet ﷺ **refused**, hoping their descendants would one day worship Allah — a sublime display of mercy.

Then, in this darkest period, Allah honoured His Messenger with the **Isra' and Mi'raj.** In a single miraculous night, the Prophet ﷺ was carried (on the **Buraq**) from the Ka'bah (**al-Masjid al-Haram**) to Jerusalem (**al-Masjid al-Aqsa**) — the **Isra'** — where he **led all the previous prophets in prayer** (a symbol of his leadership of all prophethood). Then he **ascended through the seven heavens** — the **Mi'raj** — meeting prophets at each level (Adam, Isa & Yahya, Yusuf, Idris, Harun, Musa, Ibrahim), reaching the **Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary (Sidrat al-Muntaha)** and the divine Presence beyond all creation. There, **the five daily prayers were prescribed** — originally fifty, reduced (on Musa's advice and the Prophet's ﷺ repeated requests) to five in practice but fifty in reward. He returned the same night. This chapter explores the Year of Sorrow, Ta'if, and the Night Journey — divine consolation and the gift of *salah*.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Year of Sorrow and Ta'if

After the boycott (Episode 46), the Prophet ﷺ was struck by profound personal loss — the **Year of Sorrow**:
- **Khadijah** (may Allah be pleased with her) died — his beloved wife of 25 years, the first believer, his comfort and supporter in every hardship (Season 11).
- **Abu Talib** died — his uncle who had protected him for decades (though, heartbreakingly, he did not embrace Islam before death, despite the Prophet's ﷺ pleas — see §7).

With his protector gone, Quraysh grew bolder in their abuse. Seeking a new audience, the Prophet ﷺ travelled to **Ta'if** (home of Thaqif), but its leaders rejected him and incited their youth and slaves to **pelt him with stones** until his blessed feet bled. Wounded and grieving, he made one of the most moving du'as in the Seerah — a prayer of pure reliance, complaining only of *his own weakness* to Allah, and saying: *"If You are not angry with me, I do not care... but Your protection is more expansive for me."* When **the angel of the mountains** came and offered to **crush Ta'if** between two mountains, the Prophet ﷺ **declined**, saying: *"Rather, I hope that Allah will bring forth from their loins those who will worship Allah alone"* (**Sahih al-Bukhari 3231 / Muslim 1795**).
>
> **Tafsir/lesson:** This is the **summit of prophetic mercy.** At his lowest, rejected and bleeding, with the power to destroy his persecutors offered to him, the Prophet ﷺ chose **mercy and hope** over revenge — thinking of future generations. (Indeed, Ta'if later became a city of Islam.) This is *"a mercy to the worlds"* (21:107) in action.

### 3.2 Al-Isra' — The Night Journey to Jerusalem

In this darkest period, Allah honoured and consoled His Messenger with an unparalleled miracle:

> ✅ **Surah Al-Isra (17:1)**
>
> **Translation:** "Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram (Makkah) to al-Masjid al-Aqsa (Jerusalem), whose surroundings We have blessed, to show him of Our signs. Indeed, He is the Hearing, the Seeing."
>
> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 3887 / Muslim 162, 164:** The Prophet ﷺ described how his chest was opened and purified again (cf. Season 11), and he was mounted on the **Buraq** — a swift, miraculous steed — and carried **in a single night** from the Ka'bah to **al-Masjid al-Aqsa in Jerusalem.** There, he **led all the previous prophets in prayer** as their imam.
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** The **Isra'** (night journey) linked the two holiest mosques and connected the Prophet ﷺ to the legacy of all the prophets — and his **leading them in prayer** symbolized that he is the **seal and leader of all prophethood**, inheriting and completing their mission. Al-Aqsa, the second mosque ever built (Season 05, E21), thus became the Muslims' **first qiblah** (before it was changed to the Ka'bah in Madinah).

### 3.3 Al-Mi'raj — The Ascension Through the Heavens

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 349 / 3887 / Muslim 162-164:** From Jerusalem, the Prophet ﷺ **ascended through the seven heavens** (the **Mi'raj**), meeting a prophet at each level:
> - **1st heaven:** Adam (the father of mankind).
> - **2nd:** Isa (Jesus) and Yahya (John).
> - **3rd:** Yusuf (given "half of all beauty").
> - **4th:** Idris.
> - **5th:** Harun (Aaron).
> - **6th:** Musa (Moses).
> - **7th:** Ibrahim (leaning against al-Bayt al-Ma'mur, the heavenly Ka'bah — Season 01, E02).
> Then he was taken to **Sidrat al-Muntaha** (the Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary — the limit beyond which none pass), saw the great signs of his Lord (53:13-18), and was brought into the divine Presence (in a manner befitting Allah).
>
> **Tafsir:** The Mi'raj is the Prophet's ﷺ ascension to the highest reaches of creation and the divine Presence — the greatest honour given to any human, and a direct **consolation** after the Year of Sorrow: as the world rejected him, the heavens received him. Meeting all the prophets affirmed the **unity of the prophetic chain** (the thesis of this entire encyclopedia — "the prophets are brothers," Bukhari 3442).

### 3.4 The Gift of the Five Prayers

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 349 / Muslim 162:** At the divine Presence, Allah prescribed **fifty prayers a day** for the Muslims. As the Prophet ﷺ descended, **Musa** advised him to return and ask for a reduction, knowing the people could not bear fifty. The Prophet ﷺ returned repeatedly, and Allah reduced them — until they were **five prayers in practice, but fifty in reward** ("the word does not change with Me," and "whoever intends a good deed..."). Out of shyness before Allah, the Prophet ﷺ did not ask for further reduction.
>
> **Tafsir:** The **five daily prayers** — the central pillar of a Muslim's daily life — were uniquely prescribed **directly, above the seven heavens**, not through ordinary revelation on earth. This signifies the **supreme importance of *salah***: it is the believer's daily *mi'raj*, the direct connection to Allah. That five carry the reward of fifty is Allah's mercy and generosity. Prayer was the gift Allah gave His Messenger to console and strengthen the believers — a daily ascension of the heart, granted in the year of his greatest grief.

### 3.5 The Test of Faith — and Abu Bakr "as-Siddiq"

When the Prophet ﷺ told Makkah of the Night Journey, the disbelievers **mocked** it as impossible (Makkah to Jerusalem and back in one night?). Some weak believers wavered. They ran to **Abu Bakr**, expecting him to doubt. His response sealed his title forever: *"If he said it, then it is true... I believe him about news of the heavens [revelation], so why would I not believe him about this?"* — earning him the title ***as-Siddiq*** ("the great affirmer of truth"). The Prophet ﷺ, when challenged to describe Jerusalem (which he had visited only that night), described **al-Aqsa** accurately, and accurately reported a caravan he had passed — proofs of the journey's reality.
>
> **Lesson:** The Night Journey was a **test of faith** — separating those whose belief rested on *trust in the Prophet ﷺ and Allah's power* from those who limited reality to what they could explain. Abu Bakr's instant faith is the model: trusting the truthful one even when the matter exceeds the mind's grasp.

## 5. The Themes

1. **Allah's honour follows the believer's hardship.** In the Year of Sorrow — bereaved, rejected, stoned — the Prophet ﷺ was given the greatest miracle and honour. *After hardship comes ease* (94:5-6). The heavens received him when earth rejected him.
2. **Mercy over revenge.** Offered the destruction of Ta'if, the Prophet ﷺ chose hope for their descendants (Bukhari 3231) — the embodiment of "a mercy to the worlds" (21:107).
3. **Prayer is the believer's mi'raj.** *Salah* was prescribed directly in the heavens — the daily, direct connection to Allah, the central pillar, and the gift of consolation. Five in practice, fifty in reward — pure mercy.
4. **The unity of all prophets.** The Prophet ﷺ led them in prayer and met them in the heavens — he is the seal and leader of the one prophetic message (the thesis of this whole encyclopedia).
5. **Faith trusts beyond the visible.** The Night Journey tested whether belief rested on trust in Allah's power or on the limits of human reason. Abu Bakr "as-Siddiq" is the model of faith in the unseen.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **After your hardest season, expect Allah's mercy.** The Prophet's ﷺ darkest year held his greatest honour. Never lose hope; relief and elevation often follow the deepest trials.
2. **Choose mercy when you have the power to harm.** The Prophet's ﷺ refusal to destroy Ta'if is the gold standard — respond to rejection with hope for guidance, not vengeance.
3. **Guard your prayers — they are your daily ascension.** *Salah* was the gift of the heavens. Treat the five prayers as the precious, direct link to Allah that they are.
4. **Complain only to Allah.** At Ta'if, the Prophet ﷺ poured out his weakness to Allah alone, with trust, not despair. Make Allah the address of your sorrows.
5. **Trust the truthful even beyond your understanding.** Be like Abu Bakr — faith rests on the trustworthiness of the source and the power of Allah, not on whether everything fits our limited reasoning.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The Night Journey was just a dream/vision, not real."**
> ✅ The majority of scholars hold it was a **real, physical and spiritual journey** (body and soul), based on 17:1 ("took His *Servant* by night") and the authentic hadith. The Quraysh wouldn't have mocked a mere dream. (A minority view it as a vision, but the stronger position is that it was real.)

> ❌ **"Abu Talib was secretly a Muslim because he protected the Prophet ﷺ."**
> ✅ The authentic narrations indicate **Abu Talib died upon the religion of his forefathers**, despite the Prophet's ﷺ plea at his deathbed (Sahih al-Bukhari 1360). This was a deep grief for the Prophet ﷺ — and another proof that **love and protection don't equal faith** (faith over family, again). *(His exact final state is known to Allah; the texts indicate he did not declare Islam.)*

> ❌ **"The five prayers are a burden Islam imposed harshly."**
> ✅ They were **reduced from fifty to five out of mercy**, kept at the **reward of fifty** (Bukhari 349). They are a gift and a mercy — the believer's daily connection to Allah, not a burden.

> ❌ **"Muslims pray toward Jerusalem."**
> ✅ Al-Aqsa was the **first qiblah** (honoured by the Isra'), but the qiblah was later changed to the **Ka'bah in Makkah** (2:144, in Madinah). Muslims pray toward the Ka'bah.

## 8. Weak / Disputed Points (Labelled)

---
**📖 Note — The order of prophets in the heavens & the timing of the Isra'**

- The specific prophets at each heaven are from authentic hadith (Bukhari 3887). Minor variations in ordering exist between narrations; the core is sound.
- The **exact date/year** of the Isra' (commonly placed around year 10-11 of the mission, near the Hijrah) is debated in the Seerah; it is **not** a matter of doctrine.
---
**⚠️ Folklore — Elaborate Mi'raj embellishments**

- Later popular literature added many fanciful details to the Mi'raj (descriptions of punishments, elaborate scenes) — some from weak sources. **Rely on the authentic hadith** (Bukhari/Muslim) and avoid the embellished folk versions.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was the Year of Sorrow?**
A: The year the Prophet ﷺ lost both **Khadijah** (his wife) and **Abu Talib** (his protective uncle) — a time of deep grief and increased persecution.

**Q: What happened at Ta'if?**
A: The Prophet ﷺ sought support there but was **rejected and stoned**; offered the city's destruction, he **refused**, hoping its descendants would believe — a peak of mercy.

**Q: What is the Isra' and Mi'raj?**
A: The miraculous **night journey** from Makkah to Jerusalem (Isra') and the **ascension** through the heavens to the divine Presence (Mi'raj), during which the five daily prayers were prescribed.

**Q: Why were the prayers reduced from fifty to five?**
A: Out of **Allah's mercy**, at Musa's urging and the Prophet's ﷺ requests — kept at the **reward of fifty** (Bukhari 349). Salah is a gift, not a burden.

**Q: Was the Night Journey physical or a dream?**
A: The **stronger, majority view** is that it was a **real, physical-and-spiritual journey** (17:1; authentic hadith), not merely a dream.
