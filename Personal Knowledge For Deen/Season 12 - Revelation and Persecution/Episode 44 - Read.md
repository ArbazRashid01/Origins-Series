### The Early Revelation and the First Believers

> *"O you who covers himself [with a garment], arise and warn!"*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Muddaththir (74:1-2)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Revelation has begun (Season 11). This chapter covers the **first phase of the Prophet's ﷺ mission** — the command to "arise and warn," the **secret call** in the early years, the first believers who entered Islam (the *Sabiqun*, the foremost), the nature of the early Makkan revelation, and the small, devoted community that formed in secret before the public proclamation (Episode 45). It is the quiet, foundational beginning of the greatest movement in human history.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

After the first revelation in Hira (Season 11), there was a pause (*fatrah*), then revelation resumed with the command: **"O you who covers himself, arise and warn! And your Lord magnify, and your garments purify..."** (Surah Al-Muddaththir 74:1-5) — Allah commissioning Muhammad ﷺ to **begin calling people to Allah.** For the first **roughly three years**, the call was conducted **quietly and privately**, building a core of sincere believers before going public.

The first to believe were a remarkable group of the **foremost (*as-Sabiqun al-Awwalun*)**:
- **Khadijah** — his wife, the first believer of all (Season 11).
- **Abu Bakr as-Siddiq** — his closest friend, the first free adult man to believe, who immediately and without hesitation accepted, and brought many others to Islam.
- **'Ali ibn Abi Talib** — his young cousin, the first child/youth to believe.
- **Zayd ibn Harithah** — his freed servant whom he loved as a son, the first freed slave to believe.
- And through Abu Bakr: **'Uthman, az-Zubayr, 'Abd ar-Rahman ibn 'Awf, Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, Talhah** — many of the future greatest Companions.

The early revelation focused on the **essentials**: the Oneness of Allah (*tawhid*), the Resurrection and accountability, the rejection of idols, gratitude to the Creator, purification of the soul, prayer, and care for the poor and the orphan. These early Makkan surahs are short, powerful, rhythmic, and deeply moving — reshaping hearts before reshaping society. The believers met **secretly**, often at the house of **al-Arqam** (Dar al-Arqam), to learn, pray, and grow. This chapter explores the commission, the first believers, and the seed of the ummah planted in secret.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 "Arise and Warn!" — The Commission

> ✅ **Surah Al-Muddaththir (74:1-7)**
>
> **Translation:** "O you who covers himself [with a garment], **arise and warn!** And your Lord magnify (*wa rabbaka fakabbir*), and your garments purify, and uncleanliness avoid, and do not confer favour to acquire more, but for your Lord be patient."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** After the first revelation and a brief pause (*fatrah*), Jibril came again, and these verses **commissioned the Prophet ﷺ as a warner** — to *arise* from seclusion and *call* humanity to Allah. Each command is foundational: **"arise and warn"** (the mission of da'wah); **"your Lord magnify"** (proclaim Allah's greatness — *tawhid*); **"your garments purify"** (outward and inner purity); **"be patient for your Lord"** (the patience the long road would require). With these words, the private spiritual experience became a **public mission** — though it would begin quietly.

### 3.2 The First Believers — The Foremost (As-Sabiqun)

The earliest converts hold an exalted, irreplaceable rank in Islam — those who believed **when belief was hardest and most dangerous**:

> ✅ **Surah Al-Waqi'ah (56:10-11):** "And the foremost, the foremost (*as-Sabiqun as-Sabiqun*) — those are the ones brought near [to Allah]."
> ✅ **Surah At-Tawbah (9:100):** "And the first forerunners among the Muhajirun and the Ansar... Allah is pleased with them and they with Him."

| First believer | Distinction |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Khadijah** | First believer of all; the Prophet's ﷺ wife and supporter (Season 11) |
| **Abu Bakr as-Siddiq** | First free adult man; the Prophet's ﷺ closest friend; "as-Siddiq" (the truthful affirmer); brought many to Islam |
| **'Ali ibn Abi Talib** | First youth/child to believe (~10 yrs); raised in the Prophet's ﷺ household |
| **Zayd ibn Harithah** | First freed slave to believe; beloved of the Prophet ﷺ |

> ✅ **On Abu Bakr (referenced in many authentic narrations):** The Prophet ﷺ said: *"I never called anyone to Islam but that they showed some hesitation — except Abu Bakr, who did not hesitate when I told him"* (meaning of a well-known report). Abu Bakr's instant faith — based on his lifelong knowledge of the Prophet's ﷺ truthfulness (*Al-Amin*, Season 11) — earned him the title **as-Siddiq** ("the great affirmer of truth"). He immediately used his wealth and standing to bring others to Islam and to free tortured slaves (Episode 46).

### 3.3 The Nature of the Early Makkan Revelation

The surahs revealed in this early Makkan period (e.g., Al-'Alaq, Al-Muddaththir, Al-Muzzammil, the short surahs of *Juz' 'Amma*) share distinct features:
- **Powerful, rhythmic, and concise** — easy to memorize, striking to the heart.
- **Focused on the essentials of faith**: the **Oneness of Allah**, the **Resurrection and Day of Judgement**, **accountability**, **Paradise and Hell**, **gratitude to the Creator**, and the **purification of the soul.**
- **Strong on social ethics**: caring for the **orphan**, feeding the **poor**, condemning **arrogance, greed, and hoarding wealth** (e.g., Surah Al-Ma'un, Al-Humazah, Al-Takathur).
- **Building conviction before legislation**: the detailed laws (prayer specifics, fasting, zakat amounts, etc.) came **later, in Madinah** — first, *faith and the heart* had to be firmly established.

> 📖 **A profound principle of the mission:** Islam was built **iman-first.** For ~13 years in Makkah, the focus was on *tawhid* and the Hereafter — anchoring the heart in Allah. Only after that foundation was laid did the bulk of detailed law come (in Madinah). This is a timeless model of nurturing faith: *conviction before regulation, the Creator before the commands.*

### 3.4 The Secret Community and Dar al-Arqam

For about **three years**, the call was **secret** — prudent in a hostile, idolatrous Makkah where the elite (the *mala'*, as with Nuh and every prophet) would violently oppose any threat to their idols and authority. The believers met clandestinely at **Dar al-Arqam** (the house of al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam, a young convert), where the Prophet ﷺ taught them the Qur'an, how to pray, and nurtured them into a devoted brotherhood. This small, hidden community — perhaps a few dozen — was the **seed** of the global ummah, grown in secret like a plant before it breaks the soil.

## 5. The Themes

1. **The mission begins with "warn" and "magnify your Lord."** The core of the call is *tawhid* and accountability — calling people to the Creator and the Hereafter (74:2-3).
2. **Faith before law (iman-first).** Islam spent 13 Makkan years building conviction in Allah and the Hereafter *before* most detailed legislation. The heart is built before the structure.
3. **The foremost have the highest rank.** Those who believe when it is hardest and costliest — like the Makkan *Sabiqun* — are "brought near" to Allah (56:11). Early, sincere, costly faith is precious.
4. **Great movements begin small and quiet.** A few dozen believers meeting secretly at Dar al-Arqam became a civilization. Don't despise small, sincere beginnings.
5. **Character earns instant trust.** Abu Bakr believed *immediately* because he knew the Prophet's ﷺ lifelong truthfulness — the fruit of *Al-Amin* (Season 11). Integrity opens hearts.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Build faith before rules — in yourself and others.** Anchor the heart in Allah and the Hereafter first; the commitment to obey flows from love and conviction, not mere instruction.
2. **Be among the foremost in good.** Allah honours those who race to faith and righteousness when it's hard (56:10-11). Don't wait for the easy moment to do right.
3. **Sincere effort in obscurity matters.** The secret years at Dar al-Arqam were the foundation. Quiet, unseen devotion builds what public success later reveals.
4. **Your reputation is your da'wah.** The Prophet's ﷺ character made Abu Bakr believe instantly. Live so that your integrity itself invites others to good.
5. **Patience is commanded from day one.** "For your Lord, be patient" (74:7) — the path of truth requires patience before it requires anything else.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Islam was spread by the sword from the start."**
> ✅ The first ~13 years were **purely peaceful da'wah** — secret, then public preaching — during which the Muslims were the **persecuted, not the persecutors** (Episode 46). The early mission was conviction, patience, and endurance, not force.

> ❌ **"The detailed laws of Islam came all at once."**
> ✅ Revelation was **gradual** over 23 years. The Makkan period focused on *faith* (tawhid, the Hereafter); most detailed law came in Madinah — a wise, staged nurturing.

> ❌ **"The Prophet ﷺ sought followers for power."**
> ✅ Early belief brought **danger and loss**, not power. The *Sabiqun* gained nothing worldly; their faith was pure sincerity (9:100).

> ❌ **"The secret phase shows the Prophet ﷺ was being deceptive."**
> ✅ It was **prudent wisdom** in a hostile environment — building a strong core before public confrontation, a sound strategy in da'wah (and the message was never *false*, just not yet broadcast).

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Who was the very first to believe?**
A: **Khadijah** (the Prophet's ﷺ wife) overall; **Abu Bakr** among free adult men; **'Ali** among youth; **Zayd** among freed slaves.

**Q: How long was the call kept secret?**
A: About **three years** of private da'wah before the public proclamation (Episode 45).

**Q: What did the early revelation focus on?**
A: The essentials — **tawhid**, the **Resurrection and accountability**, purification of the soul, prayer, gratitude, and care for the poor and orphan. Detailed laws came later in Madinah.

**Q: Why begin secretly?**
A: Prudence in a hostile Makkah — to build a strong, devoted core before facing the inevitable persecution from the idol-worshipping elite.

**Q: Why are the early believers so revered?**
A: They believed when it was **hardest and most dangerous**, with no worldly gain — the purest faith. Allah calls them "the foremost, brought near" (56:10-11) and is "pleased with them" (9:100).
