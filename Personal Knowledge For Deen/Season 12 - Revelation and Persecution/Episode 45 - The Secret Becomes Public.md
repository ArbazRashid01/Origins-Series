# Season 12 · Episode 45
# The Secret Becomes Public
### The Call on Mount Safa and the Fury of Quraysh

> *"So declare openly what you are commanded, and turn away from the polytheists."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Hijr (15:94)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

After three years of secret da'wah (Episode 44), Allah commanded the Prophet ﷺ to take the message **public.** This chapter covers the dramatic open proclamation — beginning with his own family and the famous warning on **Mount Safa** — and the reasons Quraysh reacted with such **fury**: not because they doubted his honesty, but because *tawhid* threatened their idols, their status, their economy, and their pride. It explains *why* the opposition was so fierce and sets up the persecution of the believers (Episode 46).

---

## 1. Episode Summary

When the believers had grown strong enough, Allah commanded: *"And warn your closest kindred"* (26:214) and *"declare openly what you are commanded"* (15:94). The Prophet ﷺ first gathered his **own clan (Banu Hashim)** and warned them. Then he climbed **Mount Safa** and called out to all of Quraysh by their clan names. When they gathered, he asked: *"If I told you that an army was behind this mountain about to attack you, would you believe me?"* They replied: *"Yes — we have never known you to lie."* He said: **"Then I am a warner to you before a severe punishment."** His own uncle **Abu Lahab** responded with a curse — "May you perish! Is this why you gathered us?" — earning a Qur'anic condemnation (Surah Al-Masad, 111).

Quraysh's reaction escalated from mockery to **active hostility.** Crucially, their opposition was **not** because they thought Muhammad ﷺ was lying (they had called him *Al-Amin* for 40 years) — it was because his message of **one God** threatened everything they valued:
1. **Their idols** and the religion of their forefathers (pride and tradition).
2. **Their status** — Makkah's prestige and their leadership of the Arabs rested on being custodians of the idol-filled Ka'bah.
3. **Their economy** — the pilgrimage to the idols was a major source of income.
4. **Their pride and equality before God** — *tawhid* meant the slave and the master, the rich and the poor, were equal before Allah, threatening the tribal hierarchy.

They tried bribery (offering him wealth, kingship, the most beautiful women) — to which he gave his immortal reply: *"By Allah, if they placed the sun in my right hand and the moon in my left to abandon this matter, I would not — until Allah makes it prevail or I perish in it."* This chapter explores the public call, the reasons for the opposition, and the Prophet's ﷺ unshakable resolve.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Command to Go Public

> ✅ **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:214):** "And warn your closest kindred."
> ✅ **Surah Al-Hijr (15:94):** "So declare openly what you are commanded, and turn away from the polytheists."
>
> **Tafsir:** Allah commanded the Prophet ﷺ to begin with **those nearest to him** (his own clan) — for sincerity in da'wah starts at home, and to show he sought no exemption for his own family. Then the broader command: **"declare openly"** — the secret phase was over; the message would now be proclaimed to all.

### 3.2 The Call on Mount Safa

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 4770 / Muslim 208** — *Authentic.* When *"warn your closest kindred"* was revealed, the Prophet ﷺ climbed **Mount Safa** and called out, "O Banu Fihr! O Banu 'Adiyy!" (the clans of Quraysh) until they gathered. He said: **"If I told you that horsemen were in the valley about to attack you, would you believe me?"** They said: **"Yes — we have never experienced any lie from you."** He said: **"Then I am a warner to you before a severe punishment."** His uncle **Abu Lahab** said: *"May you perish! Did you gather us only for this?"* — and Allah revealed **Surah Al-Masad** in response.
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** This is a pivotal moment. The Prophet ﷺ **used his lifelong reputation as evidence**: "you have never known me to lie about worldly matters — so why would I lie about Allah?" Their own admission ("you never lie") was the proof against them. His honesty (*Al-Amin*, Season 11) was now his credential. Abu Lahab's hostile outburst marked the beginning of open opposition — even from his own family.

### 3.3 Surah Al-Masad — The Condemnation of Abu Lahab

> ✅ **Surah Al-Masad (111:1-5)**
>
> **Translation:** "May the hands of Abu Lahab be ruined, and ruined is he. His wealth will not avail him or that which he gained. He will burn in a Fire of [blazing] flame. And his wife [as well] — the carrier of firewood. Around her neck is a rope of twisted fiber."
>
> **Tafsir:** A remarkable surah: it names **Abu Lahab** (the Prophet's ﷺ own uncle) and his wife and condemns them by name — and predicts he will **die a disbeliever.** This is also a subtle **miracle/prophecy**: the surah was revealed years before Abu Lahab died, yet he never even *pretended* to accept Islam to falsify it — he died exactly as the surah foretold. It teaches that **kinship to the Prophet ﷺ saves no one** (echoing Nuh's son, Lut's wife — Seasons 3, 5): only faith saves. Wealth and lineage ("his wealth will not avail him") are worthless against Allah.

### 3.4 Why Quraysh Opposed So Fiercely

A crucial point of understanding: Quraysh's hostility was **not** rooted in doubting the Prophet's ﷺ honesty. The Qur'an itself reveals this:

> ✅ **Surah Al-An'am (6:33):** "We know that you are saddened by what they say. And indeed, they do not call you untruthful, but it is the verses of Allah that the wrongdoers reject."
>
> **Tafsir:** They knew he was truthful — they rejected the **message**, not the man. Why? The message of *tawhid* threatened:
> 1. **Their idols and ancestral religion** — admitting Islam meant their forefathers were wrong (pride and tradition — the same barrier as Nuh's and every prophet's people).
> 2. **Their leadership** — Makkah's prestige among the Arabs flowed from guarding the (idol-filled) Ka'bah; pure *tawhid* threatened their religious authority.
> 3. **Their economy** — the polytheistic pilgrimage was lucrative; abolishing the idols threatened their income.
> 4. **The social hierarchy** — *tawhid* declared all humans equal before Allah; the proud chieftains could not stomach being equal to slaves and the poor (hence their demand that the Prophet ﷺ dismiss the "lowly" believers — echoing Nuh's people, S3E10).
> In short: **truth threatened their power, pride, and profit** — the eternal reasons the established order resists guidance.

### 3.5 The Bribes and the Immortal Refusal

When mockery and pressure failed, Quraysh sent **'Utbah ibn Rabi'ah** to offer the Prophet ﷺ a deal: *if he wanted **wealth**, they would make him the richest; if **status**, they would make him their **king/leader**; if **marriage**, the most beautiful women; if he was **possessed**, they would find him a cure* — anything, if he would just **stop attacking their gods.** The Prophet ﷺ responded by reciting Qur'an (Surah Fussilat) until 'Utbah was stunned. And in a famous report, when his uncle Abu Talib, under pressure, asked him to ease off, the Prophet ﷺ declared:

> **"O my uncle, by Allah, if they placed the sun in my right hand and the moon in my left, on the condition that I abandon this matter, I would not abandon it until Allah makes it prevail or I perish in it."** *(Widely reported in the Seerah.)*
>
> **Tafsir:** This is the **defining statement of his resolve.** No worldly inducement — wealth, power, women, comfort — could buy him off, because his mission was **from Allah**, not for himself. It also proves his sincerity: a man seeking wealth or power would have *taken the deal*; he refused everything. His only goal was that the truth prevail.

This exposes that most rejection of truth is *willful*, not intellectual — a recurring theme from Iblis (S1E4) to Pharaoh (S7) to Quraysh.

## 5. The Themes

1. **Truth threatens the established order — not because it's false, but because it costs the powerful.** Quraysh rejected *tawhid* to protect their idols, status, economy, and pride (6:33) — the same reasons elites rejected every prophet.
2. **Lineage saves no one.** Abu Lahab, the Prophet's ﷺ own uncle, was condemned by name (111) — only faith saves (Nuh's son, Lut's wife, again).
3. **The message cannot be bought.** "If they placed the sun in my right hand..." — the Prophet's ﷺ refusal of every bribe proves his sincerity and the divine origin of his mission.
4. **Equality before Allah is revolutionary.** *Tawhid* abolished the tribal hierarchy — the slave equal to the master before God. This terrified the proud and liberated the oppressed.
5. **Most rejection of truth is willful arrogance.** They knew he was honest (6:33) yet refused — the disbelief of *istikbar* (pride), not ignorance.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Stand for truth even when it threatens your interests.** Quraysh chose pride and profit over truth they recognized. Don't let your "idols" — status, wealth, ego — keep you from accepting what you know is right.
2. **Your integrity is your strongest argument.** The Prophet ﷺ leveraged his lifelong honesty: "you never knew me to lie." Build a reputation that itself testifies to your truthfulness.
3. **Truth is not for sale.** No worldly offer is worth abandoning your principles. The "sun and moon" refusal is the gold standard of conviction.
4. **Begin reform with your own circle.** "Warn your closest kindred" (26:214) — sincerity in calling to good starts at home, without exempting your own family.
5. **Faith, not family, determines worth before Allah.** Abu Lahab's blood-tie to the Prophet ﷺ availed him nothing. Each soul stands on its own faith.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Quraysh rejected Islam because they thought Muhammad ﷺ was a liar."**
> ✅ The Qur'an states they **knew he was truthful** (6:33); they rejected the *message* because it threatened their **idols, power, economy, and pride** — not because they doubted him.

> ❌ **"The Prophet ﷺ wanted wealth or power."**
> ✅ He **refused** explicit offers of wealth, kingship, and women ("sun and moon" refusal). A man seeking gain would have accepted; his refusal proves his sincerity.

> ❌ **"Surah Al-Masad against Abu Lahab is petty/personal."**
> ✅ It is a **prophecy and a principle**: it foretold Abu Lahab would die a disbeliever (which he did, never even faking conversion to disprove it), and it teaches that **kinship and wealth don't save** — only faith does.

> ❌ **"Islam reinforced tribal hierarchy."**
> ✅ The opposite — *tawhid* declared **all equal before Allah**, which is precisely why the proud chieftains opposed it. Islam liberated the slave and the poor.

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: How did the Prophet ﷺ go public?**
A: By warning his clan, then climbing **Mount Safa** and addressing all Quraysh, using his reputation ("you never knew me to lie") to deliver the warning (Bukhari 4770).

**Q: Why did Quraysh oppose him if they knew he was honest?**
A: They rejected the **message**, not the man (6:33) — *tawhid* threatened their idols, leadership, economy, and the tribal hierarchy. Pride and interest, not doubt.

**Q: What was Surah Al-Masad about?**
A: It condemned **Abu Lahab** (the Prophet's ﷺ hostile uncle) and his wife by name, foretelling he'd die a disbeliever — teaching that lineage and wealth don't save.

**Q: Did they try to bribe the Prophet ﷺ?**
A: Yes — wealth, kingship, women, a "cure" — if he'd stop attacking the idols. He refused with the "sun and moon" declaration, proving his sincerity.

**Q: What does the opposition teach us?**
A: That truth threatens entrenched power and pride; that most rejection of truth is **willful**, not honest; and that conviction must be unshakable and unbuyable.
