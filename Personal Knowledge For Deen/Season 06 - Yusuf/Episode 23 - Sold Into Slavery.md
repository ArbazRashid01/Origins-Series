# Season 06 · Episode 23
# Sold Into Slavery
### From the Well to the House of the 'Aziz

> *"And thus We established Yusuf in the land... And Allah is predominant over His affair, but most of the people do not know."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Yusuf (12:21)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

The well becomes a doorway. This chapter follows Yusuf from the bottom of the pit to the heart of Egyptian power — found by a caravan, sold as a slave, and raised in the household of a high official (the **'Aziz**). It is a story of how Allah turns betrayal into elevation, and how Yusuf's character and Allah's favour mark him for greatness even in captivity. It bridges the betrayal (Episode 22) and the great test of temptation (Episode 24).

---

## 1. Episode Summary

After Yusuf was abandoned in the well, **a passing caravan** stopped to draw water; their water-drawer let down his bucket and pulled up not water but a boy. They concealed him as "merchandise" and took him to **Egypt**, where they **sold him for a paltry price** — a few coins — treating the future prophet as cheap goods. He was bought by a high-ranking Egyptian official, the **'Aziz** (often identified with the title of a minister/treasurer), whose wife is later called by tradition "**Zulaykha**."

The 'Aziz, sensing something special in the boy, instructed his wife to **treat Yusuf honourably**, saying, "Perhaps he will benefit us, or we may adopt him as a son." The Qur'an then states one of the season's key themes: *"And thus We established Yusuf in the land."* Even as a sold slave in a foreign country, Yusuf was being **positioned by Allah** — given a home in the house of power, taught wisdom and knowledge, and granted Allah's favour. The Qur'an notes Allah "gave him judgment and knowledge" and that **"Allah is predominant over His affair"** — the unseen hand of destiny was guiding every step that the brothers thought would destroy him.

This chapter explores the rescue, the sale, the providence of being placed in the 'Aziz's home, and the maturation of Yusuf into a man of beauty, wisdom, and integrity — setting up the supreme test of his chastity (Episode 24).

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Rescue That Was a Sale

> ✅ **Surah Yusuf (12:19-20)**
>
> **Translation:** "And there came a company of travelers; then they sent their water-drawer, and he let down his bucket. He said, 'Good news! Here is a boy.' And they concealed him as merchandise; and Allah was Knowing of what they did. And they sold him for a reduced price — a few dirhams — and they were, concerning him, of those content with little."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** Note the layers of injustice and providence intertwined: the caravan men, instead of freeing a lost child, **hid him as goods** to profit. They **sold him cheaply** — a future prophet, priceless in Allah's sight, traded for a handful of coins by people who didn't recognize his worth. Yet the phrase *"Allah was Knowing of what they did"* signals that every human transaction was unfolding under Allah's watchful plan. The world undervalued Yusuf; Allah was elevating him.

### 3.2 The House of the 'Aziz — "Thus We Established Him"

> ✅ **Surah Yusuf (12:21)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَقَالَ الَّذِي اشْتَرَاهُ مِن مِّصْرَ لِامْرَأَتِهِ أَكْرِمِي مَثْوَاهُ عَسَىٰ أَن يَنفَعَنَا أَوْ نَتَّخِذَهُ وَلَدًا ۚ وَكَذَٰلِكَ مَكَّنَّا لِيُوسُفَ فِي الْأَرْضِ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Wa qala alladhi ishtarahu min Misra li-mra'atihi akrimi mathwahu 'asa an yanfa'ana aw nattakhidhahu waladan. Wa kadhalika makkanna li-Yusufa fi al-ard.*
>
> **Translation:** "And the one from Egypt who bought him said to his wife, 'Make his stay comfortable. Perhaps he will benefit us, or we may adopt him as a son.' And thus We established Yusuf in the land that We might teach him the interpretation of events. And Allah is predominant over His affair, but most of the people do not know."
>
> **Tafsir:** The 'Aziz (the minister who bought him) instinctively recognized Yusuf's nobility and ordered him treated **honourably** — even contemplating *adopting* him. The Qur'an then reveals the divine purpose: *"Thus We established Yusuf in the land"* — the betrayal and slavery were the very mechanism by which Allah placed Yusuf in the corridors of Egyptian power, where he would one day rule. The closing line is the theme of the whole surah: ***"Allah is predominant (ghalib) over His affair, but most people do not know."*** Human beings scheme; Allah's plan prevails. The brothers thought they had ruined Yusuf; they had unknowingly **launched** him.

### 3.3 Maturity, Wisdom, and Knowledge

> ✅ **Surah Yusuf (12:22):** "And when he reached maturity, We gave him judgment (*hukm*) and knowledge ('*ilm*). And thus We reward the doers of good (*muhsinin*)."
>
> **Tafsir:** Yusuf grew into a man of **wisdom, sound judgment, and divinely granted knowledge** (including the interpretation of dreams). The phrase "thus We reward the *muhsinin*" indicates Yusuf's *excellence* (*ihsan*) — his integrity, gratitude, and devotion even as a slave — was the reason for Allah's favour. He did not let captivity embitter him or corrupt him; he excelled in his character, and Allah elevated him for it.

## 4. The Theme: Allah's Plan Prevails Through Apparent Setbacks

This chapter crystallizes one of Surah Yusuf's greatest teachings: **what looks like catastrophe is often the hidden machinery of destiny.**

- Thrown in a well → found by a caravan.
- Sold as a slave → placed in the house of Egypt's elite.
- A captive in a foreign land → being positioned to one day **govern** that very land.

Every step the brothers and the slavers intended as harm or profit was a step Allah used to *establish* Yusuf. As the surah repeats, **"Allah is predominant over His affair"** (12:21). The believer learns to trust that Allah's plan operates *especially* through the moments that seem darkest — a lesson Yusuf himself will articulate at the story's triumphant end (Episode 25).

> 💡 This is also why the surah comforted the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ: rejected by his people, he was being *positioned* by Allah for the establishment of Islam — just as Yusuf's "ruin" was his rise.

## 6. Lessons

1. **Allah's plan prevails — especially through hardship.** "Allah is predominant over His affair" (12:21). The well and the slave-market were not detours from Yusuf's destiny; they *were* the road to it. Trust Allah when life seems to be unraveling.
2. **Your worth is set by Allah, not by how others price you.** Yusuf was sold for a few coins by people blind to his value — yet he was a prophet destined to rule. Never let others' undervaluation define you.
3. **Excellence (*ihsan*) attracts Allah's favour.** Yusuf maintained integrity and excellence even in captivity, and "thus We reward the doers of good" (12:22). Be excellent wherever Allah places you, however lowly the position.
4. **Honour the vulnerable in your care.** The 'Aziz's instinct to treat Yusuf honourably ("make his stay comfortable") is praised in the telling — a reminder to treat servants, dependents, and strangers with dignity.
5. **Gifts are tests.** Yusuf's beauty (Muslim 162) would soon become his hardest trial. Every blessing — looks, wealth, intelligence, status — is also a test of how you guard it.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Yusuf's slavery shows Allah abandoned him."**
> ✅ The opposite — "thus We **established** Yusuf in the land" (12:21). His slavery was the means of his rise. Allah was orchestrating his elevation through it.

> ❌ **"Yusuf was bitter and broken by his betrayal and enslavement."**
> ✅ He excelled in character and was granted "judgment and knowledge" (12:22). He responded to injustice with integrity, not bitterness.

> ❌ **"The 'Aziz's wife's name 'Zulaykha' is in the Qur'an."**
> ✅ It is **not** — the Qur'an calls her only "the wife of the 'Aziz." "Zulaykha" is from later tradition (Isra'iliyyat), not the text.

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — Names and prices, the caravan's route, the 'Aziz's name and title details**

- **Narration:** The 'Aziz named "Qitfir/Potiphar," his wife "Zulaykha," the exact sale price and buyers, the caravan's origin.
- **Source:** Isra'iliyyat and tradition.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'an gives "the 'Aziz" and "his wife" and "a few dirhams." Names like Zulaykha/Qitfir are traditional, not established. Useful as labels, not as fact.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Who found Yusuf in the well?**
A: A passing caravan's water-drawer, who pulled him up with the bucket and announced, "Good news, here is a boy!" (12:19).

**Q: How much was Yusuf sold for?**
A: "A few dirhams" — a reduced price (12:20), reflecting how those who sold him undervalued him.

**Q: What is the "'Aziz"?**
A: A title for the high Egyptian official (minister/treasurer) who bought Yusuf and raised him in his household.

**Q: Why did Allah let Yusuf be enslaved?**
A: As the very means of "establishing" him in Egypt (12:21) — positioning him for his future role. Allah's plan worked through the hardship.

**Q: Is "Zulaykha" the 'Aziz's wife's real name?**
A: It's a traditional name (Isra'iliyyat); the Qur'an calls her only "the wife of the 'Aziz."
