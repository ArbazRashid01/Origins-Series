# Season 06 · Episode 24
# Temptation & Prison
### The Test of Chastity, and the Dreams Behind Bars

> *"He said, 'My Lord, prison is more beloved to me than that to which they invite me...'"*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Yusuf (12:33)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This is the moral summit of Yusuf's story — and one of the great tests of chastity and integrity in all of scripture. In the house of the 'Aziz, the beautiful young Yusuf is relentlessly tempted by the master's wife, and chooses the **dungeon over disobedience to Allah.** This chapter covers the seduction, Yusuf's God-conscious refusal, the proof of his innocence, the gathering of the noblewomen, his imprisonment, and the famous **dreams of the prisoners** that begin his path to power. It is the definitive lesson on guarding oneself for Allah's sake.

> 📖 **Tone note:** As with all sensitive material, this is told at the Qur'an's level of dignity — the moral and spiritual lesson, without graphic detail.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Having grown into a man of remarkable beauty and integrity, Yusuf became the object of his master's wife's desire. She **locked the doors** and called him to herself. Yusuf's response is the heart of the story: **"Ma'adha-Allah!" — "[I seek] refuge in Allah!"** — refusing out of gratitude to his master who had honoured him, and out of fear of Allah, declaring "the wrongdoers never succeed." The Qur'an notes he *"would have inclined to her had he not seen the proof (burhan) of his Lord"* — meaning Allah protected him at the critical moment, and he fled toward the door.

She tore his shirt from behind as he fled; at the door they met the 'Aziz himself. She instantly **accused Yusuf** of attempting her. But a witness pointed to the decisive evidence: *if his shirt is torn from the front, she is truthful; if from the back, she lies.* The shirt was torn **from behind** — proving Yusuf's innocence and her guilt. The 'Aziz told Yusuf to "turn away from this" and told his wife to seek forgiveness.

The scandal spread among the city's women, who mocked the 'Aziz's wife. She then **invited them to a banquet**, gave each a knife, and had Yusuf appear before them — and they were so stunned by his beauty that **they cut their hands** without feeling it, exclaiming "this is no man; this is a noble angel!" She openly admitted her intent and threatened Yusuf with prison if he refused. Yusuf turned to Allah: *"Prison is more beloved to me than what they call me to."* And so, though innocent, **Yusuf was imprisoned** — choosing dignity and obedience over a comfortable sin.

In prison, two fellow inmates had dreams; Yusuf, after first calling them to *tawhid*, **interpreted their dreams** accurately — one would be freed and serve the king, the other executed. He asked the one to be freed to mention him to the king — but that man forgot, and Yusuf remained in prison for "several years." This chapter explores the temptation, the proof, the imprisonment, and the dreams that would eventually free him (Episode 25).

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Temptation and the Refuge in Allah

> ✅ **Surah Yusuf (12:23-24)**
>
> **Translation:** "And she, in whose house he was, sought to seduce him. She closed the doors and said, 'Come, you.' He said, **'[I seek] refuge in Allah (*Ma'adha-Allah*). Indeed, he (your husband) is my master, who has made good my residence. Indeed, wrongdoers do not succeed.'** And she certainly determined [a desire] for him, and he would have inclined to her had he not seen the proof (*burhan*) of his Lord. And thus [it was] that We should avert from him evil and immorality. Indeed, he was of Our chosen, sincere servants (*al-mukhlasin*)."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** Yusuf's refusal rests on **three pillars**: (1) **Taqwa** — "I seek refuge in Allah"; his first instinct was Allah. (2) **Gratitude and loyalty** — betraying his master who honoured him would be a vile return for kindness. (3) **Certainty in justice** — "wrongdoers never succeed." On the phrase "*he would have inclined had he not seen the proof of his Lord*": scholars explain this honours Yusuf — it shows he was a *human* with natural desire (not passionless), yet Allah's "*burhan*" (a sign/awareness that strengthened his faith) intervened so he did **not** act. Crucially, the verse ends by calling him **one of "*al-mukhlasin*"** — the very category Iblis admitted he could never reach (S1E5: 38:83)! Yusuf's *sincerity* was his shield against the most powerful temptation. **He fled toward the door** — physically removing himself from sin, the prophetic strategy.

### 3.2 The Proof of Innocence — The Shirt From Behind

> ✅ **Surah Yusuf (12:25-29)**
>
> **Translation:** "And they raced to the door, and she tore his shirt from the back, and they found her husband at the door. She said, 'What is the recompense of one who intended evil for your wife but that he be imprisoned or a painful punishment?' [Yusuf] said, 'It was she who sought to seduce me.' And a witness from her family testified: 'If his shirt is torn from the front, then she has told the truth and he is of the liars. But if his shirt is torn from the back, then she has lied and he is of the truthful.' So when he saw his shirt torn from the back, he said, 'Indeed, it is of your (women's) plan. Indeed, your plan is great. Yusuf, ignore this. And [my wife], ask forgiveness for your sin...'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Yusuf was falsely accused — the eternal experience of the wronged innocent. But Allah cleared him through **rational evidence**: the direction of the tear. Torn from behind meant he was *fleeing* and she was *pursuing* — proof of his innocence. Even so, the 'Aziz, to protect his household's reputation, hushed it up rather than fully vindicate Yusuf publicly. Injustice lingered.

### 3.3 The Banquet and the Cut Hands

> ✅ **Surah Yusuf (12:30-32)**
>
> **Translation:** "...So when she heard of their scheming talk, she sent for them and prepared a banquet and gave each of them a knife and said [to Yusuf], 'Come out before them.' When they saw him, they admired him greatly and cut their hands and said, 'Perfect is Allah! This is not a man; this is none but a noble angel.' She said, 'That is the one about whom you blamed me. I sought to seduce him, but he firmly refused...'"
>
> **Tafsir:** When the city's women gossiped about her, she devised a vindication: she gathered them, gave them fruit and knives, and revealed Yusuf — and they were so overwhelmed by his beauty that they **sliced their own hands without noticing.** It was both a defense ("now you see why I couldn't resist") and a proof of Yusuf's extraordinary, Allah-given beauty (Muslim 162, Episode 23). She then **openly confessed** her attempt and Yusuf's refusal — and threatened him with prison if he still refused.

### 3.4 "Prison Is Dearer to Me Than Sin"

> ✅ **Surah Yusuf (12:33)**
>
> **Translation:** "He said, 'My Lord, prison is more beloved to me than that to which they invite me. And if You do not avert their plan from me, I might incline toward them and become of the ignorant.' So his Lord responded to him and averted from him their plan. Indeed, He is the Hearing, the Knowing."
>
> **Tafsir:** This is one of the most powerful du'as in the Qur'an. Yusuf — young, handsome, a slave with no power, facing relentless temptation from powerful women — **chose the dungeon over disobedience.** And notice his humility: he did not boast of his strength; he *begged Allah* to protect him, admitting that without Allah's help even he might falter. This is true *taqwa*: not self-confidence in one's own willpower, but desperate reliance on Allah to keep one firm. Allah answered and protected him.

### 3.5 Prison: Da'wah and the Dreams

> ✅ **Surah Yusuf (12:36-40):** Two young men entered prison with Yusuf. Each had a dream and asked Yusuf (recognizing his righteousness) to interpret it. Before answering, Yusuf **first called them to *tawhid*** — "Are diverse lords better, or Allah, the One, the Prevailing?... You worship not besides Him except names you and your fathers have named..." (12:39-40). *Even in prison, his priority was calling to Allah.* Then he interpreted: one would be freed and serve the king wine; the other would be crucified. He asked the one to be saved to **mention him to the king** — but Shaytan made him forget, and Yusuf "remained in prison several years" (12:42).
>
> **Tafsir:** Yusuf's conduct in prison is a masterclass: he used his hardship as a **platform for da'wah**, prioritizing the message of *tawhid* even over the men's immediate concern (their dreams). He served others (interpreting truthfully, even the hard news). And when his one hope of release (the freed man) forgot him, he endured **years more** of unjust imprisonment with patience. The "several years" of forgotten suffering is a profound lesson on enduring delayed justice.

| # | Hadith | Grade | Teaching |
|---|--------|:-----:|----------|
| 1 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 660 / Muslim 1031** | ✅ | "The seven whom Allah shades" — including **a man invited by a beautiful woman of status who says, 'I fear Allah'** — echoing Yusuf |
| 2 | **Sahih Muslim 162** | ✅ | Yusuf given a great share of beauty (the Mi'raj) |
| 3 | **Qur'an 12:23-42** | ✅ core | The temptation, proof, prison, and dreams |

> ✅ **On #1 (Bukhari 660):** The Prophet ﷺ listed seven categories shaded by Allah on the Day when there is no shade but His — among them **"a man whom a woman of beauty and status calls [to sin], but he says, 'I fear Allah.'"** This is, in essence, the **Yusuf archetype** — and it shows that resisting temptation for Allah's sake is among the highest, most rewarded acts of faith. Yusuf is the living embodiment of this hadith.

## 5. Lessons

1. **"I seek refuge in Allah" — the believer's first response to temptation.** Yusuf's instinct was Allah (12:23). Train the heart so that *taqwa*, not desire, speaks first.
2. **Flee from sin — physically.** Yusuf ran for the door (12:25). Don't negotiate with temptation; remove yourself from it. Proximity to sin is danger.
3. **Sincerity (*ikhlas*) is the shield Shaytan cannot pierce.** Yusuf is called one of *al-mukhlasin* (12:24) — the exact group Iblis conceded he can't reach (38:83). Sincerity to Allah is the ultimate protection against temptation.
4. **Choose hardship with Allah over comfort in sin.** "Prison is dearer to me than what they call me to" (12:33). The believer prefers a hard, halal life to an easy, haram one.
5. **Don't trust your own willpower — beg Allah.** Yusuf didn't say "I'm strong enough"; he prayed, "if You do not avert their plan, I might incline" (12:33). True strength is desperate reliance on Allah.
6. **Turn every hardship into da'wah and service.** In prison, Yusuf called to *tawhid* and served others. Wherever you are confined or tested, be a source of guidance and good.
7. **Patience through delayed justice.** Forgotten by the freed man, Yusuf endured years more — innocent — without bitterness. Allah's timing is perfect even when justice seems delayed.

## 6. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The verse '(he would have inclined) had he not seen the proof' means Yusuf nearly sinned / was weak."**
> ✅ It shows Yusuf was a **real human with desire** (not passionless), and that **Allah protected him** so he did *not* act — which is *greater* honour, not a flaw. He is explicitly called one of the sincere (*mukhlasin*) whom Allah safeguards (12:24).

> ❌ **"Yusuf went to prison because he did something wrong."**
> ✅ He was **innocent** — imprisoned to silence a scandal, after *choosing* prison over sin (12:33). His imprisonment was an injustice he bore for Allah's sake.

> ❌ **"It teaches that women are inherently the source of temptation."**
> ✅ The story is about *one* powerful woman's wrongdoing and Yusuf's response; the Bukhari 660 hadith frames the lesson **universally** (resisting illicit desire for Allah's sake). Temptation and the duty to resist apply to all. The Qur'an also shows righteous women throughout (Maryam, Asiyah, etc.).

> ❌ **"Yusuf relied on his own strength to resist."**
> ✅ He explicitly **begged Allah** for protection (12:33), modeling reliance over self-confidence.

## 7. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — Graphic and speculative details**

- **Narration:** Lurid expansions of the seduction scene; speculation about what "the proof (*burhan*)" was (e.g., seeing his father's image, a vision, writing on the wall) — with many conflicting reports; the names and number of the women.
- **Source:** Isra'iliyyat and tafsir speculation; the Qur'an leaves *burhan* unspecified and the scene dignified.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The *burhan* was a divine sign that protected him — its exact form is **unknown** and unimportant. Avoid the graphic and speculative additions.

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Did Yusuf almost sin?**
A: No — the verse (12:24) shows he had natural human desire but **Allah's "proof" protected him** so he did not act. This *honours* him: he was tempted at the highest level yet kept pure by Allah's aid and his own sincerity.

**Q: What was the "proof (*burhan*)" Yusuf saw?**
A: A divine sign/awareness that strengthened him at the critical moment. Its exact form is **unspecified** in the Qur'an; speculations are Isra'iliyyat (§7).

**Q: Why was Yusuf imprisoned if he was innocent?**
A: To bury the scandal and protect the 'Aziz's household — and because Yusuf himself preferred prison to sin (12:33). It was an injustice he endured for Allah's sake.

**Q: How long was Yusuf in prison?**
A: "Several years" (12:42) — after the freed cupbearer forgot to mention him. The exact number isn't specified (tradition says around seven years, unverified).

**Q: What's the connection to the "seven shaded by Allah" hadith?**
A: Yusuf is the archetype of "a man tempted by a woman of beauty and status who says 'I fear Allah'" (Bukhari 660) — among the most beloved acts to Allah.
