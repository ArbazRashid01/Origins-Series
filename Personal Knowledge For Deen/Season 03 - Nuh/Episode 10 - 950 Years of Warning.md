# Season 03 · Episode 10
# 950 Years of Warning
### Nuh (Noah), the First Messenger, and the Birth of Idolatry

> *"And We certainly sent Nuh to his people, and he remained among them a thousand years minus fifty years."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-'Ankabut (29:14)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Season 02 ended with the first death and the warning that envy and forgetting had entered the human family. Now generations have passed. The pure worship of Allah that Adam taught has slowly decayed into the **first idolatry in human history** — and so Allah sends the **first Messenger (Rasul)** to a disbelieving people: **Nuh (Noah), peace be upon him.** This chapter covers how idolatry began, who Nuh was, and his astonishing 950-year mission of patient calling.

> 📖 **Method note:** Nuh's story is told across many surahs (especially the entire *Surah Nuh*, ch. 71, plus Hud 11, Al-Mu'minun 23, Al-Qamar 54, Al-'Ankabut 29). We build the picture from these. The famous detail of *how* idolatry began is itself authentically narrated by Ibn 'Abbas in al-Bukhari.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

For ten generations after Adam, humanity remained upon *tawhid* (pure monotheism). Then idolatry crept in — not by an evil intention at first, but through **excessive veneration of righteous men**. After several pious worshippers (Wadd, Suwa', Yaghuth, Ya'uq, and Nasr) died, their grieving people erected images to remember them; over generations, knowledge faded and those images became **idols that were worshipped**. This is the Qur'an's and Sunnah's account of how *shirk* (associating partners with Allah) entered the world — a lesson in how good intentions, unchecked, can decay into the gravest sin.

Into this darkened world Allah sent **Nuh**, the first Messenger to a rejecting people. He called them, openly and secretly, night and day, with wisdom, warnings, and reminders of Allah's blessings — for **950 years**. Yet only a small number believed. The elites mocked him, called him a madman, accused him of seeking superiority, and told the masses not to abandon their gods. The chapter portrays the extraordinary *patience* (*sabr*) of a prophet who preached for nearly a millennium against relentless rejection — and sets up the coming of the Ark and the Flood (Episodes 11-12).

## 3. Background: How Idolatry Began (The Most Important Lesson of the Season)

This is one of the most instructive passages in all of Islamic history, because it explains the *origin* of the very sin every prophet would come to fight.

> ✅ **Surah Nuh (71:23):** "And they said, 'Never leave your gods, and never leave Wadd or Suwa' or Yaghuth or Ya'uq or Nasr.'"

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 4920** — *Authentic.* Ibn 'Abbas (may Allah be pleased with him) explained these five names: **"These were the names of righteous men from the people of Nuh. When they died, Shaytan inspired their people to set up statues at their gathering-places and name them after these men. They did so, and the statues were not worshipped — until those people died and knowledge was lost. Then the statues were worshipped."**
>
> **Explanation:** Idolatry did **not** begin with people deciding to reject God. It began with **love and grief for the righteous**, expressed through images "to inspire devotion." Over generations, the *purpose was forgotten* and the *images were worshipped*. This is why Islam is so strict about (a) building over graves, (b) images/statues of the venerated, and (c) excessive veneration of saints and the righteous — because this is the documented *historical doorway* through which *shirk* first entered humanity. The Prophet ﷺ warned against it on his very deathbed (cursing those who take the graves of their prophets as places of worship — Sahih al-Bukhari 1330).

> 💡 **The principle:** Shaytan rarely opens with "worship an idol." He opens with "honour the righteous a little more than is due." Guarding *tawhid* means guarding even its *approaches*.

## 4. Who Was Nuh? (Peace Be Upon Him)

Nuh is one of the five greatest Messengers (*Ulu al-'Azm* — "those of firm resolve": Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, and Muhammad ﷺ). He is honoured throughout the Qur'an:

- **The first Messenger (Rasul)** sent to a disbelieving people — confirmed by the great intercession hadith, where humanity on the Day of Judgement comes to Nuh as *"the first Messenger Allah sent to the people of the earth"* (**Sahih al-Bukhari 4712**).
- Called *"a grateful servant"* (*'abdan shakura*, 17:3).
- Saluted by Allah Himself: *"Peace upon Nuh among the worlds"* (37:79).
- His prophethood and that of his descendants is noted; *"and We placed in his descendants prophethood and the Scripture"* (57:26).

> ⚠️ **On his lifespan:** The Qur'an states he called his people for "a thousand years minus fifty" — **950 years** (29:14). Whether this is his *total* life or only the *duration of his mission* is discussed by scholars (most read it as the duration of his call among them, with his full life being longer). Either way, it conveys an almost unimaginable span of patient preaching.

## 5. The Story: The 950-Year Mission

### 5.1 The Call

> ✅ **Surah Nuh (71:1-4)**
>
> **Translation:** "Indeed, We sent Nuh to his people, [saying], 'Warn your people before there comes to them a painful punishment.' He said, 'O my people, indeed I am to you a clear warner: that you worship Allah, fear Him, and obey me — He will forgive you of your sins...'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Nuh's message is the message of *every* prophet: worship Allah alone, leave false gods, and obey the Messenger. The reward offered is forgiveness and respite. This is the unchanging core of all revelation — the same *tawhid* established "before time" (Season 01) now being restored to a fallen humanity.

### 5.2 The Tireless Methods

> ✅ **Surah Nuh (71:5-9)**
>
> **Translation:** "He said, 'My Lord, indeed I invited my people night and day, but my invitation increased them only in flight. And indeed, every time I invited them that You may forgive them, they put their fingers in their ears, covered themselves with their garments, persisted, and were arrogant with [great] arrogance. Then I invited them publicly. Then I announced to them and confided to them secretly...'"
>
> **Tafsir:** This is a masterclass in *da'wah* (calling to Allah) and in **patience**. Nuh varied every method — **night and day**, **publicly and privately**, with warnings and with gentle reminders of Allah's blessings (rain, wealth, children, gardens — 71:10-12). He left no approach untried. And still they "put their fingers in their ears." The lesson: a caller's duty is *sincere, persistent, varied effort* — the *result* is with Allah.

### 5.3 The Rejection of the Elite

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:27):** "The eminent ones (*al-mala'*) who disbelieved among his people said, 'We do not see you but as a man like ourselves, and we do not see you followed except by those who are the lowest of us, [and] at first suggestion. And we do not see in you any superiority over us; rather, we think you are liars.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Note the recurring pattern that will repeat with *every* prophet in this encyclopedia:
> 1. The **elite (*al-mala'*)** lead the rejection — because the message threatens their power and pride.
> 2. They sneer at the **early believers as "the lowest"** (the poor and weak) — class arrogance.
> 3. They demand the prophet be more than "a man like us."
> Nuh's reply (11:29-31) is dignified: he asks no payment, he will not drive away the believers however "low" the elite consider them, and he claims no divine powers — only that he is a warner.

## 6. The Qur'anic Spotlight: Surah Nuh & Key Verses

> ✅ **Surah Al-'Ankabut (29:14):** the 950 years — the headline of his patience.
> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:36):** Allah finally informs Nuh that **no more of his people will believe** beyond those who already have — releasing him from hope in them and preparing the judgement.
> ✅ **Surah Nuh (71:26-27):** Nuh's grave final prayer: "My Lord, do not leave upon the earth from among the disbelievers an inhabitant. Indeed, if You leave them, they will mislead Your servants and not beget except [every] wicked disbeliever." — *a prayer made only after 950 years of certainty that they were beyond reform, and after divine permission (11:36).*

## 7. Authentic Hadith — Gathered and Explained

| # | Hadith | Grade | Teaching |
|---|--------|:-----:|----------|
| 1 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 4920** — Ibn 'Abbas on how the five idols arose from venerating righteous men | ✅ | The origin of idolatry |
| 2 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 4712** — the great intercession: Nuh is "the first Messenger to the people of earth" | ✅ | Nuh's rank as first Messenger |
| 3 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 1330 / Muslim 531** — the Prophet's ﷺ warning against taking graves as places of worship | ✅ | Guarding the approaches to shirk |
| 4 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 3339 (theme)** — on the Day of Judgement Nuh and his nation testify; the ummah of Muhammad ﷺ witnesses | ✅ | Nuh delivered the message faithfully |

> ✅ **On #1 and #3 together:** These two hadiths form a single, urgent teaching that runs from Nuh's time to the Prophet's ﷺ deathbed: the road to idolatry begins with **over-veneration of the righteous and their graves/images.** The Prophet ﷺ closed that road deliberately because he knew — from the very story of Nuh — where it leads.

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — Nuh's exact age, the number of believers, physical descriptions**

- **Narration:** Precise figures (e.g., "only 80 believed," exact total lifespan of 1,000+ years, the colour of his beard, etc.).
- **Source:** Mostly Isra'iliyyat and varying reports.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'anic "950 years [of calling]" is firm; specific extra numbers are **not** reliably established. Some figures appear in tafsir without sound chains.
---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 2 — Elaborate tales of Nuh's interactions / a fourth son's full biography**

- **Source:** Storyteller tradition.
- **Rely on it?** ❌ Stick to the Qur'anic account.

## 9. Important People

**Nuh (peace be upon him):** First Messenger to a disbelieving people; one of the five *Ulu al-'Azm*; "a grateful servant" (17:3). Model of supreme patience and varied, sincere da'wah. His mission spanned 950 years.

**The *mala'* (elite/chieftains):** The arrogant leaders who rejected him and mobilized the masses against him — the prototype of every later rejecter (Pharaoh, Abu Jahl, etc.).

**The five "righteous men" turned idols (Wadd, Suwa', Yaghuth, Ya'uq, Nasr):** Once-pious figures whose veneration decayed into the first idolatry. (Strikingly, these same idol-names resurfaced among the pagan Arabs centuries later — 71:23.)

## 10. Important Places

- **The land of Nuh's people:** Traditionally located in **Mesopotamia** (the region of modern Iraq), the cradle of early civilization — though the Qur'an does not specify. This region's later flood-memory (echoed in Mesopotamian flood epics) is discussed in §13.

## 11. Important Objects / Concepts

- **The five idols:** The first false gods — a case study in how *shirk* is manufactured.
- **Da'wah methods:** Night/day, public/private — Nuh's toolkit of sincere calling.
- **Sabr (patience):** The defining virtue of this season — patience measured in *centuries*.

## 12. Lessons

1. **Guard the gateways to shirk.** Idolatry began with *honouring the righteous too much*. Keep worship for Allah alone, and beware veneration that creeps toward worship — even of prophets and saints.
2. **Your job is effort; results are Allah's.** Nuh did everything right for 950 years and "succeeded" with only a few. By Allah's measure, **he was a complete success** — because success is in faithful effort, not in numbers.
3. **Patience can be a lifetime — or longer.** If a prophet preached for nine and a half centuries without bitterness, our trials of years or decades gain perspective.
4. **The elite and the ego are the great barriers to truth.** Pride and vested interest, not lack of evidence, kept Nuh's people away. The same will be true of every nation to come.
5. **Truth is not measured by popularity.** The "lowest" and fewest were the *believers*; the powerful majority were astray. Never judge truth by the size of the crowd.

## 13. Misconceptions & Modern Discussions

> ❌ **"The Qur'anic flood is just a copy of the Mesopotamian/Biblical flood myths."**
> ✅ Flood narratives in many ancient cultures (Mesopotamian *Gilgamesh*/*Atrahasis*, the Biblical account) are, from the Islamic view, *distant memories of a real event* preserved imperfectly by different peoples — not evidence the Qur'an "borrowed." The Qur'an presents the *corrected, monotheistic* account, free of the polytheistic elements in the older epics. (Balanced position; details of the flood's scope are in Episode 12.)

> ❌ **"Nuh prayed for his people's destruction out of cruelty."**
> ✅ He prayed *only after 950 years* and *only after Allah informed him none more would believe* (11:36). It was a prayer of justice after exhaustive mercy, not vengeance.

> ❌ **"Idolaters always intended to reject God."**
> ✅ The first idolatry came from *misguided love* of the righteous (Bukhari 4920) — a warning that sincerity is not enough; worship must also be *correct*.

## 14. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Was Nuh really the first prophet?**
A: Adam was the first *Prophet* (Nabi); Nuh was the first *Messenger (Rasul)* sent to a disbelieving people (Bukhari 4712). A Messenger is sent with a new law/warning to a rejecting nation.

**Q: Did he really preach for 950 years?**
A: The Qur'an says he called his people "a thousand years minus fifty" (29:14). Most scholars read this as the duration of his mission. Human lifespans in the earliest eras are presented as far longer than today.

**Q: Who were Wadd, Suwa', Yaghuth, Ya'uq, and Nasr?**
A: Righteous men of Nuh's people whose images, made to honour them, became idols over generations (Bukhari 4920). Their names reappeared among pre-Islamic Arab idols.

**Q: Why is Islam so strict about graves and images of the pious?**
A: Precisely *because* of this story — it is the documented origin of shirk. The Prophet ﷺ closed that door to protect *tawhid* (Bukhari 1330).
