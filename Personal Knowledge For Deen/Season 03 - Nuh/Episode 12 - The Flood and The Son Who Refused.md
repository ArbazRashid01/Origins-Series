# Season 03 · Episode 12
# The Flood & The Son Who Refused
### When the Earth Drowned, and a Father Lost His Son to Disbelief

> *"O my son, board with us and be not with the disbelievers." [The son] said, "I will take refuge on a mountain to protect me from the water." [Nuh] said, "There is no protector today from the decree of Allah, except for whom He gives mercy."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Hud (11:42-43)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

The Ark is built; the believers have boarded. Now comes the climax of Season 03 — the **Flood** that cleansed the earth, and the **heartbreak of Nuh's own son**, who refused to board and drowned. This chapter holds two of the most profound lessons in the Qur'an: that **divine justice is certain**, and that **faith — not family, lineage, or proximity to a prophet — is what saves a person.** It closes with Nuh's grief, Allah's correction, and the new beginning of humanity.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

When the sign came, the heavens opened with torrential rain and the earth burst forth with springs, until the waters met and rose in **waves like mountains**. The Ark sailed upon them, carrying the believers and the pairs of creatures. As the water rose, Nuh saw his **son** standing apart and called out with a father's anguish: *"O my son, board with us, and do not be with the disbelievers!"* But the son, trusting in his own plan, replied that he would climb a mountain to escape the water. Nuh's reply is one of the most piercing lines in scripture: *"There is no protector today from the decree of Allah, except for whom He has mercy."* A wave came between them, and the son was among the drowned.

The disbelieving nation perished entirely. The Ark came to rest on **Mount Judi**, and Allah commanded the waters to subside: *"O earth, swallow your water; and O sky, withhold [your rain]."* Then came the deeply human epilogue: Nuh, grieving, appealed to Allah regarding his son — "*he is of my family*" — and Allah gently **corrected** him: the son was *not* of his true "family," because *"his deed was other than righteous."* Nuh immediately repented for asking about something he had no knowledge of. The chapter ends with the survivors descending in peace and blessing — a renewed humanity, and the reason Nuh is sometimes called the "second father of mankind."

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Deluge

> ✅ **Surah Al-Qamar (54:11-12):** "Then We opened the gates of the heaven with rain pouring down, and caused the earth to burst with springs, and the waters met for a matter already predestined."
>
> **Tafsir:** The Flood came from **above and below** simultaneously — sky and earth united in a decree "already predestined." This was not random weather; it was *measured judgement*, executing what had been written.

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:42):** "And it sailed with them through waves like mountains..."
>
> **Tafsir:** The imagery — a small wooden ark amid mountainous waves — captures both the terror of the judgement and the absolute security of those under Allah's protection ("under Our eyes," 11:37).

### 3.2 The Son Who Refused

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:42-43)**
>
> **Arabic:** ...وَنَادَىٰ نُوحٌ ابْنَهُ وَكَانَ فِي مَعْزِلٍ يَا بُنَيَّ ارْكَب مَّعَنَا وَلَا تَكُن مَّعَ الْكَافِرِينَ ۝ قَالَ سَآوِي إِلَىٰ جَبَلٍ يَعْصِمُنِي مِنَ الْمَاءِ ۚ قَالَ لَا عَاصِمَ الْيَوْمَ مِنْ أَمْرِ اللَّهِ إِلَّا مَن رَّحِمَ ۚ وَحَالَ بَيْنَهُمَا الْمَوْجُ فَكَانَ مِنَ الْمُغْرَقِينَ
>
> **Transliteration:** *...wa nada Nuhun ibnahu wa kana fi ma'zilin: ya bunayya irkab ma'ana wa la takun ma'a al-kafirin. Qala sa-awi ila jabalin ya'simuni mina al-ma'. Qala la 'asima al-yawma min amrillahi illa man rahim. Wa hala baynahuma al-mawju fakana mina al-mughraqin.*
>
> **Translation:** "And Nuh called to his son, who was apart [from them], 'O my son, board with us and do not be with the disbelievers.' [The son] said, 'I will take refuge on a mountain to protect me from the water.' [Nuh] said, 'There is no protector today from the decree of Allah, except for whom He gives mercy.' And the waves came between them, and he was among the drowned."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** This is the emotional and theological heart of the entire season. A prophet who spent 950 years saving strangers could not save his own son — because **guidance is from Allah, and the son chose disbelief.** The son's fatal error was *self-reliance against the decree of Allah*: "I'll climb a mountain" — trusting his own plan over the only refuge (faith and the Ark). Nuh's words, *"there is no protector today from the decree of Allah, except for whom He has mercy,"* are eternal: when Allah's judgement comes, no mountain, wealth, ally, or scheme protects — only His mercy, which comes through faith and obedience.

### 3.3 The Subsiding and the Resting Place

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:44)**
>
> **Translation:** "And it was said, 'O earth, swallow your water, and O sky, withhold [your rain].' And the water subsided, and the matter was accomplished, and the ship came to rest on [Mount] Judi. And it was said, 'Away with the wrongdoing people.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** A breathtaking verse — Allah commands the very earth and sky as a master commands a servant, and they obey instantly. The Ark settles on **Mount Judi**. The judgement is complete.

### 3.4 The Father's Grief and Allah's Correction

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:45-47)**
>
> **Translation:** "And Nuh called to his Lord and said, 'My Lord, indeed my son is of my family; and indeed, Your promise is true; and You are the most just of judges.' He said, 'O Nuh, indeed he is not of your family; indeed, he is [one whose] work was other than righteous, so do not ask Me for that of which you have no knowledge. Indeed, I advise you, lest you be among the ignorant.' [Nuh] said, 'My Lord, I seek refuge in You from asking that of which I have no knowledge. And unless You forgive me and have mercy upon me, I will be among the losers.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Here is one of the most profound redefinitions in scripture. Nuh, in grief, reminds Allah of the promise to save "his family." Allah's correction is gentle but firm: *"he is not of your family"* — because **true kinship in the sight of Allah is the kinship of faith, not blood.** A disbelieving son is not, in the way that matters, part of a prophet's "family." And notice Nuh's *immediate* response — like Adam, like every true believer — he does not argue; he **repents** ("I seek refuge in You... forgive me"). Even a great prophet's natural fatherly love had to bow to the truth that faith outranks lineage.

## 4. The Central Theme: Faith Saves, Not Lineage

This season delivers one of Islam's most important teachings, dramatized through Nuh's family:

- **Nuh's son** — blood of a prophet — **perished**, because he disbelieved.
- **Nuh's wife** — likewise — is named in the Qur'an among those who betrayed (66:10), an example of how even a prophet's spouse is judged by her own faith.
- **The "lowest" strangers** who believed — **were saved** on the Ark.

> ✅ **Surah At-Tahrim (66:10):** "Allah presents an example of those who disbelieved: the wife of Nuh and the wife of Lut. They were under two of Our righteous servants but betrayed them, so those prophets did not avail them against Allah at all..."
>
> 💡 **The principle:** No one is saved by *association* — not by being the child, spouse, or companion of the righteous. Each soul stands on its **own faith and deeds.** This dismantles every notion of "saved by bloodline" or "guaranteed by proximity to holiness." (It also comforts: a person from a disbelieving family — like Asiyah, Pharaoh's believing wife, 66:11 — can be saved by their *own* faith.)

## 6. Was the Flood Global or Regional? (Scholarly & Academic Discussion)

A genuine question with a range of views:

- **Opinion 1 — Global/universal flood.** *Evidence:* Nuh's prayer that none of the disbelievers be left on "the earth" (71:26); the description of total destruction of his people; the traditional reading that all post-Flood humanity descends from those on the Ark (hence Nuh as "second father of mankind"). *Held by:* many classical scholars.
- **Opinion 2 — Regional flood (covering the land of his people).** *Evidence:* The Qur'an consistently frames prophets as sent **to their own people** (*"to 'Aad their brother Hud," "to Thamud their brother Salih"*), and the punishment as falling on *that nation*; the Arabic "*al-ard*" can mean "the land" (their region) rather than the entire globe; this also fits the absence of a universal flood layer in the geological record. *Held by:* a number of modern scholars and researchers.

📖 **Conclusion:** Both views exist within Islam. The Qur'an's **certain** teaching is that **Nuh's disbelieving nation was completely destroyed** by the Flood, the believers were saved, and Allah's judgement was just and total *upon them*. Whether it covered the whole planet or the whole inhabited region of that time is a matter where scholars differ, and the encyclopedia does not force one. *(Where the texts allow more than one reading, we present both — Methodology §6.)*

## 7. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — The son's name and dialogue, the wife's detailed betrayal, the duration of the Flood, the number of survivors**

- **Narration:** That the son was named "Kan'an" or "Yam"; precise day-counts of rain and flooding; exact survivor numbers (e.g., "80, then they all died except Nuh's three sons"); detailed accounts of the wife mocking Nuh.
- **Source:** **Isra'iliyyat** and storyteller tradition; not from sound chains.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The son's *disbelief and drowning* is Qur'anic; his *name* and the extra figures are unverified.
---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 2 — Post-Flood details (the raven/dove sent out, the rainbow covenant, repopulation specifics)**

- **Source:** Largely Biblical/Isra'iliyyat.
- **Rely on it?** ❌ Not part of the authentic Islamic account. The Qur'an's epilogue is the peaceful, blessed descent (11:48).
---
**🕰️ Note on "second father of mankind":** Some reports say all later humanity descends from Nuh's three sons. This is *traditionally* held (and supports the global-flood view), but the specific genealogies (Shem/Ham/Japheth as ancestors of races) are **Isra'iliyyat** and not a firm part of creed.

## 8. Important People

**Nuh (peace be upon him):** Now seen at his most human — a grieving father — and at his most prophetic — instantly submitting to Allah's correction and repenting. He demonstrates that even overwhelming natural love must yield to the truth of faith.

**Nuh's son (the one who refused):** The tragic embodiment of self-reliance against Allah's decree. Blood of a prophet, lost by his own disbelief. His "I'll climb a mountain" is the eternal symbol of trusting one's own plan over Allah's refuge.

**Nuh's wife:** Cited in the Qur'an (66:10) as one who betrayed her righteous husband — a warning that even a prophet's spouse is judged by her own faith.

**The believing remnant:** "A few" who boarded and became the seed of a renewed, blessed humanity (11:48).

## 10. Lessons

1. **Faith saves; lineage does not.** Nuh's son drowned; strangers were saved. No one rides into salvation on another's righteousness — not even a prophet's child. Each soul answers for itself.
2. **No refuge from Allah's decree except His mercy.** "There is no protector today... except for whom He has mercy" (11:43). Wealth, power, schemes, and "mountains" fail; only faith and Allah's mercy avail.
3. **Even the greatest love must bow to truth.** Nuh loved his son, yet accepted Allah's verdict and repented for even asking. Truth outranks our deepest attachments.
4. **Self-reliance against Allah is the road to ruin.** The son's "I'll handle it myself" mirrors the arrogance of Iblis and every doomed nation. Humility before the decree is salvation.
5. **Judgement is real and just — but mercy precedes it.** 950 years of warning came *before* the Flood. Allah never punishes without exhaustive prior mercy and warning. (Cf. "We do not punish until We send a Messenger" — 17:15.)
6. **A new beginning always follows.** The Flood ended not in despair but in "peace and blessings" (11:48). Allah's justice clears the ground for renewal.

## 11. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"A prophet can guarantee his family's salvation."**
> ✅ **No** — Nuh could not save his own son (11:43, 45-46). Salvation is by *individual* faith, not by relation to the righteous.

> ❌ **"Nuh sinned by asking about his son."**
> ✅ He asked out of natural love and a sincere (if mistaken) reading of Allah's promise. Allah *corrected* him, and Nuh *repented*. Prophets are protected from major sin; this was a moment of human grief, immediately righted.

> ❌ **"The Qur'an just copies the Biblical flood (Ararat, the dove, the rainbow)."**
> ✅ The Qur'an differs in key details (Mount **Judi**, not Ararat; no dove/rainbow covenant) and centres the *moral and monotheistic* message. The shared core reflects a *real remembered event*, told correctly here.

> ❌ **"The Flood story endorses collective punishment of the innocent."**
> ✅ The nation was destroyed only after 950 years of warning and after *every* believer was saved on the Ark. Allah "does not punish until He sends a Messenger" (17:15). The judgement fell on the persistent, warned rejecters.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was the son's name?**
A: The Qur'an doesn't name him. Tradition offers "Kan'an"/"Yam," but these are Isra'iliyyat, not established.

**Q: How could a prophet's son disbelieve?**
A: Because faith is a *choice*, not an inheritance. Guidance is from Allah; even living beside a prophet doesn't force belief (cf. Abu Talib, the Prophet's ﷺ uncle, Season 12).

**Q: Was the whole earth flooded?**
A: Scholars differ (global vs. regional — §6). What's certain: Nuh's disbelieving nation was wholly destroyed, the believers saved.

**Q: Why is Nuh called the "second father of mankind"?**
A: On the view that all later humanity descends from those on the Ark. The detailed genealogies are Isra'iliyyat, but the honour reflects his role as the survivor-patriarch after the Flood.

**Q: What happened to the Ark?**
A: It rested on Mount Judi; Allah says He preserved it "as a sign" (54:15). Specific modern "discovery" claims are unverified (Episode 11 §6).
