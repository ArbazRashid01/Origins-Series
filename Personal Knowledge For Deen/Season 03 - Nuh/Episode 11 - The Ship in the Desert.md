# Season 03 · Episode 11
# The Ship in the Desert
### Building the Ark by Revelation, Amid Mockery

> *"And construct the ship under Our observation and Our inspiration, and do not address Me concerning those who have wronged; indeed, they are to be drowned."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Hud (11:37)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Episode 10 ended with Nuh's 950-year mission exhausted and Allah's decree that none more would believe. Now comes one of the most striking acts of faith in scripture: Allah commands Nuh to **build a massive ship** — by divine instruction, far from any sea — while his people **laugh at him**. This short but powerful chapter is about **obedience without full understanding, faith under ridicule, and the preparation for judgement.**

---

## 1. Episode Summary

After releasing Nuh from hope in his people (11:36), Allah commanded him to **build an ark** under direct divine supervision and inspiration ("*build it under Our eyes and Our inspiration*"). Nuh obeyed — though, by all appearances, building an enormous boat on dry land made no sense. As he worked, the chieftains of his people **passed by and mocked him**, ridiculing the "prophet turned carpenter." Nuh answered with quiet certainty: *"If you ridicule us, then we will ridicule you just as you ridicule. And you are going to know who will get a punishment that disgraces him."*

The Ark was to carry the believers (a small number), Nuh's family (except those who disbelieved), and **a pair of every kind of living creature**. The chapter explores the dimensions and nature of the Ark (what is authentically known vs. the elaborate folklore), the meaning of building "under Allah's eyes," and the spiritual lessons of obeying a command whose wisdom is not yet visible — the antidote to the doubt Iblis specialized in.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Command to Build

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:37)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَاصْنَعِ الْفُلْكَ بِأَعْيُنِنَا وَوَحْيِنَا وَلَا تُخَاطِبْنِي فِي الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا ۚ إِنَّهُم مُّغْرَقُونَ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Wasna'i al-fulka bi-a'yunina wa wahyina wa la tukhatibni fi alladhina zalamu, innahum mughraqun.*
>
> **Translation:** "And construct the ship under Our eyes (observation) and Our inspiration, and do not address Me concerning those who have wronged; indeed, they are to be drowned."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** Two phrases carry the weight. *"Under Our eyes"* (*bi-a'yunina*) — an affirmed attribute (Allah's "eyes," accepted without "how," S1E1) meaning under His direct protection and care: the Ark was built under divine supervision, so it could not fail. *"And Our inspiration"* (*wa wahyina*) — Allah taught Nuh *how* to build it; this was no ordinary human design but a **revealed blueprint**. And the closing command — "do not plead for the wrongdoers" — shows the time for mercy had fully passed after nearly a millennium.

### 3.2 Faith Under Mockery

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:38)**
>
> **Translation:** "And he constructed the ship, and whenever the chieftains of his people passed by him, they ridiculed him. He said, 'If you ridicule us, then we will ridicule you just as you ridicule. And you are going to know who will get a punishment that will disgrace him and upon whom will descend an enduring punishment.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Imagine the scene: an old prophet, building a colossal boat *in a dry land with no sea in sight*, surrounded by jeering crowds calling him insane. Nuh does not waver. His faith is anchored not in the *visible logic* of the task but in the *certainty of the Commander*. This is the precise opposite of the doubt-whisper Iblis specializes in ("does this even make sense?"). **Obedience to a clear divine command does not wait for the wisdom to become visible.**

### 3.3 The Sign and the Loading

> ✅ **Surah Hud (11:40)**
>
> **Translation:** "Until, when Our command came and the oven overflowed (*fara at-tannur*), We said, 'Load upon it of each [creature] two mates and your family — except those about whom the word has preceded — and those who have believed.' But none believed with him, except a few."
>
> **Tafsir:** The **agreed signal** to board was a strange one: *the oven gushing forth water* (*fara at-tannur*) — water erupting from where fire normally burns, a sign that the unstoppable deluge had begun. Onto the Ark went:
> - **A pair (male and female) of each kind** of animal — to preserve life through the catastrophe.
> - **Nuh's family** — *except* those who disbelieved (notably his son and his wife — Episode 12).
> - **The believers** — "but none believed with him except a few," underscoring how small the saved remnant was after 950 years.

## 4. What Do We Actually Know About the Ark?

A careful, evidence-based picture:

**Established (Qur'an):**
- It was built by **divine instruction** (11:37; 23:27).
- It carried **pairs of creatures**, the believers, and Nuh's faithful family (11:40).
- It was made "of planks and nails" (*dhati alwahin wa dusur*, 54:13).
- It sailed amid waves "like mountains" (11:42) and later **came to rest on Mount Judi** (11:44).
- Allah preserved it "as a sign" (29:15; 54:15).

**NOT reliably established (Isra'iliyyat — see §6):**
- Exact dimensions (the precise cubits, three decks for humans/animals/birds, the wood being teak/gopher, the years it took to build, the exact number of each animal). These figures circulate widely but lack sound chains.

> 📖 We affirm the Qur'anic essentials and treat the elaborate engineering specs as unverified tradition.

## 6. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — Exact dimensions, decks, wood, and build-time**

- **Narration:** That the Ark was a specific length/width/height (various cubit figures), had three levels (beasts below, humans middle, birds above), was made of a particular wood, and took a specific number of years/decades to build; that Nuh first had to *plant and grow* the trees.
- **Source:** **Isra'iliyyat** (paralleling Biblical specs) and storyteller tradition; no sound marfu' chain.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ Interesting cultural detail, **not established fact**. The Qur'an gives "planks and nails" and divine design — that is what we affirm.
---
**⚠️ Folklore 2 — The remains of the Ark "discovered" / specific modern locations**

- **Narration:** Periodic claims that the Ark has been found on a specific mountain (often Ararat, per the Biblical tradition; the Qur'an says **Judi**).
- **Source:** Popular/sensational reports; archaeologically unverified.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'an names **Judi** (11:44) as its resting place and says Allah preserved it "as a sign" — but specific modern "discovery" claims are unverified and should not be presented as proven.

## 7. Important People

**Nuh (peace be upon him):** Now the obedient builder — embodying faith that acts on Allah's command *before* understanding its purpose. His response to mockery (dignified, certain) models the believer under ridicule.

**The mocking chieftains:** Their jeering at the "boat in the desert" is the arrogance of those who judge divine commands by worldly logic — about to be silenced by the Flood.

**The believing remnant:** "A few" — the faithful who would board and be saved.

## 9. Lessons

1. **Obey first; understand later.** Nuh built a ship in a desert because *Allah commanded it* — not because it made visible sense. True faith acts on the command of the All-Wise even before the wisdom appears.
2. **Ridicule is the cost of conviction.** The believer who stands on truth will be mocked. Nuh's calm, certain reply — "you will soon know" — is the model: no insecurity, no abandoning the task.
3. **Salvation is prepared *before* the storm.** The Ark was built during the calm, by faith. Spiritual preparation (prayer, good deeds, repentance) is done *before* the trial arrives, not during it.
4. **"Under Allah's eyes."** When you act in obedience to Allah, you are under His care and supervision — your effort cannot ultimately fail, even if the world calls it foolish.
5. **The few, prepared, are saved; the many, heedless, are lost.** A recurring theme: truth's adherents are often few, but they are the ones who board the Ark.

## 10. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Building the Ark proves Nuh was irrational."**
> ✅ He acted on **divine inspiration and instruction** (11:37). It was the *highest* rationality — trusting the Creator over appearances. The "irrational" ones were those who ignored a 950-year warning.

> ❌ **"The Qur'an gives the Ark's exact size and three decks."**
> ✅ It does **not**. Those specifics are Isra'iliyyat. The Qur'an says "planks and nails" and a divinely taught design.

> ❌ **"The Ark landed on Mount Ararat."**
> ✅ The **Qur'an says Mount Judi** (11:44), not Ararat (which is the Biblical tradition). Modern "discovery" claims are unverified.

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: How big was the Ark and how many animals?**
A: The Qur'an doesn't give dimensions or counts — only "a pair of each kind." Specific figures are unverified tradition.

**Q: What does "the oven overflowed" (fara at-tannur) mean?**
A: The agreed sign to board: water gushing forth from the oven (where fire normally is), signalling the Flood had begun. Some scholars read *tannur* more broadly as the surface of the earth bursting with water.

**Q: Did Nuh take every species on earth?**
A: The Qur'an says "of each [kind], two mates." Scholars discuss the scope (see Episode 12 on whether the Flood was global or regional, which bears on this). The point is the *preservation of life* by Allah's command.

**Q: How long did it take to build?**
A: Unknown from authentic sources. Estimates in tradition vary widely and aren't established.
