# Season 09 · Episode 36
# Swallowed by the Sea
### Yunus (Jonah), the Whale, and the Prayer From the Depths

> *"There is no deity except You; exalted are You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Anbiya (21:87)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter completes Season 09 with the unforgettable story of **Yunus (Jonah), peace be upon him** — the prophet who, in frustration at his people's rejection, left his post *before* Allah permitted, was swallowed by a great fish (whale), and from the darkness of its belly cried out the prayer that Allah loves above all in distress. It is a story of human impatience, sincere repentance, Allah's mercy, and — uniquely — an entire nation that **believed and was saved.** It is the perfect counterpart to Ayyub's patience: where Ayyub shows patience rewarded, Yunus shows impatience corrected by mercy.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

**Yunus** was sent to the people of **Nineveh** (in ancient Assyria, near modern Mosul, Iraq) — a large city sunk in idolatry. He called them to Allah, but they persistently rejected him. In **frustration and impatience**, Yunus left his people and his post — departing "in anger," before Allah had given him permission to leave — assuming Allah would not test him with difficulty over it (21:87: "and he thought that We would not decree [anything] upon him").

He boarded a ship, which became dangerously overloaded in a storm; the crew cast lots to lighten it, and the lot fell to Yunus (by Allah's decree). He was thrown into the sea, and a **great fish (whale)** swallowed him whole. In the crushing **darkness — of the fish's belly, the deep sea, and the night** — Yunus realized his error and turned to Allah with one of the most powerful supplications in all of revelation: **"La ilaha illa anta, subhanaka, inni kuntu mina az-zalimin"** — *"There is no deity except You; exalted are You; indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers."*

Allah heard him, forgave him, and **delivered him** — the fish cast him onto the shore, weak and ill, where Allah caused a gourd plant to grow to shade and nourish him. Allah then sent Yunus *back* to his people — and found that they had, **uniquely among all the nations of the prophets, ALL believed** and were spared the punishment. The Qur'an honours this: *"Why was there no town that believed so its faith benefited it — except the people of Yunus?"* (10:98). This chapter explores Yunus's impatience, the whale, the great du'a, his deliverance, and the believing city — the lessons of not abandoning one's post, the power of repentance, and Allah's boundless mercy.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Mission and the Impatient Departure

> ✅ **Surah Al-Anbiya (21:87)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَذَا النُّونِ إِذ ذَّهَبَ مُغَاضِبًا فَظَنَّ أَن لَّن نَّقْدِرَ عَلَيْهِ...
>
> **Translation:** "And [mention] the man of the fish (*Dhun-Nun*, i.e. Yunus), when he went off in anger and thought that We would not decree [hardship] upon him..."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** Yunus was sent to Nineveh; when his people persisted in rejection, he left them **"in anger" (*mughadiban*)** — frustrated at their stubbornness — *before* receiving Allah's permission to abandon his post. This was his mistake: a prophet's duty is to persevere with patience (like Nuh's 950 years!) and await Allah's command. Yunus's leaving early — assuming Allah "would not constrain him" over it — was a lapse of patience, not disbelief. The Qur'an calls him *Dhun-Nun* ("the one of the fish") and *Sahib al-Hut* ("the companion of the whale"). Importantly, Allah commands the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ: *"So be patient for the decision of your Lord, and be not like the companion of the fish (Yunus)..."* (68:48) — *using Yunus as a lesson in the necessity of patient perseverance.*

### 3.2 The Storm, the Lots, and the Whale

> ✅ **Surah As-Saffat (37:139-142)**
>
> **Translation:** "And indeed, Yunus was among the messengers. When he ran away to the laden ship, and he drew lots and was among the losers. Then the fish swallowed him, while he was blameworthy."
>
> **Tafsir:** Yunus boarded an overloaded ship; when it was endangered (a storm), the crew cast lots to decide who to cast overboard to save the rest — a practice of the time. The lot fell to Yunus (by Allah's decree, repeatedly, even after re-drawing, per tradition). He was thrown into the sea, and Allah sent a **great fish (whale)** to swallow him whole — *alive*, preserved by Allah in its belly. The sea, the ship, the lots, the fish — all were Allah's means of bringing Yunus to the moment of return.

### 3.3 The Prayer From the Darkness — The Greatest Du'a of Distress

> ✅ **Surah Al-Anbiya (21:87-88)**
>
> **Arabic:** ...فَنَادَىٰ فِي الظُّلُمَاتِ أَن لَّا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنتَ سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ ۝ فَاسْتَجَبْنَا لَهُ وَنَجَّيْنَاهُ مِنَ الْغَمِّ ۚ وَكَذَٰلِكَ نُنجِي الْمُؤْمِنِينَ
>
> **Transliteration:** *...fanada fi az-zulumati an la ilaha illa anta subhanaka inni kuntu mina az-zalimin. Fastajabna lahu wa najjaynahu mina al-ghamm, wa kadhalika nunji al-mu'minin.*
>
> **Translation:** "...And he called out within the darknesses, 'There is no deity except You; exalted are You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers.' So We responded to him and saved him from the distress. And thus do We save the believers."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** In the **"darknesses" (*zulumat*)** — the darkness of the fish's belly, within the darkness of the deep sea, within the darkness of night — Yunus uttered what became the most beloved du'a of distress. Notice its three perfect components:
> 1. **Tawhid** — "There is no deity except You": affirming Allah's absolute oneness.
> 2. **Tasbih** — "exalted are You": glorifying Allah, declaring Him far above any fault.
> 3. **Confession** — "I have been of the wrongdoers": owning his own mistake, blaming no one but himself.
> No demand, no complaint — just *tawhid*, *praise*, and *humble admission*. The Prophet ﷺ said: **"The supplication of Dhun-Nun when he was in the belly of the fish: 'La ilaha illa anta, subhanaka, inni kuntu mina az-zalimin' — no Muslim ever supplicates with it for anything but that Allah responds to him"** (**Jami' at-Tirmidhi 3505**, *graded sahih by many*). Allah declares the universal principle: *"And thus do We save the believers"* (21:88) — this rescue is available to *every* believer who turns to Allah with sincere repentance in distress.

### 3.4 The Deliverance and the Gourd

> ✅ **Surah As-Saffat (37:145-146):** "But We threw him onto the open shore while he was ill, and We caused to grow over him a gourd vine (*yaqtin*)."
>
> **Tafsir:** Allah commanded the fish to cast Yunus onto the shore — weak, ill, his skin tender from the ordeal. In His mercy, Allah grew a **gourd plant** over him for shade, nourishment, and healing (the broad leaves for shade, the fruit for food, and such plants repel flies). Every detail of his recovery was a mercy. He who had abandoned his post in impatience was restored by the boundless mercy of the Lord he turned back to.

### 3.5 The City That Believed — Uniquely

> ✅ **Surah Yunus (10:98)**
>
> **Translation:** "Then has there not been a [single] city that believed so its faith benefited it except the people of Yunus? When they believed, We removed from them the punishment of disgrace in worldly life and gave them enjoyment for a time."
> ✅ **Surah As-Saffat (37:147-148):** "And We sent him to [his people of] a hundred thousand or more, and they believed, so We gave them enjoyment [of life] for a time."
>
> **Tafsir:** This is one of the most remarkable points in all the prophetic stories. **Yunus's people — over a hundred thousand — ALL believed**, and Allah lifted the punishment that had begun to approach them. The Qur'an highlights this as *unique*: among all the nations of the prophets, the people of Yunus were the **one entire city that believed in time** and was saved (most nations rejected and were destroyed — Nuh's people, 'Aad, Thamud, Pharaoh). So Yunus's story ends doubly merciful: **the prophet was forgiven and saved, and his entire nation believed and was spared.** A story drenched in mercy from beginning to end.

## 5. The Themes — Patience vs. Impatience (Completing Season 09)

Season 09 pairs two prophets as a **study in contrasts**, both ending in mercy:

| | **Ayyub (E35)** | **Yunus (E36)** |
|---|-----------------|-----------------|
| The trial | Catastrophic *loss* (wealth, children, health) | His people's *rejection* of his call |
| His response | **Patience** — held his post, never complained | **Impatience** — left his post in frustration |
| The turning point | A gentle du'a affirming Allah's mercy | A du'a of *tawhid* + praise + confession in the dark |
| The outcome | Restored and **doubled** | Forgiven, delivered, and his **whole nation believed** |
| The lesson | Patience is rewarded | Even a lapse, met with sincere repentance, is forgiven |

Together they teach: **patience is the ideal (Ayyub), but when we fall short (Yunus), sincere repentance brings Allah's mercy and rescue.** "And thus do We save the believers" (21:88).

## 6. Lessons

1. **Don't abandon your post in frustration.** Yunus's lapse was leaving early, before Allah's command. Persevere in your duties — to family, faith, community — and await Allah's relief rather than quitting in anger.
2. **The du'a of Yunus rescues from any distress.** "La ilaha illa anta, subhanaka, inni kuntu mina az-zalimin" — memorize it, say it in hardship; the Prophet ﷺ promised Allah responds (Tirmidhi 3505). Its structure — *tawhid*, praise, confession — is the perfect prayer of return.
3. **Own your mistakes** — "I have been of the wrongdoers" (21:87). Yunus blamed no one but himself. Sincere repentance begins with honest admission.
4. **No darkness is beyond Allah's reach.** From the belly of a whale, in the deep sea, at night, Allah heard and rescued him. However "buried" you feel, Allah hears your call.
5. **Allah's mercy is vast.** Yunus's story ends with the prophet forgiven *and* his entire nation saved — the only people of a prophet to all believe in time (10:98). Never underestimate Allah's mercy or a people's capacity to turn back.
6. **Patience is the goal; repentance is the safety net.** Be like Ayyub in patience; and when you slip like Yunus, return at once — Allah saves the believers.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Yunus disbelieved or rebelled against Allah."**
> ✅ No — his lapse was **leaving his post early in frustration**, a shortfall in patience, *not* disbelief. He remained a noble prophet, honoured by the Prophet ﷺ (Bukhari 3395). Allah calls him "blameworthy" (37:142) for the lapse but forgave and elevated him.

> ❌ **"Being swallowed by a whale and surviving is impossible, so it's a myth."**
> ✅ It was a **miracle** — Allah preserved Yunus alive in the fish by His power (21:88). Miracles are deliberate breaks in the normal order; the lesson is Allah's mercy and might, not marine biology.

> ❌ **"Yunus's people were destroyed like other rejecting nations."**
> ✅ The **opposite** — they **all believed** and were **uniquely spared** (10:98). The one entire city that turned back in time.

> ❌ **"We can belittle Yunus because of his mistake."**
> ✅ The Prophet ﷺ explicitly forbade this: "No one should say I am better than Yunus ibn Matta" (Bukhari 3395). All prophets are revered.

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — The duration in the whale, the number of fish, the details of Nineveh's repentance**

- **Narration:** Specific counts of days Yunus spent in the whale (3, 7, 40, etc.), descriptions of the fish being swallowed by a bigger fish, and elaborate accounts of how Nineveh repented (the king leading them out with animals, etc.).
- **Source:** Isra'iliyyat and tradition; the Qur'an doesn't specify the duration or these details.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'anic essentials (swallowed, the du'a, deliverance, the city's belief) are certain; durations and elaborations are unverified.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was Yunus's mistake?**
A: He **left his people and his post in frustration** before Allah permitted it — a lapse of patience, not disbelief. Allah corrected and forgave him.

**Q: What is the du'a of Yunus?**
A: *"La ilaha illa anta, subhanaka, inni kuntu mina az-zalimin"* — "There is no deity except You; exalted are You; indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers" (21:87). The Prophet ﷺ said Allah answers whoever supplicates with it (Tirmidhi 3505).

**Q: How long was he in the whale?**
A: The Qur'an doesn't say; traditions vary (3, 7, 40 days — unverified). The point is his repentance and rescue.

**Q: Did his people get destroyed?**
A: No — **uniquely, they all believed** and were spared the punishment (10:98) — the one nation of a prophet to turn back in time.

**Q: Why does the Qur'an tell the Prophet ﷺ "don't be like the man of the fish" (68:48)?**
A: As a lesson in **patient perseverance** — not to leave the mission in frustration, but to await Allah's decree with patience.
