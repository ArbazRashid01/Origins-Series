# Season 02 · Episode 09
# The First Murder
### The Two Sons of Adam — Sacrifice, Envy, and the First Blood

> *"So the soul of the other permitted to him the murder of his brother, and he killed him and became among the losers."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:30)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Humanity has descended to earth (Episode 08). Now the children of Adam multiply, and the test of the *khalifah* immediately produces its first tragedy: **the first human death is a murder — a brother killing his brother.** This chapter closes Season 02 by showing that the war Iblis declared (Season 01) now lives *inside the human heart and the human family*. It is the story of envy, the first crime, the first burial, and the eternal principle Allah draws from it about the sanctity of life.

> 📖 **Method note:** The Qur'an tells this in Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:27-32) **without naming** the two sons. The names "Habil and Qabil" (Abel and Cain) come from later tradition/Isra'iliyyat. We use them for convenience but flag their source.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Two sons of Adam each offered a **sacrifice (qurban)** to Allah. From one it was accepted; from the other it was not. Instead of examining his own state, the rejected brother was consumed by **envy and rage**, and threatened to kill his brother. The righteous brother responded with extraordinary God-consciousness: he refused to retaliate, declared he feared Allah, and said he would rather his brother bear the sin than he himself become a murderer.

But envy won. The aggressor killed his brother — the first murder, the first death, the first blood spilled on earth. Then came confusion: he did not even know what to do with the body, until **Allah sent a raven** scratching the ground to show him how to bury the dead. Overwhelmed with regret (but regret without true repentance), he became "among the losers."

Allah then declares, from this very story, one of the most powerful ethical principles in all scripture: **that to kill one innocent soul is as though one has killed all of humanity, and to save one soul is as though one has saved all of humanity** (5:32). The chapter thus ends Season 02 on the theme of the **sanctity of human life** — and the warning that unchecked envy (the sin of Iblis) leads to the destruction of others and oneself.

## 3. Background: The First Generations

After the descent, Adam and Hawwa had children, and humanity began to multiply. Adam was their Prophet and teacher, instructing them in the worship of Allah. Into this first family came the first test of brotherhood — and it ended in blood. The Qur'an's purpose in telling it is explicitly ethical: it is addressed (in Surah Al-Ma'idah) to the Children of Israel and to all humanity as a lesson on the gravity of unjust killing.

> ⚠️ **On the names and the "marriage" backstory:** Later tradition (largely Isra'iliyyat) supplies names (Habil/Abel the shepherd, Qabil/Cain the farmer) and a motive involving a dispute over marrying a sister. **The Qur'an mentions none of this.** The Qur'anic motive is plainly stated: **envy over the accepted sacrifice** (5:27). We treat the names as conventional labels and the marriage story as unverified (§8).

## 4. The Story: Told From the Qur'an (Surah Al-Ma'idah 5:27-31)

### 4.1 The Two Sacrifices

> ✅ **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:27)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَاتْلُ عَلَيْهِمْ نَبَأَ ابْنَيْ آدَمَ بِالْحَقِّ إِذْ قَرَّبَا قُرْبَانًا فَتُقُبِّلَ مِنْ أَحَدِهِمَا وَلَمْ يُتَقَبَّلْ مِنَ الْآخَرِ قَالَ لَأَقْتُلَنَّكَ ۖ قَالَ إِنَّمَا يَتَقَبَّلُ اللَّهُ مِنَ الْمُتَّقِينَ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Watlu 'alayhim naba'a abnay Adama bil-haqq, idh qarraba qurbanan fatuqubbila min ahadihima wa lam yutaqabbal mina al-akhar. Qala la-aqtulannak. Qala innama yataqabbalullahu mina al-muttaqin.*
>
> **Translation:** "And recite to them the story of Adam's two sons, in truth, when they both offered a sacrifice, and it was accepted from one of them but was not accepted from the other. [The latter] said, 'I will surely kill you.' [The former] said, 'Allah only accepts from the righteous (*al-muttaqin*).'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** The key phrase is the righteous brother's reply: *"Allah only accepts from the muttaqin (the God-conscious)."* The acceptance of a deed depends not on its outward size but on the **sincerity and piety of the heart** behind it. The rejected brother's correct response would have been self-examination and repentance; instead, his envy turned outward into a death threat. **Envy blinds a person to their own fault and projects rage onto the blameless.**

### 4.2 The Righteous Brother's Restraint

> ✅ **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:28-29)**
>
> **Translation:** "'If you should raise your hand against me to kill me, I shall not raise my hand against you to kill you. Indeed, I fear Allah, Lord of the worlds. Indeed, I want you to bear my sin and your sin so you will be among the companions of the Fire. And that is the recompense of the wrongdoers.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** His restraint is *not* weakness — it is *taqwa* (God-consciousness). He refuses to be the **initiator** of violence. (Scholars clarify: this does not forbid self-defense in general; it reflects his choice not to become an aggressor-killer, and possibly the law of that time.) His statement "I want you to bear my sin and your sin" means: *if you do this, you will carry the guilt of the murder and its consequences* — i.e., he would rather his brother bear that burden than himself become a murderer. The Prophet ﷺ referenced this restraint as the model for times of *fitnah* (civil strife): **"Be like the better of the two sons of Adam"** (cf. Sunan Abi Dawud 4257, on holding back one's hand in discord).

### 4.3 The Murder

> ✅ **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:30)**
>
> **Translation:** "So his soul permitted to him the murder of his brother, and he killed him and became among the losers."
>
> **Tafsir:** The phrase "*his soul permitted him*" (*tawwa'at lahu nafsuhu*) captures how the lower self (*nafs*), inflamed by envy and unchecked, gradually *normalizes* the unthinkable until the hand acts. This is the anatomy of every great sin: a whisper, entertained, becomes a desire, indulged, becomes a deed, committed. The first murder was the fruit of the first **unrepented envy** — the very sin of Iblis (S1E4), now reproduced in a human heart.

### 4.4 The Raven and the First Burial

> ✅ **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:31)**
>
> **Translation:** "Then Allah sent a raven searching/scratching in the ground to show him how to hide the disgrace (the corpse) of his brother. He said, 'O woe to me! Have I failed to be like this raven and so hide the body of my brother?' And he became of the regretful."
>
> **Tafsir:** A staggering image: the first human killer is so lost that a *bird* must teach him what to do with the body he created. He is humiliated before a raven. His cry "O woe to me!" is **regret** (*nadam*) — but the Qur'an pointedly does *not* say he repented and was forgiven; it says he became "of the regretful" / "the losers." **Regret over consequences is not the same as repentance to Allah.** (Contrast Adam in Episode 08, whose regret *became* sincere repentance and forgiveness.)

## 5. The Eternal Principle: The Sanctity of Life (5:32)

> ✅ **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:32)**
>
> **Arabic:** ...مَن قَتَلَ نَفْسًا بِغَيْرِ نَفْسٍ أَوْ فَسَادٍ فِي الْأَرْضِ فَكَأَنَّمَا قَتَلَ النَّاسَ جَمِيعًا وَمَنْ أَحْيَاهَا فَكَأَنَّمَا أَحْيَا النَّاسَ جَمِيعًا
>
> **Transliteration:** *...man qatala nafsan bi-ghayri nafsin aw fasadin fi al-ardi faka'annama qatala an-nasa jami'an, wa man ahyaha faka'annama ahya an-nasa jami'an.*
>
> **Translation:** "...whoever kills a soul — unless for a [lawful] life or for corruption in the land — it is as if he had slain all of mankind; and whoever saves one — it is as if he had saved all of mankind."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** From the first murder, Allah legislates the **supreme value of innocent human life**. To take one innocent life is, in its enormity, like attacking humanity itself; to save one is like saving humanity. This verse is one of the most quoted in the world on the sanctity of life. Note the lawful exceptions ("for a life" — i.e., judicial retribution for murder; "or corruption in the land" — i.e., lawful punishment for grave crimes through due process), which prevent the verse from being misused to forbid all justice — while its overwhelming thrust is the **prohibition and gravity of unjust killing.**

## 6. Authentic Hadith — Gathered and Explained

| # | Hadith | Grade | Teaching |
|---|--------|:-----:|----------|
| 1 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 3335 / Muslim 1677** — "No soul is killed unjustly except that a share of its blood falls upon the first son of Adam, because he was the first to establish murder" | ✅ | The first killer bears partial responsibility for every later murder |
| 2 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 7152 (theme) / Sunan Abi Dawud 4257** — in *fitnah*, "be like the better of the two sons of Adam" (restrain your hand) | ✅/☑️ | The righteous brother as the model in strife |
| 3 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 6533 / Muslim 1678 (theme)** — the first matter judged among people on the Day of Judgement regarding their blood (rights) | ✅ | Bloodshed is the first thing reckoned between people |

> ✅ **On #1 (Bukhari 3335):** The Prophet ﷺ said that whenever a person is murdered unjustly, **a portion of the sin falls upon the first son of Adam**, because *he was the one who introduced murder into the world.* This is a profound teaching on **moral precedent**: the one who pioneers an evil bears a share in all who follow it (just as one who pioneers a good earns from all who follow it — Sahih Muslim 1017). It elevates the first murder from a private crime to the origin of a stream of evil.

## 7. Lessons & Themes

- **Envy (*hasad*) is a killer.** It destroyed the relationship, then the brother, then the killer himself. It is the sin of Iblis reborn in humanity. The Prophet ﷺ warned: "Beware of envy, for it devours good deeds as fire devours wood" (Sunan Abi Dawud 4903, *with discussion on grading*).
- **Self-examination vs. projection.** When his deed was rejected, the loser blamed his *brother* instead of fixing his *own heart*. The believer's response to failure is to look inward and improve.
- **Acceptance depends on taqwa, not appearance** (5:27). Allah weighs the heart.
- **Restraint can be the highest courage** (5:28). Refusing to initiate bloodshed is a prophetic ideal in times of discord.
- **Regret is not repentance** (5:31). Feeling bad about consequences differs from humbly turning back to Allah and changing.
- **Every life is sacred** (5:32) — the enduring legislative fruit of this tragedy.

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 1 — The names, professions, and the "sister" motive**

- **Narration:** That the sons were Qabil (Cain, a farmer) and Habil (Abel, a shepherd); that the real cause was a quarrel over marrying a beautiful twin sister, with the sacrifice as a tiebreaker.
- **Source:** **Isra'iliyyat**; the Qur'an names neither the sons nor any marriage dispute. The Qur'anic cause is **envy over the accepted sacrifice** (5:27).
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ Names may be used conventionally; the marriage backstory is **not established** and should not be taught as fact.
---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 2 — Elaborate details (a fire descending to consume the accepted offering, Iblis advising the killer step-by-step, the mountain that "wept")**

- **Source:** Storyteller embellishment.
- **Rely on it?** ❌ Not as fact. The Qur'anic account is complete for the lesson.

## 9. Important People

**The righteous son (traditionally Habil/Abel):** The first human to die, and the first martyr-like victim of injustice. His God-consciousness, restraint, and refusal to initiate violence make him a moral exemplar praised by the Prophet ﷺ (the "better of the two sons").

**The aggressor son (traditionally Qabil/Cain):** The first murderer; ruled by envy; the originator of bloodshed who bears a share in every unjust killing thereafter (Bukhari 3335). A warning embodied.

**Adam (peace be upon him):** Father of both — experiencing the first human grief and loss, the bitter fruit of life on earth.

## 10. Important Places & Objects

- **The place of sacrifice/altar:** Where the two offerings were presented (unspecified location).
- **The raven (*ghurab*):** Allah's unexpected teacher of burial — origin of the human practice of burying the dead.
- **The grave:** The first burial; the dignity of concealing and honouring the dead, learned here.
- **The sacrifice (*qurban*):** An act of worship whose acceptance hinges on *taqwa* — a theme that returns powerfully with Ibrahim (Season 05).

## 11. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The brothers fought over a woman."**
> ✅ The Qur'an states the cause was **envy over the accepted sacrifice** (5:27). The "sister" motive is unverified Isra'iliyyat.

> ❌ **"The murderer repented and was forgiven."**
> ✅ The Qur'an says he *regretted* and became "of the losers" (5:31) — it does **not** describe sincere repentance or forgiveness. Regret over consequences ≠ tawbah.

> ❌ **"5:32 forbids all killing, including lawful justice/self-defense."**
> ✅ The verse itself names lawful exceptions (judicial retribution, punishment for grave corruption through due process). Its thrust is the gravity of **unjust** killing.

> ❌ **"The righteous brother was a coward for not defending himself."**
> ✅ His restraint was **taqwa**, praised by the Prophet ﷺ as the model in *fitnah* — refusing to be an aggressor-killer, not a failure of courage.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What were the sons' names?**
A: The Qur'an doesn't say. Tradition calls them Habil (Abel) and Qabil (Cain) — convenient labels from Isra'iliyyat, not Qur'anic fact.

**Q: How did the brother know his sacrifice was accepted?**
A: The Qur'an doesn't detail the sign. Tradition mentions a fire consuming the accepted offering, but that's unverified. The point is *that* one was accepted and one wasn't — and the test was in the response.

**Q: Why was one sacrifice rejected?**
A: "Allah only accepts from the muttaqin" (5:27) — acceptance depends on the piety/sincerity of the offerer, not the offering's outward value.

**Q: Does the first killer really share in all later murders?**
A: Per Bukhari 3335, a share of the sin of every unjust killing falls on him as the *originator* of murder — a teaching on the weight of setting an evil precedent.

**Q: Why does this story close Season 02?**
A: Because it shows the war Iblis declared (Season 01) now operating *within* the human family through envy — and it establishes the sanctity of life that all later prophetic missions defend. It is the bridge from "creation" to "human history."
