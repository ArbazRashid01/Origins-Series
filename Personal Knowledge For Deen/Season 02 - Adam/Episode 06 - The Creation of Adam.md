# Season 02 · Episode 06
# The Creation of Adam
### The First Human, The First Prophet, The Honoured Khalifah

> *"And He taught Adam the names — all of them."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Baqarah (2:31)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

Season 01 built the unseen stage: Allah, the Throne, the angels, the jinn, and the fallen Iblis with his vow against humanity. Now the central figure of the entire test walks onto that stage — **Adam (peace be upon him)**, the first human, the father of all mankind, and the **first Prophet.**

This chapter covers *how* Adam was created, *why* he was honoured above the angels, the meaning of his role as **khalifah** (vicegerent), and the famous "names" Allah taught him. It is the bridge between the world of the unseen and the human story that fills the rest of this encyclopedia.

> 📖 **Method reminder:** The creation of Adam is *ghayb* — known only through the Qur'an and authentic Sunnah. A great deal of folklore (Adam's exact height debates, the soil's "colours," the precise hour) exists; the authentic core is given here, and the rest labelled.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

After announcing to the angels that He would place a *khalifah* on the earth (Season 01, Episode 04), Allah fashioned Adam **with His own hands** from clay/earth gathered from across the world. He shaped the body, left it for a period, then **breathed into it from His (created) spirit (*ruh*)** — and Adam came to life. His first recorded action was a sneeze, upon which he praised Allah, and the angels taught him the greeting of *salam*.

Allah then demonstrated humanity's unique gift: **knowledge.** He taught Adam "the names of all things" — and when the angels could not name them, Adam did. This established that the human being, though made of humble clay, carries a capacity for learning, language, and understanding that even the angels do not possess. It is *this* — knowledge and the ability to choose — that makes humanity worthy of being Allah's *khalifah*.

The chapter sets up the great contrast already seeded in Episode 04: the angels submitted in humility ("we have no knowledge except what You taught us"), and Iblis refused in arrogance. Adam stands between them — made of clay, yet crowned with knowledge and honour.

## 3. Background: Why a Creature of Clay?

In Season 01 we learned the three substances: angels from **light** (pure obedience), jinn from **fire** (volatile free will), and now humanity from **clay** (*tin*). Why clay? Clay is humble, lowly, of the earth — yet it is also **fertile**: it holds water, grows life, can be shaped, and bears fruit. The symbolism is profound. The human being is *grounded* and *capable of growth*, neither locked into obedience like the angels nor consumed by the "heat" of pride like the disobedient jinn.

Crucially, humanity's greatness does not lie in its substance (clay is humble) but in two gifts Allah added: the **ruh** (spirit) breathed into us, and **knowledge** (the capacity to learn the "names"). This is the answer to the angels' earlier question — "will You place one who sheds blood?" Allah's reply, "I know what you do not know" (2:30), is demonstrated here: this clay creature can *know*, can *choose*, and can therefore *worship by choice* and even *repent* — a moral grandeur the angels do not share.

## 4. The Story: Told in Full From the Qur'an

### 4.1 Fashioned by Allah's Hands, From Clay

The Qur'an describes Adam's material in stages, using different words that scholars understand as phases of the same process:

> ✅ **Surah Al-Hijr (15:26):** "And We did certainly create man out of clay from an altered black mud (*salsalin min hama'in masnun*)."
> ✅ **Surah Ar-Rahman (55:14):** "He created man from clay like that of pottery (*salsalin kal-fakhkhar*)."
> ✅ **Surah Sad (38:75):** Allah rebukes Iblis: "What prevented you from prostrating to one whom I created with **My two Hands**?"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir, as-Sa'di):** The stages — *turab* (dust/soil), then *tin* (clay/mud), then *hama' masnun* (smooth dark mud), then *salsal kal-fakhkhar* (dried, sounding clay) — describe the transformation of the material. Adam being created "with Allah's two Hands" is a special honour, affirmed (as with all Attributes) **without asking "how"** (S1E1 §4.3). It distinguishes Adam's creation from everything else, which Allah created by the word *"Be."*

> ☑️ **Sunan Abi Dawud 4693 / Jami' at-Tirmidhi 2955** — *graded sound (sahih/hasan).* The Prophet ﷺ said: **"Allah created Adam from a handful that He took from all of the earth, so the children of Adam come in accordance with [the colour and nature of] the earth: among them are the red, the white, the black, and those between; the easy, the difficult, the bad, and the good."**
>
> **Explanation:** A beautiful hadith explaining human **diversity** — every skin tone, every temperament — as coming from the varied soils of the whole earth. This is the Qur'anic basis for racial equality: we are *literally* all from the same source, differing only as the earth itself differs. (Foreshadows the Farewell Sermon, Season 14.)

### 4.2 The Spirit Breathed In

> ✅ **Surah Al-Hijr (15:29)**
>
> **Arabic:** فَإِذَا سَوَّيْتُهُ وَنَفَخْتُ فِيهِ مِن رُّوحِي فَقَعُوا لَهُ سَاجِدِينَ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Fa-idha sawwaytuhu wa nafakhtu fihi min ruhi faqa'u lahu sajidin.*
>
> **Translation:** "And when I have proportioned him and breathed into him of My [created] spirit, then fall down to him in prostration."
>
> **Tafsir:** "My spirit" (*ruhi*) is an attribution of *honour* — like "the House of Allah" (the Ka'bah) — not that the spirit is part of Allah's essence. The *ruh* is a created thing, and its true nature is among the things Allah kept hidden: "They ask you about the spirit. Say: the spirit is from the command of my Lord, and you have not been given of knowledge except a little" (Surah Al-Isra 17:85). When the spirit reached Adam's body, he came alive.

### 4.3 The First Sneeze and the First Salam

> ☑️ **Reported in collections (e.g., Ibn Hibban, al-Hakim — graded sound by some):** When the spirit reached Adam's head, he sneezed and said *"Al-hamdu lillah"* (all praise to Allah), and Allah said to him *"Yarhamuk Allah"* (may Allah have mercy on you). This is cited as the origin of the *tashmit* (the response to a sneeze). The angels then greeted him with peace. ☑️ *(Sound supporting report.)*

### 4.4 The Knowledge That Crowned Him (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:31-33)

This is the heart of the chapter — the proof of humanity's worthiness.

> ✅ **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:31-33)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَعَلَّمَ آدَمَ الْأَسْمَاءَ كُلَّهَا ثُمَّ عَرَضَهُمْ عَلَى الْمَلَائِكَةِ فَقَالَ أَنبِئُونِي بِأَسْمَاءِ هَٰؤُلَاءِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَادِقِينَ ۝ قَالُوا سُبْحَانَكَ لَا عِلْمَ لَنَا إِلَّا مَا عَلَّمْتَنَا ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ الْعَلِيمُ الْحَكِيمُ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Wa 'allama Adama al-asma'a kullaha thumma 'aradahum 'ala al-mala'ikati faqala anbi'uni bi-asma'i ha'ula'i in kuntum sadiqin. Qalu subhanaka la 'ilma lana illa ma 'allamtana, innaka anta al-'Alimu al-Hakim.*
>
> **Translation:** "And He taught Adam the names — all of them. Then He showed them to the angels and said, 'Inform Me of the names of these, if you are truthful.' They said, 'Glory be to You! We have no knowledge except what You have taught us. Indeed, it is You who is the Knowing, the Wise.' He said, 'O Adam, inform them of their names.' And when Adam had informed them of their names, He said, 'Did I not tell you that I know the unseen of the heavens and the earth, and I know what you reveal and what you conceal?'"
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** "The names of all things" means the names and natures of all created things — the language and conceptual knowledge that underlies human civilization. The angels, despite their nearness to Allah, did not have this knowledge; their humble admission ("we have no knowledge except what You taught us") is the model response, and the **opposite** of Iblis's arrogance. Adam's ability to name everything proved *why* a knowing, choosing creature deserves to be *khalifah*: knowledge, taught by Allah, is the foundation of humanity's honour and responsibility.

## 5. The Meaning of *Khalifah* (Vicegerent)

> ✅ **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:30):** "Indeed, I will make upon the earth a *khalifah* (successive authority)."

*Khalifah* means a successor or steward — one who exercises responsibility on the earth by Allah's authority. Humanity is entrusted with the earth: to cultivate it, govern it justly, worship Allah on it, and pass the trust to the next generation. This is both an **honour** (we are stewards) and a **test** (stewards are accountable). The whole drama of the prophets and nations in this encyclopedia is the story of humanity succeeding or failing at this trust.

> ✅ **Surah Al-Ahzab (33:72):** "Indeed, We offered the Trust (*al-amanah*) to the heavens and the earth and the mountains, and they declined to bear it and feared it; but man bore it. Indeed, he was unjust and ignorant [of its weight]." — *The "Trust" is moral responsibility — the burden of free will that only humanity (and jinn) accepted.*

## 6. Authentic Hadith — Gathered and Explained

| # | Hadith | Grade | Teaching |
|---|--------|:-----:|----------|
| 1 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 3326 / Muslim 2841** — Adam created 60 cubits tall; people kept shrinking; he greeted the angels and they said "as-salamu 'alaykum wa rahmatullah" | ✅ | Adam's creation, height, and the origin of salam |
| 2 | **Sunan Abi Dawud 4693** — Adam from a handful of all the earth → human diversity | ☑️ | The basis of racial equality |
| 3 | **Sahih Muslim 2789** — *(disputed, see S1E1)* the creation across the week | ⚠️ | Treat with the caution noted in Episode 01 |
| 4 | **Sahih al-Bukhari 3336 / Muslim 2643** — the covenant; Allah took Adam's descendants from his back as a "handful" | ✅ | The primordial covenant (see §7) |
| 5 | **Sahih Muslim 2612** — "When one of you fights, let him avoid the face, for Allah created Adam in His image (*'ala suratihi*)" | ✅ | A debated text — affirm meaning, avoid tashbih (see §9) |

> ✅ **On #1 (Bukhari 3326):** The Prophet ﷺ said Allah created Adam **sixty cubits tall**, and that he was commanded to greet a group of angels and listen to their reply, for it would be his greeting and that of his descendants. They said *"As-salamu 'alayka wa rahmatullah"* (adding "and the mercy of Allah"). The hadith adds: *"everyone who enters Paradise will be in the form of Adam"* (sixty cubits) — and that creation kept diminishing in height since. This is the origin of the Islamic greeting of peace.

## 7. The Primordial Covenant (Mithaq) — A Profound Connected Event

A foundational event connected to Adam's creation: before sending humanity into the world, Allah took a covenant from all of Adam's descendants.

> ✅ **Surah Al-A'raf (7:172)**
>
> **Translation:** "And [mention] when your Lord took from the children of Adam — from their loins — their descendants and made them testify of themselves, [asking], 'Am I not your Lord?' They said, 'Yes, we have testified.' [This] — lest you say on the Day of Resurrection, 'Indeed, we were of this unaware.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Allah extracted the souls/descendants of Adam and they all bore witness that He is their Lord. This is why the recognition of God (*fitrah* — the innate disposition toward tawhid) is built into every human being. We are all born already having affirmed Allah's Lordship. (Connected hadith: "Every child is born upon the *fitrah*..." — Sahih al-Bukhari 1358.)

## 9. Weak, Disputed & Misunderstood Narrations (Labelled)

---
**📖 Clarification 1 — "Allah created Adam in His image" (Sahih Muslim 2612)**

- **The text is authentic**, but its meaning is debated. The soundest scholarly position: it affirms an *attribute relationship* without *tashbih* (comparison) — Allah gave Adam a form by His will; it does **not** mean Allah resembles a human (that would contradict 42:11, "nothing is like Him"). Some scholars say the pronoun "His" refers to Adam's own form (i.e., Adam was created complete, not evolving through stages). 
- **Verdict:** Affirm the text; **do not** use it to imagine Allah in human shape. (Pure *tashbih* is rejected.)
---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat 2 — Elaborate details of Adam's creation**

- **Narration:** Stories of the angels who fetched the soil and "argued" with the earth, the exact 40-year periods the clay lay, the order of body parts coming alive, etc.
- **Source:** Mostly Isra'iliyyat / storyteller detail.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ Not as fact. The authentic core (clay, the spirit, the names, 60 cubits) is enough.
---
**⚠️ Disputed 3 — "Adam's height of 60 cubits" interpretation**

- The hadith (Bukhari 3326) is **authentic**. What's debated is reconciling it with archaeology (ancient human remains are normal-sized). Scholarly responses: (a) it refers to Adam specifically and Paradise-forms, not a claim about all early fossils; (b) Allah's power is not bound by our findings. *Affirm the authentic text; hold the reconciliation humbly.*

## 10. Important People

**Adam (peace be upon him):** The first human, first Prophet, and father of mankind (*Abu al-Bashar*). Created from clay by Allah's hands, given the spirit, taught the names, honoured by the angels' prostration. Tall (60 cubits per Bukhari 3326). Will be the first to be addressed on the Day of Judgement and the one people first come to in intercession (Sahih al-Bukhari 4712 — the great intercession hadith). A Prophet who received revelation and taught his children to worship Allah.

**The Angels (revisited):** Their humble "we have no knowledge except what You taught us" (2:32) is the model of the believer's posture before Allah.

**Hawwa (Eve):** Adam's wife, created as his companion (detailed in Episode 07).

## 11. Important Places

- **The Garden (Al-Jannah):** Where Adam was settled after creation (Episode 07). (Scholarly difference on whether it was the eternal Paradise or a garden — discussed in Episode 08.)
- **The Earth:** The destined home of the *khalifah* and his descendants.

## 12. Important Objects / Concepts

- **The clay (*tin / salsal*):** Humanity's humble, fertile substance.
- **The Ruh (spirit):** The divine breath of life; its nature is hidden knowledge (17:85).
- **"The Names":** Language, knowledge, the capacity to learn — humanity's crown.
- **The Fitrah:** The innate disposition toward recognizing Allah, rooted in the primordial covenant (7:172).

## 13. Lessons

1. **Knowledge is humanity's honour.** Adam was elevated not by his substance but by what Allah *taught* him. Seeking beneficial knowledge is a deeply human act of fulfilling our purpose.
2. **Humility is the angelic trait; arrogance is the satanic one.** "We have no knowledge except what You taught us" (the angels) vs. "I am better than him" (Iblis). Choose the former.
3. **All races are one family.** We are literally from the same handful of earth (Abu Dawud 4693). Racism contradicts the very story of our creation.
4. **We are stewards, not owners.** The earth is an *amanah* (trust). We will be asked how we cared for it and for each other.
5. **You were made to recognize Allah.** The *fitrah* means faith is your natural state; misguidance is a deviation *from* your nature, not your nature itself.

## 14. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Adam evolved from earlier hominids."**
> ✅ The Islamic position from the texts is that Adam was a **special, direct creation** by Allah from clay, given the spirit — the first of his kind, not the product of descent from another species. (Muslims hold a range of views on biological evolution generally, but the *special creation of Adam himself* is the position grounded in the Qur'an.)

> ❌ **"'Created Adam in His image' means God looks like a man."**
> ✅ No — "nothing is like Him" (42:11). The text is affirmed without imagining resemblance. (See §9.)

> ❌ **"Adam was just a symbol/myth, not a real person."**
> ✅ The Qur'an presents Adam as a real, historical first human and Prophet, with real descendants — us. He is named 25 times.

> ❌ **"The angels objected to Adam out of jealousy."**
> ✅ Their question (2:30) was to *understand*, not to object; they immediately submitted. Jealousy/arrogance was Iblis's trait alone.

## 15. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Was Adam created in Paradise or on earth?**
A: He was created and then *settled in the Garden* (Episode 07). Whether that Garden was the eternal Paradise in the heavens or an earthly/elevated garden is a scholarly difference (Episode 08). His descent to *this* earth came after the test.

**Q: How tall was Adam really?**
A: The authentic hadith says 60 cubits (Bukhari 3326). We affirm it; the reconciliation with archaeology is held humbly (§9).

**Q: What were "the names" Adam was taught?**
A: The majority view: the names and natures of all things — the foundation of language and knowledge. It demonstrated humanity's unique capacity to learn.

**Q: Did Adam have a navel / parents?**
A: He had no parents — he was created directly. Speculation about navels etc. is idle; the texts don't address it, so neither do we.

**Q: Was Adam a Prophet?**
A: Yes. He received revelation from Allah and guided his children. He is the first Prophet, though the first *Messenger* sent to a disbelieving people is generally considered to be Nuh (Season 03).
