# Season 05 · Episode 21
# Building the Ka'bah
### The First House of Worship, and the Prayer That Summoned a Prophet

> *"And [mention] when Ibrahim was raising the foundations of the House, with Isma'il, [saying], 'Our Lord, accept this from us. Indeed, You are the Hearing, the Knowing.'"*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Baqarah (2:127)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter closes Ibrahim's season and ties his entire life together: the father and his now-grown son **Isma'il** build the **Ka'bah** — the Sacred House in Makkah toward which over a billion Muslims pray five times a day, and around which the Hajj revolves. We cover the construction, the rituals Ibrahim was taught, the **Black Stone (Hajar al-Aswad)** and the **Maqam Ibrahim**, the call to Hajj, and — most movingly — **Ibrahim's prayer for a future Messenger to arise from his descendants**, a prayer answered thousands of years later in the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. It is the bridge from the patriarch of monotheism to the final Prophet.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

Years after the events of the valley and the sacrifice, Allah commanded Ibrahim and his son **Isma'il** to **build (or rebuild/raise) the foundations of the Ka'bah** — the first house established for the worship of Allah on earth. Together, father and son laid the stones; the Qur'an captures them working and praying simultaneously: *"Our Lord, accept this from us."* When Ibrahim needed to reach higher, he stood on a stone that softened to bear him and preserved his footprint — the **Maqam Ibrahim** ("the Station of Ibrahim"), still beside the Ka'bah today. The **Black Stone** was set in its corner.

Allah then taught Ibrahim the rites of pilgrimage and commanded him to **proclaim the Hajj to all mankind** — a call that, by Allah's power, reaches every pilgrim across all generations. The Ka'bah was sanctified as a place of security and the centre of monotheistic worship — purified from idols, dedicated to Allah alone.

Most significantly, while building the House, Ibrahim made a series of profound prayers for Makkah and his descendants — including the prayer that bound his story to the very end of this encyclopedia: *"Our Lord, and send among them a Messenger from themselves, who will recite to them Your verses and teach them the Book and wisdom and purify them."* (2:129). The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ later said: *"I am the [answer to the] supplication of my father Ibrahim."* This chapter shows how the patriarch's faith planted the seed of Islam's holiest site and its final Prophet.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The First House of Worship

> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:96):** "Indeed, the first House [of worship] established for mankind was that at Bakkah (Makkah) — blessed and a guidance for the worlds."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** The Ka'bah is described as the **first house of worship** established for humanity. (Whether its foundations existed from earlier — e.g., from Adam's time — and Ibrahim *re-raised* them, or whether he founded them anew, is discussed by scholars; the Qur'an's verb "*raising the foundations*" in 2:127 suggests pre-existing foundations.) Either way, Ibrahim and Isma'il are the builders the Qur'an honours.

### 3.2 Father and Son, Building and Praying

> ✅ **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:127-128)**
>
> **Arabic:** وَإِذْ يَرْفَعُ إِبْرَاهِيمُ الْقَوَاعِدَ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ وَإِسْمَاعِيلُ رَبَّنَا تَقَبَّلْ مِنَّا ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
>
> **Transliteration:** *Wa idh yarfa'u Ibrahimu al-qawa'ida mina al-bayti wa Isma'il: Rabbana taqabbal minna, innaka anta as-Sami'u al-'Alim.*
>
> **Translation:** "And when Ibrahim was raising the foundations of the House, with Isma'il, [they prayed]: 'Our Lord, accept this from us. Indeed, You are the Hearing, the Knowing. Our Lord, and make us Muslims [in submission] to You and from our descendants a Muslim nation [submitting] to You...'"
>
> **Tafsir (as-Sa'di):** A beautiful image: the two prophets do hard physical labour — lifting stones in the desert heat — yet their hearts and tongues are turned to Allah, **begging Him to accept their work.** They don't take pride in building Allah's House; they fear it won't be accepted. This is the spirit of *ihsan*: doing great deeds while feeling utterly dependent on Allah's acceptance. They also pray for their *descendants* to be people of submission — a prayer that shapes the rest of history.

### 3.3 The Maqam Ibrahim and the Black Stone

- **Maqam Ibrahim** — Allah commands: *"And take the Station of Ibrahim as a place of prayer"* (2:125). By tradition, when Ibrahim needed to build higher, he stood on a stone that miraculously softened and rose, leaving his footprints; it stands near the Ka'bah, and pilgrims pray two units behind it after Tawaf.
- **The Black Stone (Hajar al-Aswad)** — set into the eastern corner of the Ka'bah. The Prophet ﷺ said: *"The Black Stone descended from Paradise"* (**Jami' at-Tirmidhi 877**, *graded sound by many*). 'Umar (may Allah be pleased with him) famously kissed it and said: *"I know you are only a stone that can neither benefit nor harm. Had I not seen the Prophet ﷺ kiss you, I would not have kissed you"* (**Sahih al-Bukhari 1597**) — a crucial statement of *tawhid*: even the most honoured stone is **not** worshipped; we follow the Sunnah, not the stone.

### 3.4 The Call to Hajj

> ✅ **Surah Al-Hajj (22:26-27)**
>
> **Translation:** "And [mention] when We designated for Ibrahim the site of the House, [saying], 'Do not associate anything with Me, and purify My House for those who circumambulate (Tawaf), stand [in prayer], and bow and prostrate. And proclaim to the people the Hajj; they will come to you on foot and on every lean camel; they will come from every distant pass.'"
>
> **Tafsir:** Ibrahim was told to **call humanity to the pilgrimage** — and though his voice was that of one man in an empty valley, Allah caused that call to echo across all time. **Every pilgrim who has ever circled the Ka'bah is, in a sense, answering Ibrahim's call.** The core purpose is stated first: *"do not associate anything with Me, and purify My House"* — the Ka'bah is the monument of pure *tawhid*, to be kept free of all idolatry.

### 3.5 The Prayers of Ibrahim for Makkah

While building, Ibrahim made several supplications recorded in the Qur'an (2:126-129; 14:35-41):
- For **Makkah to be a city of security** (2:126; 14:35).
- For its people to be **provided with fruits** despite the barren valley (2:126; 14:37) — answered as Makkah became a trade and pilgrimage hub.
- To **keep him and his sons away from worshipping idols** (14:35) — the constant fear of the monotheist.
- And the climactic prayer:

> ✅ **Surah Al-Baqarah (2:129):** "Our Lord, and send among them a Messenger from themselves who will recite to them Your verses and teach them the Book and wisdom and purify them. Indeed, You are the Exalted in Might, the Wise."

## 4. "I Am the Supplication of My Father Ibrahim" (The Great Connection)

This is the verse that links Ibrahim directly to the final Prophet — and to the entire second half of this encyclopedia.

> ✅ **Musnad Ahmad / authenticated narrations** — The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: **"I am the supplication of my father Ibrahim, and the glad tidings of [my brother] 'Isa..."** (referring to 2:129 and to Isa's prophecy in 61:6). *Graded sound (sahih li-ghayrihi) by scholars.*
>
> **Explanation:** Thousands of years before the Prophet ﷺ was born, Ibrahim — standing in the desert of Makkah, lifting the stones of the Ka'bah — prayed for a Messenger to arise *from his descendants* to teach his people. That Messenger was **Muhammad ﷺ**, a direct descendant of Isma'il, born in the very city Ibrahim built around. When the Prophet ﷺ began calling to Allah at the Ka'bah (Seasons 11-12), he was the **living answer to Ibrahim's prayer.** This single thread connects the patriarch of monotheism (Season 05) to the seal of the prophets (Seasons 11-14) — the spine of the whole story.

## 6. Lessons

1. **Worship Allah while begging Him to accept it.** Ibrahim and Isma'il built His House yet prayed "accept this from us" (2:127). Never let pride enter your good deeds; fear for their acceptance keeps the heart humble.
2. **The Ka'bah is for *tawhid*, not stone-worship.** 'Umar's words at the Black Stone (Bukhari 1597) are the essence: we honour what the Prophet ﷺ honoured, but we worship **only Allah.** The Ka'bah unites Muslims toward one Lord; it is not itself worshipped.
3. **A sincere prayer can echo across millennia.** Ibrahim's call to Hajj reached every pilgrim ever; his prayer for a Messenger reached Muhammad ﷺ. Your sincere du'a may bear fruit far beyond your lifetime.
4. **Allah builds greatness from barren places.** A waterless valley became the spiritual centre of the world. Do not measure Allah's plans by present appearances.
5. **Legacy is built on faith and submission.** Ibrahim's monuments were not for his own glory but for the worship of Allah and the guidance of generations. That is the legacy that lasts.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Muslims worship the Ka'bah / the Black Stone."**
> ✅ **Absolutely not.** Muslims worship **Allah alone** and merely *face* the Ka'bah for unity in prayer. 'Umar declared the Black Stone "neither benefits nor harms" — we kiss it only following the Prophet's ﷺ example, not because it has power (Bukhari 1597). Worshipping the stone would be the very *shirk* the House was built to oppose.

> ❌ **"Ibrahim built the Ka'bah from nothing as the very first structure ever."**
> ✅ The Qur'an says he was **"raising the foundations"** (2:127), suggesting pre-existing foundations (some say from Adam's era). It is the "first House of worship for mankind" (3:96), built/re-raised by Ibrahim and Isma'il.

> ❌ **"The footprint in the Maqam Ibrahim is worshipped or is the actual relic to bow to."**
> ✅ The Maqam is honoured as a place of prayer ("take it as a place of prayer," 2:125), not an object of worship. Reverence ≠ worship.

> ❌ **"Hajj is a pagan custom Islam adopted."**
> ✅ Hajj traces to **Ibrahim's** divinely commanded pilgrimage (22:27). The pagans later corrupted it with idols; the Prophet ﷺ *restored* it to its original pure monotheism (Season 14).

## 8. Weak / Isra'iliyyat Narrations (Labelled)

---
**⚠️ Isra'iliyyat / Tradition 1 — Adam first building the Ka'bah; angels building it before Adam**

- **Narration:** That Adam built the original Ka'bah (or the angels built it before him), that it was raised to the heavens during the Flood, etc.
- **Source:** Tradition and some reports without firm marfu' chains.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ The Qur'an credits **Ibrahim and Isma'il** with raising the foundations (2:127); pre-Ibrahim building accounts are traditional, not firmly established. The phrase "raising the foundations" *allows* for earlier foundations, but the details are not certain.
---
**⚠️ Tradition 2 — Detailed dimensions and the exact placement process of the Black Stone by Ibrahim**

- **Source:** Tradition; the famous account of the Prophet ﷺ himself placing the Black Stone (resolving a tribal dispute) is from his *pre-prophetic* life and is authentic (Season 11), but Ibrahim's exact process is less documented.
- **Rely on it?** ⚠️ Honour the Qur'anic essentials; treat construction minutiae as secondary.

## 9. Important People

**Ibrahim (peace be upon him):** Builder of the Ka'bah, proclaimer of Hajj, and the one whose prayers shaped Makkah and summoned the final Prophet. His season ends with him as the father of monotheism, whose legacy is the holiest site in Islam.

**Isma'il (peace be upon him):** Co-builder of the Ka'bah, ancestor of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and the Arabs. His line carries the prophetic mission to its conclusion.

**The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ (foreshadowed):** The answer to Ibrahim's prayer (2:129) — descended from Isma'il, born in Makkah, who would restore the Ka'bah to pure *tawhid*.

## 12. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: Did Ibrahim build the Ka'bah from scratch?**
A: The Qur'an says he was "raising the foundations" (2:127), implying pre-existing foundations (tradition links these to Adam). He and Isma'il are the honoured builders.

**Q: Do Muslims worship the Ka'bah or the Black Stone?**
A: **No.** They worship Allah alone and *face* the Ka'bah for unity. 'Umar declared the Black Stone powerless (Bukhari 1597); it is honoured only in following the Prophet ﷺ.

**Q: What is the Maqam Ibrahim?**
A: The "Station of Ibrahim" — the stone bearing his footprint, which Allah commanded be taken as a place of prayer (2:125). Pilgrims pray behind it after Tawaf.

**Q: How is Ibrahim connected to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ?**
A: The Prophet ﷺ is a direct descendant of Isma'il and the **answer to Ibrahim's prayer** (2:129); he said, "I am the supplication of my father Ibrahim." Ibrahim built the city and House where the final revelation would begin.

**Q: Why do Muslims face Makkah in prayer?**
A: As a unifying qiblah commanded by Allah — turning the worship of all Muslims worldwide toward the first House of pure monotheism, built by Ibrahim.
