# Season 14 · Episode 55
# The Final Days
### The Passing of the Messenger ﷺ — and the Legacy That Remained

> *"Muhammad is not but a messenger. Messengers have passed before him. So if he was to die or be killed, would you turn back on your heels?"*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Aal 'Imran (3:144)**

## How To Use This Chapter

This is the **final chapter of the entire encyclopedia.** It covers the **last illness and passing of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ (11 AH / 632 CE)** in Madinah, his final words and instructions, the grief and steadfastness of the Companions (especially Abu Bakr's historic speech), and — most importantly — the **legacy that remained**: a completed religion, a preserved Qur'an, a transformed Arabia, and a message of *tawhid* that would reach the ends of the earth. It closes the story that began "before time existed" (Season 1) and ties the entire encyclopedia together.

## 1. Episode Summary

A few months after the Farewell Pilgrimage (Episode 54), the Prophet ﷺ fell ill with what would be his **final illness** — a severe fever, in **11 AH (632 CE)**, when he was about **63**. Sensing his time, he gave his **final instructions**: he emphasized the **prayer (salah)** and the **just treatment of those under one's authority** ("the prayer, the prayer, and those whom your right hands possess"); he asked forgiveness from anyone he might have wronged; he prohibited taking graves as places of worship (guarding *tawhid* to his last breath — echoing his warning about Nuh's people, Season 3); and he affirmed that **a servant had been given the choice between this world and what is with Allah, and had chosen what is with Allah** (the Companions later understood he was speaking of himself).

In his final days, too weak to lead, he appointed **Abu Bakr** to lead the prayers — a sign of his successor. He passed away in the room of his beloved wife **'A'ishah**, his head resting against her, with his final words being: ***"O Allah, [with] the highest companions"*** (*Allahumma, ar-Rafiq al-A'la*) — choosing the company of Allah and the prophets.

His death struck the Muslims with overwhelming grief; some, like **'Umar**, could not accept it. Then **Abu Bakr** delivered the words that steadied the entire ummah: he confirmed the Prophet ﷺ had died, and declared — *"Whoever worshipped Muhammad, then Muhammad has died; but whoever worshipped Allah, then Allah is Ever-Living and never dies"* — reciting **Qur'an 3:144.** This re-anchored the community on **worship of Allah alone**, not the person of the Prophet ﷺ — the very *tawhid* that was his entire message.

The Prophet ﷺ left behind no wealth, but an immeasurable legacy: a **perfected religion** (5:3), the **preserved Qur'an**, his **Sunnah**, a transformed and united Arabia, and a community that would, within decades, carry the message of *tawhid* across the world. This chapter — and this encyclopedia — closes where it must: with the death of the final Messenger and the eternal life of the One he called us to worship.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Final Illness and Instructions

A few months after the Farewell Pilgrimage, the Prophet ﷺ was struck by a severe **fever** — his final illness. Knowing his end was near, he focused on what mattered most. Among his **final instructions** (preserved in authentic narrations):

> ✅ **Final emphasis on prayer and justice (Sunan / Musnad Ahmad — well-known):** In his last moments, the Prophet ﷺ repeatedly urged: ***"The prayer, the prayer (*as-salah, as-salah*), and those whom your right hands possess (dependents/servants)"*** — emphasizing the *salah* (the pillar prescribed in the heavens, Season 12) and *justice and kindness to the vulnerable* as the parting priorities of the faith.

> ✅ **Guarding tawhid to the end (Sahih al-Bukhari 435 / Muslim 531):** Even on his deathbed, the Prophet ﷺ warned: ***"The curse of Allah is upon the Jews and Christians who took the graves of their prophets as places of worship"*** — warning his ummah against the very doorway to *shirk* (over-veneration of graves, the origin of idolatry in Nuh's people, Season 3). His last concern was protecting *tawhid*.

> ✅ **The choice given (Sahih al-Bukhari 4435-4436):** He said that ***"a servant has been given the choice between this world and what is with Allah, and he has chosen what is with Allah."*** Only Abu Bakr understood — and wept — realizing the Prophet ﷺ was speaking of **himself**, choosing to return to his Lord.

He also asked the Companions if he had wronged anyone, offering to make amends — humble and just to the very end.

### 3.2 Appointing Abu Bakr to Lead

As his illness worsened and he became too weak to come to the mosque, the Prophet ﷺ instructed: ***"Tell Abu Bakr to lead the people in prayer"*** (**Sahih al-Bukhari 664 / Muslim 418**). 'A'ishah, his wife (Abu Bakr's daughter), worried this would be too hard for her grieving father, but the Prophet ﷺ insisted. This was understood by the Companions as a clear **sign of his chosen successor** — for the one entrusted to lead the prayer was fittest to lead the community. Abu Bakr led the prayers in the Prophet's ﷺ final days.

### 3.3 The Passing — "The Highest Companions"

The Prophet ﷺ passed away in **Rabi' al-Awwal, 11 AH (632 CE)**, at about **63 years of age**, in the room and against the chest of his beloved wife **'A'ishah** (may Allah be pleased with her). She reported his final moments:

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 4437-4438 / Muslim 2444:** In his last moments, he raised his gaze and said: ***"O Allah, [join me with] the Highest Companions (*Allahumma, ar-Rafiq al-A'la*)"*** — three times — choosing the company of Allah, the prophets, the truthful, and the martyrs (cf. 4:69). Then his soul departed. 'A'ishah noted he died on a day when his head was between her chin and chest.
>
> **Tafsir/lesson:** Even his final breath was a turning *toward Allah* — choosing nearness to his Lord over remaining in this world. His death was the death of a human being (as the Qur'an emphasizes), returning to the One who sent him.

### 3.4 The Grief — and Abu Bakr Steadies the Ummah

The news shattered the Muslims. The man who had transformed their lives, led them from darkness to light, was gone. Some were paralyzed with grief; **'Umar ibn al-Khattab**, in his anguish, even **drew his sword and declared the Prophet ﷺ had not died** but had gone to his Lord temporarily (as Musa did), threatening anyone who said otherwise. The young community teetered on the edge of crisis.

Then **Abu Bakr as-Siddiq** arrived. He went in, uncovered the Prophet's ﷺ face, kissed him, and said, *"You are pure in life and in death."* Then he came out to the people and delivered the speech that **saved the ummah** from collapsing into person-worship:

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 1241-1242, 3667-3668:** Abu Bakr said: ***"O people, whoever used to worship Muhammad — then Muhammad has died. But whoever worshipped Allah — then Allah is Ever-Living and never dies."*** Then he recited:
>
> ✅ **Surah Aal 'Imran (3:144):** *"Muhammad is not but a messenger. Messengers have passed before him. So if he was to die or be killed, would you turn back on your heels? And whoever turns back on his heels will never harm Allah at all; but Allah will reward the grateful."*
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** This was the **decisive moment that re-anchored Islam on pure *tawhid*.** 'Umar later said: *"By Allah, it was as if I had never heard this verse until Abu Bakr recited it"* — the people were stunned into clarity. Abu Bakr's point was the **entire message of the encyclopedia**: the Prophet ﷺ was a **human messenger** who would die like all before him (Adam, Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa — all passed); the One who **never dies** is **Allah**, and He alone is to be worshipped. The community was saved from the gravest error — making the Prophet ﷺ into an object of worship — and redirected to the *tawhid* he had spent his life teaching. (Shortly after, the Companions chose **Abu Bakr as the first Caliph (*Khalifah*)**, beginning the era of the Rightly-Guided Caliphs.)

### 3.5 The Legacy That Remained

The Prophet ﷺ left **no wealth or property** to inherit (prophets do not bequeath wealth — Season 8; Bukhari 1470). What he left was infinitely greater:
- **A perfected religion** (5:3) — Islam, complete and whole.
- **The preserved Qur'an** — memorized in the hearts of thousands of Companions and written down, **protected by Allah** (*"Indeed, it is We who sent down the Reminder, and indeed, We will be its Guardian"* — 15:9) — unchanged to this day.
- **His Sunnah** — his example, preserved in the hadith.
- **A transformed Arabia** — from feuding, idolatrous *Jahiliyyah* (Season 11) to a unified ummah worshipping the One God, in a single generation.
- **A community of Companions** — who, within decades, carried *tawhid* to Persia, Byzantium, Africa, and beyond — fulfilling the universal mission Ibrahim's prayer began (Season 5).

> 📖 **The closing reflection:** The Prophet ﷺ was *"a mercy to the worlds"* (21:107) and the **seal of the prophets** (*Khatam an-Nabiyyin*, 33:40) — the last in the chain that began with Adam. His mission was not to build a personal kingdom but to **restore the worship of the One God** — the same *tawhid* declared "before time existed" (Season 1), carried by every prophet, and now completed and preserved for all humanity until the end of time.

## 5. The Themes — The Closing of the Story

1. **Even the greatest Messenger is a mortal servant** (3:144). The Prophet ﷺ died like every prophet before him — Adam, Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa (and Isa will too, after his return). **Only Allah is Ever-Living.** This guards *tawhid* against deifying any human.
2. **The message, not the man, is eternal.** The Prophet ﷺ passed, but the **perfected religion (5:3)** and the **preserved Qur'an (15:9)** remain — Allah's guidance secured for all time.
3. **Tawhid to the last breath.** His final concerns — the prayer, justice to dependents, and the warning against grave-worship — were all about preserving the pure worship of Allah. He guarded *tawhid* even while dying.
4. **Death is a return to Allah** ("the Highest Companions"). The believer's end is a turning toward the Lord — the Prophet ﷺ chose nearness to Allah over the world.
5. **The legacy of a mercy to the worlds** (21:107). In 23 years, the Prophet ﷺ transformed humanity — leaving not wealth, but guidance, a community, and a message that would reach the ends of the earth.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Worship Allah, not people — even the righteous.** Abu Bakr's words (3:144) are the eternal safeguard: attach your worship to the **Ever-Living Allah**, who never dies, not to any human, however beloved. This is the heart of *tawhid* and the lesson of the whole encyclopedia.
2. **Prioritize prayer and justice — to your last breath.** The Prophet's ﷺ final instructions ("the prayer, the prayer, and those under your authority") show what matters most: connection to Allah and justice to the vulnerable.
3. **Guard against the doorways to shirk.** His deathbed warning about graves teaches lifelong vigilance over pure worship — even good intentions (venerating the righteous) can decay into *shirk* (Season 3).
4. **Face loss with faith, not collapse.** Abu Bakr's steadiness in catastrophe is the model: grief is human, but the believer holds to Allah, who remains when all else passes.
5. **Build a legacy of guidance, not just wealth.** The Prophet ﷺ left no money, but a perfected faith and a transformed world. Invest in what outlasts you — knowledge, righteousness, and benefit to others.
6. **You are an heir to this message.** The Prophet ﷺ said, "Convey from me, even a verse" — the trust of *tawhid*, completed at his passing, now rests with every believer, including you.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"The Prophet ﷺ did not really die / is divine."**
> ✅ The Qur'an is emphatic: *"Muhammad is not but a messenger... if he was to die or be killed..."* (3:144); *"Indeed, you are to die, and indeed, they are to die"* (39:30). He was a **mortal human servant** of Allah. Abu Bakr's speech established this against the early grief-driven denial. **Only Allah never dies.**

> ❌ **"Muslims worship Muhammad ﷺ."**
> ✅ **Absolutely not** — Abu Bakr's very first act after the Prophet's ﷺ death was to declare: *"Whoever worshipped Muhammad, he has died; whoever worshipped Allah, He never dies"* (3:144). Muslims worship **Allah alone**; the Prophet ﷺ is His servant and messenger, deeply loved and followed, but never worshipped.

> ❌ **"Islam ended or was lost when the Prophet ﷺ died."**
> ✅ The religion was **perfected** before his death (5:3), the **Qur'an preserved** (15:9), and the community carried it onward. The mission continued through the Companions to the whole world.

> ❌ **"He left great wealth and a kingdom for his family."**
> ✅ He left **no wealth to inherit** (prophets don't bequeath property). His legacy was the religion, the Qur'an, his Sunnah, and a transformed ummah — guidance, not gold.

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: When and how did the Prophet ﷺ die?**
A: In **Rabi' al-Awwal, 11 AH (632 CE)**, at about **63**, after a severe fever, in the room of his wife 'A'ishah. His final words were "O Allah, [with] the Highest Companions."

**Q: What were his final instructions?**
A: To safeguard the **prayer** and be just to **those under one's authority**; to ask forgiveness from any he may have wronged; and a warning against **taking graves as places of worship** (guarding *tawhid*).

**Q: What did Abu Bakr say when the Prophet ﷺ died?**
A: *"Whoever worshipped Muhammad — he has died. Whoever worshipped Allah — Allah is Ever-Living and never dies,"* reciting 3:144 — re-anchoring the ummah on the worship of Allah alone.

**Q: Do Muslims worship the Prophet ﷺ?**
A: **No.** Abu Bakr's first act after his death made this explicit. Muslims **love and follow** the Prophet ﷺ but **worship Allah alone** — the very *tawhid* the Prophet ﷺ taught.

**Q: What did the Prophet ﷺ leave behind?**
A: No wealth, but an immeasurable legacy: a **perfected religion** (5:3), the **preserved Qur'an** (15:9), his **Sunnah**, a united Arabia, and a community that carried *tawhid* to the world.
