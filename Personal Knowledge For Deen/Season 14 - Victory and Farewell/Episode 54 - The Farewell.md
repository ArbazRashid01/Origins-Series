# Season 14 · Episode 54
# The Farewell
### The Final Pilgrimage and the Sermon That Summed Up Everything

> *"This day I have perfected for you your religion and completed My favour upon you and have approved for you Islam as religion."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:3)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter covers the **Farewell Pilgrimage (Hajjat al-Wada', 10 AH)** and the **Farewell Sermon (Khutbat al-Wada')** — the Prophet's ﷺ only Hajj, before ~100,000+ Muslims, where he summarized the entire message of Islam in a single, timeless address: human equality, sanctity of life and property, women's rights, the end of *Jahiliyyah*, the abolition of usury and blood-feuds, and holding fast to the Qur'an and Sunnah. It is also where Allah declared the religion **perfected and complete (5:3)** — signalling the mission's fulfillment, just before the Prophet's ﷺ final days (Episode 55).

---

## 1. Episode Summary

In **10 AH (632 CE)**, with Arabia largely united under Islam, the Prophet ﷺ performed his **first and only Hajj** — the **Farewell Pilgrimage** — accompanied by over **100,000 Muslims.** It was the practical demonstration of the rites of Hajj that Ibrahim had first proclaimed (Season 5), now restored to pure *tawhid* and performed by the Prophet ﷺ himself, who said: *"Take your rites of pilgrimage from me."*

At **'Arafat**, the spiritual peak of Hajj, he delivered the **Farewell Sermon** — a concise summation of Islam's core principles, addressed to all of humanity through the assembled multitude. Its themes included:
- **The sanctity of life, property, and honour** — inviolable, "like the sanctity of this day, this month, this land."
- **The complete abolition of *Jahiliyyah*** — all blood-feuds of the pre-Islamic era cancelled, all **usury (riba)** abolished, beginning with that of his own family.
- **Human equality** — *"no Arab is superior to a non-Arab, nor a white to a black, except by *taqwa* (righteousness)"* — one of history's clearest declarations against racism.
- **The rights of women** — enjoining kind and just treatment of wives, reminding men they have rights *and* responsibilities.
- **Brotherhood** — Muslims are brothers; one's life and property are sacred to another.
- **The two things left behind** — *"I have left among you that which, if you hold fast to it, you will never go astray: the Book of Allah (Qur'an)"* (and in other narrations, his Sunnah).
- **The accountability before Allah** — *"You will surely meet your Lord, and He will ask you about your deeds."*
- He repeatedly asked the crowd, *"Have I conveyed [the message]?"* They said yes — and he said, *"O Allah, bear witness."*

It was during this pilgrimage (at 'Arafat) that the climactic verse was revealed: **"This day I have perfected for you your religion..."** (5:3) — Allah declaring the message **complete.** The Companions sensed this signalled the Prophet's ﷺ mission was ending. This chapter explores the pilgrimage, the sermon, and the perfection of the religion.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Only Hajj

By **10 AH**, Islam had spread across Arabia, and the Ka'bah had been cleansed (Episode 53). The Prophet ﷺ announced he would perform **Hajj** — his first and, as it turned out, only one. Over **100,000 Muslims** (some narrations say more) gathered to perform it with him. He demonstrated each rite precisely — the *talbiyah* (*"Labbayk Allahumma Labbayk"*), the *tawaf*, the *sa'i*, the standing at 'Arafat, Muzdalifah, Mina, the stoning, the sacrifice — saying: ***"Take your rites of pilgrimage from me, for I do not know whether I will perform Hajj after this year"*** (**Sahih Muslim 1297**). This established the Hajj as Muslims perform it to this day — **fulfilling the pilgrimage Ibrahim first proclaimed** (Season 5, 22:27), now in its perfected, monotheistic form.

### 3.2 The Farewell Sermon at 'Arafat

On the **Day of 'Arafah** (the 9th of Dhul-Hijjah), the spiritual climax of Hajj, the Prophet ﷺ addressed the vast assembly from atop his camel — the **Farewell Sermon (Khutbat al-Wada')**, preserved across authentic narrations (**Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, Musnad Ahmad**). Its principles are the **summation of Islam's social and ethical teaching:**

> ✅ **On the sanctity of life and property (Sahih al-Bukhari 1739 / Muslim 1679):** *"Your blood, your property, and your honour are sacred to one another, like the sanctity of this day of yours, in this month of yours, in this land of yours."*

> ✅ **On abolishing Jahiliyyah (Sahih Muslim 1218):** *"Every matter of Jahiliyyah is under my feet (abolished). The blood-vengeance of the Jahiliyyah is abolished... and the usury (riba) of Jahiliyyah is abolished, and the first riba I abolish is that of [my uncle] al-'Abbas."* — *He began the abolition with his own family, modeling that the law applies first to oneself.*

> ✅ **On human equality (Musnad Ahmad 23489 — well-known):** *"O people, your Lord is One, and your father (Adam) is one. There is no superiority of an Arab over a non-Arab, nor a non-Arab over an Arab, nor of a white over a black, nor a black over a white — **except by *taqwa* (righteousness)**."* — *One of the clearest anti-racism declarations in human history, rooting human equality in common origin (echoing the creation of Adam from all the earth, Season 2) and Qur'an 49:13.*

> ✅ **On the rights of women (Sahih Muslim 1218 / Tirmidhi 1163):** *"Fear Allah regarding women, for you have taken them as a trust from Allah... they have rights over you, and you have rights over them. Treat them well."*

> ✅ **On what he left behind (Sahih Muslim 1218 / Muwatta):** *"I have left among you that which, if you hold fast to it, you will never go astray: **the Book of Allah**"* (and in the famous narration: *"the Book of Allah and my Sunnah"*).

> ✅ **The conveyance and the witness (Sahih al-Bukhari 1741):** Repeatedly he asked, *"Have I conveyed [the message]?"* The crowd answered, "Yes!" And he raised his finger to the sky and said: ***"O Allah, bear witness"*** (*Allahumma fashhad*) — three times.
>
> **Tafsir/lesson:** The sermon is the **constitution of Islamic ethics** — establishing the inviolability of life, property, and dignity; the equality of all humanity; the rights of women; the abolition of exploitation (riba) and tribal vengeance; and the anchoring of the ummah in the **Qur'an and Sunnah.** The Prophet ﷺ delivered it as a *trust to be conveyed* to all generations — and we, reading it now, are among those it reached.

### 3.3 The Perfection of the Religion

During this pilgrimage, at 'Arafat, came one of the last and most momentous verses of the Qur'an:

> ✅ **Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:3)**
>
> **Arabic:** الْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِي وَرَضِيتُ لَكُمُ الْإِسْلَامَ دِينًا
>
> **Translation:** "This day I have perfected for you your religion and completed My favour upon you and have approved for you Islam as religion."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** This verse declared that the **religion was complete** — the message that began with Adam, was carried by every prophet (Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa...), and was sealed by Muhammad ﷺ, was now **perfected and whole.** Nothing was to be added to it. When 'Umar heard this verse, he is reported to have **wept** — recognizing that *what is perfected and complete has reached its end*, and that the Prophet's ﷺ time was drawing near. This verse is also a foundation against innovation (*bid'ah*): a perfected religion needs no additions.

> ✅ **On 5:3 (Bukhari 45):** A Jewish man told 'Umar that if such a verse ("This day I have perfected your religion") had been revealed to *them*, they would have made that day a festival. 'Umar replied that he knew **the day and place it was revealed**: the Day of 'Arafah, a Friday, during the Farewell Pilgrimage — both of which are *already* days of celebration for the Muslims. This authentically anchors the verse to the Farewell Hajj — the moment Islam was declared **complete.**

## 5. The Themes

1. **Islam is complete and perfected** (5:3). The message from Adam to Muhammad ﷺ reached its fulfillment — nothing to add, nothing to remove. The whole arc of this encyclopedia culminates here.
2. **Human equality is foundational.** "No Arab over a non-Arab... except by *taqwa*" — Islam's clearest declaration that worth is by **righteousness**, not race, colour, or lineage (49:13; echoing Adam from all the earth, S2).
3. **Sanctity of life, property, and honour.** These are inviolable trusts — the bedrock of a just society.
4. **The end of exploitation and vengeance.** *Jahiliyyah*'s blood-feuds and usury (riba) are abolished — the Prophet ﷺ starting with his own family, modeling that justice begins at home.
5. **Hold fast to the Qur'an and Sunnah.** The two anchors against misguidance — the Prophet's ﷺ parting trust to the ummah.
6. **Conveying the message is a trust.** "Have I conveyed? O Allah, bear witness" — the responsibility now passes to every generation, including us.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Your faith is complete — live it fully.** Allah perfected the religion (5:3); seek guidance within it, and beware adding to it. What the Prophet ﷺ left suffices.
2. **Judge people only by righteousness.** The Farewell Sermon abolished racism and tribalism forever — "except by *taqwa*." Treat all people as equals before Allah.
3. **Honour the rights of others — especially the vulnerable.** Life, property, honour, and the rights of women are sacred trusts. Justice and kindness are not optional.
4. **Apply the law to yourself first.** The Prophet ﷺ abolished *his own family's* riba first. Lead by example; hold yourself to the standard before others.
5. **Hold fast to the Qur'an and Sunnah.** "You will never go astray" if you cling to them — the parting guidance for the whole ummah.
6. **You have received the message — now convey it.** "O Allah, bear witness" — the trust the Companions carried now reaches you; live it and pass it on.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Islam was incomplete or evolved after the Prophet ﷺ."**
> ✅ Allah declared it **perfected and complete** during the Farewell Pilgrimage (5:3). The religion was whole at the Prophet's ﷺ passing — a foundation against later innovations (*bid'ah*).

> ❌ **"Islam doesn't address racism / human equality."**
> ✅ The Farewell Sermon is one of history's **clearest anti-racism declarations**: "no Arab over a non-Arab, no white over a black, except by *taqwa*" (cf. 49:13). Equality is foundational, not incidental.

> ❌ **"Islam ignores women's rights."**
> ✅ The Prophet ﷺ devoted part of his final, most important sermon to **enjoining the kind and just treatment of women** and affirming their rights — a central, not peripheral, concern.

> ❌ **"The Farewell Sermon is a later invention."**
> ✅ Its core is preserved in the **most authentic collections** (Bukhari, Muslim — esp. Jabir's detailed account, Muslim 1218), delivered before 100,000+ witnesses. It is among the best-documented speeches in antiquity.

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was the Farewell Pilgrimage?**
A: The Prophet's ﷺ **first and only Hajj** (10 AH), performed with 100,000+ Muslims, where he demonstrated the rites and delivered the Farewell Sermon — the summation of Islam.

**Q: What did the Farewell Sermon establish?**
A: The sanctity of life, property, and honour; the **abolition of Jahiliyyah** (blood-feuds, usury); **human equality** ("no superiority except by *taqwa*"); the **rights of women**; brotherhood; and holding fast to the **Qur'an and Sunnah.**

**Q: What does "This day I have perfected your religion" (5:3) mean?**
A: That Islam — the message from Adam to Muhammad ﷺ — was **completed and perfected**; nothing is to be added. A foundation against innovation.

**Q: Why did 'Umar weep at this verse?**
A: Because he understood that **what is perfected and complete has reached its end** — signalling the Prophet's ﷺ mission, and life, were drawing to a close.

**Q: How is the sermon relevant to us today?**
A: The Prophet ﷺ commanded those present to **convey it to those absent** — so its message of equality, justice, and faith reaches every generation, including ours. We are among those it was meant for.
