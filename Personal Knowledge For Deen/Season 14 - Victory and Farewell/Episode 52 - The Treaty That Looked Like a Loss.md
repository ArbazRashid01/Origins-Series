# Season 14 · Episode 52
# The Treaty That Looked Like a Loss
### Hudaybiyyah — The "Defeat" That Allah Called a Clear Victory

> *"Indeed, We have given you a clear conquest (*fath*)."*
> — **Qur'an, Surah Al-Fath (48:1)**

---

## How To Use This Chapter

This chapter opens the final season with one of the most profound lessons in the Seerah: the **Treaty of Hudaybiyyah (6 AH)** — an agreement whose terms seemed humiliating to the Muslims, which many Companions struggled to accept, yet which Allah called a **"clear victory" (*fath mubin*)** — and which proved, within two years, to be exactly that. It teaches that **Allah's wisdom often hides in what looks like loss**, and that trust in the Prophet ﷺ and patience open doors no battle could.

---

## 1. Episode Summary

In **6 AH**, the Prophet ﷺ saw a true dream that he and the Muslims entered Makkah and performed the pilgrimage. So he set out with ~1,400 unarmed Companions in *ihram* (pilgrim garb), intending **'Umrah** (the lesser pilgrimage) — not war. Quraysh, fearing the loss of face if the Muslims entered Makkah, blocked them at **Hudaybiyyah** (on the outskirts). After tense negotiations — during which the Companion **'Uthman** was sent into Makkah and rumored killed, prompting the famous **Pledge of Ridwan** (the Companions pledging under a tree to fight to the death, which Allah praised in 48:18) — a **treaty** was concluded.

Its terms seemed **deeply unfavourable** to the Muslims:
1. The Muslims would **return this year without performing 'Umrah**, and could come the next year for only three days.
2. A **ten-year truce.**
3. Any Makkan who fled to the Muslims would be **returned** to Quraysh; but any Muslim who defected to Quraysh would **not** be returned.
4. Even the document's wording was conceded — Quraysh refused "Muhammad the Messenger of Allah" and "Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Rahim," so the Prophet ﷺ allowed "Muhammad ibn 'Abdullah" and "Bismika Allahumma."

The Companions were **distraught** — even 'Umar questioned it: "Are we not upon the truth? Then why should we accept humiliation in our religion?" The Prophet ﷺ calmly affirmed: *"I am the Messenger of Allah, and I will not disobey Him, and He will not let me be lost."* On the return journey, Allah revealed **Surah Al-Fath**: *"Indeed, We have given you a clear conquest"* (48:1). And so it proved: the **truce** brought peace, allowing Islam to be **heard and to spread rapidly** (more people accepted Islam in the two years after Hudaybiyyah than in all the years before); it gave the Muslims security and prestige as an equal power; and Quraysh's own violation of it would lead directly to the **Conquest of Makkah** (Episode 53). The "loss" was the doorway to the greatest victory. This chapter explores the treaty, the Companions' test, and Allah's hidden wisdom.

## 3. The Story

### 3.1 The Journey for 'Umrah

The Prophet ﷺ saw a **true dream** (a form of revelation) that the Muslims entered the Sacred Mosque safely (later referenced in 48:27). So in **6 AH**, he set out with ~**1,400 Companions**, deliberately **unarmed for war** (only travelers' swords), wearing *ihram* and driving sacrificial animals — making unmistakably clear they came for **worship ('Umrah), not battle.** This put Quraysh in a bind: to block pilgrims from the Sacred House would shame them before all Arabia.

### 3.2 The Pledge of Ridwan

Quraysh barred the Muslims at **Hudaybiyyah**. Negotiations went back and forth. The Prophet ﷺ sent **'Uthman ibn 'Affan** into Makkah to assure Quraysh of their peaceful intent — but 'Uthman was delayed, and a **rumour spread that he had been killed.** At this, the Prophet ﷺ called the Companions to a **pledge under a tree** — to **stand firm and fight to the death** to avenge 'Uthman if needed. This was the **Bay'at ar-Ridwan** (the Pledge of [Allah's] Pleasure):

> ✅ **Surah Al-Fath (48:18)**
>
> **Translation:** "Certainly was Allah pleased with the believers when they pledged allegiance to you under the tree, and He knew what was in their hearts, so He sent down tranquillity (*sakinah*) upon them and rewarded them with an imminent conquest."
>
> **Tafsir:** Allah Himself declared His **pleasure (*ridwan*)** with the ~1,400 who took this pledge — a unique honour; they are forever "the People of the Tree." ('Uthman turned out to be alive.) The pledge demonstrated the Companions' total devotion — willing to die for the Prophet ﷺ and their faith.

### 3.3 The Treaty and Its Hard Terms

A treaty was then negotiated with the Quraysh envoy Suhayl ibn 'Amr. Its terms appeared **one-sided against the Muslims:**
1. **Return this year** without 'Umrah; come next year for **three days** only.
2. A **ten-year truce** (cessation of war).
3. **Asymmetry on defectors:** a Qurayshi who came to the Muslims (without their guardian's permission) must be **returned**; a Muslim who defected to Quraysh would **not** be returned.
4. **The document's wording:** Suhayl objected to *"Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Rahim"* (demanding "Bismika Allahumma") and to *"Muhammad the Messenger of Allah"* (demanding "Muhammad ibn 'Abdullah"). The Prophet ﷺ **conceded both** — even erasing "Messenger of Allah" himself (when 'Ali declined to).

> ✅ **Sahih al-Bukhari 2731-2732 (the full account):** The Companions, who had come expecting to enter Makkah, were **deeply distressed** by these terms. **'Umar ibn al-Khattab** went to the Prophet ﷺ and the agitated, asking: *"Are you not truly the Messenger of Allah? Are we not upon the truth and our enemies upon falsehood? Then why should we accept this humiliation in our religion?"* The Prophet ﷺ replied with calm certainty: ***"I am the Messenger of Allah, I do not disobey Him, and He is my Helper."*** ('Umar later said he gave much charity in expiation for his agitation that day.)

### 3.4 "A Clear Victory" — Allah's Hidden Wisdom

On the journey back to Madinah, deflated and confused, the Muslims received a stunning revelation:

> ✅ **Surah Al-Fath (48:1-3)**
>
> **Arabic:** إِنَّا فَتَحْنَا لَكَ فَتْحًا مُّبِينًا
>
> **Translation:** "Indeed, We have given you a clear conquest (*fath mubin*), that Allah may forgive you... and complete His favour upon you and guide you to a straight path, and that Allah may aid you with a mighty victory."
>
> **Tafsir (Ibn Kathir):** Allah called the *treaty itself* a **"clear conquest"** — astonishing the Companions who saw only concession. And within **two years**, its wisdom became undeniable:
> - **Peace opened hearts.** The ten-year truce ended the constant warfare; Arabs could now travel, hear the Qur'an, and reason about Islam freely. **More people accepted Islam in the two years after Hudaybiyyah than in the entire 19 years before it** — including future giants like **Khalid ibn al-Walid** and **'Amr ibn al-'As.** Scholars note the Muslims roughly **doubled or more** in number in this period.
> - **The "defector" clause backfired on Quraysh.** Muslims forced to return to Makkah (like Abu Jandal and Abu Basir) formed a band that disrupted Quraysh's trade caravans until **Quraysh themselves begged to cancel that clause.**
> - **Recognition as an equal power.** Quraysh negotiating a treaty *acknowledged* the Muslims as a sovereign state, not rebels — a huge gain in legitimacy and prestige.
> - **It set up the Conquest.** When Quraysh later **violated the truce** (attacking a tribe allied with the Muslims), it gave the Prophet ﷺ the just cause to march on Makkah — which fell **peacefully** (Episode 53).
>
> **The lesson is monumental:** what the Companions experienced as **humiliating defeat** was, in Allah's plan, the **greatest opening** — the very thing that made the conquest of Makkah and the spread of Islam possible. *"Perhaps you hate a thing and it is good for you"* (2:216).

## 5. The Themes

1. **Allah's wisdom hides in apparent loss.** The treaty looked like humiliation but was "a clear conquest" (48:1). *"Perhaps you hate a thing and it is good for you"* (2:216). Judge outcomes by Allah's measure, not by appearances.
2. **Trust the guidance even when you don't understand.** 'Umar's distress was honest, but the Prophet's ﷺ "I do not disobey Him, and He is my Helper" was the model — submitting to Allah's command when its wisdom isn't yet visible (echoing Nuh's ark, Ibrahim's sacrifice).
3. **Peace can achieve what war cannot.** The truce let Islam be *heard*, and it spread faster in peace than in years of conflict. Da'wah flourishes in calm and open dialogue.
4. **Devotion proven in crisis** (the Pledge of Ridwan, 48:18). The Companions' willingness to die earned Allah's explicit pleasure.
5. **Patience and flexibility over pride.** The Prophet ﷺ conceded wording and terms (even erasing "Messenger of Allah") for the *greater long-term good* — wisdom over ego.

---

## 6. Lessons

1. **Don't judge by appearances — trust Allah's plan.** What feels like a setback may be Allah opening a far greater door. "Perhaps you hate a thing and it is good for you" (2:216). Be patient through confusing "losses."
2. **Submit to guidance even when the wisdom is hidden.** Like the Prophet ﷺ at Hudaybiyyah, trust that Allah "will not let you be lost" — obedience precedes understanding.
3. **Choose long-term good over short-term pride.** The Prophet ﷺ accepted humbling terms for a greater future. Swallowing pride for a wiser outcome is strength, not weakness.
4. **Peace and patience are powerful tools.** Sometimes the path to victory is a treaty, not a battle; dialogue, not confrontation. Open hearts, don't just win fights.
5. **Stand firm with the truthful in crisis** (the Pledge of Ridwan). Loyalty proven under pressure earns Allah's pleasure.

## 7. Misconceptions — Corrected

> ❌ **"Hudaybiyyah was a humiliating defeat for the Muslims."**
> ✅ Allah called it **"a clear conquest" (48:1)**, and history proved it: the truce let Islam spread so fast that the Muslims multiplied many times over within two years, gaining the strength that took Makkah peacefully. It was a **strategic triumph in disguise.**

> ❌ **"The Prophet ﷺ was weak to accept such terms / erase 'Messenger of Allah.'"**
> ✅ It was **wisdom and far-sighted leadership** — conceding minor points for an immense long-term gain. His confidence was in Allah ("He will not let me be lost"), not in winning the moment's argument.

> ❌ **"'Umar's questioning shows the Companions doubted Islam."**
> ✅ 'Umar's distress was **honest zeal**, not doubt in the faith — he wanted no humiliation for Islam. He submitted to the Prophet ﷺ and later regretted his agitation. It shows the Companions were human, and the value of trusting the Prophet's ﷺ wisdom.

> ❌ **"Islam advances only through war."**
> ✅ Hudaybiyyah proves the opposite — **peace** achieved what battles hadn't, letting the message be heard. The greatest expansion came through a *truce*, not a fight.

## 11. Questions People Usually Ask (FAQ)

**Q: What was the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah?**
A: A **ten-year truce** (6 AH) with Quraysh, with terms that seemed unfavourable to the Muslims (returning without 'Umrah that year, returning Makkan defectors), which Allah nonetheless called **"a clear conquest"** (48:1).

**Q: Why did Allah call a "humiliating" treaty a victory?**
A: Because the **peace** it brought let Islam spread rapidly (the Muslims multiplied many times over in two years), gained them recognition as a power, and set up the peaceful Conquest of Makkah. The wisdom was hidden but immense.

**Q: What was the Pledge of Ridwan?**
A: When 'Uthman was rumored killed, ~1,400 Companions **pledged under a tree** to fight to the death — and **Allah declared His pleasure with them** (48:18).

**Q: Why did the Prophet ﷺ erase "Messenger of Allah" from the document?**
A: As a **concession** for the greater long-term good, trusting Allah. It cost nothing real (he *was* the Messenger of Allah regardless) and secured a treaty that became a triumph.

**Q: What's the main lesson of Hudaybiyyah?**
A: **Trust Allah's wisdom over appearances** — "perhaps you hate a thing and it is good for you" (2:216). Apparent setbacks can be the doorway to your greatest victories.
