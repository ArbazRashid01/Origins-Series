**2 Reels + 1 Documentary (~16 min — the most intimate season)**
*Theme: The two doors Allah always leaves open — patience (sabr) and prayer (dua). Grouped by theme, not strict chronology.*

# PART A — REEL SCRIPTS
---

## 🎬 S9 • E35 — "THE MAN WHO LOST EVERYTHING" *(Ayyub)*
**Citations:** Qur'an 21:83 (his dua) · 21:84 (restored, doubled) · 38:41 (Shaytan's touch) · 38:42 (the cool spring) · 38:43 (family doubled) · 38:44 (patient, excellent servant).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Usne apna maal kho diya. Apni aulaad. Apni sehat. Aur ek baar bhi Allah se shikaayat nahi ki."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Ayyub had wealth, family, health — then lost it all, plus a long illness. For years he was patient, no complaint. His dua had no grievance, only adab: "adversity has touched me, and You are the most merciful." *Climax:* Allah healed him (a cool spring) and restored his family doubled — "We found him patient; what an excellent servant." *Reflection:* sabr isn't silence in pain — it's keeping good thoughts of Allah (husn al-zann); when everything goes, never let that go.
**🔄 Open loop:** Patience saved Ayyub; another prophet's impatience landed him in a whale. → E36.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** The Qur'an doesn't detail his illness — graphic "worms" versions are Isra'iliyyat (avoid).

## 🎬 S9 • E36 — "SWALLOWED BY THE SEA" *(Yunus)* — *Finale*
**Citations:** Qur'an 21:87 (left in anger; the dua in darkness) · 37:139–142 (the fish) · 37:143–144 (had he not glorified Allah…) · 37:145–146 (the shore + the plant) · 37:147–148/10:98 (his people believed) · Bukhari ("none better than Yunus").
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Wo apni qaum se umeed haar kar chala gaya. Aur khud ko paaya — ek machhli ke pet mein, teen andheron ke beech."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Yunus left his people before Allah's permission. At sea, lots were cast; he was swallowed by a great fish. Three darknesses — night, sea, the fish's belly. *Climax:* the famous dua — "La ilaha illa Anta, Subhanaka, inni kuntu min az-zalimeen" — admitting fault, not blaming others; Allah saved him. Cast ashore, ill, with a plant grown over him; his people (100,000+) believed. *Reflection:* no darkness blocks the way to Allah — admit your fault, turn back, and watch Him make a way.
**🔄 Open loop (→ S10):** From prophets tested by suffering, to a different miracle — an old man's prayer, a chosen woman, a fatherless child.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Yunus's departure was a slip he repented (he was a noble prophet). "Three darknesses" = the common tafsir of *zulumat* (21:87).

# PART B — YOUTUBE DOCUMENTARY

## 📌 Title: "When God Tests Those He Loves" · ⏱️ ~15–17 min *(intimate, focused)*

**Overview:** Two stories at the heart of every believer's struggle — a man who lost everything and never complained, and a prophet who found Allah in the deepest darkness. The two doors: *sabr and dua.*

### 🎥 COLD OPEN
A bare tree; an underwater silence; a faint glow of prayer. *"Aksar, jinse Allah sabse zyada pyaar karta hai, unhe sabse zyada aazmaata hai… jab koi raasta nazar na aaye, insaan kya kare?"* TITLE.

### 🎙️ INTRODUCTION
Note: this season is grouped by **theme** (divine tests), not strict timeline (Ayyub's era uncertain). The Prophet ﷺ said the most severely tested are the prophets, then those like them (Tirmidhi, hasan/sahih). Timeline bar "steps aside."

### CHAPTER 1 — The Patience of Ayyub *(E35)* — *sabr* & *husn al-zann*; the complaint-free dua; restoration. *Illness undescribed — Isra'iliyyat avoided.* **Why it matters:** real patience = good expectation of Allah in pain.
### CHAPTER 2 — The Prayer in the Dark *(E36)* — the slip & sincere return; the dua; the unique mercy to his people (10:98); *"none better than Yunus"* (Bukhari). **Why it matters:** no darkness blocks the way to Allah — admit fault and return.

### 🏁 FINALE
Recap. Main lesson: when pain comes from outside → *sabr*; when from your own fault → *dua* + return. Being tested isn't a sign of Allah's anger — often the opposite. → Season 10 (a miracle birth).

### 🎞️ POST-CREDIT TEASER (Season 10)
A quiet temple; a radiant light by a palm. *"Ek bachcha jiski koi misaal nahi — bina baap ke paida hua — aur jab logon ne uski maa par ungli uthaayi, us bachche ne paalne se baat ki. Isa (A.S.) ki sachhi kahani — Islam ki nazar se."*

### 🎨 Visual Direction
Navy/black + gold; **somber deep gold** (+ stormy teal for Yunus). Restrained, intimate. Signature: the bare tree + cool spring; the three nested darknesses with one glow of dhikr. Aniconism (illness/swallowing implied). Honesty cards: illness undescribed; thematic grouping.
### 🔊 Sound Direction
Vocals-only nasheed + ambient. Aching, stripping-away tone → tender dua → water-burst relief; a crushing underwater silence → one growing dhikr pulse → release on the shore.
### 📚 References Used
**Qur'an:** 21:83–84 · 38:41–44 · 21:87–88 · 37:139–148 · 10:98. **Hadith:** Tirmidhi (the most tested are the prophets — hasan/sahih); Bukhari ("none better than Yunus"). **Flagged:** Ayyub's illness details (avoided); "three darknesses" (tafsir); thematic grouping.
