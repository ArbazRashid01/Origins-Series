**4 Reels + 1 Documentary (~33 min)**
*Theme: "The best of stories" (12:3) — patience, chastity, forgiveness, and a divine plan hidden in every wound.*

> Almost entirely from Surah Yusuf (12) — rich recorded dialogue, low Isra'iliyyat risk.

# PART A — REEL SCRIPTS
---

## 🎬 S6 • E22 — "THE DREAM & THE JEALOUS BROTHERS"
**Citations:** Qur'an 12:4 (the dream) · 12:5 (Yaqub's warning) · 12:8 (resentment) · 12:9–10 (the plot; the well) · 12:11–14.
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Usne apne bhaiyon ko ek khwaab sunaya. Aur us ek khwaab ne unhe use maar daalne par majboor kar diya."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Young Yusuf's dream — 11 stars, sun & moon prostrating. Yaqub: don't tell your brothers, Shaytan is a clear enemy. The brothers' envy → hatred; they plotted to kill or cast him away; one said "throw him in a well." They asked their father to send him "to play." *Reflection:* the same envy that ruined Iblis blinds even blood-brothers — guard your heart.
**🔄 Open loop:** Only the brothers returned — with a bloodied shirt. → E23.

## 🎬 S6 • E23 — "SOLD INTO SLAVERY"
**Citations:** Qur'an 12:15 (the well; Allah's reassurance) · 12:16–18 (false story; *sabrun jameel*) · 12:19 (the caravan) · 12:20 (sold cheap) · 12:21 (the house in Egypt).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Apne hi sage bhaiyon ne use kuein mein phenk diya. Aur phir chand sikkon mein bech diya."*
**🎙️ Narration:** In the well, Allah reassured Yusuf he'd one day remind them of this. The brothers brought a false "wolf" story; Yaqub: "rather your souls enticed you — beautiful patience (sabrun jameel)." A caravan found him; sold in Egypt; bought by a powerful man who told his wife to honor him. *Reflection:* what looks worst hides the next door — the well led to Egypt; trust Allah's unseen plan.
**🔄 Open loop:** The most powerful woman in the house desired him; the doors were locked. → E24.

## 🎬 S6 • E24 — "TEMPTATION & PRISON"
**Citations:** Qur'an 12:22–23 ("Ma'adhAllah") · 12:24 (he was protected) · 12:25–29 (the torn shirt) · 12:30–32 (the women) · 12:33–35 (chose prison).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Use sab kuch mil sakta tha. Lekin usne gunaah ke badle jail chuni."*
**🎙️ Narration:** The temptation; his refusal — "Ma'adhAllah!" He fled; the shirt tore from behind (proving innocence). The women cut their hands in awe; she threatened prison; Yusuf prayed prison was dearer than sin. *Reflection:* real strength is what you give up for Allah — even when no one is watching.
**🔄 Open loop:** Years forgotten in prison — until the king had a dream no one could explain. → E25.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** The woman is **unnamed in the Qur'an** ("Zulaykha" = tradition). Avoid romanticized folklore. 12:24: Yusuf was divinely protected & did not sin.

## 🎬 S6 • E25 — "FROM PRISONER TO POWER" *(Finale)*
**Citations:** Qur'an 12:36–42 (prison da'wah; dreams) · 12:43 (the king's dream) · 12:46–49 (interpretation) · 12:50–53 (clearing his name; "I do not absolve myself") · 12:54–57 · 12:92 (forgiveness) · 12:100 (the dream fulfilled).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Ek bhula hua qaidi… Misr ka doosrа sabse taaqatwar aadmi ban gaya. Aur wajah bani — ek khwaab."*
**🎙️ Narration:** In prison he called to tawhid and interpreted dreams; the freed man forgot him; he stayed years. The king's dream (7 fat/lean cows, 7 green/dry ears); Yusuf interpreted (store for 7, famine 7, then relief). He first cleared his name (the women confessed). Rose to power; the famine brought his brothers — unrecognized; he forgave: "no blame on you today." The childhood dream came true. *Reflection:* every sorrow was a step to the throne; when life is shattered, Allah is building something beautiful; forgiveness is the greatest strength.
**🔄 Open loop (→ S7):** His family settled in Egypt; generations later a Pharaoh enslaved their descendants and ordered baby boys killed — and a mother placed her baby in a river.

## 📌 Title: "Yusuf: From the Well to the Throne" · ⏱️ ~32–34 min *(one continuous emotional epic)*

**Overview:** Allah called it "the best of stories" and told it as one complete tale. A boy betrayed, enslaved, falsely accused, imprisoned — who never let go of Allah and rose to rule Egypt. Spine: *Allah's timing is never late, never wasted.*

### 🎥 COLD OPEN
Children's laughter → a shaft of light into a dark well; flashes (11 bowing stars, a bloody shirt, locking doors, prison bars, a granary, brothers bowing). *"Allah ne ise 'sabse khoobsoorat kahani' kaha… jab tumhari poori zindagi tukde tukde ho jaaye — kya tab bhi Allah ka plan chal raha hota hai?"* TITLE.

### 🎙️ INTRODUCTION
Recap S5; Ibrahim → Ishaq → Yaqub → Yusuf. The unique full-surah narrative ("Ahsan al-Qasas," 12:3). Timeline → "YAQUB'S HOUSE."

### CHAPTER 1 — The Dream *(E22)* — the dream; a father who understood; envy → plot. **Why it matters:** don't envy Allah's gifts to others; ask Him instead.
### CHAPTER 2 — The Well and the Caravan *(E23)* — Allah's reassurance; *sabrun jameel* (grief without complaint); sold; Allah's hidden hand. **Why it matters:** beautiful patience = trusting Allah even in loss.
### CHAPTER 3 — The Test of Chastity *(E24)* — divine protection (12:24); the torn-shirt verdict; iffah; choosing hardship over sin. **Why it matters:** real strength is what you renounce for Allah when no one sees.
### CHAPTER 4 — From the Well to the Throne *(E25)* — prison da'wah; clearing his name (justice + dignity); "I do not absolve myself" (12:53); the masterclass in forgiveness; the dream fulfilled. **Why it matters:** Allah builds beauty from broken pieces; forgiveness is the highest power.

### 🏁 FINALE
Recap. Main lesson: trust + patience + forgiveness. Yusuf's final dua (12:101) — not for power, but "make me die as a Muslim and join me with the righteous." → Season 7 (Musa): a baby in a river.

### 🎞️ POST-CREDIT TEASER (Season 7)
A basket on a river; pyramids; a staff, a burning bush, a splitting sea. *"Ek baadshah jo khud ko khuda kehta tha… Agle season mein — taareekh ka sabse bada takraao."*

Navy/black + gold; **regal blue + gold** accent. Signature: 11 bowing stars (set up → fulfilled); shaft in the well; torn-shirt verdict; granary + bowing brothers. Aniconism. Honesty card: woman unnamed; Yusuf protected (12:24).
