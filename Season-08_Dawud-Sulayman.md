# PART A — REEL SCRIPTS

## 🎬 S8 • E32 — "THE BOY WHO KILLED A GIANT"
**Citations:** Qur'an 2:246 (asking for a king) · 2:247 (Talut chosen for merit) · 2:249 (the river test) · 2:249–250 ("how often a small group…" + dua) · 2:251 (Dawud kills Jalut) · 4:163/34:10–11/21:79 (Zabur; glorifying mountains; softened iron).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Ek poori fauj ek dैt ke saamne darr se jam gayi. Aur ek charwaaha ladka ek ghulel ke saath aage badhа."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Bani Israel asked for a king; Allah chose Talut (for knowledge & strength, not wealth). The river test thinned the army to the faithful few; facing Jalut's host they prayed for patience. Young Dawud killed Jalut; Allah gave him kingship, wisdom, and the Zabur — mountains & birds glorified with him; iron softened for him. *Reflection:* victory comes from yaqeen, not numbers; your weakness becomes strength with Allah.
**🔄 Open loop:** His son would inherit a power no human ever had. → E33.
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Talut/Jalut = Saul/Goliath; reject Isra'iliyyat slander of Dawud. *Doc add:* Dawud's fast & recitation (Bukhari/Muslim).

## 🎬 S8 • E33 — "THE KING WHO COMMANDED THE WIND"
**Citations:** Qur'an 27:16 (inherited; bird speech) · 27:17 (his ranks) · 27:18–19 (the ant; gratitude) · 21:81/34:12–13 (wind; jinn) · 38:35 (the unique kingdom) · 27:20 (the missing hoopoe).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Wo cheentiyon se baat kar sakta tha, hawa ko hukum de sakta tha. Uski fauj mein insaan, jinn, aur parinde shaamil the."*
**🎙️ Narration:** Sulayman inherited from Dawud — taught the speech of birds; armies of men, jinn, and birds. An ant warned its colony; Sulayman smiled and thanked Allah ("enable me to be grateful"). The wind and jinn served him; he prayed for a kingdom no one after him would have. *Reflection:* power is a test — gratitude vs. arrogance; the more you're given, the bigger your responsibility.
**🔄 Open loop:** The missing hoopoe returned with news of a sun-worshipping queen. → E34.

## 🎬 S8 • E34 — "THE QUEEN OF SHEBA" *(Finale)*
**Citations:** Qur'an 27:22–23 (the hoopoe's news) · 27:24 (sun-worship) · 27:35–36 (the gift refused) · 27:38–40 (the throne in a blink) · 27:44 (the glass floor; her submission) · 34:14 (Sulayman's death).
**🎣 HOOK:** *"Usne palak jhapakne se pehle ek malka ka poora takht mangwa liya — sirf use sach dikhaane ke liye."*
**🎙️ Narration:** The hoopoe reported Saba's queen — given everything, a great throne, but worshipping the sun. Sulayman's letter; she sent a gift to test him; he refused it ("what Allah gave me is better"). One with knowledge of the Book brought her throne in the blink of an eye; she mistook the glass floor for water; then submitted — "I submit with Sulayman to Allah." *Reflection:* real power guides, doesn't crush; true greatness is bowing to the truth.
**🔄 Open loop (→ S9):** Even Sulayman couldn't escape death — he died on his staff; the jinn didn't know until a termite ate it (proving jinn don't know the unseen). Next: a different test — suffering (Ayyub & Yunus).
**⚠️ Guardrail:** Queen **unnamed** ("Bilqis" = tradition); throne-mover unnamed; avoid all Isra'iliyyat folklore about her.

## 📌 Title: "Dawud & Sulayman: The Kingdom of the Prophets" · ⏱️ ~24–27 min

**Overview:** Two prophet-kings given power unlike anyone before — a shepherd who toppled a giant and whose voice made mountains sing, and his son who commanded wind, jinn, and animal speech. Spine: *power is a test, not a trophy.*

A giant's shadow; flashes (sling; glorifying mountains; an ant pausing an army; the wind carrying a throne; a queen bowing on glass; a king on a staff). *"Jab Allah kisi ko itni taaqat de ke wo hawa ko hukum de sake — kya wo use Allah ke qareeb laati hai, ya door?"* TITLE.
